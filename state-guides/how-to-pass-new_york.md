# How to Pass Your New York Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the New York State Office of Children and Family Services (OCFS), Division of Child Care Services. If your center is inside New York City, you have a second regulator: the NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DOHMH), Bureau of Child Care. **NYC centers carry two permits and get inspected by two agencies.** Annual unannounced monitoring is required under the federal CCDBG Act. Complaint inspections are always unannounced.

- OCFS home: https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/
- OCFS inspection search: https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/looking/
- NYC Child Care Connect: https://a816-healthpsi.nyc.gov/ChildCare/childCareList.action

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — New York Social Services Law § 390. This is the statute that requires every day care to be licensed or registered. [https://law.justia.com/codes/new-york/sos/article-6/title-1/390/](https://law.justia.com/codes/new-york/sos/article-6/title-1/390/)
- **Day Care Centers rulebook (13+ children)** — 18 NYCRR Subpart 418-1. [https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-york/title-18/chapter-II/subchapter-C/article-2/part-418/subpart-418-1](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-york/title-18/chapter-II/subchapter-C/article-2/part-418/subpart-418-1)
- **Small Day Care Centers** — 18 NYCRR Subpart 418-2.
- **Group Family Day Care Homes** — 18 NYCRR Part 416.
- **Family Day Care Homes** — 18 NYCRR Part 417.
- **School-Age Child Care** — 18 NYCRR Part 414.
- **OCFS inspection and enforcement explainer (PDF)** — https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/assets/docs/DCCS-Inspections-Violations-Enforcement.pdf
- **NYC only: NYC Health Code Article 47** — applies to every center sited in the five boroughs, on top of state rules.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Under 6 weeks | 1:3 | 6 |
| 6 weeks – 18 months | 1:4 | 8 |
| 18 – 36 months | 1:5 | 12 |
| 3 years | 1:7 | 18 |
| 4 years | 1:8 | 21 |
| 5 years | 1:9 | 24 |
| 6 – 9 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 10 – 12 years | 1:15 | 30 |

Inspectors count heads on the spot. Children must be within the teacher's line of sight at all times. No one under 18 may supervise alone. Staff may not use personal devices for entertainment while supervising.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive unannounced and sign in.
2. They check the license or registration is posted.
3. They walk each room and count children against staff.
4. They check that teachers have children in line of sight.
5. They pull child records: emergency forms, medical statements, immunizations.
6. They pull staff records: fingerprint clearances, SCR checks, training hours.
7. They review medication logs and incident reports.
8. They check fire exits, drills, and the written emergency plan.
9. They close out and post findings to the facility's Program Profile on the OCFS portal within 1–3 weeks.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio and line-of-sight failures** — 18 NYCRR § 418-1.8.
2. **Missing or expired SCR (State Central Register) check** or fingerprint clearance for a staff member.
3. **Missing child medical statement** (must be within 30 days of admission, updated annually for kids under 6).
4. **Training hour shortfalls** — 15 pre-service plus 30 every 2 years under 18 NYCRR § 418-1.14.
5. **Mandated reporter training** not completed before unsupervised contact.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **OCFS-5188** — Child Enrollment / Emergency Information Record.
- **OCFS-LDSS-7002** — Child's Medical Statement (within 30 days of admission; updated annually for kids under 6).
- **Immunization record** per Public Health Law § 2164 (DTaP, polio, MMR, varicella, Hib, PCV, hep B, flu for preschool).
- **Signed authorization for emergency medical treatment.**
- **Signed permission slips** for transportation, field trips, photos, and web use.
- **Parent handbook acknowledgement.**
- **Daily attendance log.**
- **Incident/accident reports** signed by a parent.
- **OCFS-LDSS-4441** — Medication Administration Record when applicable.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **OCFS-6001** — Staff/Volunteer Application with 3 references.
- **OCFS-6002** — Staff Medical Statement + TB screening (within 30 days of hire; updated every 2 years).
- **OCFS-6003** — fingerprint-based criminal history check.
- **OCFS-4930** — Statewide Central Register (SCR) child-abuse database check.
- **Mandated reporter training** — Social Services Law § 413 — completed before unsupervised contact.
- **15 hours pre-service health and safety training** plus **30 hours every 2 years** (18 NYCRR § 418-1.14).
- **CPR and first aid** — at least one current-certificate holder on site at all times.
- **Shaken Baby Syndrome training** for staff working with children under 3.
- **Aspire registry** profile.

## What must be on your walls
- **Current license or registration** — posted where parents see it.
- **Most recent inspection report** or a link parents can find.
- **Emergency evacuation plan** in each room.
- **Staff qualifications** summary.
- **Parent rights / mandated reporter notice.**

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** monthly, documented.
- **Shelter-in-place / evacuation** drills per the written emergency plan.
- **Daily attendance log.**
- **Medication administration log** for every dose.
- **Incident / accident reports** same-day with parent signature.
- **Fire safety inspection** annually by the local fire authority.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Is the license posted?
2. Count each room. Do you meet ratio and line-of-sight right now?
3. Are staff phones put away?
4. Pull three child files. Is the medical statement within date and immunizations current?
5. Pull three staff files. SCR check, fingerprint, and medical statement — all current?
6. Is the 15-hour pre-service on file for every staff member?
7. Is the fire-drill log current for this month?
8. Is the medication cabinet locked?
9. Are outlet covers in place in infant / toddler rooms?
10. If you're in NYC — is your Article 47 permit posted too?

## What happens if you fail
OCFS uses three classes of violations: **Class I** (serious), **Class II** (public health), **Class III** (minor). Class II and III can carry fines. OCFS publishes a running Enforcement Action list of facilities on probation, suspension, or pending revocation. Your Program Profile shows every violation for **the prior 2 years**. Source: OCFS "Inspections, Violations, and Enforcement" explainer (https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/assets/docs/DCCS-Inspections-Violations-Enforcement.pdf).

## The weird New York gotcha nobody tells you about
If you run a center in the five boroughs of New York City, you are **regulated twice** — by OCFS under 18 NYCRR Part 418, and by NYC DOHMH under NYC Health Code Article 47. That means two permits, two inspection schedules, and two public records to keep clean. DOHMH publishes every inspection daily on NYC Open Data — it refreshes faster than OCFS does. Out-of-state operators who expand into NYC routinely miss the DOHMH permit and get cited on day one.
