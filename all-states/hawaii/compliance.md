# Hawaii — Child Care Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority

**Hawaii Department of Human Services (DHS), Benefit, Employment & Support Services Division (BESSD), Child Care Program — Child Care Licensing Units.**

DHS contracts with **PATCH (People Attentive to Children) Hawaii** as the statewide Child Care Resource & Referral (CCR&R) agency and administers the DHS Early Childhood Registry through PATCH.

Statewide Child Care Licensing Units (by island/area):
- Oahu Unit 1 (Downtown Honolulu): (808) 587-5266
- Oahu Unit 2 (Waipahu / West Oahu): (808) 675-0470
- East Hawaii (Hilo): (808) 981-7290
- West Hawaii (Kona), Maui, Kauai units each report to BESSD

## Primary Regulatory Framework

Rules are codified in **Hawaii Administrative Rules (HAR) Title 17 — Department of Human Services, Subtitle 6 — BESSD.** Active facility-type chapters:

- **HAR 17-891.2** — Registration of Family Child Care Homes
- **HAR 17-892.1** — Licensing of Group Child Care Centers and Group Child Care Homes
- **HAR 17-895.1** — Licensing of Infant and Toddler Child Care Centers
- **HAR 17-896** (before/after school facilities — repealed; school-age care now rolled into center rules)

Enabling statute: **Hawaii Revised Statutes (HRS) Chapter 346, Part V (§346-151 et seq.)** — Child Care Facilities.

## Facility Types Regulated

| Facility Type | HAR Chapter | Capacity |
|---|---|---|
| Family Child Care Home (registered) | 17-891.2 | Up to 6 children |
| Group Child Care Home (licensed) | 17-892.1 | 7–12 children |
| Group Child Care Center (licensed, ages 2+) | 17-892.1 | 13+, site-specific |
| Infant and Toddler Child Care Center | 17-895.1 | 6 weeks–36 months |

Note: Hawaii's **Group Child Care Center** rules do **not** permit care for children under 24 months — infants and toddlers must be served in a licensed **Infant/Toddler Child Care Center** under HAR 17-895.1.

## Key Licensing Forms

- **DHS 1300 / 1301 series** — Child Care Center License Application
- **DHS 1305** — Family Child Care Home Registration Application
- **DHS 1306** — Group Child Care Home License Application
- **PATCH Registry Application** — staff education/training evaluation (required before staff can count toward ratios)
- **Form 314-C** — Criminal History Check / CANIS clearance request
- **HRS Chapter 321-22 Physician's Statement** — child & staff TB clearance

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes

**Group Child Care Center (HAR 17-892.1) — ages 2 and older only:**

| Age | Staff:Child Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| 2 years | 1:8 | 8 |
| 3 years | 1:12 | 12 |
| 4 years | 1:16 | 16 |
| 5+ years | 1:20 | 20 |

Ratio is determined by **age of the youngest child in the group** when ages are mixed.

**Infant and Toddler Child Care Center (HAR 17-895.1):**

| Age | Staff:Child Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| 6 weeks–12 months | 1:3 to 1:4 | 6–8 |
| 12–24 months | 1:3 to 1:4 | 6–12 |
| 18–36 months | 1:5 to 1:6 | 10–12 |

**Family Child Care Home:** 1:2 for infants under 12 months; 1:6 for older children; maximum 6 total.

## Required Child Records

Each child's file must contain:
- Enrollment application (parent-completed; includes emergency contacts, authorized pickup)
- Up-to-date immunization record per **HRS §302A-1154** and DOH requirements (DTaP, IPV, MMR, Hepatitis B, Varicella, Hib, PCV)
- Tuberculosis (TB) clearance — physician-signed Form 314 (once before entry)
- Physical examination / health assessment on admission (Form 14)
- Authorization for emergency medical care
- Signed field-trip / transportation permissions
- Food allergy / dietary restriction notes
- Daily attendance records
- Accident / incident reports

## Required Staff Records

Each employee file must contain:
- DHS Early Childhood Registry listing (PATCH-issued) with verified education/training
- Criminal history check (state & FBI fingerprint) and Child Abuse & Neglect Registry (CANIS) clearance — required for all staff, volunteers, and household members 18+
- TB clearance (annual/biennial per DOH)
- First Aid & CPR (infant/child) — current certification
- Annual 16-hour in-service training log (per DHS rules)
- Orientation records (policies, mandated reporter)
- Copy of degree/ECE credential (for lead teachers)

## Inspection Cadence

- **Pre-license on-site inspection** by DHS licensing worker before any license is issued
- **Annual on-site monitoring visit** during the license year
- **Unannounced visits** in response to complaints or follow-up
- Fire, sanitation, and building inspections conducted by county (Honolulu, Hawaii, Maui, Kauai) fire marshals and Department of Health

## Renewal Cadence

Licenses are issued for **one year** and must be **renewed annually** for the first four years. After four years of clean performance a license may be renewed on a **two-year** cycle (conditional). Renewal applications are due **30 days before expiration**.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Infant and toddler care is a separate license** (HAR 17-895.1) and cannot be offered under a Group Center license.
- **Ratios have no formal group-size ceiling** at most ages — a center may have 1 staff per 20 five-year-olds in the same room.
- **Staff qualifications are evaluated by PATCH**, not by DHS directly — a staff member must have a current Early Childhood Registry evaluation before counting toward required staff-to-child ratios.
- **TB clearance is required for staff and children**; this is unusual among U.S. states.
- **Hawaii is an island jurisdiction** — licensing unit coverage is divided by island, which adds travel/logistics burden for state inspectors and can lengthen complaint response times.
- **HRS §346-153.5** authorizes DHS to collect and publish licensing history (inspection reports, citations) via the Hawaii Child Care Provider Search Portal.
