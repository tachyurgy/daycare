# How to Pass Your South Carolina Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **South Carolina Department of Social Services (DSS), Division of Early Care and Education** — which operates publicly as "SC Child Care Services." DSS is the sole state licensing agency for child care. You get **at least one unannounced annual inspection** (S.C. Code § 63-13-520). Complaints trigger unannounced visits anytime. Every deficiency cited in the last **36 months** is posted on your public provider page at scchildcare.org, with a severity code (**High / Medium / Low**). Public sites:
- SC Child Care Services: https://www.scchildcare.org/
- Public provider search (with compliance tab): https://www.scchildcare.org/search.aspx

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — S.C. Code § 63-13-20 through § 63-13-1100 (Child Care Licensing Law). https://www.scstatehouse.gov/code/t63c013.php
- **The rulebook inspectors use** — S.C. Regs. Chapter 114, Article 5, Subarticle 1:
  - **R. 114-500 series** — Licensed Child Care Centers (13+ children).
  - **R. 114-510 series** — Group Child Care Homes (7–12 children).
  - **R. 114-520 series** — Family Child Care Homes (≤6 children).
- **Full chapter PDF** — https://www.scstatehouse.gov/coderegs/Chapter%20114.pdf
- **Ratio reference card (DSS 2966)** — https://dss.sc.gov/resource-library/forms_brochures/files/2966.pdf
- **Background check authority** — S.C. Code § 63-13-180.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From Regulation 114-503 / 114-504. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age | One staff for every... |
|---|---|
| Birth – 1 year | 5 |
| 1 – 2 years | 6 |
| 2 – 3 years | 8 |
| 3 – 4 years | 12 |
| 4 – 5 years | 17 |
| 5 – 6 years | 20 |
| 6 – 12 years | 23 |

Ratios apply **at all times children are present, including off-site**. Space rule: 35 sq ft indoors per child; 75 sq ft outdoors. Water activities, transport, and sleep periods have extra supervision rules (R. 114-514).

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced.
2. Checks license is posted and current. Confirms required postings (license, last inspection report, evacuation plan, complaint number).
3. Walks every room, counts children, compares to staff present.
4. Pulls staff files — **SLED + FBI fingerprint + Central Registry** clearance first.
5. Pulls child files — DSS 3086 enrollment, immunization, allergy plan.
6. Reviews fire / health inspection reports on file (DSS 2905).
7. Exit. Deficiencies post on your scchildcare.org provider page within days, tagged **High / Medium / Low**.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
Per the **Children's Trust of South Carolina 4-year analysis of DSS records** (which counted **8,537 serious violations statewide over 4 years**):
1. **Improper supervision** — **3,044 high-severity violations** topped the list.
2. **Out-of-ratio** — **2,605 high-severity violations** came second.
3. **Background check gaps** — SLED, FBI, and Central Registry must all be complete **before** unsupervised child contact (S.C. Code § 63-13-180). Conditional hiring is not allowed.
4. **Health Assessment (DSS 2926) missing** — must cover operator, all caregivers, and household members 16+ in home settings.
5. **Annual 15 hours of in-service training** not logged.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **DSS 3086** — Child's Enrollment Form (or provider equivalent).
- **Parent/guardian contact and emergency contact**.
- **Authorization** to release child, transport, seek emergency medical care.
- **Current Immunization Certificate** — SC DHEC Form 2740 or equivalent (per S.C. Code § 44-29-180).
- **Medical/health record and allergies**.
- **Individual medication authorization + log**.
- **Daily attendance** (arrival/departure).
- **Accident/incident reports**.
- **Written withdrawal documentation**.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **SLED state criminal records check**.
- **FBI fingerprint clearance**.
- **Central Registry (child abuse/neglect) clearance**.
- All three required **before** unsupervised child contact under S.C. Code § 63-13-180.
- **DSS 2926** — Health Assessment. For operator, all caregivers, and (in home settings) every household member 16+.
- **Current CPR + First Aid** on at least one staff member on duty.
- **DSS-approved pre-service health & safety training**.
- **Bloodborne pathogens training**.
- **15 hours of annual in-service training** (child growth, safety, program management).
- **Orientation record / TB screening**.
- **Personnel file** — employment dates, performance reviews.

## What must be on your walls
- Current DSS license.
- **Most recent inspection report** (rule requires posting).
- **Emergency evacuation plan**.
- **Complaint phone number**.
- Menus.
- Safe Sleep policy in infant rooms.
- Expulsion / discipline policy (BRIGHT Act-compliant).

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** — per your emergency plan.
- **Severe weather / tornado drill** — per plan.
- **Daily attendance**.
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident reports**.
- **Fire / Health inspections on file** — via DSS 2905.
- **Transportation**: all vehicles carry attendance logs; transport staff complete vehicle-specific training.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff on the floor. The #1 thing that gets cited is supervision; the #2 is ratio.
2. Pull 3 random staff files — SLED + FBI + Central Registry on top, DSS 2926 Health Assessment, 15-hour in-service log.
3. Pull 3 random child files — DSS 3086, DHEC 2740 immunization, allergy plan.
4. Infant rooms: safe sleep, no loose bedding.
5. License posted, most recent inspection report posted, evacuation plan posted, complaint phone number posted — all four required.
6. Fire drill log — current month initialed?
7. Medication cabinet — locked, in-date, matching parent authorization.
8. Pull up your scchildcare.org provider page — any High or Medium finding in the last 36 months not marked corrected?
9. If you transport, vehicle attendance log in the van.
10. Playground — fence, gate, no hazards.

## What happens if you fail
DSS issues deficiencies with a severity code. **High** = serious risk to child health and safety; **Medium** = significant risk; **Low** = non-compliance without immediate safety risk. A plan of correction is required; re-inspection follows. Serious or repeat noncompliance leads to probation, fine, suspension, or revocation — all tracked on the provider detail page. Appeals go through the DSS administrative process. Note: the Children's Trust study noted that many facilities continue to operate despite multiple serious violations — which is precisely why transparency via the public page matters so much.

## The weird South Carolina gotcha nobody tells you about
South Carolina's public scchildcare.org page shows **every deficiency in the last 36 months with a High / Medium / Low severity tag**, regulation cite, description, and whether it was corrected. Improper supervision alone produced **3,044 high-severity violations statewide in a 4-year window** per the Children's Trust of SC audit, followed by 2,605 out-of-ratio high-severity findings. A parent scrolling scchildcare.org/provider/<your id>/ can literally read your citation history. Second gotcha: **religious-exempt centers still have mandated reporter duties and background-check duties** (S.C. Code § 63-13-60) — the exemption is narrower than most owners think. Third: **Health Assessment (DSS 2926)** applies not just to your staff but in home-based settings to **every household member 16 or older** — miss a household member and it's a straight cite.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a South Carolina owner
Biggest fear: a High-severity deficiency on their public scchildcare.org page — supervision and out-of-ratio are the top two categories and they stay visible for 36 months. Drop this in 30 seconds: "Children's Trust found 8,537 serious violations in a 4-year window — 3,044 of them supervision, 2,605 out-of-ratio. Every one hit a public provider page. Are you tracking SLED + FBI + Central Registry clearance before hire, DSS 2926 health assessments for every household member 16+, and 15 hours of annual training?" Today they're using paper DSS forms, DHEC immunization printouts, and nothing for the 36-month severity-tagged record. The close: "We keep High-severity findings off your scchildcare.org page — because in SC, that page is the first thing parents read."
