## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Iowa Code Chapter 237A** — Child Care Facilities
- **Iowa Administrative Code (IAC) 441 (Human Services) Title XII, Chapter 109** — Child Care Centers
- **IAC 441 Chapter 110** — Child Development Homes (registered)
- **IAC 441 Chapter 120** — Child Care Home Registration & providers
- **IAC 441 Chapter 170** — CCDBG / subsidy

## Facility Types Regulated

| Type | Threshold | Regulation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Child Care Center | 7+ children for ≥2 consecutive hours | IAC 441-109 — licensed |
| Licensed Preschool | Same as center, part-day | IAC 441-109 |
| Child Development Home Category A | Up to 5 children + 1 school-age | IAC 441-110 — registered |
| Child Development Home Category B | Up to 8 with helper | IAC 441-110 — registered |
| Child Development Home Category C | Up to 12 with helper (Cat C-1 = 11, C-2 = 12 w/ extra staff) | IAC 441-110 |
| Non-registered Child Care Home | ≤5 unrelated children | IAC 441-110 |

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (IAC 441-109.8)

**Child Care Centers:**

| Age | Ratio |
|-----|-------|
| 0–12 months | 1:4 |
| 13–23 months | 1:4 |
| 2-year-olds | 1:6 |
| 3-year-olds | 1:8 |
| 4-year-olds | 1:12 |
| 5-year-olds (kindergarten-age) | 1:15 |
| School-age (6+) | 1:15 |

**Mixed-age group:** ratio of youngest child. Combined 3–5 yr groups: 1:12.

**Opening / closing two-hour exception:** one staff may care for ≤7 children provided ≤4 are under 2.

**When >8 children present** (including in transport vehicle), **at least 2 adults** must be on premises.

**Registered Development Homes:** Category A–C ratios above (IAC 441-110.7).

## Required Child Records

- Enrollment information & emergency contacts
- **Immunization record** per Iowa Code 139A (or religious/medical exemption)
- Physical exam within 6 months of enrollment
- Allergy & diet statement; Physical Care Plan (470-0720) if applicable
- Authorization for emergency care & pickup
- Daily attendance
- Incident / injury report log
- Child-abuse reporting acknowledgment signed by parent

## Required Staff Records

- Application & reference checks
- **Iowa Centralized Employee Registry (CER)** enrollment on hire
- **Record checks required:** Iowa criminal history (DCI), Iowa child abuse registry, Iowa sex offender registry, Iowa dependent adult abuse registry; **FBI fingerprint** for new hires after 9/30/2018; **out-of-state** child-abuse & criminal checks if lived outside IA in past 5 yrs
- Health statement (TB if risk factors)
- **Mandatory reporter training** within 6 months and every 3 years (Iowa Code 232.69)
- **Universal precautions / bloodborne pathogens** initial + annual
- Orientation before unsupervised contact
- **Annual training — 10 hours (≥2 in Iowa's Early Learning Standards)** for all staff; directors 12 hrs
- CPR + Pediatric First Aid for all staff caring for children (updated every 2 yrs)
- SIDS + safe sleep training for infant staff
- Medication administration training (if administering)

## Inspection Cadence

- **Annual unannounced on-site visit** for each licensed center (IAC 441-109.3)
- Initial + renewal applications require pre-licensing inspection
- Additional visits for complaints, self-reported incidents, or sanctions
- Reports public at DHS Child Care Compliance Report site

## Renewal Cadence

- **License term: up to 2 years** (most centers on 2-year cycle)
- **Annual monitoring** required during the 2-year term
- Provisional license (6 months) may be issued to new facilities
- Registration (Child Development Home) is **2-year**

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Iowa QRS — "Iowa Quality Rating System"** — 5-level voluntary rating; most subsidy reimbursement differentials tied to rating.
- **Quality Improvement System (QI)** — ratings displayed on Iowa Child Care Connect.
- **Mandatory reporter training** — newer 2019 legislation (HF 2502) tightened requirements to 1 hr + 2 hrs every 3 yrs.
- **Safe Sleep & ITS-SIDS** — required training and written policy in infant rooms.
- **Water safety** — centers within proximity of open water must have a written water plan.
- **Tornado drills** — monthly March–November.
- **Fire drills** — monthly; documentation retained.
- **Disaster/shelter-in-place** — annual.
- **Immunization** — Iowa follows ACIP schedule; religious exemption via Form 470-0603 (varicella, MMR, DTaP/Tdap, etc.).
- **Nutrition** — CACFP alignment for subsidy participants.
- **Iowa Child Care Connect** — open data platform surfaces vacancies, compliance status, ratings; providers strongly encouraged to maintain listings.
- **Pesticide / radon** notification to parents if applicable.
