## 1. TEXAS

### 1.1 Regulatory Agency

**Texas Health and Human Services Commission (HHSC), Child Care Regulation (CCR) Division**

- Website: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/providers/child-care-regulation
- Formerly under the Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS); now under HHSC
- Child Care Licensing portal: https://childcare.hhs.texas.gov/Child_Care/

### 1.2 Inspection Frequency & Process

**Frequency:**
- Licensed operations receive **at least one annual unannounced inspection**
- New operations receive an **initial inspection before licensing** and additional monitoring during the first year
- **Complaint-driven inspections** occur at any time in response to reports
- Follow-up inspections occur after deficiencies are cited to verify correction

**Inspection Process:**
- Inspections are conducted by CCR Licensing Representatives who must carry and present valid HHSC identification
- The inspector completes a **Health and Safety Audit Checklist** or conducts a standard-by-standard monitoring inspection
- Key forms used:
  - **CLASS Form 2936** - Child-Care Facility Inspection (inspector selects minimum standard subchapter)
  - **CLASS Form 2939** - Child-Care Facility Assessment (documents compliance)
- Inspectors review all areas accessible to children, including outdoor areas, equipment, storage, and documentation
- When a violation is observed, the inspector issues a **deficiency** and notifies the operation in writing; it is posted on the operation's public online record

**Reference:** [HHSC Inspection Handbook Section 4100](https://www.hhs.texas.gov/handbooks/child-care-regulation-handbook/4100-inspecting-child-care-operations)

### 1.3 Inspection Checklists & Forms

Texas publishes inspection-related forms on the HHSC website:

- **Child Day Care Regulation Forms page:** https://www.hhs.texas.gov/providers/child-care-regulation/child-day-care-regulation-forms
- **CCR Handbook Forms:** https://www.hhs.texas.gov/handbooks/child-care-regulation-handbook/ccrh-forms
- **Minimum Standards for Child-Care Centers (Chapter 746 PDF):** https://www.hhs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/documents/doing-business-with-hhs/provider-portal/protective-services/ccl/min-standards/chapter-746-centers.pdf
- **Inspection & Compliance History (public lookup):** https://childcare.hhs.texas.gov/Public/InspectionAndComplianceHistory

### 1.4 Key Compliance Areas

**Staff-Child Ratios (Chapter 746, Centers Licensed for 13+ Children):**

| Age Range | Ratio (Children:Staff) | Maximum Group Size |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| 0-11 months | 4:1 | 10 |
| 12-17 months | 5:1 | 13 |
| 18-23 months | 9:1 | 18 |
| 2 years | 11:1 | 22 |
| 3 years | 15:1 | 30 |
| 4 years | 18:1 | 35 |
| 5 years | 22:1 | 35 |
| 6-8 years | 26:1 | 35 |
| 9-12 years | 26:1 | 35 |

**Reference:** [Chapter 746, Subchapter E - Texas Administrative Code](http://txrules.elaws.us/rule/title26_chapter746_subchaptere)

**Safety Requirements:**
- Background checks required for all caregivers, including fingerprint-based FBI checks
- Fire safety and building inspections required
- Compliance with physical plant standards (indoor/outdoor space requirements)
- Emergency preparedness plans required
- Safe sleep practices for infants

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Child records (enrollment forms, emergency contacts, immunization records, allergy/medical information)
- Staff records (background check results, training documentation, qualifications)
- Incident/injury reports
- Daily attendance records
- Fire/emergency drill logs

**Training Requirements:**
- **24 hours of pre-service training** (8 hours must be completed before being given responsibility for children)
- Topics must cover: developmental stages, supervision, safety, health and nutrition
- **Directors:** minimum 30 hours of annual training relevant to ages of children served
- **Caregivers:** ongoing annual training hours required
- CPR and first aid certification required

### 1.5 Fines & Penalties for Non-Compliance

Texas HHSC uses a progressive enforcement system:

**Administrative Penalties (Fines):**
- **$1,000** per violation for abuse, neglect, or exploitation of a child
- **$500** for failure to timely report to parent/guardian an injury requiring medical treatment or hospitalization
- **$50** for failure to report to parents that the facility was cited for a violation constituting abuse/neglect/exploitation ($50 per day the violation continues)
- Administrative penalties are imposed for **high-weight deficiencies** such as background check violations

**Other Enforcement Actions (escalating):**
- **Corrective Action:** Required when an operation has a pattern of deficiencies or a single serious deficiency
- **Probation:** Operation continues under heightened monitoring and conditions
- **Adverse Action:** Can include closing an operation, adding permanent restrictions/conditions to a permit, or license revocation
- **Due Process:** Operations have 30 days to request a hearing after receiving CLASS Form 2994 (Notice of Recommendation for Administrative Penalty)

**Reference:** [HHSC Administrative Penalties Handbook Section 7500](https://www.hhs.texas.gov/handbooks/child-care-regulation-handbook/7500-administrative-penalties) | [CCR Enforcement Actions](https://www.hhs.texas.gov/providers/child-care-regulation/ccr-enforcement-actions)

### 1.6 Online Portals

- **CLASS (Child Care Licensing Automation Support System):** The primary system used by HHSC for licensing management. Providers log in at https://childcare.hhs.texas.gov/Child_Care/
- **CLASS Document Library:** Electronic records management; providers upload compliance documents here (replaced the CCR Digital Storage SharePoint site in August 2023)
- **eApplication System:** Online submission of licensing applications; accessible at https://childcare.hhs.texas.gov/Child_Care/Search_Texas_Child_Care/CCLNET/Source/eApplication/eApplicationInfo.aspx
- **Search Texas Child Care (public):** Public-facing database for looking up provider compliance history

## 2. CALIFORNIA

### 2.1 Regulatory Agency

**California Department of Social Services (CDSS), Community Care Licensing Division (CCLD)**

- Website: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/community-care-licensing
- Child Care Licensing page: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/child-care-licensing
- CCLD operates through **21 Regional Offices** statewide
- Educational resource portal: https://ccld.childcarevideos.org/

### 2.2 Inspection Frequency & Process

**Frequency:**
- **Annual/routine inspections** are required for all licensed facilities
- **Random unannounced inspections** can occur at any time
- **Complaint-driven inspections** in response to reports
- Follow-up inspections to verify correction of cited deficiencies

**Inspection Process:**
- Inspections are conducted by **Licensing Program Analysts (LPAs)**
- California uses the **CARE Tools (Compliance and Regulatory Enforcement Tools)** framework, developed under the Inspection Process Project
- LPAs conduct a full tour of the facility, examining all areas accessible to children
- They check cabinets, closets, storage, locks/latches, exterior areas, and equipment
- All required records are reviewed for accuracy and currency
- Posted items (license, emergency numbers, etc.) are confirmed as up-to-date and visible
- Ratios and capacity are verified on the spot

**Nine Inspection Domains for Child Care Centers:**
1. Care and Supervision
2. Children's Records
3. Food Service
4. Personal Rights
5. Physical Plant
6. Reporting Requirements
7. Staff Records
8. Staffing Ratio and Capacity
9. Toddler Component

**Six Inspection Domains for Family Child Care Homes:**
1. Care and Supervision
2. Facility Administration
3. Personal Rights
4. Physical Plant
5. Records
6. Staffing Ratio and Capacity

**Reference:** [CDSS Inspection Process Project](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/community-care-licensing/inspection-process)

### 2.4 Key Compliance Areas

**Staff-Child Ratios (Title 22 - Child Care Centers):**

| Age Group | Ratio (Staff:Children) | Notes |
|-----------|----------------------|-------|
| Infants (0-2 years) | 1:4 | Infant care component |
| Toddlers/Preschool (2-6 years) | 1:12 | General preschool |
| School-Age (6-14 years) | 1:15 | After-school programs |

**Reference:** [Title 22, Section 101216.3 - Teacher-Child Ratio](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/california/22-CCR-101216.3)

**Safety Requirements:**
- Fire clearance from State Fire Marshal or local fire authority
- Health and safety assessment of facility
- Compliance with physical plant standards
- Safe outdoor play areas with appropriate fencing
- Adequate indoor space per child
- Hazardous materials properly stored and locked

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Children's records (enrollment, emergency contacts, immunizations, health assessments)
- Staff records (qualifications, background checks, training logs, health assessments)
- Facility records (fire clearance, license, insurance)
- Incident reports
- Sign-in/sign-out sheets
- Medication administration logs

**Training Requirements:**
- Background checks including DOJ Live Scan fingerprinting, FBI check, Child Abuse Central Index (CACI) check
- Health and safety training covering preventive health practices, CPR/first aid, safe sleep practices
- Orientation required before operating
- Ongoing professional development hours required annually
- CPR and pediatric first aid certification required for all staff

### 2.5 Fines & Penalties for Non-Compliance

California uses a **Type A / Type B citation system** with civil penalties:

**Type A Citations (Immediate Threat to Health/Safety):**
- Issued when there is a direct and immediate risk to the health, safety, or personal rights of children
- **$50 per violation per day**, up to a maximum of **$150 per violation per day** until correction is made
- If resulting in death or serious injury, escalated penalties apply

**Type B Citations (Potential Threat):**
- Issued when, without correction, a violation could become a risk to health, safety, or personal rights
- Corrective action required within specified timeframe

**Progressive Penalties for Repeat Violations:**
- **2nd citation within 12 months:** Immediate civil penalty of **$150 per violation**, then $150/day until corrected
- **3rd citation within 12 months:** Immediate **$150 per violation**, plus $150/day; potential for probation or license action

**Other Enforcement Actions:**
- Probation
- License suspension
- License revocation
- Temporary suspension order (for immediate danger situations)
- Exclusion of specific individuals from facility

**Reference:** [CCLD Civil Penalty Information](https://ccld.childcarevideos.org/child-care-center-operators/what-is-a-civil-penalty/)

Most application materials and compliance documents are submitted through local Regional Offices. Contact your local CCLD Regional Office or email inspectionprocess@dss.ca.gov for guidance.

## 3. FLORIDA

### 3.1 Regulatory Agency

**Florida Department of Children and Families (DCF)**

- Website: https://www.myflfamilies.com/services/licensing/child-care-licensure
- Child Care Laws & Requirements: https://www.myflfamilies.com/services/child-family/child-care/child-care-laws-and-requirements
- Governed by Florida Statute Chapter 402 and Florida Administrative Code Chapter 65C-22

### 3.2 Inspection Frequency & Process

**Frequency:**
- **Minimum of two unannounced inspections per year** (per Florida Administrative Code 65C-20.012)
- **Pre-licensing inspections** by local county health department and local fire marshal before a license can be issued
- **Complaint-driven inspections** at any time in response to reports
- **Re-inspection visits** to verify correction of violations

**Inspection Process:**
- DCF licensing counselors conduct unannounced site visits
- Inspectors evaluate compliance against standards in the **Child Care Facility Handbook**
- Inspections cover health and safety, ratios, training, paperwork, supervision, nutrition, sanitation, and physical environment
- Facility must maintain at least **35 square feet of indoor space per child** and **45 square feet of outdoor play space per child** (fenced)
- Both facility and personnel records are reviewed on-site

**Reference:** [DCF Child Care Programs & Inspections Guide (PDF)](https://www.myflfamilies.com/sites/default/files/2023-07/Child-Care-Programs-and-Inspections-Guide.pdf)

### 3.3 Inspection Checklists & Forms

- **Child Care Facility Standards Classification Summary (CF-FSP 5316):** https://www.myflfamilies.com/sites/default/files/2022-12/ChildCareFacilityClassificationSummary.pdf
- **Registered Family Child Care Home Health & Safety Checklist (CF-FSP 5274):** https://www.myflfamilies.com/sites/default/files/2022-12/RegisteredFamilyChildCareHomeHealthandSafetyChecklist.pdf
- **School-Age Child Care Facility Handbook:** https://www.myflfamilies.com/sites/default/files/2022-12/School-Age-Child-Care-Licensing-Handbook.pdf
- **DCF Forms & Applications page:** https://www.myflfamilies.com/services/child-family/child-care/child-care-providers-and-staff/forms-and-applications
- **DCF Licensing Desk Reference Guide:** https://www.myflfamilies.com/sites/default/files/2022-12/cfop_170-20_child_care_licensing_program_desk_reference_guide.pdf
- **CARES Public Search (inspection results):** https://caressearch.myflfamilies.com/PublicSearch

### 3.4 Key Compliance Areas

**Staff-Child Ratios (Florida Statute 402.305(4)):**

| Age Group | Ratio (Staff:Children) |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Birth through 1 year | 1:4 |
| 1 year to under 2 years | 1:6 |
| 2 years to under 3 years | 1:11 |
| 3 years to under 4 years | 1:15 |
| 4 years to under 5 years | 1:20 |
| 5 years and older | 1:25 |

*When children 2+ are in care, the ratio is based on the age group with the largest number of children in the group.*

**Reference:** [Florida Statute 402.305](https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&URL=0400-0499/0402/Sections/0402.305.html)

**Safety Requirements:**
- Level 2 background screening for all owners, directors, staff, and anyone with direct child contact (Florida Statute 435.04)
- Fingerprinting and criminal database screenings
- Fire safety inspection and local health department approval before licensing
- 35 sq ft indoor space per child; 45 sq ft outdoor per child (fenced)
- Safe sleep environment compliance
- Emergency preparedness plans

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Enrollment records for each child
- Immunization records
- Emergency contact information
- Daily attendance records
- Fire drill logs (monthly drills required)
- Staff training records and credentials
- Insurance documentation
- Incident/injury reports

**Training Requirements:**
- **40-hour introductory training course** for child care facility personnel (covering child development, health/safety, identifying abuse/neglect, age-appropriate activities) -- must be DCF-approved
- **30-hour Family Child Care Home training** for home-based providers (with competency exam)
- Pediatric CPR and first aid certification required
- Annual in-service training hours required
- **Florida Child Care Professional Credential** available (CF-FSP Form 5270)

### 3.5 Fines & Penalties for Non-Compliance

Florida uses a **four-class violation system** with progressive enforcement:

**Class I Violations (Most Serious - Immediate Danger):**
- Conditions presenting immediate danger that could cause serious physical or emotional harm
- Examples: Background screening violations, improper ratios, inadequate supervision, capacity breaches, failure to report serious injuries/deaths
- Fine: **$100-$500 per day per violation**
- 3rd violation of same standard: License suspension, denial, or revocation

**Class II Violations (Direct/Potential Threat):**
- Threaten directly or potentially the physical/emotional health, safety, or security of children
- Examples: Non-compliance with CPR/First Aid certs, missing first aid kits, physical facility deficiencies, sanitation violations
- 2nd violation of same standard: **$50 per violation**
- 3rd violation: **$60 per day per violation**
- 4th violation: Probation (up to 6 months) + **$75 per day**
- 5th+ violation: License suspension/denial/revocation + **$100 per day**

**Class III Violations (Indirect/Potential Threat):**
- Threaten indirectly or potentially child welfare
- Examples: Incomplete enrollment documentation, missing fire drill records, lack of insurance proof, absent attendance logs
- 3rd violation of same standard: **$25 per violation**
- 4th violation: **$30 per day per violation**
- 5th violation: Probation (up to 6 months) + **$40 per day**
- 6th+ violation: License suspension/denial/revocation + **$50 per day**

**Class IV Violations (Least Serious):**
- Conditions that may threaten indirectly or potentially child safety but don't rise to Class I-III severity
- Progressive corrective action approach

**2025-2026 Legislative Update:** Florida HB 47 authorized DCF to create up to three classification levels for violations directly related to health and safety, with $3,038,070 in recurring funds appropriated for FY 2025-2026.

**Reference:** [Florida Administrative Code 65C-22.010 - Enforcement](https://flrules.elaws.us/fac/65c-22.010) | [DCF Enforcement Fact Sheet](https://www.myflfamilies.com/document/29241)

## 4. NEW YORK

### 4.1 Regulatory Agency

**New York State Office of Children and Family Services (OCFS), Division of Child Care Services (DCCS)**

- Website: https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/
- Regulations page: https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/regulations/
- **Exception:** In New York City, group child care centers are regulated by the **NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DOHMH)** under the NYC Health Code

### 4.2 Inspection Frequency & Process

**Frequency:**
- **Regular inspections**, both announced and unannounced, throughout the life of the license
- **Pre-licensing inspections** before issuance of license/registration
- **Complaint-driven investigations** at any time
- **Unannounced follow-up inspections** to verify correction of violations

**Inspection Process:**
- Inspectors (regulators) conduct on-site visits using the **OCFS Inspection Checklists**
- Checklists reflect substantial and commonly observed regulatory requirements, with additional regulatory citations added as needed
- Inspectors check: safety features, sanitation, activity space, emergency exits, fire extinguishers, outdoor play areas, staff qualifications, ratios, documentation, and more
- When violations are found, OCFS takes enforcement steps including **Corrective Action Plans (CAPs)** to bring the program into compliance

**Investigation Sequence for Complaints:**
1. Complaint submission and preliminary review
2. Jurisdiction confirmation and plausibility assessment
3. Unannounced on-site investigation with staff interviews
4. Formal OCFS determination and administrative action

### 4.3 Inspection Checklists & Forms

OCFS publishes actual inspection checklists used by regulators:

- **Day Care Center (DCC) Inspection Checklist:** https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/assets/docs/CCFS-Inspection-Checklist-DCC.pdf

- **Day Care Center Regulations (Part 418-1, full text):** https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/regulations/418-1-DCC.pdf
- **Definitions (Part 413):** https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/regulations/413-Definitions.pdf
- **All Regulations page:** https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/regulations/
- **Programs Subject to Enforcement Action (public database):** https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/looking/ccfs-fie.php
- **Provider Information page (forms & resources):** https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/info-for-providers.php

### 4.4 Key Compliance Areas

**Staff-Child Ratios (18 NYCRR 418-1.8 - Day Care Centers):**

| Age Group | Age Range | Ratio (Staff:Children) | Max Group Size |
|-----------|-----------|----------------------|----------------|
| Infant | Birth-5 months | 1:4 | 8 |
| Young Toddler | 6-17 months | 1:4 | 8 |
| Older Toddler | 18 months-2 years | 1:5 | 12 |
| Preschool (3s) | 3 years | 1:7 | 18 |
| Pre-K (4s) | 4 years | 1:8 | 21 |
| School-Age | 5 years | 1:9 | 24 |
| School-Age | 6+ years | 1:10 | 24 |

*Mixed-age rule: The ratio for the youngest child in the room applies to the entire group. Infants may never be mixed with other age groups.*

**Reference:** [18 NYCRR 418-1.8 - Supervision of Children](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-york/18-NYCRR-418-1.8)

**Safety Requirements:**
- Background checks: State criminal history, sex offender registry, child abuse/neglect registry, FBI fingerprinting -- required for all staff, substitutes, and volunteers with unsupervised access
- 45 square feet of usable indoor space per child (excluding hallways, bathrooms, kitchens, storage)
- 65 square feet of outdoor play space per child, with fencing at least 4 feet high
- Fire safety and building code compliance
- Safe sleep practices
- Emergency/evacuation plans

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Child enrollment records with emergency contacts
- Immunization records
- Health assessments for children
- Staff qualifications and training records
- Background check clearance documentation
- Daily attendance records
- Incident/injury reports
- Fire/emergency drill logs
- Medication administration records

**Training Requirements:**
- Pre-service training/orientation required
- Health and safety training (topics include: prevention of child abuse/maltreatment, shaken baby syndrome, SIDS prevention, medication administration, nutrition, etc.)
- CPR and first aid certification
- Ongoing annual professional development hours
- All directors and teachers must meet specific educational/experience qualifications

### 4.5 Fines & Penalties for Non-Compliance

**Civil Penalties:**
- OCFS can assess daily civil penalties of **up to $500 per day per violation**
- Fines are generally imposed only for **non-rectifiable violations**

**Non-Rectifiable Violations That Trigger Fines:**
- Failure to provide adequate, competent supervision
- Failure to provide adequate sanitation
- Violation of the same regulation more than once within six months
- Operating without a required license/registration
- Failure to report suspected child abuse or maltreatment
- Submission of fraudulent documentation to OCFS

**Enforcement Actions (escalating):**
- Corrective Action Plans (CAPs) with verification inspections
- Temporary license suspension (immediate when imminent threat to safety identified)
- Civil penalties (daily fines)
- License revocation (permanent, for most serious breaches)
- Permanent disqualification of personnel involved in substantiated abuse
- Mandatory Corrective Action Plans with follow-up verification

**Due Process:**
- Providers receive written notice of proposed enforcement
- Right to a fair hearing before an Administrative Law Judge (ALJ) under 18 NYCRR 413.5(a)
- Hearings are conducted by the OCFS Bureau of Special Hearings

**Reference:** [OCFS Day Care Enforcement FAQ](https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/special-hearings/FAQ-day-care.php)

### 4.6 Online Portals

- **FAMS (Facility Application and Management System):** https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/providers/fams.php - Online portal for licensing applications and communication between providers and licensors. Currently available for Day Care Centers and School Age Child Care programs. Providers must complete OCFS-approved orientation first. Support: ocfs.sm.FAMSCBCHelp@ocfs.ny.gov
- **CCFS (Child Care Facility Search - public):** https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/looking/ccfs-search.php - Public search for licensed/registered programs
- **CCAA (Child Care Assistance Application Portal):** https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/ccap/ccaa-resources.php - For child care assistance applications

## 5. ILLINOIS

### 5.1 Regulatory Agency

**Illinois Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS)**

- Website: https://dcfs.illinois.gov
- Licensing information: https://sunshine.dcfs.illinois.gov/Content/Licensing/BecomeLicensed.aspx
- **Note:** The Illinois Department of Human Services (IDHS) also plays a role in child care subsidy programs

### 5.2 Inspection Frequency & Process

**Frequency:**
- **At least one annual unannounced monitoring visit** for all licensed facilities
- **Permit monitoring:** At least monthly during a facility's initial permit period
- **60-day monitoring:** Within 60 days of a provider receiving their initial license
- **Additional unannounced visits** as needed for repeat/chronic violations, egregious violations, or other concerns
- Almost all monitoring is **unannounced** -- licensees do not know when their licensing representative will visit

**Inspection Process:**
- DCFS licensing representatives conduct on-site inspections
- Pre-licensing: Intensive inspection of facility/home before license issuance to ensure all safety and health regulations are met
- Ongoing: Compliance checks against Rule 407 (Day Care Centers), Rule 406 (Day Care Homes), or Rule 408 (Group Day Care Homes)
- Monthly fire safety inspections are a new requirement
- Inspectors review physical environment, staffing, ratios, documentation, health/safety practices
- Licensing compliance monitoring is governed by **Procedures 383**

**License Duration:** 3 years (with annual monitoring in between)

**Reference:** [Illinois Licensing and Monitoring Process](https://www.illinoiscaresforkids.org/toddler/early-care-and-education/licensing-and-monitoring-process) | [DCFS Procedures 383 (PDF)](https://dcfs.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/dcfs/documents/about-us/policy-rules-and-forms/documents/procedures/procedures-383.pdf)

### 5.4 Key Compliance Areas

**Staff-Child Ratios (89 Ill. Admin. Code 407.190 - Day Care Centers):**

| Age Group | Ratio (Staff:Children) | Maximum Group Size |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Infants (6 weeks-14 months) | 1:4 | 12 |
| Toddlers (15-23 months) | 1:5 | 15 |
| Two-year-olds | 1:8 | 16 |
| Three-year-olds | 1:10 | 20 |
| Four-year-olds | 1:10 | 20 |
| Five-year-olds (preschool) | 1:20 | 20 |
| School-age (with kindergarteners) | 1:20 | 30 |

*Mixed-age rule: Ratio and max group size are based on the youngest child in the group.*
*Exception: An early childhood teacher + early childhood assistant may supervise up to 30 children when all are at least 5 years old.*

**Reference:** [89 Ill. Admin. Code 407.190 - Grouping and Staffing](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/illinois/Ill-Admin-Code-tit-89-SS-407.190) | [Section 407 (JCAR)](https://www.ilga.gov/commission/jcar/admincode/089/089004070D01900R.html)

**Safety Requirements:**
- Background checks including all federal Child Care and Development Block Grant Act (CCDBG) components
- Fingerprint-based criminal history check
- Sex offender registry check
- Child abuse/neglect registry check
- Fire safety inspection (monthly fire drills required)
- Compliance with physical plant and space requirements
- Safe outdoor play areas
- Prohibition of cannabis use and storage on premises (provider certification required)

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Child enrollment and emergency information
- Immunization records
- Health examination records
- Daily attendance records
- Staff qualifications and training records
- Background check documentation
- Incident/injury reports
- Fire drill logs (monthly)
- Child support order compliance certification

**Training Requirements:**
- **15 hours of in-service training per year** for all caregivers
- Pre-service training/orientation required
- Background checks as part of initial requirements
- CPR and first aid certification
- Directors and lead teachers must meet specific educational qualifications
- Topics must include child development, health, safety, and nutrition

### 5.5 Fines & Penalties for Non-Compliance

**Financial Penalties:**
- Fines start at **$500 per violation** and escalate based on violation severity and duration
- Background check violations can result in **immediate licensing suspension and facility closure**

**Enforcement Actions (escalating):**
- Corrective action plans
- Probationary licensing
- License suspension (temporary; may regain by satisfying terms)
- License revocation (permanent loss of ability to operate)
- Refusal to renew license

**Public Disclosure:**
- DCFS publishes compliance violations in a **public database** (DCFS Sunshine website)
- Includes name, address, and nature of any license discipline
- Public disclosure creates reputational consequences for non-compliant providers

**Governing Framework:**
- Rule 383 (Licensing Compliance, Monitoring, and Enforcement)
- Illinois Child Care Act, Section 8 (sanctions for failure to comply)
- Rules 406, 407, 408 (specific licensing standards)

**Reference:** [DCFS Sunshine - Investigative Actions](https://sunshine.dcfs.illinois.gov/Content/Licensing/InvestigationActions.aspx) | [Procedures 383 (PDF)](https://dcfs.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/dcfs/documents/about-us/policy-rules-and-forms/documents/procedures/procedures-383.pdf)
