# Maine — Child Care Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority

**Maine Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS), Office of Child and Family Services (OCFS), Children's Licensing and Investigation Services (CLIS).**

- Headquarters: 109 Capitol Street, 11 State House Station, Augusta, ME 04333-0011
- Main: (207) 287-3707
- Child Care Licensing Unit: (207) 287-9300 / (800) 791-4080
- Children's Licensing and Investigation: (207) 287-5020

CLIS licenses, monitors, investigates, and enforces compliance for Child Care Facilities and Family Child Care Providers. OCFS also administers the Rising Stars QRIS and the Child Care Affordability Program (CCAP).

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **10-148 CMR Chapter 32** — "Child Care Facility Licensing Rule" (Child Care Centers, Nursery Schools, Small Child Care Facilities, Other Programs). Effective 2021-09-27; most recent substantive revision.
- **10-148 CMR Chapter 33** — Family Child Care Provider Licensing Rule.
- Enabling statute: **22 M.R.S. §§8301-A through 8304** — Licensure of Child Care Facilities and Family Child Care Providers.
- Related: **22 M.R.S. §8302-A** (background checks), **Title 22, Chapter 1671** (immunization).

## Facility Types Regulated

| Facility Type | Definition | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Child Care Center | 13+ children (center-based) | 10-148 Ch. 32 |
| Small Child Care Facility | 3–12 children (not in provider's home) | 10-148 Ch. 32 |
| Nursery School | Half-day program, typically ages 2.5–5 | 10-148 Ch. 32 |
| Family Child Care Provider | Provider's home, up to 12 children | 10-148 Ch. 33 |
| Head Start / private school birth-5 programs | Included under Ch. 32 scope | 10-148 Ch. 32 |

## Key Licensing Forms

- **Child Care Licensing Application** (OCFS-CLIS)
- **Background Check Authorization Form** (required before any staff/volunteer presence; state BCI + FBI fingerprint + Maine child abuse registry)
- **Child Health Information Form** — immunization + physical
- **Emergency Contact & Authorized Pick-up Form**
- **Fire Marshal's Inspection Report** (State Fire Marshal's Office)
- **Life Safety Code compliance form** for facilities serving children under 2.5

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes

**Small Child Care Facilities (3–12 children) — 10-148 CMR Ch. 32 §7:**

| Age | Ratio |
|---|---|
| Infants (6 weeks–2 years) | 1:4 |
| Toddlers (2–5 years) | 1:8 |
| School-age (5+ years) | 1:12 |

**Larger Child Care Centers (13+ children) — 10-148 CMR Ch. 32 §7:**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (6 weeks–1 year) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Young toddlers (1–2.5 years) | 1:4 or 1:5 | 10–12 |
| Older toddlers (2.5–3.5 years) | 1:7 | 21 |
| Preschool (3–under 5) | 1:8 or 1:10 | 20–24 |
| School-age (5–12) | 1:13 | — |

**Nursery Schools:** 1:12 ratio, maximum group size 30.

At least two child care staff members must be present whenever 7 or more children are present. Group sizes are determined by the **age of the youngest child** in the group.

## Required Child Records

- Enrollment application
- Immunization record (current; 30-day grace after first day of attendance)
- Physical examination on admission
- Emergency medical authorization
- Emergency contact & authorized pickup list
- Food allergy / dietary restriction documentation
- Incident / accident reports
- Daily attendance
- Authorization for photos / field trips / transportation
- **Record retention:** minimum 3 years after child leaves care (10-148 CMR Ch. 32 §5)

## Required Staff Records

- Application and resume
- State BCI + FBI fingerprint + child abuse registry results (pre-employment; every 5 years thereafter)
- Two reference checks
- Health screening statement
- Evidence of qualifications (high school diploma minimum for all staff; ECE credits or CDA for lead positions)
- Current CPR and pediatric First Aid certification
- Annual 30 hours of training (center-based lead staff); Maine Roads to Quality registry
- Orientation and mandated-reporter training
- Performance evaluations
- **Record retention:** 3 years after staff leave employment

## Inspection Cadence

Per **10-148 CMR Ch. 32 §4**: the Department shall make **at least one unannounced inspection** during the term of the license. That inspection **must take place between 6 and 18 months after license issuance**. Additional unannounced inspections occur in response to complaints or self-reported incidents.

Fire Marshal inspection is required at initial licensure and every 2 years thereafter.

## Renewal Cadence

Licenses are issued for **two (2) years** (biennial). Renewal application is due **at least 60 days before expiration**. If a timely renewal is filed, the existing license continues in effect until the Department makes a final decision. Biennial renewal fee for facilities serving 12 or fewer children is $240 (higher for larger facilities).

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Outdoor play is mandatory**: Children attending more than 4 hours and older than 12 months must get a minimum of **60 minutes of active outdoor play daily**. Infants must go outside at least once per day (10-148 CMR Ch. 32 §14). This includes winter weather (notable in a cold-climate state — providers must document exceptions).
- **Rising Stars QRIS** is voluntary but rated; the public Maine Licensed Child Care Search surfaces the star rating.
- **Small facility tier (3–12 children)** uniquely occupies space between family child care and large centers; rules are simpler but still inspected at the same cadence.
- **Inspection timing window (6–18 months)** is statutorily defined — unlike states that say "annually," Maine locks in the inspection into a defined range within the 2-year license period.
- **Permitted to contract with CDSS sites** (Child Development Services System) for children under 5 — integrates early intervention with child care.
- **Staff training hours** (30 per year for lead center staff) are higher than the federal floor.
