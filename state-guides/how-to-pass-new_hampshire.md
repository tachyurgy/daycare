# How to Pass Your New Hampshire Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In New Hampshire, your licensor is a **Child Care Licensing Unit (CCLU) specialist** inside the **Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS), Office of Program Support**. CCLU licenses, monitors, investigates complaints, and can hand you an administrative fine. You'll get **at least one on-site monitoring visit per license cycle** (NH's cycle is 3 years — unusual) and **unannounced complaint visits** anytime. Fire Marshal inspects every 2 years. DHHS Food Protection Section inspects if you prepare meals.

- NH DHHS Child Care Licensing: https://www.dhhs.nh.gov/programs-services/childcare-parenting-childbirth/child-care-licensing
- NH Child Care Search (consumer portal): https://new-hampshire.my.site.com/nhccis/NH_ChildCareSearch

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **Statute:** RSA 170-E — Child Day Care, Residential Care and Child-Placing Agency Licensing.
- **Regulations:** **He-C 4002** — NH Child Care Program Licensing Rules (readopted 2025-08-26). PDF: https://www.dhhs.nh.gov/sites/g/files/ehbemt476/files/documents2/he-c-4002-formatted.pdf
- **Background check rules:** He-C 4002.12, aligned with RSA 170-E:7 and federal CCDBG.
- **Immunization law:** RSA 141-C:20-a.
- **Right-to-Know / public records:** RSA 91-A.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
**Infant and Toddler Program (He-C 4002.37):**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| 6 weeks – 12 months | 1:4 | 12 |
| 13 – 24 months | 1:5 | 15 |
| 25 – 35 months | 1:6 | 18 |

**Preschool / typical ceilings** (He-C 4002.35 and 4002.38): 1:8 for 3-year-olds, 1:12 for 4 – 5-year-olds.

**Family Child Care (He-C 4002.34):** 1 provider, up to 6 kids total; no more than 3 under age 3; no more than 2 under 18 months. Family Group: provider + assistant, up to 12 kids.

**Warning:** Unlike most states, **NH uses AVERAGE age in mixed-age groups, not youngest**. This gives you flexibility, but you must **document the math** — an inspector will ask you to show it. A second staff person must always be in the building when 5+ children are present.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives (unannounced for monitoring; unannounced for complaints).
2. Confirms current license is posted.
3. Walks rooms; counts children and staff; checks ratios per He-C 4002.35/4002.37/4002.38 — verifies the average-age calculation for mixed rooms.
4. Pulls staff files — Personnel Information Forms, criminal background check (state + FBI + child abuse registry), references, health, qualifications, CPR/First Aid, 18-hour annual in-service log.
5. Pulls child files — enrollment, health form & physical, immunization record (RSA 141-C:20-a), emergency consents.
6. Reviews fire-drill log, emergency plan, incident reports, medication log.
7. Writes Statement of Findings and, if a **critical rule** is cited, a **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** with a firm deadline.

The inspection process is explained in DHHS's own PDF: https://www.dhhs.nh.gov/sites/g/files/ehbemt476/files/documents/2021-11/holu-child-care-licensing-inspections.pdf

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio / group-size miscounts in mixed-age rooms** — average-age math is easy to get wrong (He-C 4002.35).
2. **Background check gaps** — especially failing the 5-year refresh or missing a previous-state registry check (He-C 4002.12 / RSA 170-E:7).
3. **Missing or expired immunization records** — RSA 141-C:20-a.
4. **Staff training hours short of the 18-hour annual minimum** (He-C 4002).
5. **Critical-rule violations that trigger CAPs** — supervision lapses, unsafe sleep, missing emergency drills, locked medication failures. Any "critical" citation becomes public on NH Child Care Search.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment application with parent/guardian contacts, emergency contacts, authorized pickups.
- **Child Health Form & physical** — annual for kids under 2; every 2 years for ages 2 – 6.
- **Immunization record** per RSA 141-C:20-a (medical / religious exemption if applicable).
- Emergency medical consent.
- Allergy / medical condition list.
- Daily attendance log.
- Incident / accident reports.
- Transportation / field-trip permissions.
- **Infants:** feeding schedule, sleep patterns, developmental milestones (He-C 4002.37).

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **Personnel Information Form**.
- **Criminal background check** (state, FBI, child abuse registry) per RSA 170-E:7 — refreshed every 5 years, includes any state lived in during the prior 5 years.
- **Two references**.
- **Physician-signed health screening**.
- **Qualifications documentation** (degree, CDA, credit hours).
- **Current pediatric CPR and First Aid**.
- **18-hour annual in-service training log**.
- **Orientation + mandated-reporter training** records.
- Performance evaluations / supervision records.

## What must be on your walls
- Current NH DHHS license.
- Most recent Fire Marshal inspection.
- Food Protection inspection (if you prepare meals).
- Evacuation plan / emergency procedures.
- CCLU complaint contact.
- Daily schedule.
- Menu.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented frequency per He-C 4002.
- **Emergency / shelter drills** (weather, lockdown) — documented.
- **Daily attendance** — every room.
- **Medication administration log**.
- **Incident / accident reports**.
- **Infant sleep checks** (He-C 4002.37).

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff in every room; for mixed-age rooms, average the ages and check the ratio.
2. Confirm 2 staff are on site whenever 5+ kids are present.
3. Post current license + Fire Marshal + Food Protection inspections.
4. Check a random staff file: is the 5-year background check recent? Is the in-service log at 18+ hours?
5. Pull a random child file: current health form, immunizations, emergency consent?
6. Medication cabinet locked and logged.
7. Outlet covers, choking-hazard check, cords out of reach.
8. Diaper-change station clean; gloves stocked.
9. Playground — fence intact, no hazards.
10. Fire-drill log — last one within required interval; evacuation map posted.

## What happens if you fail
He-C 4002 lets CCLU issue a **Statement of Findings** and, for any "critical rule" violation, a mandatory **Corrective Action Plan** with a firm deadline. Unusual among NE states, **NH can impose administrative fines** without revoking your license. Persistent or serious findings can become a **conditional license**, a **suspension**, or a **revocation**. Statements of Findings and CAPs become **public on the NH Child Care Search portal** once the CAP due date passes — parents see them. Appeal rights are governed by RSA 541-A administrative procedure.

## The weird New Hampshire gotcha nobody tells you about
**NH is one of the only states whose license runs 3 years instead of 1 or 2** (He-C 4002.02). That sounds great — until you realize the state can still hit you with an administrative **fine** at any monitoring visit. Owners lose track because "my license is good for 3 years" becomes "my training log is 3 years out of date." Second gotcha: NH calculates mixed-age ratios by **average age**, not youngest. Teachers who trained in Massachusetts or Maine assume youngest-child math and fail ratio audits for years without knowing why.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a New Hampshire owner
Their fear is a "critical-rule" citation becoming a public CAP on NH Child Care Search, next to an administrative fine. The 30-second number: 18 in-service hours per staff per year, every year — the 3-year license lulls owners into letting the log lapse. NH owners today track training and 5-year background refreshes on spreadsheets or in a binder. Lead with: "Automatic alerts for the staff whose 5-year check is expiring in 30 days, and one click to show the CCLU specialist your 18-hour log is current." Second hook: NH uses AVERAGE age in mixed rooms; most software sold to NE owners defaults to youngest-age math and miscalculates in the owner's disfavor.
