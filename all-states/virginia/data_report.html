Virginia — Child Care Compliance Data Report

Population rank: 12 · ~8.7M residents · Prepared 2026-04-18 for ComplianceKit

1. Licensing Authority

Virginia Department of Education (VDOE), Division of Early Childhood Care and Education, Office of Child Care Licensing (OCCL). Licensing transferred from VDSS to VDOE effective July 1, 2021 (HB 2299). The public-search tool still resides on the legacy dss.virginia.gov domain.

  • Agency: doe.virginia.gov/.../early-childhood-care-education
  • Consumer portal: Child Care VA Find Care
  • Public search (legacy): Search for Child Day Care (cc2.cgi)

2. Primary Regulatory Citation

  • Statute: Code of Virginia § 22.1-289.010 et seq. (formerly § 63.2-1700)
  • Rules — Centers: 8VAC20-780 (amendments effective Jan 1, 2026; proposed rulemaking 42:8, Dec 1, 2025)
  • Rules — Family Day Homes: 8VAC20-790
  • Background Checks (new): 8VAC20-821 — effective Feb 1, 2026

6. Violations & Inspection Data new

Virginia publishes one of the most comprehensive per-facility inspection archives in the country. Every licensed center, licensed family day home, voluntarily-registered home, religious-exempt center, and certified preschool has a compliance-history page with every inspection since July 1, 2003.

Sources

  • Search for Child Day Care: dss.virginia.gov/facility/search/cc2.cgi
  • Facility-view URL pattern: cc2.cgi?ID=<facility_id> (landing) · cc2.cgi?ID=<facility_id>&Inspection=<inspection_id>&rm=Inspection (report)
  • Example: facility 38205, inspection 130833
  • Consumer finder: childcare.virginia.gov/find-care
  • Fairfax County (local ordinance homes): CCMSIS

Freshness

  • Inspection reports post within ~10 business days of the inspector's close-out meeting.
  • Coverage: post-July-1-2003 online; earlier via licensing inspector / FOIA.
  • Enforcement-order announcements posted to VDOE Early Childhood press feed.

Access Strategy

  • requests / httpx against cc2.cgi — GET parameters only; deterministic URL pattern. Force cp1252 decode to handle occasional character-encoding quirks.
  • Enumerate ID in the observed 20000–50000 range (sparse); crawl each facility landing page, then fetch each linked inspection report.
  • Rate: ≤1 req/sec; long User-Agent tolerated; no captcha observed.
  • Confirmation layer: Intercept childcare.virginia.gov/find-care XHR to capture VDOE's backing JSON endpoint and cross-check facility metadata.
FOIA backup. Virginia FOIA (Va. Code § 2.2-3700 et seq.) — 5 working days + 7-day extension. VDOE FOIA: office page. Use for pre-2003 records and structured CSV exports; routine requests are typically redirected to cc2.cgi.

7. Outreach Signals (Hot-Lead Detection)

  • Conditional or Provisional license on cc2.cgi — owner has a hard deadline (6–12 months) to reach Full status. Top outreach priority.
  • Key / Core standard violation in last cycle — "key" violations are the exact checklist an inspector opens with; repeat citations signal unresolved process gaps.
  • Self-Reported Incident in past 90 days — indicates active risk-management work; good context for a needs-analysis conversation.
  • Religious-Exempt → recently converted to Licensed (compare the religious-exempt ArcGIS layer to the licensed layer) — these operators are newly under VDOE oversight and face a steep learning curve.
  • 2026-02-01 background-check rule (8VAC20-821) applies retroactively to staff whose last check is older than 5 years — every center has a backlog to clear.

8. Next-Step Data Acquisition

  • requests scraper: dss.virginia.gov/facility/search/cc2.cgi — enumerate IDs 20000–50000; download each facility landing page + all linked inspections. Budget ~8 worker-hours with polite rate limiting.
  • ArcGIS cross-source: pull the five supplemental layers (religious-exempt, voluntary-registered FDH, NOVA local ordinance, Head Start, certified preschools) to build a complete "regulated child care" universe beyond the 2,856 in the current leads CSV.
  • FOIA: one request per fiscal year for adverse-action orders (revocation, denial, non-renewal) to complement cc2.cgi with final enforcement dispositions.
  • Email append: Leads CSV lacks phone/email; run against Apollo / Hunter using business name + address. Owner titles often on facility landing page.
  • Fairfax/NOVA supplement: scrape fairfaxcounty.gov/ofcsearch and each of Arlington/Alexandria/Prince William/Falls Church permit systems for NOVA coverage (VDOE doesn't license these).
