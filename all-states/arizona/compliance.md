# Arizona — Child Care Licensing Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority
- **Agency:** Arizona Department of Health Services (ADHS), Bureau of Child Care Licensing.
- **URL:** https://www.azdhs.gov/licensing/childcare-facilities/index.php
- **Public provider search:** https://azchildcareprovidersearch.azdes.gov/ (DES locator)
- **Related:** Arizona Department of Economic Security (DES), Child Care Administration — administers subsidy and certified family home program (DES-certified homes are regulated separately by DES, not ADHS).

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Child Care Facilities statute:** A.R.S. Title 36, Chapter 7.1 (§§ 36-881 to 36-897.01).
- **Child Care Facility Rules:** Arizona Administrative Code Title 9, Chapter 5 (9 A.A.C. 5), Articles 1–8 (R9-5-101 through R9-5-809). Significant rewrite effective **July 2024**; certain provisions (including 24-hr annual training) phased through **August 2026**.
- **Child Care Group Homes:** A.A.C. Title 6, Chapter 5, Article 55 (regulated by DES, not ADHS).
- **DES Certified Family Home Program:** A.A.C. R6-5-5201 et seq. (provider's home caring for ≤4 unrelated children for subsidy).

## Facility Types Regulated
- **Child Care Center** (ADHS): non-residential, 5+ unrelated children ≤14 yrs, <24 hrs/day — licensed by ADHS.
- **Child Care Group Home** (DES): provider's residence, 5–10 children — certified by DES.
- **Certified Family Child Care Home** (DES): provider's residence, ≤4 children — certified by DES if participating in subsidy.
- **License-exempt:** facilities operated by schools, tribal entities, federal/military installations, short-term parent-activity care, or care by relative.

## Key Licensing Forms (ADHS)
- **Child Care License Application** (ADHS form, submitted via ADHS Licensing portal).
- **Staff Records form/packet** per R9-5-402 (personnel file requirements).
- **DHS Child Care Inspection Checklist** (center-rules-instrument-checklist.pdf).
- **Immunization Record — Arizona School Entry (Arizona Blue Card)** — from Arizona Immunization Program Office / ADHS.
- **Medication Consent** — state-approved form per R9-5-507.
- **Incident Report / Serious Injury or Death** notification (R9-5-407).
- **Emergency and Disaster Plan** (written, on file).
- **Quality First** registration (separately through First Things First) for QRIS rating.

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (R9-5-404)
| Age band | Ratio (staff:children) |
|---|---|
| Infants (<1 year) | 1:5 (or 2:11 team) |
| 1-year-olds | 1:6 (or 2:13) |
| 2-year-olds | 1:8 |
| 3-year-olds | 1:13 |
| 4-year-olds | 1:15 |
| 5-year-olds (not yet school-age) | 1:20 |
| School-age children | 1:20 |

- Ratio is determined by age of **youngest** child in group.
- Maximum group size not prescribed as a single number; governed by physical-space rules in R9-5-501 (min 35 sq ft indoor / child; 75 sq ft outdoor / child).
- When 6+ children present, a facility director (or designee meeting R9-5-401) + 1 additional staff must be on-site; when ≤5 present, 1 director/designee + 1 backup reachable within 15 minutes.
- Volunteers meeting qualifications may count in ratio; student aides and lifeguards cannot.

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment form including emergency contact, authorized pick-up, allergies, health conditions.
- **Immunization record** per Arizona School Entry Schedule (A.R.S. § 36-883.02); medical, religious, or personal belief exemption documented on ADHS form.
- Record of any medications, special-care instructions.
- Daily attendance.
- Written medication authorization per dose/day, and medication log.
- Parent-signed acknowledgment of facility's parent handbook, safe-sleep practices, field-trip policies.
- Incident reports.
- Child records retained per R9-5-506 (duration specified in rule).

## Required Staff Records
- **Fingerprint Clearance Card (Arizona DPS Level I):** all staff, volunteers, and household members must hold a current Level I (enhanced) fingerprint clearance card. Checked against state & FBI databases and Central Registry.
- **DCS Central Registry check** — required prior to hire.
- **Resume/work history** and documentation of qualifications per R9-5-401 (director qualifications), R9-5-403 (staff qualifications).
- **TB screening / health declaration** at hire.
- **Orientation** within 30 days covering emergency response, CPR/first aid location, mandated reporting, medication, signs of abuse.
- **Annual continuing education:** increasing to **24 hours/year** under 2024 rule update; programs have until **August 2026** to comply with the new requirement (previously 18 hrs).
- **CPR/First Aid:** At least one staff member with current pediatric CPR and first aid certification on-site during operating hours.
- **Medication administration training** for any staff administering medication.
- **Documented observation / evaluation** annually.
- **Staff attendance** (arrival/departure time) must be documented daily per R9-5-402.

## Inspection Cadence
- **Child Care Centers (ADHS):** **annual unannounced** inspection (comprehensive licensing survey). Mid-year unannounced follow-up may occur. Fire and sanitation inspections annual.
- **Child Care Group Homes (DES):** inspected **twice annually**, all unannounced.
- **Complaint-driven** inspections at any time.
- ADHS posts inspection reports publicly at https://azdhs.gov/licensing/childcare-facilities/.

## Renewal Cadence
- Child Care Center license: ADHS issues licenses that remain in effect until **surrendered, revoked, suspended, or otherwise terminated** (no fixed expiration) — see A.R.S. § 36-883. Licensees must file updates annually (staffing, ownership, capacity) and pay annual fees.
- The **annual inspection** functions as the de facto renewal checkpoint.
- Material changes (capacity increase, new location, change of ownership) require amendment and re-inspection.
- Group Homes (DES): 1-year certification, renewed annually.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Level I Fingerprint Clearance Card** is the gating credential — no other hiring action may proceed without it.
- **2024/2026 rule update (9 A.A.C. 5):** increased training hours, clarified ratios, and tightened emergency preparedness requirements. ADHS published implementation tip sheet at https://www.qualityfirstaz.com/adhs-rules-update/.
- **Arizona School Entry Immunization** schedule is state-specific — includes Hepatitis A for child care populations; exemptions via ADHS Blue Card personal-belief form.
- **Safe-sleep:** back-sleeping required; no bumpers, loose bedding, or inclined sleepers (R9-5-509).
- **SIDS / Shaken Baby Syndrome** training required for infant-room staff.
- **Emergency and Disaster Plan** must include wildfire and monsoon/flood scenarios (Arizona-specific). Evacuation drills monthly.
- **Extreme heat protocols:** indoor temperature requirements and outdoor-play heat-index restrictions specific to AZ climate.
- **Quality First** (Arizona QRIS through First Things First) is separate voluntary rating — required for "First Things First-funded" slots.
- **Arizona Early Learning Standards** — non-mandatory curriculum framework aligned with QRIS.
