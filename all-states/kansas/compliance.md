# Kansas — Child Care Compliance Overview

## Licensing Authority

**Kansas Department of Health and Environment (KDHE) — Child Care Licensing Program**
- Address: 1000 SW Jackson St, Topeka, KS 66612
- Phone: 785-296-1270
- Web: https://www.kdhe.ks.gov/280/Child-Care-Licensing
- Online Facility Compliance Search: https://khap.kdhe.ks.gov/OIDS/

**Transition note:** Kansas HB 2045 (2025) creates the **Office of Early Childhood (OEC)**, which will assume child care licensing from KDHE with an initial transition milestone of **July 1, 2026**.

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Statute:** K.S.A. 65-501 et seq. (Maternity Centers and Child Care Facilities Act).
  - K.S.A. 65-504 — Licenses: contents, posting, inspections, temporary permits
  - K.S.A. 65-508 — Rulemaking authority
  - K.S.A. 65-512 — Inspections
- **Regulations:** Kansas Administrative Regulations (K.A.R.) Article 28-4, specifically:
  - K.A.R. 28-4-420 – Definitions (preschools and child care centers)
  - K.A.R. 28-4-426 – Administration
  - **K.A.R. 28-4-428 – Staff requirements** (amended August 2, 2024)
  - K.A.R. 28-4-428a – Education and training requirements
  - K.A.R. 28-4-440 – Programs serving infants and toddlers

## Facility Types Regulated

- **Family child care home** (registered; up to 10 children depending on age mix)
- **Group day care home** (licensed; up to 12 children)
- **Preschool** (primarily educational setting)
- **Child care center** (> 12 children; ratios by age)
- **School-age program** / drop-in program
- Maternity centers (under the same licensing act)

## Key Licensing Forms

- **Child Care Facility Application** (filed with KDHE Child Care Licensing Program)
- **KDHE Facility Compliance Inspection Report**
- **Fire marshal approval** (KS State Fire Marshal)
- **Sanitation inspection approval**
- **Criminal History & Background Check Request** (KBI + FBI + sex offender registry + child abuse/neglect central registry)
- **Health status statement** (annual update for each staff member)

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (K.A.R. 28-4-428, effective Aug 2, 2024)

| Ages of children | Minimum staff : child | Max children per unit |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (under 12 mo) | **A:** 1:3 **or B:** 1:4 | **A:** 9 / **B:** 8 |
| Infants + other children under age 6 | 1:6 (incl. max 3 infants) | 12 (incl. max 6 infants) |
| Toddlers (1 yr) | 1:6 | 12 |
| 2 years → under 3 | 1:7 | 14 |
| 2.5 years → under school-age | 1:12 | 24 |
| School-age | 1:16 | 32 |

Only one infant ratio (A or B) may be used at any one time within a given infant unit. Staff-to-child ratio is considered in compliance when a counted staff member leaves the unit briefly (< 5 min) with another staff member remaining, or up to 20 minutes with a substitute.

## Required Child Records

- Enrollment information (name, address, DOB, parent contacts)
- Immunization certification (per K.S.A. 65-508 / KDHE rules)
- Health assessment on file
- Emergency contact & authorized pick-up
- Allergies, chronic conditions, medication authorizations
- Daily attendance record
- Annual update of immunization & health status

## Required Staff Records (K.A.R. 28-4-428a)

- Background check — KBI state criminal history + FBI fingerprint check + sex offender registry check + child abuse/neglect central registry check (K.A.R. 28-4-428)
- **Annual health status form** (updated more often as needed)
- Education & training documentation (early childhood credential, college coursework)
- **SIDS & safe-sleep training** (mandatory for anyone caring for infants; 4 of training hours must address SIDS within first year)
- **Medication administration** training (if applicable)
- **CPR & First Aid certification**
- Orientation records
- Annual continuing education hours documentation
- Program Director requirements (facilities > 60 children: dedicated PD; > 100 children: PD with 1 yr admin experience; > 160 children: asst PD required)

## Inspection Cadence

- **Initial inspection** prior to license issuance (K.S.A. 65-512).
- Authority to enter at any time (compliance monitoring) without prior notice.
- Registered family day care homes: KDHE commences inspection per statutory schedule.
- Facility inspection results publicly searchable (last 3 years) via KDHE OIDS system.

## Renewal Cadence

- License renewed **annually** (K.S.A. 65-504).
- Annual fee schedule (K.S.A. 65-508):
  - Family day care home: statutory base rate
  - Group day care home: statutory base rate
  - Other child care facility: $75 + $1 × maximum capacity
- Failure to renew within 30 days of expiration incurs a **late fee equal to the renewal fee**.
- Revoked or non-renewed licenses not eligible to reapply for 1 year.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **August 2, 2024 regulation update** added slots — updated K.A.R. 28-4-428 expanded permissible group sizes and mixed-age ratios.
- **Transition to Office of Early Childhood (OEC):** KDHE directed to update rules by Oct 1, 2025, with OEC assumption of licensing functions July 1, 2026 (HB 2045, 2025 session).
- **Volunteer counting:** volunteers ≥ 16 yrs old may count in the staff:child ratio; volunteers ≥ 14 yrs old may be present but must be supervised.
- **Age-transition rule:** children may move to next age-appropriate unit within a 30-day window before/after reaching minimum age.
- **Annual emergency plan review** and at least one annual emergency drill required.
- **SIDS/safe-sleep** training mandatory for anyone caring for infants.
- **Food-related training** and sudden infant death syndrome training are required within first year of employment.
