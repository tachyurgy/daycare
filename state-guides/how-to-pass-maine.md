# How to Pass Your Maine Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In Maine, your licensor is a **Children's Licensing and Investigation Services (CLIS) specialist** from the **Maine DHHS Office of Child and Family Services (OCFS)**. You'll get at least **one unannounced inspection per 2-year license cycle**, and that inspection must land **between 6 and 18 months** after your license is issued (10-148 CMR Ch. 32 §4). Complaint-driven visits can happen anytime. Fire Marshal inspects at licensure and every 2 years after.

- Maine OCFS Child Care Licensing: https://www.maine.gov/dhhs/ocfs/provider-resources/child-care-licensing
- Maine Licensed Child Care Search: https://search.childcarechoices.me/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **Statute:** 22 M.R.S. §§ 8301-A through 8304 — Licensure of Child Care Facilities and Family Child Care Providers; §8302-A (background checks).
- **Regulations:** **10-148 CMR Chapter 32** — Child Care Facility Licensing Rule (centers, small facilities, nursery schools). PDF: https://www.maine.gov/dhhs/sites/maine.gov.dhhs/files/inline-files/Rules-for-the-Licensing-of-Child-Care-Facilities-10-148-Ch-32.pdf
- **10-148 CMR Chapter 33** — Family Child Care Provider Licensing Rule.
- **Immunization:** Title 22, Chapter 1671.
- **Public records:** 1 M.R.S. §408-A (FOAA).

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
**Larger Child Care Centers (13+ children) — 10-148 CMR Ch. 32 §7:**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (6 wk – 1 yr) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Young toddlers (1 – 2.5 yr) | 1:4 or 1:5 | 10 – 12 |
| Older toddlers (2.5 – 3.5 yr) | 1:7 | 21 |
| Preschool (3 – under 5) | 1:8 or 1:10 | 20 – 24 |
| School-age (5 – 12) | 1:13 | — |

**Small Child Care Facilities (3 – 12 children):** Infants 1:4; toddlers 2 – 5 yrs 1:8; school-age 1:12.

**Nursery Schools:** 1:12, max group 30.

**Warning:** The **age of the youngest child** sets the ratio for any mixed-age group. **At least two staff** must be present whenever **7 or more children** are present.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced (within the 6 – 18-month window after license issuance, per Ch. 32 §4).
2. Confirms current license + BrightStars rating signage posted.
3. Walks every room; counts kids and staff; checks ratios and group sizes.
4. Pulls staff files — background check package (BCI + FBI fingerprint + child abuse registry), references, health screening, qualifications, CPR/First Aid, 30-hour annual training log.
5. Pulls child files — enrollment, immunizations (with the 30-day grace), physical exam, emergency consents.
6. Checks outdoor play documentation (mandatory 60 minutes daily for children over 12 months in care 4+ hours — Ch. 32 §14).
7. Writes deficiencies and a corrective action plan (CAP). Reports stay on Maine Licensed Child Care Search for 3 rolling years.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Missing or insufficient outdoor play documentation** — Ch. 32 §14 requires 60 minutes daily for kids over 12 months and time outside for infants too. Maine is cold; owners skip this and get cited.
2. **Ratio and group-size violations** — especially when the youngest-child rule flips a mixed room.
3. **Background check gaps** — missing FBI fingerprint or expired 5-year refresh (22 M.R.S. §8302-A).
4. **Child record gaps** — immunization past the 30-day grace, missing physical exam.
5. **Annual training shortfall** — Maine requires 30 hours/year for lead center staff, tracked in Maine Roads to Quality.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
Required by 10-148 CMR Ch. 32 §5:
- **Enrollment application**.
- **Current immunization record** (30-day grace after first day of attendance).
- **Physical exam on admission**.
- Emergency medical authorization.
- Emergency contacts + authorized pickup list.
- Food allergy / dietary restriction documentation.
- Incident / accident reports.
- Daily attendance.
- Authorization for photos, field trips, transportation.
- **Retain records 3 years** after child leaves care.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application and resume.
- **State BCI + FBI fingerprint + child abuse registry** clearance pre-employment, refreshed every 5 years.
- Two reference checks.
- Health screening statement.
- **High school diploma minimum** for all staff; ECE credits or CDA for lead roles.
- **Current pediatric CPR and First Aid**.
- **30 hours of annual training** (center-based lead staff) logged in **Maine Roads to Quality**.
- Orientation + mandated-reporter training.
- Performance evaluations.
- **Retain 3 years** after staff leave.

## What must be on your walls
- Current OCFS license.
- Most recent Fire Marshal inspection.
- BrightStars / Rising Stars for ME rating (if participating).
- Evacuation plan / emergency procedures.
- Daily schedule.
- Menu.
- OCFS complaint contact.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented per rule.
- **Emergency / lockdown / weather drills** — documented.
- **Daily attendance** by room.
- **Outdoor play log** (60 minutes, per Ch. 32 §14).
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident / accident reports**.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff per room; verify youngest-child ratios.
2. Confirm 2+ staff present whenever 7+ kids are on site.
3. Post current license + latest Fire Marshal report.
4. Outdoor play log — today's entry filled in.
5. Pull a random staff file: 5-year background refresh current? 30-hour log on track? CPR current?
6. Pull a random child file: current immunizations and physical?
7. Medication locked, log current.
8. Diaper-change stations clean; gloves stocked.
9. Playground — fence intact; no frozen trip hazards in winter.
10. Fire-drill log within required interval; exit maps posted.

## What happens if you fail
Under 10-148 CMR Ch. 32, the Department can cite deficiencies and require a CAP. Statute authorizes fines of **$50 – $500 per incident** — but journalism (Maine Monitor / Bangor Daily News, 2024) has reported that out of 16,000+ citations since 2021 across 1,481 facilities, Maine has issued **64 conditional licenses and zero revocations/fines**. Enforcement posture is monitoring-and-correction, not punitive — but inspection reports are **public on search.childcarechoices.me** for a rolling 3 years, so parents and the press do see them. Appeals follow 22 M.R.S. administrative hearing procedure.

## The weird Maine gotcha nobody tells you about
**Maine mandates at least 60 minutes of active outdoor play every day** for children older than 12 months in care more than 4 hours — including winter. Infants must be taken outside at least once daily (10-148 CMR Ch. 32 §14). Owners in Aroostook or Piscataquis counties think they can skip this November through March. They get cited. Document every day: date, duration, weather, and any medical / temperature-related exception. Second gotcha: your one required unannounced inspection lands somewhere in a **12-month window** (6 – 18 months into your license). Owners relax in month 5 and get caught in month 6.
