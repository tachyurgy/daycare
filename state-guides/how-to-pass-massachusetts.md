# How to Pass Your Massachusetts Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Massachusetts Department of Early Education and Care (EEC)**. They are EEC licensors. Visits are tied to your license renewal cycle — centers, family child care homes, and small-group/school-age programs get a routine licensing visit roughly every **2 years**, plus unannounced monitoring visits in between. Complaints trigger visits at any time. Inspection findings land on the public "Find Child Care" portal within 90 days of case closure and stay up for as long as 5 years. Public sites:
- EEC home: https://www.mass.gov/orgs/department-of-early-education-and-care
- Public search (Find Child Care): https://childcare.mass.gov/findchildcare

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — M.G.L. c. 15D (the EEC statute). https://www.mass.gov/lists/eec-laws-and-regulations
- **The rulebook inspectors use** — 606 CMR 7.00. https://www.mass.gov/doc/family-group-school-age-child-care-programs-606-cmr-7/download
- **Background check rule** — 606 CMR 14.00 (Criminal and Background Record Checks).
- **Ratio rule** — 606 CMR 7.10. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/massachusetts/606-CMR-7-10
- **Health and sanitation overlay** — 105 CMR 430.00 (MA DPH).
- **Mandated reporter law** — M.G.L. c. 119, § 51A.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From 606 CMR 7.10 (center-based). Massachusetts is one of the tightest states in the country on infants and toddlers. Mixed-age groups default to the ratio of the youngest child. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age band | One staff for every... | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (birth – 15 months) | 3 | 7 |
| Toddlers (15 – 33 months) | 4 | 9 |
| Preschool (33 months – Kindergarten) | 10 | 20 |
| Kindergarten | 13 | 26 |
| School age (Grade 1 – 14 yrs) | 13 | 26 |

**Family Child Care:** up to 6 children under age 14; no more than 3 under age 2; no more than 2 under 15 months. With an approved assistant, up to 10.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives — routine visits are scheduled around renewal; monitoring visits can be unannounced.
2. Checks license is posted and matches the program you're running (FCC, Small Group/School Age, or Large Group/School Age).
3. Walks every room. Counts children. Checks the **staff-to-child ratio sign** posted at each group area.
4. Pulls staff files — looking first for the current **BRC (Background Record Check)**.
5. Pulls child files — immunizations, lead screening, IHCP if any.
6. Reviews emergency evacuation / shelter-in-place plan, sleep arrangements, and health care policy.
7. Reviews any pending **Corrective Action Plans (CAPs)** from a prior visit.
8. Exit meeting. Findings are posted to the Find Child Care page within 90 days of case closure; a CAP is issued with a due date.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
Per EEC enforcement patterns and the **November 2024 State Auditor's report** (which called out gaps in investigations, background-check review, and training):
1. **BRC lapses** (606 CMR 14.00) — a staff member or household member not re-cleared every 3 years, or missed a fingerprint check.
2. **Out-of-ratio or over group size** (606 CMR 7.10) — especially the 1:3 infant ratio.
3. **Missing annual 20-hour professional development** for Teacher/Lead Teacher.
4. **Lead paint inspection** missing for pre-1978 buildings serving kids under 6.
5. **Individual Health Care Plan (IHCP)** not on file for a child with a chronic condition or allergy.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment application** with emergency contacts.
- **Physical exam** — within 12 months of enrollment; annually for under-2, every 2 years for 2+.
- **Lead screening** per MA DPH (at 9–12 months and at ages 2, 3, and 4).
- **Immunization records** per the MA DPH schedule (MMR, DTaP, polio, Hep B, varicella, Hib, PCV, Hep A, flu, rotavirus per age).
- **Individual Health Care Plan (IHCP)** for any chronic condition or allergy.
- **Authorizations** — emergency medical, photo, transportation, authorized pickup list.
- **Signed parent handbook acknowledgment**.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **Background Record Check (BRC)** — refreshed **every 3 years**. Covers CORI, SORI, DCF, fingerprint-based national check. Household members 15+ also required for FCC.
- **EEC Professional Certification** — Teacher, Lead Teacher, or Director I/II, logged in the EEC Professional Qualifications Registry.
- **Current pediatric First Aid + CPR**.
- **20 hours annual professional development** for Teacher and Lead Teacher.
- **Annual orientation** (new staff) per 606 CMR 7.09(22).
- **Medication Administration Program (MAP)** certification — required if administering meds.
- **Mandated Reporter training** — M.G.L. c. 119, § 51A.
- **Physical / TB screening** where applicable.

## What must be on your walls
- Current EEC license.
- **Staff-to-child ratio posted at each group area** (explicit rule).
- Emergency evacuation and shelter-in-place plan.
- Health Care Policy (per 606 CMR 7.11).
- Menus.
- ABCs of safe sleep (Alone, on Back, in Crib) in infant rooms.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** — per your emergency plan; document monthly is typical practice.
- **Shelter-in-place / lockdown drill** — documented.
- **Injury and Incident Reports** — logged and shared with parents.
- **Monthly enrollment and compliance attestations** (for C3 grant recipients).
- **Transportation log** — any vehicle used requires written plan, annual vehicle inspection, and driver BRC.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff. **Especially in infant and toddler rooms — 1:3 and 1:4 leave no slack.**
2. Is the staff-to-child ratio sign posted in each room?
3. Walk the infant room: ABCs of sleep, no soft bedding.
4. Pull 3 random staff files — BRC date (under 3 years old?), 20-hour PD log, CPR card.
5. Pull 3 random child files — physical, lead screening, IHCP.
6. Review any open Corrective Action Plan from last visit — are items actually corrected?
7. Lead paint certificate for your building (if pre-1978 + kids under 6)?
8. Emergency plan posted, up to date.
9. Meds secured, MAP-certified staff on the floor.
10. License posted and current.

## What happens if you fail
EEC issues a **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** with a due date. Serious findings escalate to a license condition, provisional status, suspension, or revocation. The CAP and any substantiated complaints publish on findchildcare.mass.gov within 90 days of case closure and remain for up to 5 years. Appeals go through the EEC administrative process. Criminal activity surfaced through a BRC is itself a revocation trigger.

## The weird Massachusetts gotcha nobody tells you about
Massachusetts layers **three overlapping rulebooks** on the same program: 606 CMR 7 (EEC licensing), 606 CMR 14 (background checks), and 105 CMR 430 (DPH sanitation/health). A finding under any one of them shows up on your public record. On top of that, if you take **C3 stabilization grants**, you owe monthly enrollment and compliance attestations — miss them and EEC pauses your funding. And for any pre-1978 building serving children under 6, a **lead paint inspection** is a flat requirement — skipping it is one of the easiest and most expensive cites.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Massachusetts owner
Biggest fear: a Boston 25 News "25 Investigates" segment, or a quiet BRC lapse that gets flagged during the next monitoring visit. The November 2024 State Auditor report made every operator paranoid. Drop this in 30 seconds: "Your BRC is a 3-year cycle under 606 CMR 14, your Teachers need 20 PD hours a year, and after the November 2024 Auditor's report EEC is tightening monitoring — are you tracking every staff BRC date and every open CAP in one place, or still in a binder?" Today they are using a mix of the EEC Professional Qualifications Registry for certifications, a binder for BRC dates, and spreadsheets for the 20-hour PD. The close: "We watch every BRC clock and every open CAP so you never have a lapsed file when a licensor walks in."
