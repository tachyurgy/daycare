01 Licensing Authority

Indiana Family and Social Services Administration (FSSA) — Office of Early Childhood and Out-of-School Learning (OECOSL) / Bureau of Child Care. Consumer portal: in.gov/fssa/carefinder. Search: Provider Search. Contact: 877-511-1144, carefinder@fssa.in.gov.

02 Primary Regulatory Citation

  • 470 IAC 3-4.7 — Child Care Centers Licensing — Cornell LII
  • 470 IAC 3-1.1 — Licensed Child Care Homes
  • 470 IAC 3-4.5 — Unlicensed Registered Child Care Ministries
  • Enabling statute: Indiana Code 12-17.2
  • IC 12-17.2-2-1(9): prohibits publication of physical addresses of licensed child care homes — important scraping constraint

Indiana flags "critical health & safety violations" with a red notification on each provider's search page. No CSV, Socrata, ArcGIS, or API feed of violations exists. Bulk harvest path requires a headless browser against the ASP.NET app (session-cookie + viewstate dependency). APRA path: request statewide extract from FSSA via FSSA public records; 7 calendar days to acknowledge, fees capped at actual-cost copying.

07 Outreach Signals

  • Federal CCDBG compliance pain. Every licensed provider must document 10-topic pre-service training; ComplianceKit's training-hours tracker is a direct fit.
  • PTQ level progression is revenue-linked. Moving from PTQ 1 → 3 unlocks On My Way Pre-K eligibility and higher CCDF reimbursement — a quantifiable ROI story.
  • Tornado drill logging (required Mar–Jun monthly) is a MO/IN-specific need that paper-based calendars routinely miss; digital drill logger is a wedge.
  • Home provider privacy constraint (IC 12-17.2-2-1(9)) means marketing can't use home addresses — but phone-based outbound works; ~2,500 licensed homes are hard to reach via mail.
  • Registered Child Care Ministries (~1,000) are statutorily exempt from full licensing but must still register & meet minimum health/safety rules — underserved segment with low competition.
