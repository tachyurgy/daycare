# Montana — Child Care Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority

**Montana Department of Public Health and Human Services (DPHHS), Early Childhood and Family Support Division (ECFSD), Child Care Licensing Program.**

- Bureau: Early Childhood Services Bureau (ECSB)
- Licensing site: https://dphhs.mt.gov/ecfsd/childcare/childcarelicensing/
- Quality Assurance Division (for the provider search portal): https://dphhs.mt.gov/qad

ECFSD licenses and registers child day care facilities, contracts with Child Care Resource and Referral (CCR&R) agencies regionally, administers the STARS to Quality QRIS, and operates the Best Beginnings Scholarship.

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Administrative Rules of Montana (ARM) Title 37, Chapter 95 — "Licensing of Day Care Facilities."**
  - **ARM 37.95.101–107** — general requirements & definitions
  - **ARM 37.95.108** — registration and licensing procedures
  - **ARM 37.95.141** — child care facility records
  - **ARM 37.95.172** — supervision at all times
  - **ARM 37.95.205–227** — child care center rules
  - **ARM 37.95.601+** — family and group day care homes
  - **ARM 37.95.623** — CHILD CARE CENTERS: CHILD-TO-STAFF RATIOS
- Enabling statute: **MCA Title 52, Chapter 2, Part 7** — Day Care Facilities; also MCA §52-2-704 (licensing authority).
- **House Bill 422 (2023)** revised provider-to-child ratios (e.g., 1-year-olds: 6:1 instead of 4:1; 6+ year-olds: 20:1 instead of 14:1). Current rules reflect HB 422 changes.

## Facility Types Regulated

| Facility Type | Description | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Family Child Care Home | Registered; provider's home; up to 6 children | ARM 37.95.601+ |
| Group Child Care Home | Registered; provider's home; up to 12 children | ARM 37.95.601+ |
| Child Care Center | Licensed; non-residential; 13+ children | ARM 37.95.205-227 |
| Drop-In Day Care Center | Short-term care; special rules | ARM 37.95.1105+ |

## Key Licensing Forms

- **DPHHS-CCL-1** — Child Care Center License Application
- **DPHHS-CCL Family/Group Registration Application** (Rev. 12/21 or later)
- **Practitioner Registry Application** (required via Early Childhood Practitioner Registry before staff count toward ratios)
- **Child Care Background Check Form** — state, FBI, Child Protective Services clearance (Centralized Background Check Unit)
- **Fire Marshal Inspection Report** (state or local fire authority)
- **Health Authority Certificate of Approval** (local public-health sanitarian, per ARM 37.95.128/139/140)

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes

**Child Care Centers (ARM 37.95.623) — post-HB 422:**

| Age | Ratio |
|---|---|
| Birth–12 months | 1:4 |
| 13–23 months (1-year-olds) | 1:6 |
| 2 years | 1:8 |
| 3 years | 1:10 |
| 4–5 years | 1:12 |
| 6 years and over | 1:20 |

Ratios may be doubled at nap time for children 2+ when specific conditions are met.

**Family / Group Child Care Homes:**
- Family home: up to 6 children (including the provider's own under age 6)
- Group home: up to 12 children with second caregiver
- Under 2 years old: no more than 3 when only one caregiver is present (facilities serving only under-2 maintain 1:4)

## Required Child Records

Per **ARM 37.95.141:**
- Enrollment form (name, DOB, parents, emergency contacts, authorized pickup)
- Immunization record compliant with DPHHS school immunization schedule
- Physician's statement / health history
- Medication administration authorization (if applicable)
- Emergency medical consent
- Food allergy / dietary restriction notes
- Daily attendance
- Accident / incident reports
- Authorization for field trips, transportation, photos
- Retained for period specified in ARM 37.95.141

## Required Staff Records

- Application with work history
- **Early Childhood Practitioner Registry** level verification (required for all staff counted in ratios; exempt if working <500 hours/year or facility only serves ages 5+)
- Comprehensive background check (state, FBI fingerprint, CPS registry)
- Health screening
- Orientation records: CPR/First Aid (within 30 days), Infant Safety Essentials (2 hours, within 30 days), Health & Safety Orientation (6 hours within 90 days), Together We Grow (3 hours within 90 days)
- **Annual 16 hours of continuing education** (must be complete before Practitioner Registry level expiration)
- Health & Safety Review (6 hours every 3 years)
- Current CPR and First Aid certificates
- Performance evaluations

## Inspection Cadence

- **Pre-license inspection** by DPHHS licensing specialist
- **Annual renewal inspection** — fire marshal approval required annually and submitted at renewal
- **Health authority inspection** at initial and as warranted (per ARM 37.95.128/139/140)
- **Unannounced monitoring** and complaint visits at DPHHS discretion
- License information is updated at DPHHS's public Provider Search at least once per month

## Renewal Cadence

Licenses are issued for **one year** (annual renewal). Renewal applications are due **at least 30 days before expiration**. Annual inspection report from the state fire marshal (or official designee) must accompany renewal.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Provider Search portal** (https://dphhs.mt.gov/ecfsd/childcare/childcarelicensing/providersearch) is powered by SansWrite and publishes inspection reports and complaint history. Providers should assume transparency.
- **HB 422 (2023) loosened ratios** relative to pre-2023 rules — e.g., 1-year-olds went from 1:4 to 1:6; this is a meaningful operational change worth cross-checking if a provider's licensing history spans 2023.
- **Early Childhood Practitioner Registry** is the gatekeeper for staff qualifications and must be maintained annually.
- **Exemption for drop-in care:** ARM 37.95.1105+ has distinct rules for drop-in centers (common in ski/tourism areas).
- **Extensive geographic spread + low density:** Montana has ~930 licensed facilities across a huge area — inspection travel is a meaningful DPHHS operational factor. Complaint response is occasionally slow in rural counties.
- **Tribal lands** (CSKT, Crow, Blackfeet, Fort Peck, Fort Belknap, Northern Cheyenne, Rocky Boy's) may operate under separate tribal jurisdiction; some tribal programs are state-licensed, others are not — verify individually.
- **Sanitarians are local, not state** — local health department sanitarian inspection is distinct from DPHHS licensing.
