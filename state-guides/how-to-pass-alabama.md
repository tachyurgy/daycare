# How to Pass Your Alabama Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Alabama Department of Human Resources (DHR), Child Care Services Division**. You get **at least one unannounced annual inspection** (Chapter 660-5-26). New centers or those with prior deficiencies may get quarterly monitoring visits. Complaints trigger unannounced visits anytime. Fire Marshal and Health Department do their own separate annual inspections. Heads up: **Alabama does not publish a per-facility public inspection portal**, so deficiency reports must be posted **at the facility** — individual parents see them in person. If you want full inspection history, you have to file a FOIA request with DHR. Public directories (roster only, no findings):
- DHR Child Care: https://dhr.alabama.gov/child-care/
- Day Care Directory (centers): https://apps.dhr.alabama.gov/daycare/daycare_search
- Day Care Homes Directory: https://apps.dhr.alabama.gov/Child_Care_Services/Lic_Day_Care_Homes

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — Ala. Code § 38-7-1 et seq. (Alabama Child Care Act of 1971). https://alisondb.legislature.state.al.us/alison/codeofalabama/1975/38-7-1.htm
- **The rulebook for centers** — Ala. Admin. Code Chapter 660-5-26 (Minimum Standards for Day Care Centers and Nighttime Centers).
- **The rulebook for homes** — Ala. Admin. Code Chapter 660-5-27 (Family / Group Day Care Homes and Nighttime Homes).
- **Child Care Program rule** — Ala. Admin. Code r. 660-5-20-.04. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/alabama/Ala-Admin-Code-r-660-5-20-.04
- **Background Check statute** — Ala. Code § 38-13.
- **2018 Child Care Safety Act** (Act 2018-390) — closed the religious-exemption loophole for programs receiving state/federal funds. https://alison.legislature.state.al.us/files/pdf/search/alison_2018RS/PublicActs/2018-390.htm
- **Center Minimum Standards PDF (the actual document inspectors reference)** — https://dhr.alabama.gov/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/No-Highlighted-MS-for-CENTERS-revised.pdf

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From Rule 660-5-26 (centers). Mixed-age groups default to the youngest child's ratio. Ratios apply **at all times** including nap, transport, and water activities (which require tightened ratios). Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age Group | One staff for every... |
|---|---|
| 0 – 18 months (infants) | 5 |
| 18 months – 2½ years | 7 |
| 24 – 36 months | 8 |
| 2½ – 4 years | 11 |
| 4 years – school-age | 18 |
| School-age | 21 (commonly applied; verify current handbook) |

Space: 35 sq ft indoors per child; 75 sq ft outdoors. Swimming pools > 3 ft require an extra adult plus a CPR-certified lifeguard.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced.
2. Checks license is posted and current. Looks at required postings — license, exempt-status notice (if applicable), discipline policy, emergency plan.
3. Walks every room, counts children, compares to staff present.
4. Pulls staff files — **ABI + FBI fingerprint** + **CA/N Central Registry (SCAN)** clearance first.
5. Pulls child files — Alabama Certificate of Immunization (blue card IMM 50/51), emergency info, medication consents.
6. Reviews discipline policy, emergency plan, drill logs, incident reports.
7. Confirms current **Fire Marshal** inspection and **Health Department** sanitation inspection on file with **no outstanding violations**.
8. Exit conference. Deficiency report is created and **must be posted at the facility** where parents can see it.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
Based on Chapter 660-5-26 and DHR enforcement patterns:
1. **Out-of-ratio / supervision lapses** — especially during transitions and at pickup.
2. **ABI + FBI fingerprint or CA/N Central Registry clearance missing** — can't have the person on the floor without both.
3. **Blue card immunization missing** — must be the Alabama Certificate of Immunization (IMM 50/51) or a valid exemption.
4. **Current fire or health inspection not on file with no outstanding violations** — DHR requires both at renewal.
5. **Discipline policy not written / not distributed to parents** — corporal punishment is explicitly prohibited; written policy is mandatory.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **DHR-CCS-1618** — Child Application / Enrollment.
- **Current Alabama Certificate of Immunization** (blue card IMM 50/51) or religious/medical exemption per Ala. Admin. Code 420-4-3.
- **Physical / health examination records** as required.
- **Medication authorization + administration log**.
- **Signed authorization** for transportation, field trips, emergency medical care.
- **Daily attendance** (sign-in/sign-out).
- **Incident / accident reports**.
- **Allergy / special-needs plan**.
- **Written notice of discipline policy receipt**.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **ABI + FBI fingerprint clearance** — required **before** unsupervised child contact.
- **SCAN / CA/N Central Registry clearance** — required for all staff and volunteers.
- **DHR-CCS-1207A** — Personnel / Staff Information.
- **Current infant-child First Aid + CPR** (at least one certified staff on duty at all times).
- **TB screening / health statement**.
- **Mandatory orientation**.
- **At least 12 clock hours of annual in-service training** per 660-5-26.
- **Safe Sleep + Shaken Baby Syndrome training** documentation.
- **Mandated reporter acknowledgment**.
- **Performance evaluations / discipline records**.
- **Background checks renewed** per Ala. Code § 38-13-7 (typically every 5 years or on triggering events).

## What must be on your walls
- Current DHR license.
- **Deficiency report from the most recent inspection** (rule requires it be posted where parents can see).
- Emergency Preparedness and Evacuation Plan (DHR-CCS-1503).
- Discipline Policy (DHR-CCS-1532).
- Menus.
- Exempt-status notice (for religiously exempt facilities).
- Complaint phone: (334) 242-1425 or (866) 528-1694.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** — per emergency plan.
- **Severe weather / tornado drill** — per plan (AL tornado risk).
- **Lockdown drill** — per plan.
- **Daily attendance** (sign-in/sign-out).
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident/accident reports**.
- **Annual vehicle inspection** (if transporting) + child-check ("child left behind") protocol on every trip.
- **Annual Fire Marshal inspection** on file.
- **Annual Health Department sanitation inspection** on file.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff. Watch transitions, pickup, and nap.
2. Pull 3 random staff files — ABI + FBI + SCAN/CA-N clearance, 12-hour in-service log, CPR card.
3. Pull 3 random child files — IMM 50/51 blue card, DHR-CCS-1618 enrollment, allergy plan.
4. Infant rooms: back sleep, no loose bedding, Safe Sleep training documented.
5. License, deficiency report, discipline policy, emergency plan all posted?
6. Current Fire Marshal inspection on file with no open violations?
7. Current Health Department sanitation inspection on file with no open violations?
8. Transportation: if you transport today, vehicle attendance log + child-check protocol in the van.
9. Fire drill log and tornado drill log — initialed for this month/season?
10. Discipline policy distributed and acknowledged in every child's file (no corporal punishment).

## What happens if you fail
DHR issues deficiencies under 660-5-26 or 660-5-27. A corrective action plan is required. Escalation runs through probation, suspension, revocation, or fine per the Child Care Act. Alabama does **not** publish findings online, so enforcement shows up in two places: the in-facility deficiency posting, and the state-press-release / news-media channel. The 2018 Child Care Safety Act closed the religious-exemption loophole for any program that accepts state or federal funds (including CCDBG subsidies) — so exempt status is not a permanent shield.

## The weird Alabama gotcha nobody tells you about
**Alabama is one of the few states without a public per-facility inspection portal.** The DHR directories show only contact info — no inspection history, no deficiency history. That's unusual: South Carolina, Maryland, Wisconsin, Minnesota, and most peer states publish every finding. In Alabama, deficiency reports must be **posted at the facility** per Minimum Standards, but the only way to do bulk research is a FOIA request to DHR (typically 30–60 business days for batch data). That's great if you want privacy — and a problem if a parent wants to do homework. The other Alabama quirk: the **2018 Child Care Safety Act** (Act 2018-390). Before this law, any church-affiliated day care could self-declare "exempt" and skip licensing entirely. After 2018, if you accept state or federal funds — including CCDBG subsidies — you must be licensed. But Alabama still allows **non-funded church-operated day cares** to elect exemption, as long as they post notice of exempt status and give parents written disclosure. So two near-identical programs across the street from each other may be under very different rules.

## For Magnus — how to sell to an Alabama owner
Biggest fear: a loud parent, a news story, or a CPS referral, because there is no public portal to pre-explain a deficiency — so any story that breaks breaks without their version of the facts online. Drop this in 30 seconds: "660-5-26 makes you post the deficiency report at the facility, the 2018 Child Care Safety Act killed the CCDBG religious-exempt loophole, and ABI + FBI + SCAN must all be done before a staff member is on the floor. Are you tracking background-check renewal dates, the 12-hour annual in-service, and making sure the Fire Marshal and Health Department inspections stay current?" Today they are using paper forms, a binder for the in-facility deficiency posting, and no system for the annual fire/health inspection clock. The close: "We keep your file stack ready for a DHR visit at any hour — and we give you the paper trail you'd need if a parent ever files a complaint."
