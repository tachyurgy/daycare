# Nebraska — Child Care Compliance Overview

## Licensing Authority

**Nebraska Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) — Division of Public Health, Licensure Unit, Office of Children's Services Licensing**
- Address: PO Box 94986, Lincoln, NE 68509-4986
- Phone: 1-800-600-1289 (in NE) / 402-471-9278 (Lincoln)
- Web: https://dhhs.ne.gov/Pages/Child-Care-Licensing.aspx

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Statute:** Nebraska Child Care Licensing Act (Neb. Rev. Stat. §71-1908 et seq.)
- **Regulations:** **Title 391 Nebraska Administrative Code (NAC) — Children's Services Licensing**
  - **Chapter 2:** Family Child Care Home I (up to 10 children, one provider)
  - **Chapter 3:** Child Care Centers (> 12 children); also Family Child Care Home II (up to 12 children, two providers)
  - **Chapter 4+:** School-age-only, preschool, dual-license rules
- Effective/operative: 2/20/13 & 5/20/13 for current Title 391

## Facility Types Regulated

- **Family Child Care Home I** — up to 10 children, one provider, in the provider's home.
- **Family Child Care Home II** — up to 12 children, two providers, in primary provider's home OR separate building.
- **Child Care Center** — > 12 children, ratios determined by ages.
- **Preschool** — primarily educational setting.
- **School-Age-Only Center** — children 5–13 only.
- **Dual License** — centers with ≤ 12 children may apply for dual licensing allowing use of Family Child Care Home II staff:child ratios.

All newly licensed programs receive a **Provisional License** for their first year of operation.

## Key Licensing Forms

- **Child Care License Application** (initial / provisional)
- **Dual License Application and Agreement Form**
- **Health Information Report** (per staff, per 391 NAC 3-006.03F)
- **Background Check Request** (Nebraska State Patrol + FBI fingerprint + sex offender registry + CPS registry)
- **Fire Inspection Approval** (state fire marshal or delegated authority)
- **Sanitation Inspection Approval** (DHHS or delegated authority)
- **Annual Immunization Report** (per 173 NAC 4 and Neb. Rev. Stat. §§71-1913.01 – 71-1913.03)

## Child-to-Staff Ratios (391 NAC 3-006.15C — Child Care Center)

| Age of Children in Care | Children per Staff |
|---|---|
| 6 weeks – 18 months | 4 : 1 |
| 18 months – 3 years | 6 : 1 |
| 3 years | 10 : 1 |
| 4 and 5 years | 12 : 1 |
| School-age | 15 : 1 |

Compliance is determined **center-wide**. Only staff involved in direct care count. At least two staff members must be on premises at all times except when ≤ 12 children in care, or when all children are school-age and ≤ 15.

For Family Child Care Home I/II the ratios are more restrictive and rely on the home's license type and staffing.

## Required Child Records (391 NAC 3-006.18 & related)

- Enrollment record (name, DOB, parent contacts, emergency contacts)
- **Immunization record** within 30 days of enrollment (per Neb. Rev. Stat. §71-1913.01–03 and 173 NAC 4); updated at each new immunization
- Physician/APRN/PA certification of medical exemption OR parental non-immunization statement
- Health history & allergy / medical condition information
- Medication authorization and log
- Authorized pick-up list
- Attendance record
- Emergency Action Plan acknowledgment

## Required Staff Records (391 NAC 3-006.03 and 3-006.04/05)

- Application & employment history
- **Criminal History Record Checks** (Nebraska State Patrol + FBI fingerprint + federal indices)
- **Registry checks:** Child/Adult Central Registry + Sex Offender Registry (Sex Offender Registration Act, Neb. Rev. Stat. §§29-4001 – 29-4014)
- **Report of any law enforcement contact** (3-006.03C, 03D)
- **Repeat registry checks** (3-006.03E; periodic)
- **Health Information Report** (3-006.03F)
- Director qualifications documentation (3-006.04)
- Teacher qualifications — certificated or non-certificated (3-006.05)
- Substitute / volunteer / parent helper records (3-006.06)
- Orientation & training (3-006.08)

## Inspection Cadence (391 NAC 3-005)

- **Initial Licensure Inspection** (3-005.01): on-site inspection within 30 days of complete provisional-license application.
- **Provisional-to-Operating License Inspection** (3-005.02): unannounced inspection within 30 days of operating-license application.
- **Annual and Semi-Annual License Inspections** (3-005.03):
  - **At least once per year** for centers licensed for ≤ 29 children.
  - **At least twice per year** for centers licensed for ≥ 30 children.
- **Follow-up inspection** (3-005.04): within 60 days of an inspection revealing violations (or sooner if the violation requires immediate correction).
- **Monitoring inspections** (3-005.05): unannounced, at any time.
- **Fire / sanitation inspections** (3-005.09) conducted by other entities, submitted to DHHS.
- Compliance reports provided to the facility within 10 working days of inspection.

## Renewal Cadence

- Provisional license: one year; DHHS sends expiration notice ≥ 90 days before expiration (3-003.01D).
- Failure to complete compliance within 90 days after provisional expiration results in the application being processed as a provisional-to-operating inspection.
- Operating license is renewed via annual license inspection + updated staff/health records.
- **Temporary / Voluntary Cease of Operation:** up to one year (extendable); no routine inspections during cease period.
- Weekly updated roster is published by DHHS Licensure Unit.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Annual immunization report** filing is mandatory (173 NAC 4).
- **Safety training** and **child care management training** are required for directors (3-006.08A, 3-006.08B).
- **Communicable disease** notification to all enrolled families on the same day any reportable illness is observed or reported.
- **Exclusion policy** required in writing — must address fever, diarrhea, vomiting, chickenpox, measles, etc.
- **Step Up to Quality** (NE's QRIS): participation & rating tracked on DHHS roster.
- **Subsidy contract (Title XX / CCSP):** separate status tracked on roster.
- **Dual-license option** allows small centers (≤ 12 children) to operate under Family Child Care Home II ratios — useful for micro-centers.
- Each center's **licensed capacity** is the lesser of DHHS-approved capacity and state fire marshal–authorized capacity.
- Staff must be **awake and alert to children at all times, including during naps**.
