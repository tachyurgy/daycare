# Wisconsin Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
- **Agency:** Wisconsin Department of Children and Families (DCF)
- **Division:** Division of Early Care and Education / Bureau of Early Care Regulation (BECR)
- **URL:** https://dcf.wisconsin.gov/cclicensing

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **DCF 250** — Licensing Rules for Family Child Care Centers (4–8 children)
- **DCF 251** — Licensing Rules for Group Child Care Centers and Child Care Programs Established or Contracted for by School Boards (9+ children)
- **DCF 252** — Licensing Rules for Day Camps
- **DCF 202** — Child Care Certification (county/tribal certified providers; separate pathway)
- **Enabling statute:** Wis. Stat. ch. 48.65 (licensed child care)

## Facility Types Regulated
- **Group Child Care Center** (DCF 251) — 9+ children; non-residential
- **Family Child Care Center** (DCF 250) — 4–8 children; typically operated in a residence
- **Day Camp** (DCF 252)
- **Certified Child Care** (DCF 202) — provisionally regulated providers caring for ≤ 3 children not related to them (county-certified pathway), required to accept Wisconsin Shares subsidy

## Key Licensing Forms
- **DCF-F-CFS0003 — Application for a Child Care License**
- **DCF-F-CFS0062 — Background Record Check Request**
- **DCF-F-CFS0055 — Child's Health History & Emergency Care Plan**
- **DCF-F-CFS0056 — Child Medication Consent**
- **DCF-F-CFS0077 — Staff Record**
- **DCF-F-0078 — Staff-to-Child Ratio Worksheet**
- **DCF-F-CFS0082 — Injury/Incident Report**
- **YoungStar** QRIS enrollment documents
- **Safe Sleep Policy** template

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (DCF 251 Table 251.055 — group centers)
| Age band | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Birth–12 months (infant) | 1:4 | 8 |
| 12–24 months (toddler) | 1:4 | 8 |
| 2 years | 1:6 | 12 |
| 2 years 9 mo–3 years | 1:8 | 16 |
| 3 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4–5 years | 1:13 | 24 |
| School age (5 yr+ attending Kindergarten or above) | 1:17 | 32 |

Mixed-age infant/toddler groups capped at 8.

**Family Child Care (DCF 250):** 1 provider may care for up to 8 children under age 7, no more than 3 under 2 and no more than 6 from a single family.

*Note:* Wisconsin has revised infant/toddler rules scheduled to take effect summer 2026; verify current edition before publication.

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment form with emergency contacts, physician info, authorized pickup list
- **Immunization record** per Wisconsin Immunization Law (ch. DHS 144)
- **Health History and Emergency Care Plan** (within 30 days)
- **Physical exam** — within 3 months after enrollment (under 2 years need annual; 2+ biennial)
- **Allergy / medical action plans**
- **Medication consent** (Form CFS0056)
- **Authorization for transportation / photos / emergency treatment**

## Required Staff Records
- **Background Record Check (BRC)** — initial (fingerprint + state/national) and rechecks every 4 years
- **Department of Justice (DOJ) check, Sex Offender Registry, DCF Child Abuse/Neglect Registry**
- **The Registry** (Wisconsin Registry) — state professional development registry; all staff must register
- **Wisconsin Child Care Teacher / Lead Teacher credential** or equivalent coursework
- **Orientation + pre-service training** within first week; child care teacher must complete **Registry-approved 80-hour coursework** (Intro to Child Care Profession / Skills & Strategies)
- **First Aid + CPR** current (infant/child)
- **Annual continuing education** — **25 hours per year for lead teachers**, 15 hours for assistants (via The Registry)
- **Shaken Baby Syndrome Prevention** training (statutory; one-time + refresher)
- **Mandated Reporter** training
- **TB screening** where applicable
- Director: Wisconsin Child Care Administrator Credential

## Inspection Cadence
- **Routine licensing inspection:** at least annually (unannounced) for group centers
- **Family child care:** at least once per license term (2-year)
- **Monitoring visits** more frequent for newer programs or those with prior violations
- **Complaint-based investigations** as triggered
- Public inspection history via Child Care Finder

## Renewal Cadence
- **Continuous licensure** (no renewal required) as long as license fee is paid annually and compliance is maintained. **Continuous** license must be actively renewed only when ownership changes or license is revoked.
- Initial **provisional license** for new providers (typically 6 months)

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **YoungStar** — 5-level QRIS; ratings published in Child Care Finder and tied to Wisconsin Shares subsidy reimbursement differentials
- **Wisconsin Shares** — subsidy program; providers must be licensed or certified and YoungStar-rated to accept
- **The Registry (wiregistry.org)** — mandatory professional registry tracking all child care staff education, training hours, and credentials
- **Shaken Baby Syndrome prevention** — Wisconsin-specific legal requirement (Wis. Stat. § 48.982); signed parent handbook section required
- **Safe Sleep (ABCs)** — written policy, staff training, parent agreement
- **Child-Care Information Center (CCIC)** — DCF-operated library/training support network
- **Biting/aggression policies** must be documented
- **Wading pools banned** in licensed care; swimming pools require lifeguard
- **Screen time** limited per DCF guidance
- **Required written policies:** health & safety, emergency, discipline, grievance, biting, sick child
- **Transportation:** if transporting, written plan, driver background + license verification, vehicle inspection
- **Continuous licensure model** eliminates routine renewal but requires ongoing compliance
