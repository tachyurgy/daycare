01

Product Vision

ComplianceKit replaces the paper binder, the clipboard on the wall, and the filing cabinet full of forms. Every daycare in America is running their compliance on paper — and they're failing inspections because of it.

Core Insight

Inspectors use checklists. We give daycare owners the same checklists, pre-filled with their data, so they can self-audit before the inspector ever shows up.

Violation Cause: Paperwork

The most common violations are all documentation failures — expired background checks, missing immunization records, incomplete fire drill logs, undocumented daily inspections. These are things software trivially solves. Every daycare director knows the pain of scrambling through file folders when the inspector walks in.

There is zero incumbent software under $100/month that serves micro-business daycare operators. Enterprise solutions exist for large chains, but the ~60,000 independent and small-chain facilities in the US have nothing purpose-built for their compliance needs. ComplianceKit fills that gap.

Three-State Launch Strategy

We launch in Texas, Florida, and California because they represent the three largest childcare markets, three distinct regulatory models, and enough diversity to validate our state-abstraction layer before expanding nationwide.

Architectural Implication

Every entity in our domain model must carry a state_code context. Ratios, required documents, credential types, violation levels, checklist templates, and drill schedules are all state-specific. The StateConfig system acts as a rule engine that parameterizes the entire application per state.

Design Principle

StateConfig is the single source of truth for all state-specific rules. Adding a new state means adding a new StateConfig entry — no code changes required for the core application logic. The UI, validation, alerting, and reporting layers all read from StateConfig to parameterize their behavior.

Key Data Flows

1. Morning Routine

1
Staff opens app

PWA loads, service worker checks for cached checklist template. Dashboard shows compliance score and any overnight alerts.

2
Daily checklist auto-loaded

Checklist template selected based on facility's state config (TX Form 1100, FL Section 3.1, or CA Title 22 custom). Items pre-populated with facility-specific details.

3
Tap through items

Staff swipes/taps each item. Photo attachment for hazard-free areas. Notes for any issues found. Each item timestamped individually.

4
Checklist stored

Completed checklist timestamped and synced to Supabase. If offline, queued in IndexedDB for background sync. Compliance score updates in real-time.

2. Child Check-In

1
Parent arrives

Staff opens attendance module, scans child's QR code or selects name from roster.

2
Signature capture

Parent signs on screen (touch signature). Check-in timestamped with authorized pickup verification.

3
Ratio auto-updates

Supabase Realtime broadcasts attendance change. RatioMeter updates across all classrooms. Alert triggered if ratio approaches or exceeds limit.

3. Credential Expiration Alert

1
Nightly cron job runs

Supabase Edge Function checks all StaffCredentials for expiry within 30/60/90-day windows.

2
ComplianceAlerts generated

Alert records created with severity level. Push notification sent to facility director via Web Push API.

3
Director takes action

Alert appears in dashboard AlertBanner. Director can tap through to the specific staff member's credential and initiate renewal workflow.

4. Inspection Preparation

1
Director taps "Inspection Readiness"

App generates comprehensive report across ALL compliance domains using StateConfig to determine which standards apply.

2
Green / Yellow / Red per area

InspectionReadiness widget shows donut chart with drill-down capability. Each category expandable to individual deficient items.

3
PDF export

React-PDF generates a report that mirrors what the inspector will check. Can be printed or shared on iPad during inspection.

5. Fire Drill

1
Staff taps "Start Drill"

DrillTimer starts counting. Drill type selected (fire / severe_weather / lockdown / earthquake).

2
Evacuation and "All Clear"

Staff taps "All Clear" when evacuation complete. Timer stops. Staff enters headcount for children and staff present.

3
Auto-logged and scheduled

Drill logged with date, time, duration, and attendance. System auto-calculates if this month's requirement is met and schedules next reminder based on StateConfig drill frequency.

Inspection Day Experience

This is the moment that matters. The inspector walks through the door, clipboard in hand, and the next 90 minutes determine whether your facility stays open. Here's how that day plays out — with and without ComplianceKit.

Without ComplianceKit
  1. Inspector arrives — director panics
  2. Director scrambles to find file folders in the cabinet
  3. Can't find immunization records for 2 children
  4. CPR cert expired last week for one teacher
  5. Fire drill log only has 3 entries (needs 12)
  6. Daily checklist? What daily checklist?
8 deficiencies, 3 high-weight, $2,500 in potential fines
With ComplianceKit
  1. Inspector arrives — director opens app: "Show me your records"
  2. Director hands inspector iPad with ComplianceKit open
  3. All 45 children's records: green checkmarks, all 21 docs per child
  4. All 12 staff credentials: current, next expiry in 4 months
  5. Fire drill log: 12 months of entries with timestamps and evacuation times
  6. Daily checklist: 260+ days of completed checklists with photos
Clean inspection, zero deficiencies. Inspector recommends app to other facilities.
The Referral Loop

When an inspector sees a clean facility powered by ComplianceKit, they talk. They visit 3-5 facilities per week. One impressed inspector becomes a passive sales channel for dozens of facilities per month. This is the organic growth engine.

Go-to-Market per State

Each state has a different entry strategy based on its regulatory landscape, public data availability, and industry network structure.

TX Texas
  • -> Partner with 2-3 childcare licensing consultants in DFW / Houston / Austin
  • -> Consultants recommend ComplianceKit to their clients as part of their service
  • -> SEO for "Texas daycare inspection checklist" and related keywords
  • -> Content marketing with state-specific compliance guides
FL Florida
  • -> Target after failed inspections (CARES database is public — we can see who got cited)
  • -> Direct outreach: "We can prevent your next violation"
  • -> Referral program with DCF licensing counselors
  • -> Leverage 2x/year inspection frequency as urgency driver
CA California
  • -> Partner with CCLD Regional Office training events
  • -> Sponsor childcare association conferences
  • -> Target the 17.1% of facilities with background check gaps
  • -> Messaging: "Is your facility citation-free?"
Growth Flywheel

Consultant partners (TX) create initial trust. Public violation data (FL) enables targeted outreach. Conference presence (CA) builds brand. All three channels feed into the same product, validated by the same inspector referral loop. Each clean inspection creates a new advocate.
