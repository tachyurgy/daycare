Oregon Regulatory Landscape

Oregon child care operations are governed by the Department of Early Learning and Care (DELC), specifically the Child Care Licensing Division (CCLD). DELC was created in July 2023 via HB 3073, which consolidated the Early Learning Division (ELD), Employment Related Day Care (ERDC), and the Office of Child Care (OCC) into a single, standalone agency. All child care rules fall under OAR Chapter 414.

Agency Structure

DELC operates through 6 regional offices plus Salem headquarters. The contact number is 1-800-556-6616. HB 3073 was a landmark reorganization, pulling early learning and child care functions out of multiple agencies into one purpose-built department. This makes Oregon's regulatory structure cleaner but also means providers are still adapting to the new organizational reality.

Two Types of Inspection Visits

Health & Safety Review

Form: CCLD-0090 (16-page checklist)

Type: Announced annual licensing visit

Comprehensive walkthrough of all operations, records, physical spaces, and compliance elements. The inspector uses the full 16-page checklist to evaluate every aspect of the facility.

Monitoring Visit

Form: CCLD-0515 (30-item checklist)

Type: Unannounced monitoring visit

Focused spot-check on critical health and safety items. Shorter and more targeted than the full H&S review, but still covers ratios, supervision, training compliance, and physical safety.

What Inspectors Check

  1. Ratios and supervision -- counting heads in each classroom against age-group requirements, verifying staff positioning
  2. Central Background Registry (CBR) enrollment -- confirming all adults with child access are CBR-approved
  3. Attendance records -- verifying daily sign-in/out logs with timestamps match actual children present
  4. Training compliance -- checking pre-service certifications (ICCHS, RRCAN, FFL) and annual training hours
  5. Hazardous items -- inspecting for accessible medications, cleaning products, sharp objects, choking hazards
  6. Required postings -- verifying all 7 classroom items and 14 public items are posted and current
  7. Health and safety conditions -- fire extinguishers, CO detectors, safe sleep practices, sanitation, outdoor play safety
  8. Documents any findings as Valid, Invalid, or Unable to Substantiate
Registered Family Homes: Operate on a different schedule -- biennial licensing visits plus annual monitoring. This means they receive fewer inspections but face the same substantive compliance requirements.

Violation System & Enforcement

Oregon uses a two-tier severity system -- Serious and Non-Serious violations -- with corresponding civil penalty ranges. Each day of noncompliance constitutes a separate violation. Serious findings must be posted publicly for 12 months.

Serious Violations -- imminent danger, life-safety
Non-Serious Violations -- all other noncompliance

Serious Violations Red

  • Children in imminent danger
  • Operating over capacity
  • Prohibited discipline or punishment of children
  • Children not supervised
  • Multiple or serious fire, health, or safety hazards
  • Extreme unsanitary conditions
  • Adults not enrolled in CBR (Central Background Registry)
  • Operating without certification

Non-Serious Violations Yellow

Any other noncompliance with OAR 414-305 or other applicable rules that does not rise to the level of a serious violation.

Cumulative Risk: Each day of noncompliance counts as a separate violation. A center operating over capacity for 5 days faces up to $12,500 in penalties ($2,500 x 5 days). Serious findings must be publicly posted for 12 full months, creating lasting reputational damage visible on Find Child Care Oregon.

Staff-Child Ratios

Oregon's ratio requirements are defined in Table 3A/3B of OAR 414-305. These ratios must be maintained at all times when children are in care, including during transitions, outdoor play, and mealtimes.

Age Group Staff : Child Ratio Max Group Size Notes
6 weeks - 23 months 1 : 4 8 children Infants and young toddlers
24 - 35 months 1 : 5 10 children Older toddlers
36 months - school age 1 : 10 20 children Preschool-age children
School-age 1 : 15 30 children Before/after school programs
Group Size Exceptions: Maximum group size limits do not apply during field trips, outdoor play, large-group activities, napping, or eating. However, staff-child ratios must still be maintained during all of these activities. This distinction is a common source of confusion and citations.

Oregon Registry Online (ORO)

The ORO is Oregon's statewide professional development tracking system. It uses a 12-step professional development ladder that maps career progression from entry-level aides to program directors. All training hours, certifications, and professional development activities are logged in the ORO. Staff orientation must be completed within 30 days of hire.

Integration Opportunity: ORO is a centralized, state-maintained system. A ComplianceKit integration with ORO would auto-sync training records, eliminate manual entry, and create a powerful competitive moat -- no other compliance tool in Oregon connects to the registry.
07

Central Background Registry (CBR)

The CBR is Oregon's unique, statewide background check system for child care. Unlike most states that require individual facility-level background checks, Oregon centralizes all checks through the CBR, administered by CCLD.

Who Must Enroll
All Adults
Any person with unsupervised access to children in care
Valid Period
5 Years
Must be renewed before expiration to maintain compliance
Processing Time
Up to 8 Weeks
Out-of-state checks can take significantly longer
Cost to Provider
Free
CCLD covers fingerprinting costs for providers

What the CBR Checks

  • Oregon State Police (OSP) -- statewide criminal history
  • DHS Child Protective Services (CPS) -- child abuse/neglect history
  • FBI fingerprint check -- national criminal database
  • National sex offender registries -- all 50 states
  • Out-of-state checks -- required if the individual has lived in another state within the past 5 years
Critical Compliance Point: Staff must be CBR-approved BEFORE starting work. An adult with unsupervised access who is not CBR-enrolled is a serious violation carrying up to $2,500 per day in penalties. With processing times up to 8 weeks for out-of-state checks, this requires careful hiring workflow management.

Documentation & Posting Requirements

Public Postings (14 Items) OAR 0260

The following items must be posted in a publicly visible location accessible to all parents and visitors:

a. Current certificate b. Rules/inspections notice c. DELC website/phone/Safety Portal d. Water test results e. Complaint information f. Abuse hotline number g. Director + substitute name h. Parent access notice i. Closure dates j. Current week's menu k. Field trip notices (48hrs) l. Neighborhood walk notices m. Restrictable disease notices n. Behavior/guidance policy

Per Classroom Postings (7 Items) OAR 0260

Each classroom or care area must have the following items posted:

a. Emergency numbers b. Evacuation diagram c. Daily schedule + weekly plan d. Menu + dietary restrictions e. Handwashing procedures (at sinks) f. Diaper changing procedures (at stations) g. Ratios and group sizes

Children's Records (12 Items per Child) OAR 0220

Each child enrolled must have a file containing 12 required items, reviewed and updated annually:

  • Emergency contact information
  • Authorized pickup persons
  • Immunization records
  • Health/medical information
  • Enrollment/registration form
  • Allergy and dietary needs
  • Parental permissions/consents
  • Special care plans (if applicable)
  • Photo/media release
  • Transportation authorization
  • Field trip permissions
  • Annual review documentation

Staff Records (12 Items per Staff Member) OAR 0240

Each staff member must have a personnel file containing 12 required items with CBR verification:

  • CBR enrollment verification
  • Pre-service training certificates
  • Current CPR/First Aid certification
  • Food Handlers Certificate
  • Annual training hour log
  • ORO registry enrollment
  • Job description/qualifications
  • Emergency contact information
  • Health screening documentation
  • Performance evaluations
  • Orientation completion record
  • Signed policies acknowledgment

Written Policies (20 Items) OAR 0200

Facilities must maintain 20 written policy items (a through t) covering all operational areas. These must be provided to parents in writing and updated as regulations change. Key policies include enrollment, discipline/guidance, health/illness, emergency procedures, transportation, field trips, media use, and grievance processes.

Why Oregon for West Coast Expansion

Oregon is the ideal beachhead for ComplianceKit's Pacific Northwest expansion. The combination of a new agency, new rules, manageable market size, and unique regulatory features creates a textbook entry opportunity.

Pacific Northwest Corridor
Natural expansion path: Oregon leads to Washington leads to California. Regulatory patterns and provider networks overlap across the I-5 corridor. Win Oregon, and the playbook scales north and south.
Focused, Dominatable Market
~3,700 facilities is a manageable TAM to achieve dominant market share. Unlike Texas (14,000+) or California, Oregon can be saturated with a focused sales effort, creating a reference market for larger states.
Brand-New Agency (July 2023)
DELC is barely two years old. Providers are confused about who to contact, what forms to use, and how the new structure works. ComplianceKit becomes the provider's guide to the new regulatory reality.
New Rules = New Urgency
OAR 414-305 took effect January 1, 2024. Everyone is scrambling to comply with 72 sections of new standards. The transition from 414-300 to 414-305 changes dozens of requirements. Perfect timing for a digital compliance tool.
ORO Integration = Competitive Moat
Oregon Registry Online is a unique state system with no equivalent in other states. Building ORO integration creates a feature that competitors cannot replicate without significant Oregon-specific investment.
Reputation Risk Drives Purchases
Serious violations posted publicly for 12 months on findchildcareoregon.org. Parents actively check these records. The reputational cost of a violation makes compliance software an easy ROI calculation for directors.
CBR = Unique Switching Cost
Central Background Registry enrollment tracking is Oregon-specific functionality. Once a provider builds their CBR workflow in ComplianceKit -- tracking 5-year renewals, out-of-state checks, and new hire approvals -- switching costs are high.
72 Sections of Complexity
OAR 414-305 contains 72 sections of detailed requirements across every operational area. This level of regulatory complexity demands software -- spreadsheets and paper binders cannot keep pace with the scope of what providers must track.

Market Entry Timeline

  1. Oregon Module Build -- Implement all 10 feature mappings from Section 9, focusing on CCLD-0090 digital walkthrough and CBR enrollment tracking as launch features
  2. Pilot Program -- Partner with 10-20 Portland-metro certified centers for beta testing against the new OAR 414-305 requirements
  3. ORO Integration Proof of Concept -- Demonstrate training hour sync with Oregon Registry Online to validate the competitive moat thesis
  4. Statewide Launch -- Target all 1,379 centers first (highest complexity, highest willingness to pay), then expand to certified and registered homes
  5. Washington Expansion -- Use Oregon customer base and regulatory pattern recognition to enter Washington market (WAC 110-300)
Revenue Opportunity: At 3,700+ facilities with an estimated $50-100/month SaaS price point, the Oregon TAM is $2.2M - $4.4M ARR. More importantly, Oregon serves as the proof point for the entire West Coast strategy. A dominant position in Oregon de-risks the far larger Washington and California market entries.
