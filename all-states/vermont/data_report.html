6. Violations & Inspection Data

VT is mandated-public by federal law. CCDBG requires public posting of regulatory non-compliance — VT implements via BFIS. Minimum 5 years of violation + corrective-action history per facility (with documented gaps flagged in the March 2026 State Auditor report).

Source

BFIS public provider profiles. Each licensing visit date expands to show facts of violation + required corrective action.

Format

Per-facility HTML (session-protected ASP.NET-style app). No bulk export JS-rendered

Classification issue

2026 State Auditor found 11 of 40 "standard" citations should have been "serious." Expect label inconsistency.

Records-request path

1 V.S.A. §§ 315-320 Public Records Act via DCF records officer (portal).

Full analysis: violations.md

7. Outreach Signals

  • Strictest infant ratio in US — 1:4 for both infants AND toddlers up to 24 months. Payroll pressure creates willingness to pay for ratio-optimization tools.
  • 90% rule — only staff spending ≥90% of assigned time with children count in ratio. Eliminates director ratio-shopping, which forces real-time tracking software.
  • Act 76 payroll tax — providers operate on thinner effective margins post-2023; ROI messaging resonates.
  • STARS tied to subsidy rates — QRIS participation is economically necessary for most programs, and STARS evidence collection maps cleanly to the Documents module.
  • Triennial license cycle for compliant programs — 3-year renewal with annual monitoring in between. Annual monitoring = annual buying touch-point.
  • 2026 audit flag → vendor trust premium — state oversight gaps are documented; providers who can show audit-ready records outperform peers in parent acquisition.
  • Chittenden County dominates — Burlington / S. Burlington / Essex / Winooski is the concentrated first-wave target.
