# Delaware — Child Care Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority

**Delaware Department of Education (DOE), Office of Child Care Licensing (OCCL).**

OCCL was transferred from the Department of Services for Children, Youth & Their Families (DSCYF) to DOE in 2015 consolidation; authoritative URL is now at education.delaware.gov.

- New Castle County office: (302) 892-5800
- Kent & Sussex Counties office: Barratt Building, 821 Silver Lake Blvd., Suite 103, Dover, DE 19904 — (302) 739-5487
- OCCL landing: https://education.delaware.gov/families/birth-age-5/occl/

OCCL licenses early care and education centers, school-age centers, family and large family child care homes. The Division of Public Health additionally issues supplementary health and safety requirements.

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **14 DE Admin. Code 101 — DELACARE: Regulations for Early Care and Education and School-Age Centers** (effective 2022-06-01). Current center rule.
- **DELACARE: Regulations for Family and Large Family Child Care Homes** (effective 2022-08-10).
- **DELACARE: Regulations for Child Placing Agencies**
- **DELACARE: Regulations for Residential Child Care Facilities and Day Treatment Programs** (effective 2024-11)
- Enabling statute: **31 Del. C., Chapter 3, Subchapter IV (Child Care)** and **14 Del. C. §122** (DOE authority).

## Facility Types Regulated

| Facility Type | DELACARE Part | Capacity |
|---|---|---|
| Family Child Care Home | Part I/II | 1 provider; up to 6 children |
| Large Family Child Care Home | Part I/III | Provider + assistant; 7–12 children |
| Early Care & Education Center | Centers Rule | 13+ children (infants through pre-K) |
| School-Age Center | Centers Rule | Primarily before/after school |

## Key Licensing Forms

- **DELACARE Center License Application** (OCCL)
- **Family Child Care Home License Application**
- **Large Family Child Care Home License Application**
- **Background Check Authorization** — DE SBI, FBI fingerprint, DE Child Protection Registry (CPR), sex offender registry
- **Child Health Appraisal** (DE Delaware Immunization Program immunizations + physical)
- **State Fire Marshal Approval Letter**
- **Division of Public Health Approval** (food service permit if preparing/serving meals)

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes

**DELACARE Early Care & Education Centers:**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (birth–12 months) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Young toddlers (12–24 months) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Older toddlers (24–36 months) | 1:8 | 16 |
| Young preschoolers (3–4 years) | 1:10 | 20 |
| Older preschoolers (4–5 years) | 1:12 | 24 |
| School-age (5+ years) | 1:15 | 30 (varies) |

Mixed-age groups are governed by the **age of the youngest** child present.

**Family / Large Family Child Care Homes:** follow DELACARE Homes rule; capacity and ratio limits track federal CCDBG floors; no more than 4 children under age 2 in a large family home; no more than 2 infants <12 months per caregiver.

## Required Child Records

- Enrollment application with parent/guardian contact, authorized pickup
- Delaware Immunization Record (immunizations required for entry per 14 Del. C. §131)
- Child Health Appraisal (physical)
- Emergency medical authorization
- Food allergy / dietary restriction documentation
- Signed authorizations (photos, field trips, transportation)
- Daily attendance
- Incident / accident reports
- Records of any non-compliance or complaints (retained for 5 years — accessible via OCCL public search)

## Required Staff Records

- Pre-employment background check package: DE SBI, FBI fingerprint, Child Protection Registry, Adult Abuse Registry, sex offender registry, out-of-state checks
- Rechecks every 5 years
- Delaware First annual professional development registry (DIEEC)
- Evidence of qualifications (minimum requirements vary by role; CDA or equivalent for lead teachers)
- Current CPR and pediatric First Aid
- Mandated reporter training (documented)
- Orientation records
- Health screening (TB-free documentation)
- Performance evaluations
- Training log (annual hours vary by role)

## Inspection Cadence

- **Pre-license inspection** by OCCL before issuing an initial license
- **Annual (unannounced) monitoring inspection** — every licensed provider receives at least one on-site visit per year
- **Complaint-driven inspections** as warranted
- **Fire marshal** inspection at initial licensure and periodically
- **Division of Public Health / food service** inspection if meals are prepared on site

## Renewal Cadence

Licenses for child care centers are issued for **one year** (annual renewal) with annual inspection requirement. Large family / family child care homes renew **annually**. Residential child care facilities (per DELACARE residential rule, 2024) require renewal application **at least 60 days before expiration**.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Radical transparency.** OCCL's public search (https://education.delaware.gov/families/birth-age-5/occl/search_for_licensed_child_care/) publishes **all non-compliances and complaints from the past five years** — including substantiated concerns that did not rise to regulation violations. Parents see this. ComplianceKit messaging should emphasize deficiency prevention, not damage control.
- **"Delaware Stars" QRIS** participation is tightly correlated with subsidy reimbursement; voluntary but common.
- **Open data.** DE publishes a live Socrata CSV of all licensed child care providers (see SOURCES.md). This is rare at state level and makes Delaware one of the easiest states for lead generation.
- **Small state, concentrated geography.** New Castle County holds ~60% of providers; Kent and Sussex each hold ~20%. Inspection logistics are simpler than most states.
- **5-year complaint retention** on the public search is longer than most states (most show 2–3 years).
- **DOE oversight** (not a health-and-human-services department) is unusual nationally — Delaware moved child care licensing under the education umbrella in 2015.
- **Mandatory health & safety reviews** for all staff (not just lead teachers); documentation must be available at inspection.
