# Oregon Child Care Compliance

> Oregon has the most material already compiled in this repo — see extensive PDFs at `planning-docs/state-docs/oregon/` including the official **CCLD-0084 Rules for Certified Child Care Centers**, **CCLD-0731 General Rules for All Child Care Facilities**, checklists, sample inspection reports, and CEN-0001/0005/0006 background-registry (CBR) documents. This compliance.md summarizes the authoritative rules; refer to those PDFs for section-by-section detail.

## Licensing Authority

**Oregon Department of Early Learning and Care (DELC), Child Care Licensing Division (CCLD).** Established 2023 — DELC succeeded the Early Learning Division (ELD) within the Oregon Department of Education. CCLD is the single state licensor for all regulated child care.

- Website: https://www.oregon.gov/delc/
- Provider portal: https://www.oregon.gov/delc/providers/pages/licensed-childcare.aspx
- Customer service: ccld.customerservice@delc.oregon.gov / 1-800-556-6616
- Public provider search / safety history: https://findchildcareoregon.org/ (incorporates former Child Care Safety Portal)

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **ORS 329A.250 – 329A.500** — Child Care Act (statutory basis).
- **OAR Chapter 414, Division 205** — General Rules for All Child Care Facilities (CCLD-0731).
- **OAR Chapter 414, Division 300 and Division 305** — Certified Child Care Centers (CCLD-0084; OARs 414-305-0000 through 414-305-1620).
- **OAR Chapter 414, Division 307** — Certified Family Child Care Homes (CCLD-0088).
- **OAR Chapter 414, Division 350** — Registered Family Child Care Homes.
- **OAR Chapter 414, Division 061** — Central Background Registry (CBR).

## Facility Types Regulated

1. **Certified Child Care Center (CC)** — non-residential care for any children from 6 weeks through age 12 (OAR 414-305-0000 et seq.).
2. **Certified School-Age Child Care Center (SC)** — serving only children age 36 months through 12 (CCLD-0542).
3. **Certified Family Child Care Home (CF)** — home-based; up to 16 children depending on assistant staffing.
4. **Registered Family Child Care Home (RF)** — home-based; up to 10 children.
5. **Recorded Programs / License-Exempt** — certain school-based, half-day preschool, and parent co-op programs (ORS 329A.250(5)(a)-(i) / OAR 414-305-0120).

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (OAR 414-305-0400)

**Table 3A** — for centers initially licensed **after July 15, 2001**:

| Age of Children | Minimum Staff:Child | Max Group Size |
|---|---|---|
| 6 weeks to 24 months | 1:4 | 8 |
| 24 months to 36 months | 1:5 | 10 |
| 36 months to school-age | 1:10 | 20 |
| School-age | 1:15 | 30 |

**Table 3B** — for centers initially licensed **on or before July 15, 2001** (grandfathered; center must choose either 3A or 3B — no combining):

| Age of Children | Minimum Staff:Child | Max Group Size |
|---|---|---|
| 6 weeks to 30 months | 1:4 | 8 |
| 30 months to school-age | 1:10 | 20 |
| School-age | 1:15 | 30 |

Notes:
- Ratio and group size must be met **at all times** except during field trips, outdoor play, planned large-group activities, napping, and eating (group-size exceptions only).
- A relaxed ratio applies during rest/sleep periods under specified conditions (no infants; sufficient back-up staff on site).
- **Mixed-Age Ratio Table** (PTA-0732) available when ≤ 16 children present; otherwise, mixed-age groups must meet the ratio for the youngest child.
- **At least one caregiver in each group must meet Teacher qualifications** (OAR 414-305-0340).

## Required Child Records

- PR-0185 Child Enrollment Form (child name, DOB, parent contact, emergency contacts, authorized pick-ups)
- Immunization record consistent with ORS 433.267 (or valid exemption)
- Health history and allergy information
- Medication authorization + administration log
- Signed authorizations for transportation, field trips, photography
- Daily attendance record (sign-in / sign-out)
- Incident and injury reports
- Special-needs / individualized care plan if applicable
- Records retained for at least 2 years after enrollment ends (per CCLD rules)

## Required Staff Records

- Application, résumé, verification of qualifications (Teacher, Aide I, Aide II, Director, Multi-site Coordinator — OAR 414-305-0320 through 414-305-0360)
- **Central Background Registry (CBR) enrollment** — CEN-0001 Application; required for every employee, volunteer with unsupervised access, household member 16+ for home-based settings, **before first day of unsupervised child contact** (OAR 414-061)
- Pediatric First Aid and CPR certification
- Orientation training: CCLD-approved health & safety training, Safe Sleep for Oregon Infants (within 30 days for infant-room staff, prior to unsupervised access), Recognizing & Reporting Child Abuse and Neglect, Food Handler's Certification (within 30 days for food-handling staff)
- Introduction to Child Care Health & Safety training (CCLD approved) and Child Development training (within 90 days)
- **Annual training: 15 clock hours** — at least 8 hours in child development or ECE, and 1 hour in health/safety/nutrition (OAR 414-305-0380).
- Director must have 10 hours in Program Management core knowledge within year 1.
- Oregon Registry Online (ORO) record
- Current medical / TB requirements as applicable

## Inspection Cadence

- **At least one on-site licensing inspection per year**, typically unannounced (CCLD-0093 Contact Report).
- **Initial** licensing inspection conducted before license issuance.
- **Sanitation inspections** (CCLD-0109 checklist) and **fire marshal** inspections required; reports filed with CCLD.
- **Complaint inspections** unannounced, conducted in response to any filed complaint (results published to Find Child Care Oregon).
- **Monitor visit** (CCLD-0515) may be conducted for technical assistance during probation or after deficiency.

## Renewal Cadence

- Certified child care center licenses are issued for a **one-year** term and must be renewed annually.
- CBR clearances must be maintained continuously — individuals must renew their CBR enrollment (typically every 2 years; also triggered by breaks in employment).
- Fire and sanitation inspection reports refreshed annually.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Central Background Registry (CBR)** — Oregon's unified background-check system covering state + FBI fingerprints + child abuse registry + national sex-offender registry. Mandatory for all child-contact staff and household members 16+ in home-based care. Portable across Oregon child care employers.
- **Safe Sleep for Oregon Infants training** — mandatory for infant-room staff prior to unsupervised access; within 30 days of hire.
- **Oregon Registry** (voluntary) — state professional development registry (ORO); directors must record 10 hours of Program Management.
- **Food Handler Card** required within 30 days for kitchen, cooks, and infant-room staff who prepare bottles.
- **Spark (QRIS)** — Oregon's quality rating and improvement system, administered by Oregon Center for Career Development in Childhood Care and Education (OCCD) and tied to CCDBG incentive payments.
- **Find Child Care Oregon** portal publishes licensing history and violations publicly (parents can research any provider).
- **Mixed-Age Ratio Table** — unique tool allowing flexibility for small/mixed-age groups.
- **Multi-Site Program** — OAR 414-305-0100 defines programs where a single organization oversees multiple single-classroom sites (capacity ≤20 each) with shared director functions — useful for church/school partnerships.
- **Mandated reporter** (ORS 419B.005) — all child-care staff; annual training required.
- **Shaken Baby / AHT** training and **active shooter / emergency preparedness** training are part of the required annual training mix.
- **Environmental** rules: lead testing of drinking water is required, radon testing as applicable.
