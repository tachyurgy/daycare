2. Primary Regulatory Citation

Statute: W.S. § 14-4-101 through § 14-4-116 (Child Care Facilities). Rulemaking authority: § 14-4-107.

DFS Rules (WY Rules & Regs):

  • Chapter 2 — Licensing Child Care Facilities (general)
  • Chapter 5 — Family Child Care Home (FCCH, 3-10 children)
  • Chapter 6 — Family Child Care Center (FCCC, up to 15 children)
  • Chapter 7 — Child Care Center (CCC, 16+ children)

Unique terminology: WY uses "certification" (vs. licensing), and enforces both group size caps and ratios independently.

7. Outreach Signals

  • Annual renewal cycle — every certification renews every 12 months. High-frequency buying trigger.
  • Three parallel certification chapters (5 / 6 / 7) — WY has unusually granular rule differences by setting size; a "which-chapter-applies-to-you" wizard is a natural top-of-funnel asset.
  • Lapsed certifications require re-application (not reinstatement) — late renewal is a costly mistake; renewal-reminder value prop is unusually strong.
  • ~500-650 total certified facilities — smallest state market in the US. Full-TAM outbound in a single quarter.
  • Cheyenne, Casper, Gillette, Laramie, Rock Springs, Sheridan, Jackson — seven population centers cover the bulk of licensed facilities; frontier county providers are a long tail with high retention but lower expansion value.
  • Voluntary STAR QRIS (1-4) — participation tied to subsidy enhancement; evidence-collection tools help providers move up tiers.
  • Group size + ratio enforced independently — a room may meet ratio but fail group-size cap. Real-time dual-metric monitoring is the differentiator.
