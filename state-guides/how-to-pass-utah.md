# How to Pass Your Utah Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector is a **licensor** from the **Utah Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS), Division of Licensing and Background Checks (DLBC)**. DLBC replaced the old DOH Child Care Licensing in the 2022 DHHS reorganization. Utah runs **one announced inspection a year** at each licensed facility for license renewal, plus **additional unannounced visits** for complaints and follow-ups. Every inspection is posted publicly on the DLBC "Find a Facility" portal and the state's unified licensee lookup at ccl.utah.gov, where any parent can see **36 months** of compliance history.

- Agency: https://dlbc.utah.gov/
- Public inspection portal: https://dlbc.utah.gov/information-for-the-public/find-a-facility/
- Sample per-inspection checklist: https://ccl.utah.gov/ccl/public/checklist/632272

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **This is the law:** Utah Code Title 26B, Chapter 2, Part 4 (Child Care Licensing Act)
- **This is the center rulebook:** Utah Admin Code **R381-100** (Child Care Centers). https://adminrules.utah.gov/public/rule/R381-100/Current%20Rules
- **This is the hourly-center rulebook:** R381-60
- **This is the home-based rulebook:** R381-70 (Residential Child Care)
- **This is the ratios rule:** R381-100-10. https://regulations.justia.com/states/utah/health/title-r381/rule-r381-100/section-r381-100-10/
- **This is the inspection form:** DLBC Center Inspection Checklist (7/2/2025 master). https://dlbc.utah.gov/wp-content/uploads/CENTER-Inspection-Checklist-7_2_2025.pdf

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

For licensed **Child Care Centers** under R381-100-10:

| Age | One staff for this many kids | Max group |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (0–12 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Toddler (12–24 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Age 2 | 1:7 | 14 |
| Age 3 | 1:12 | 24 |
| Age 4 | 1:15 | 30 |
| Age 5 (pre-K) | 1:20 | 40 |
| School-age (6–12) | 1:20 | 40 |

**Mixed-age groups:** the youngest child sets the ratio, and the oldest child **minus one age band** sets the max group size (R381-100-10).

**Naptime doubling** is permitted for children ≥18 months *only if* they are restful and a second caregiver is on-site within immediate reach.

**Two-caregiver minimum:** any single group >8 children, or any group with more than 2 infants/toddlers, must have 2 caregivers.

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up. Mixed-age rooms are the #1 source of Utah ratio citations because the math is unusual.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives (announced for annual renewal; unannounced for complaints) and shows DLBC ID.
2. Checks your posted license, ratio chart, and Care About Childcare (CAC) registry participation.
3. Walks each room and counts kids vs. staff using the R381-100-10 formula.
4. Works through the **DLBC Center Inspection Checklist** (the same 7/2/2025 master posted at dlbc.utah.gov) item by item, marking **Met / Not Met / Not Applicable / Not Reviewed**.
5. Pulls staff files: Office of Background Processing clearance (initial + annual renewal), 20-hour pre-service training, 10-hour (or 20 for director) annual training through CAC registry, CPR/First Aid.
6. Pulls child files: admission form, Utah immunization record, allergy and medication plans.
7. Reviews fire drill log, Emergency Preparedness Plan (R381-100-15), transportation plan if applicable.
8. Writes a corrective action plan and sets a due date. Assesses a **civil money penalty** if the issue is serious.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Utah
1. **Ratio out of balance** — especially in mixed-age rooms where the "oldest minus one age band" group-size math gets miscalculated (R381-100-10).
2. **Background clearance not current** — must be **renewed annually** through the Utah Office of Background Processing.
3. **Pre-service 20 hours** not completed before unsupervised contact (includes Utah basics, SIDS, shaken baby, infection control).
4. **Annual training hours short** on the CAC registry — 10 hours for caregivers, 20 for directors.
5. **Outdoor area missing shade or short on 40 sq ft per child** — R381 unique requirement.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Admission / enrollment form with emergency contacts
- Utah **immunization record** per Utah Code 53G-9-3 (or exemption form)
- Physical / health history
- Allergy and medication plans
- Pickup authorization list
- Daily attendance log
- Incident / injury reports
- IEP / IFSP if applicable

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Employment application / résumé
- **Office of Background Processing clearance** — initial + annual renewal
- TB test or risk assessment
- Orientation (≥8 hours) within first 5 days of unsupervised contact
- **Pre-service training — 20 hours** before unsupervised care (Utah basics, SIDS, shaken baby, infection control)
- **Annual training — 10 hours** (20 for directors) through the Care About Childcare registry
- CPR + Pediatric First Aid (at least one staff on site at all times)
- Medication administration training
- Mandated reporter acknowledgment (Utah Code 62A-4a-403)

## What must be on your walls
- Current DLBC license
- Ratio chart (with the age-band math explained)
- Emergency Preparedness Plan summary
- Evacuation routes per room
- Complaint hotline & DLBC contact
- Air Quality Index / heat & wildfire protocol (required during high-pollution days)

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly, documented
- **Earthquake / shelter-in-place** — per Emergency Preparedness Plan
- **Daily attendance**
- **Medication log**
- **Injury / incident log**
- **Air Quality Index monitoring** on wildfire-smoke days (Utah-specific)
- **Playground equipment** check log

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Walk every room. Count ages, count staff, compute the R381-100-10 ratio and group size. Fix mixed-age mistakes first.
2. Confirm license, ratios, emergency plan posted.
3. Check fire drill log — this month logged?
4. Open three staff files: background clearance (renewed this year?), 20-hr pre-service, annual training hours on CAC.
5. Open three child files: immunizations, emergency contacts, allergy plan.
6. Lock up chemicals, medications, firearms (firearms must be unloaded, locked, ammo stored separately, including in staff personal vehicles).
7. Sleep areas: backs only, one infant per crib.
8. Outdoor: fenced, shaded, 40 sq ft per child.
9. Check today's AQI if there's smoke — document indoor-play decision.
10. Greet the inspector. Offer them a seat. Stay calm.

## What happens if you fail
DLBC follows the "OL Guidelines for Corrective Actions and Civil Money Penalties" — the formal ladder is: **written notice → corrective action plan → civil money penalty (CMP) → conditional license → denial / revocation**. Penalties are dollar amounts posted on the inspection checklist. Appeals go through a DHHS administrative hearing. Every finding is public on DLBC's Find-a-Facility tool for 36 months; older records are available via a **Utah GRAMA** public-records request.

## The weird Utah gotcha nobody tells you about
Utah uses a unique **"mixed-age ratio/group size"** rule in R381-100-10 that nobody else uses. The ratio follows the **youngest** child in the room, but the **max group size** is set by the **oldest** child's age band **minus one**. So if you have a room with a 4-year-old and a 1-year-old, you apply the 1-year-old's 1:4 ratio, but you cap the group at the Age 3 max — not Age 4. Most owners miss this math on inspection day. The cleanest fix: never mix ages if you can avoid it, and if you must, post the mixed-age calculation on the door of every mixed-age room. Utah also requires **firearm storage rules to apply to staff personal vehicles** during transport — another one nobody sees coming.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Utah owner
Their fear is a **civil money penalty** on the posted checklist at ccl.utah.gov — dollar amounts are visible to every parent. Drop the **mixed-age group-size math** (youngest sets ratio, oldest-minus-one sets group max) in 30 seconds; almost no owner has this calibrated correctly in their head. Most are using CAC registry in one tab, a paper clipboard for ratios, and a personal calendar alert for annual background renewal. Lead with "I'll show you the R381-100-10 mixed-age calculation for every room you run, live." Salt Lake, Davis, and Utah County centers are the target; Utah grew fast 2020–2024 so there are many 2–3-year-old centers that still haven't been through a CMP yet — they're motivated to avoid the first one.
