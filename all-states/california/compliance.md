# California Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
**California Department of Social Services (CDSS), Community Care Licensing Division (CCLD) — Child Care Program Office.** CCLD operates through **21 Regional Offices** statewide. Licensing Program Analysts (LPAs) conduct all inspections.

- Headquarters: 744 P Street, Sacramento, CA 95814

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Statute:** **California Health &amp; Safety Code § 1596.70 et seq.** (California Child Day Care Facilities Act) and subsequent provisions for licensing.
- **Administrative Code:** **Title 22, California Code of Regulations, Division 12** — the binding rule set for child care centers (Chapter 1 — General Licensing; Chapter 2 — Child Care Center General Requirements; plus subdivisions for Infant Centers, School-Age Centers, Toddler Component, and Family Child Care Homes).
- **Enforcement framework:** the **Compliance and Regulatory Enforcement (CARE) Tools** — the standardized inspection framework used by LPAs across all 21 regional offices.

## Facility Types Regulated
1. **Child Care Center (CCC)** — non-residential group care; Title 22, Div. 12, Ch. 1 &amp; 2.
2. **Infant Center** — specialized center for children 0-2 years with additional requirements (quarterly infant needs/services plans, toilet-training plans, infant-feeding plans, individual infant sleeping plans).
3. **Toddler Component** — center subcategory for children ~18 months to 3 years with distinct ratio rules.
4. **School-Age Center** — care for children K-14 outside school hours.
5. **Family Child Care Home (FCCH)** — in-home care.
   - **Small FCCH:** up to 8 children
   - **Large FCCH:** up to 14 children (with assistant provider)
6. **Exempt / License-exempt** — informal, relative, and Trustline-registered in-home care (subsidy-eligible, not licensed).

## Child-to-Staff Ratios &amp; Group Sizes (Title 22)
| Age | Ratio | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (0 - 2 yr) | **1:4** | Strictest CA ratio; Infant Center additional plan requirements |
| Toddler / Preschool (2 - 6 yr) | **1:12** | — |
| School-Age (6 - 14 yr) | **1:15** | — |

**Large Family Child Care Home:** up to 14 children with a qualified assistant provider. **Small FCCH:** up to 8 with no assistant required (with age-based sub-limits).

## Required Child Records (per LIC 311A)
- LIC 700 — Identification &amp; Emergency Information
- LIC 701 / equivalent — Physician's Report
- LIC 702 — Consent for Emergency Medical Treatment
- Current immunization record per CA Department of Public Health schedule (or valid exemption)
- TB test clearance
- Signed consents: transportation, field trip, photography
- **Infant-center specific:** quarterly infant needs/services plan, toilet-training plan, infant-feeding plan, LIC 9227 individual infant sleeping plan
- Daily attendance, sign-in/sign-out
- Incident / injury reports
- LIC 9221 parental medication consent (when applicable) + LIC 622 administration log

## Required Staff Records
- LIC 508 — Criminal Record Statement
- Live Scan fingerprinting (CA DOJ + FBI) — required **pre-employment** for any adult with client contact; Child Abuse Central Index (CACI) check; sex offender registry
- Health screening + TB clearance (per Title 22)
- CPR / First Aid (pediatric) certification
- Mandated reporter training (AB 1207 + Child Abuse &amp; Neglect Reporting Act)
- Qualification documentation per Title 22 (director permit, teacher permit, aide qualifications, infant aide qualifications) — many roles tied to the **CA Commission on Teacher Credentialing Child Development Permit Matrix**
- Ongoing professional growth hours per permit-level requirements

## Inspection Cadence
- **Random unannounced inspections:** CCLD operates on a risk-based inspection schedule. Current statutory requirement (HSC § 1597.09): at a minimum, **once every three years** for licensed child care centers, with risk-based escalation.
- **Complaint-triggered inspections** always unannounced, conducted within statutory response windows (10 business days for routine, sooner for immediate-health-and-safety).
- **Initial inspection** pre-licensure.
- **Renewal inspection** at the 2-year license cycle.
- **Posting requirement:** LIC 9213 Notice of Site Visit must remain posted at the facility during/after the inspection.

## Renewal Cadence
- Licenses are issued on a **two-year (biennial)** cycle. Renewal application + updated fire/health documentation + Live Scan updates as required.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Largest state market in the US:** roughly **28,000+ licensed child care facilities** (centers + FCCH combined). Most populated MSAs: Los Angeles, San Diego, Orange County, Inland Empire, Bay Area (SF/Alameda/Santa Clara/Contra Costa), Sacramento.
- **Title 22 / CARE framework is one of the most detailed in the country** — 9 inspection domains for centers, 6 for FCCH.
- **Civil penalty assessment (LIC 421)** — citations can carry immediate civil penalties; posting may be required at the facility.
- **Trustline Registry** — separate registration for license-exempt in-home caregivers receiving subsidy payment; operated by CDSS.
- **Mandatory Child Care Initiative Project (CCIP)** — state professional-development network; provider roles aligned to the CTC Permit Matrix.
- **San Francisco + Berkeley** have additional municipal child-care regulations (health inspection, business licensing) that stack on top of Title 22.
- **Extensive public transparency:** inspection reports, deficiency listings, correction plans, complaint investigations all surfaced via the CCLD Transparency Website (April 2015 - present).
