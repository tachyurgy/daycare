Arizona — Child Care Compliance Data Report

Population rank: 14 · ~7.5M residents · Prepared 2026-04-18 for ComplianceKit

1. Licensing Authority

Arizona Department of Health Services (ADHS), Bureau of Child Care Licensing (BCCL). DES regulates certified group homes and certified family homes separately.

  • Agency: azdhs.gov/licensing/childcare-facilities
  • Consumer portal: AZ Care Check
  • DES (subsidy + certified homes): azchildcareprovidersearch.azdes.gov
  • Phones (BCCL): Phoenix 602-364-2539 · Tucson 520-628-6540 · Flagstaff 928-774-2707

2. Primary Regulatory Citation

  • Statute: A.R.S. Title 36, Chapter 7.1 (§§ 36-881 to 36-897.01)
  • Rules — Centers: 9 A.A.C. 5 (R9-5-101 to R9-5-809; major rewrite July 2024; phased implementation through August 2026)
  • Rules — Group Homes (DES): A.A.C. Title 6, Chapter 5, Article 55
  • Rules — Certified Family Homes (DES): A.A.C. R6-5-5201 et seq.

6. Violations & Inspection Data new

AZ Care Check is ADHS's public portal for Statements of Deficiency, Plans of Correction, and enforcement actions across all ADHS-licensed facilities including child care centers. "Primary Source Verified" and updated daily by ADHS staff.

Sources

  • AZ Care Check root: azcarecheck.azdhs.gov/s/
  • Facility detail URL pattern: /s/facility-details?facilityId=<18-char-sf-id>&programType=Child+Care+Facilities&searchQuery=CDC-<license> — example: 0018y000005T5SVAA0
  • Inspection detail URL pattern: /s/inspection-details?inspectionId=<id>&facilityId=<id> — example: link

Freshness

  • Daily refresh per ADHS ("maintained and updated daily by ADHS").
  • 3-year rolling public archive (statutory minimum); older via public-records request.

Access Strategy

  • Playwright (Chromium) required — Salesforce Lightning Web Components render poorly under plain HTTP fetch (CSS errors are expected).
  • Enumerate license numbers (CDC-<n>) from the Layer 17 ArcGIS roster's LICENSE_NUMBER attribute; feed as searchQuery param to facility-details.
  • Aura RPC interception: After first page load, capture /aura?r=<n> POSTs; replay with fresh tokens to accelerate subsequent fetches.
  • Print view (inspection-print-view) renders cleaner HTML — easier pipeline for downstream PDF conversion.
  • Polite rate: ≤2 req/sec.
Public-records backup. Arizona Public Records Law (A.R.S. § 39-121 et seq.) — "promptly" (typically 5–15 business days for ADHS). ADHS public records request form (PDF). Escalate via Arizona Ombudsman-Citizens' Aide.

7. Outreach Signals (Hot-Lead Detection)

  • Open Plan of Correction on AZ Care Check (SOD issued but POC not yet accepted) — 10-day countdown visible to inspectors. Ideal moment for a compliance-tool demo.
  • Enforcement action on file — civil money penalty, probation, pending revocation. Surviving the fight means the owner is motivated.
  • Repeat violations at R9-5-404 (ratios) or R9-5-402 (staff records) — these are ComplianceKit's wheelhouse.
  • 2024 rule rewrite compliance deadline (Aug 2026) — training-hour increase to 24/year is phasing in; centers not yet on track face a tight window.
  • Quality First participation gap — First Things First-funded slots require QRIS participation; non-participants may be leaving money on the table.

8. Next-Step Data Acquisition

  • Playwright scrape: 2,500 facilities × 3 most-recent SODs = ~7,500 records. Budget ~15 worker-hours at 3 concurrent browsers.
  • DES complement: file DES public-records request for enforcement actions on certified group homes (5–10 kids) and certified family homes (≤4 kids). DES does not publish deficiency reports in its provider search.
  • A.R.S. § 39-121 request: aggregate enforcement orders (revocation, civil money penalty, suspension) since 2020 to build a historical timeline.
  • Email append: leads CSV has phone; append owner/director emails via Apollo / Hunter keyed on business name + license number.
  • New-rule monitoring: subscribe to ADHS director blog for 9 A.A.C. 5 implementation updates; 2026 training deadline will drive demand.
