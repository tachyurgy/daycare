# How to Pass Your Kansas Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector is a **surveyor** from the **Kansas Department of Health and Environment (KDHE), Child Care Licensing Program**. Important: Kansas HB 2045 (2025) created a new **Office of Early Childhood (OEC)** that is set to take over child-care licensing from KDHE on **July 1, 2026**. Whoever holds the badge, the inspection rules are the same. KDHE has statutory authority to enter any licensed facility **at any time** without notice. Every citation goes onto your record, searchable for **3 years** on the public **KDHE OIDS** portal.

- Agency: https://www.kdhe.ks.gov/280/Child-Care-Licensing
- Public compliance search: https://khap.kdhe.ks.gov/OIDS/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **This is the law:** K.S.A. 65-501 et seq. (Maternity Centers and Child Care Facilities Act); inspection power in K.S.A. 65-512; renewal in K.S.A. 65-504
- **This is the rulebook:** Kansas Administrative Regulations (K.A.R.) Article 28-4
- **This is the staff-ratios rule (amended Aug 2, 2024):** K.A.R. **28-4-428**. Official guidance PDF: https://www.kdhe.ks.gov/DocumentCenter/View/41631/KAR-28-4-428-Staff-Child-Ratio-Guidance-PDF
- **This is the training rule:** K.A.R. **28-4-428a**
- **This is the regulation book (all centers & preschools):** https://www.kdhe.ks.gov/DocumentCenter/View/15396/Preschools--Child-Care-Centers-Regulation-Book-PDF

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

From K.A.R. 28-4-428 (effective August 2, 2024). Kansas offers **two infant ratio options** (A or B) — you must pick one and stick with it in any given infant unit:

| Ages of children in the unit | Minimum staff : child | Max children in the unit |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (under 12 mo) **Option A** | 1:3 | 9 |
| Infants (under 12 mo) **Option B** | 1:4 | 8 |
| Infants + other children under 6 | 1:6 (max 3 infants) | 12 (max 6 infants) |
| Toddlers (1 yr) | 1:6 | 12 |
| 2 yr → under 3 | 1:7 | 14 |
| 2.5 yr → under school-age | 1:12 | 24 |
| School-age | 1:16 | 32 |

A counted staff member may leave the unit briefly (<5 min) with another staff remaining, or up to **20 minutes with a substitute**. Longer absences break ratio.

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up. In a mixed-age room, the ratio and max-unit size of the **youngest age band** apply. Kansas has an unusual rule: **volunteers age 16+ may count in ratio** (14- to 15-year-olds may be present but must be supervised, not counted).

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced. Shows KDHE ID.
2. Checks posted license and posted ratios.
3. Walks every room and computes ratio / max-unit math against K.A.R. 28-4-428.
4. Pulls staff files: **KBI state criminal history**, **FBI fingerprint**, **sex offender registry**, **child abuse/neglect central registry** checks, health status form, SIDS/safe-sleep training (infant staff), medication administration training, CPR + First Aid, education/training log.
5. Pulls child files: enrollment, immunization certificate, health assessment, allergies & medication authorization, emergency contacts.
6. Reviews fire drill log, emergency plan, tornado drill log.
7. Files an OIDS entry citing specific K.A.R. 28-4 or K.S.A. 65 rules with corrective action.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Kansas
1. **Ratio not met** (K.A.R. 28-4-428) — particularly during staff breaks and naps.
2. **Background-check gap** — all four checks (KBI + FBI + sex offender + CANS) must be current.
3. **Annual health-status form missing** on a staff member (K.A.R. 28-4-428a).
4. **SIDS / safe-sleep training not documented** — 4 training hours must address SIDS within first year for anyone caring for infants.
5. **Annual emergency drill missing** — at least one documented annual emergency drill is required.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment information (name, address, DOB, parent contacts)
- **Immunization certification** per K.S.A. 65-508 and KDHE schedule
- Health assessment on file
- Emergency contact and authorized pick-up list
- Allergies, chronic conditions, medication authorization
- Daily attendance record
- Annual update of immunization and health status

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **KBI state criminal history check** + **FBI fingerprint check** + **sex offender registry check** + **child abuse/neglect central registry check** (K.A.R. 28-4-428)
- **Annual health status form** (updated more often as needed)
- Education & training documentation
- **SIDS & safe-sleep training** within first year (infant caregivers)
- Medication administration training (if applicable)
- **CPR & First Aid certification**
- Orientation records
- Annual continuing education hours
- Program Director requirements apply if capacity exceeds thresholds: >60 children requires a dedicated PD; >100 requires 1 year admin experience; >160 requires an assistant PD

## What must be on your walls
- Current license
- Ratio chart (with infant option A or B clearly marked)
- Evacuation routes each room
- Tornado shelter plan
- Emergency preparedness plan summary
- Complaint hotline to KDHE
- Menu (current week)

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — per emergency plan, documented
- **Tornado / severe-weather drills** — documented
- **Annual emergency drill** — at least one per year (K.A.R. 28-4-428a)
- **Daily attendance**
- **Medication log**
- **Injury / incident log**
- **Emergency plan review** — annual

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff in every room; check infant-unit option A or B.
2. Verify license, ratios, emergency plan posted.
3. Fire drill log — this month entered? Annual emergency drill logged?
4. Tornado drill log — logged?
5. Open three staff files: KBI + FBI + sex-offender + CANS dates, annual health-status form, SIDS training, CPR.
6. Open three child files: immunization current, health assessment, emergency contacts.
7. Lock chemicals and medications.
8. Cribs: backs only, firm mattress, no soft bedding, one infant per crib.
9. Playground: no broken equipment.
10. Greet the inspector. Offer a chair. Stay professional.

## What happens if you fail
KDHE posts non-compliance to OIDS (viewable to parents for 3 years). Failure to renew within 30 days incurs a **late fee equal to the renewal fee** (K.S.A. 65-504). Revoked or non-renewed licenses cannot reapply for 1 year. Serious or repeat violations lead to suspension or revocation. Appeals go through the Office of Administrative Hearings. Bulk data can also be requested through KORA (Kansas Open Records Act — 3-business-day response window). When OEC takes over licensing on July 1, 2026, expect the OIDS URL and rule numbering to migrate, but compliance expectations remain the same.

## The weird Kansas gotcha nobody tells you about
Kansas lets you **pick between two different infant ratios** — **Option A (1:3 with unit max 9)** or **Option B (1:4 with unit max 8)**. This is unusual. Most states give you one ratio and that's it. You must **choose one option per infant unit and not switch mid-inspection** — an inspector will check which option you've declared and enforce that one. Also, Kansas's **infants-plus-other-children rule (1:6 with max 3 infants)** lets you combine infants with older toddlers under specific caps. Most owners accidentally put 4 infants into a mixed unit and get cited. Keep a laminated ratio card on the wall of every unit that shows exactly which age-mix rule you're running.

Drop the **infant Option A vs. Option B rule** in 30 seconds; most owners don't realize they've effectively committed to one and will be cited if their roster drifts across both. Most KS owners are using a paper binder, KDHE reminder emails, and a Google Calendar for the 4 separate background checks. Say "I'll show you every infant-unit option A/B declaration, every staff's 4 background checks, and every child's immunization all on one screen — and it'll keep working after the OEC transition."
