# How to Pass Your Colorado Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Colorado Department of Early Childhood (CDEC), Division of Early Learning Licensing and Administration (DELLA)**. CDEC is new — it was created in **2022** when all early-learning functions moved out of CDHS. You get **at least one unannounced inspection per year** (the federal CCDBG minimum). Colorado moved to **continuous licensure**, so there is no periodic renewal, but compliance is continuously monitored. Complaints trigger visits anytime. Findings post publicly on Colorado Shines. Public sites:
- CDEC Licensing: https://cdec.colorado.gov/for-providers/child-care-licensing-and-administration
- Colorado Shines (public search + inspection history): https://www.coloradoshines.com/search

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — C.R.S. § 26.5-5-301 et seq.
- **The rulebook for centers** — 12 CCR 2509-8 § 7.702. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/colorado/12-CCR-2509-8-7.702
- **Family Child Care Home (regular)** — 12 CCR 2509-8 § 7.708.
- **Large Family Child Care Home** — 12 CCR 2509-8 § 7.707.
- **School-age center** — § 7.712.
- **General Rules for Child Care Facilities** (background checks, reporting, enforcement) — § 7.701.
- **Ratio rule (centers)** — § 7.702.52.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From 12 CCR 2509-8 § 7.702.52 (centers). Mixed-age groups default to the youngest child's ratio. **Infants cannot be combined with children over 30 months.** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age band | One staff for every... | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants 6 weeks – 12 months | 5 | 10 |
| Infants 12 – 18 months | 5 | 10 |
| 18 – 24 months | 5 | 10 |
| 2 – 3 years | 7 | 14 |
| 2.5 – 3 years | 8 | 16 |
| 3 years | 10 | 20 |
| 4 – 5 years | 12 | 24 |
| K – 1st grade (in center) | 15 | 30 |
| School age (6+ years) | 15 | 30 |

**Family Child Care Home:** up to 6 children including the provider's own under age 8; no more than 2 under age 2.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced.
2. Checks license is posted and current.
3. Walks every room, counts children, compares to staff present.
4. Pulls staff files — fingerprint FBI + CBI background, Trails Child Welfare Central Registry, Sex Offender Registry, PDIS account.
5. Pulls child files — CIIS immunization record, health statement, IHCP if any.
6. Reviews emergency plan, fire drill log, medication log, incident reports.
7. Exit conference. Report posts to the Colorado Shines program detail page typically within 30 days. Enforcement actions post to CDEC's Public Notice page within 10 business days of final action.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
Per 12 CCR 2509-8 enforcement patterns:
1. **Out-of-ratio** (§ 7.702.52) — especially with infants mixed into groups over 30 months (banned in CO).
2. **Background check gaps** (§ 7.701) — fingerprint FBI + CBI not done, Trails not run, PDIS missing.
3. **15-hour annual training** not logged in PDIS.
4. **Medical statement** on an adult or child missing/expired.
5. **Emergency preparedness plan** missing the required elements (fire, lockdown, evacuation, reunification).

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment application** with emergency contacts.
- **Immunization record** — certified in Colorado Immunization Information System (CIIS), or a signed exemption.
- **Health statement** from a licensed healthcare provider within 30 days of enrollment.
- **Individual Health Care Plan** for children with special medical needs or allergies.
- **Permission forms** — field trips, transportation, photo release.
- **Medication consent (Standing Orders)**.
- **Daily attendance record** (in/out times with signatures).

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **Fingerprint-based FBI + CBI background check** — every 5 years.
- **Trails (Colorado Automated Child Welfare) check**.
- **Sex Offender Registry / National Sex Offender Registry** check.
- **Adult medical statement** — within 30 days of employment.
- **TB risk assessment**.
- **PDIS account** (Colorado Shines Professional Development Information System).
- **Pre-service training** via PDIS (Intro to Family Child Care / Center Basics).
- **Current First Aid + CPR** (adult/child/infant).
- **Standard Precautions, Universal Safety, Emergency Preparedness** training.
- **Child Abuse Prevention / Mandated Reporter** training — initial within 30 days + annual refresher.
- **Medication Administration** training if dispensing meds.
- **Director Qualification approval** (§ 7.702.45 — degree + experience matrix).
- **15 clock hours annual ongoing training**, logged in PDIS.

## What must be on your walls
- Current CDEC license.
- Most recent inspection summary.
- Colorado Shines quality rating (if participating).
- Emergency preparedness plan and evacuation route per room.
- Safe Sleep policy in infant rooms.
- Menus.
- Gun safety / weapons policy notice.
- CCCAP fiscal agreement information (for subsidy providers).

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** — per emergency plan.
- **Lockdown drill** — per plan.
- **Evacuation + reunification drill** — per plan.
- **Daily attendance** (in/out).
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident reports**.
- **Altitude / mountain safety considerations** for higher-elevation programs.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff. Check for any infant mixed with kids over 30 months (banned combination in CO).
2. Pull 3 random staff files — fingerprint FBI + CBI (under 5 years?), Trails, PDIS account, 15-hour CE log, CPR card.
3. Pull 3 random child files — CIIS immunization, health statement, IHCP if any.
4. Infant rooms: back sleep, no loose bedding.
5. Fire drill log — current month initialed?
6. Lockdown drill log — completed per plan?
7. License posted and current.
8. Emergency plan posted and includes fire, lockdown, evacuation, reunification.
9. Firearms (if any present on site) stored unloaded, locked, separate from ammo.
10. Pull up your Colorado Shines program page — any open finding?

## What happens if you fail
CDEC issues findings under the cited rule. A Corrective Action Plan is required. Escalation ladder runs through Notice, Probationary Status, and Revocation — posted on CDEC's Public Notice Information page within 10 business days of final action. Appeals go through the state administrative process. Since Colorado is continuous licensure, there is no renewal checkpoint to clean things up — issues compound.

## The weird Colorado gotcha nobody tells you about
Colorado created **a brand-new agency** in 2022. Licensing moved from CDHS to CDEC/DELLA. The rule citations still live under the old "12 CCR 2509-8" path because the code wasn't fully remigrated, but the actual regulator is CDEC. Older enforcement records even cite CDHS in their metadata. On top of that, Colorado went to **continuous licensure** — which sounds great (no renewal hassle) but means compliance is monitored continuously with no clean-slate moment. You need ongoing systems. The unique Colorado wrinkles: **infants can't be grouped with kids over 30 months** (an outright prohibition, not just a ratio issue); firearms policy is explicit (**unloaded, locked, ammo separate**); and **gang-affiliated tattoos** get flagged in staff background review. Also: PDIS is not optional — if a staff member's training isn't logged there, for CDEC's purposes it doesn't exist.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Colorado owner
Biggest fear: Colorado Shines page showing a finding that won't clear, because continuous licensure means no natural reset moment. Drop this in 30 seconds: "CDEC is still rolling out after the 2022 transfer from CDHS. You owe 15 PDIS hours per staff per year, fingerprint FBI + CBI every 5 years, and you literally can't mix infants with kids over 30 months. Are you tracking PDIS hours, fingerprint dates, and the mixed-age prohibition in one system?" Today they are using PDIS for training, Trails for background, a paper CIIS printout for immunizations, and a plain spreadsheet tying it together. The close: "We watch every PDIS hour and every fingerprint clock so continuous licensure doesn't become continuous compliance debt."
