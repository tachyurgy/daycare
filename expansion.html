Expansion Playbook

Where we go after daycare.

I looked at every adjacent industry from the earlier doc. Here's which one to attack next, who already plays there, and why most of them are wrong for us.

Contents 1. TL;DR — my pick 2. The scoring matrix 3. Deep dive: #1 Adult Day Care 4. Deep dive: #2 Assisted Living 5. Wild card: Dental + Med Spa 6. What to avoid 7. Recommended sequence

1TL;DR — what you should actually do

The pick

Adult Day Care / Adult Day Services. Same regulator as daycare in CA, TX, and most states. Almost zero software competition. The playbook — file-per-participant, file-per-staff, unannounced inspection, expiry-driven credentials — is literally the same product with 20 field relabels.

Then, once that's installed in ~100 centers, climb up the senior-care ladder to Assisted Living. That's where the real money is — but you don't start there because PointClickCare will eat you alive if you walk in cold.

The three-ring logic

You judge any expansion vertical on four things:

  1. Ease of entry — does our existing product work? do we know the regulator? can we sell it without hiring clinical staff?
  2. Need — how badly does the operator hate their current process? what happens if they fail?
  3. Competition — who's already there, how entrenched, how bad are they?
  4. Money — TAM × ACV × retention × gross margin

No single vertical wins all four. Adult Day Care wins ease + competition by a mile but has a smaller TAM. Assisted Living wins money by a mile but has a dominant incumbent. Dental wins entry cost but has medium ACV. Trucking has huge money but the incumbents (Motive, Samsara) would annihilate us. Framed this way, the answer falls out.

3Deep dive: Adult Day Care

PICK #1 Easiest entry No real competition

Adult Day Services Centers (ADSCs) & PACE Programs

Market size
~5,500 licensed adult day centers in US (NADSA 2024). PACE programs: 155 organizations, 300+ centers, growing ~12%/yr as Medicare/Medicaid pushes community-based care.
Regulator
Same as daycare in most states! CA: CCLD (Adult Day Program licensing). TX: HHSC (same agency as child care). FL: DOEA + AHCA.
Inspection cadence
Unannounced annual + complaint-driven. Nearly identical in mechanics to our daycare playbook.
What's at stake
License revocation → loss of Medicaid waiver funding → business dies. This is existential for operators.
Typical ACV
$200–$800/month/center (larger than daycare's $99–$299).
TAM math
5,500 centers × $500/mo × 12 = $33M TAM. Smaller than daycare but with higher per-account value and dramatically lower CAC because the playbook transfers.

What the inspector asks for (and why it's the same file stacks)

  • Participant file (= child file): enrollment, emergency contacts, MD orders, care plan, medication list, functional assessment, advance directives, allergy/diet
  • Staff file (= staff file): background check, TB, CPR, orientation, continuing ed hours, any clinical licenses (LPN, CNA)
  • Facility file (= facility file): license, insurance, fire inspection, emergency plan, drill logs, staffing roster, activity calendar
  • Daily operational: sign-in/sign-out, meal records, medication administration log, incident reports
Why this is the wedge Our daycare product has ~85% feature parity with what an adult day center needs on day one. The deltas: swap "child" for "participant," add a care-plan module, add a medication administration log, add functional-status tracking (ADL/IADL scoring). That's 3–5 weeks of engineering, not 3–5 months.

Who's already there (competitors)

CareTracker (CareVoyant)
Clinical-focused, expensive, bloated. Aimed at larger PACE programs.
MatrixCare Adult Day
ResMed-owned enterprise suite. Heavy, complex, built for PACE.
AdultDayCareSoftware.com
Literal one-person outfit. Legacy UI. Not a real threat.
DayCenter / Peridot
Scheduling-focused, not compliance-focused.
Paper + Excel
The dominant incumbent. Most small centers still run on spreadsheets and manila folders. This is the 70% of the market.

Why existing players don't lock it up

  • MatrixCare / CareTracker target the top 10% (large PACE). The long tail of single-site and small multi-site ADSCs has no serious vendor.
  • The incumbents don't lead with "inspection-ready" — they lead with billing, scheduling, care planning. Nobody owns the compliance story.
  • Half the industry is 1–5 centers run by a nurse or social worker. They want a simple iPad app, not a $2K/mo clinical suite.

How the sales motion works

  • Same LinkedIn/trade-show playbook as daycare.
  • NADSA (National Adult Day Services Association) annual conference is the single best event — 400–600 attendees, mostly directors.
  • State chapters matter: California Association for Adult Day Services, Texas Adult Day Care Association. Speak at their webinars.
  • Buying cycle is short — usually the same person who runs it also signs the check. No committee.

4Deep dive: Assisted Living (where the real money is)

BIGGEST $$ Entrenched incumbent

Assisted Living Facilities (ALFs) — specifically small-to-mid operators (10–100 beds)

Market size
~30K US assisted living communities, ~1M residents. Growing steadily with aging population.
Regulator
State-specific. CA: CCLD RCFE licensing (same as daycare!). TX: HHSC. FL: AHCA. NY: DOH.
Inspection cadence
Annual unannounced + complaint-driven. 2–3× more paperwork than daycare.
Typical ACV
$500–$3,000/month/community depending on size. Enterprise chains: $10K+/mo.
TAM math
30K × $1,200/mo × 12 = ~$430M TAM. If we get 5% we're a real company.

Who owns this space

PointClickCare
The dominant player. EHR for senior care. ~25K facilities. Public company. Bloated, expensive, hated by operators but hard to rip out.
MatrixCare
ResMed-owned. Enterprise focused. Strong in CCRCs.
Yardi Senior Living Suite
Strong in the property-management-first operators. Bundled with Yardi RE.
Eldermark
Mid-market focused. OK product.
ECP (Extended Care Professional)
Compliance-adjacent, eMAR focused. Mid-market. Closest to what we'd build.
Brightree / CareAcademy
Training/credentialing niche.
Why we don't lead with this PointClickCare has a 10-year head start, clinical depth we don't have (eMAR, MDS, billing integrations), and most importantly it's deeply embedded in operational workflows. Walking into AL cold as an unknown brand means a 12–18 month sales cycle per account. You lose money.
But here's the opening The long tail — single-site and 2–5 site ALF operators — are underserved. PointClickCare is overkill and $2K+/mo for them. They want an iPad compliance app, not a clinical EHR. If we land Adult Day Care first, we build senior-care muscle and reputation, then punch into small-ALF with the same "inspection-ready" angle. By then we have referenceable customers in the senior space.

5Wild cards: Dental + Med Spa

Parallel market Doesn't require clinical credibility

Dental Practice OSHA / HIPAA / Infection Control

Market size
~200K US dental practices. ~$15B practice management software market, but compliance is a sliver with almost no SaaS.
Regulators
OSHA (BBP, Hazard Comm), HHS (HIPAA), state dental boards, CDC infection control. Annual OSHA audits are common.
Pain
Autoclave spore tests weekly, bloodborne pathogen training annual, HIPAA risk assessments annual, dental waterline compliance, radiation badges, sharps log, staff annual training. Mostly tracked in paper binders.
Typical ACV
$1,500–$3,500/year/practice. Cash-rich buyer.
TAM math
200K × $2,000/yr = $400M TAM. Massive.

Who's already there

Dentrix / Eaglesoft / Open Dental
PRACTICE management, not compliance. They are not your competitor — they're integration targets.
Dental Enhancements
Consulting + some software. Old school, expensive.
HIPAA Secure Now
HIPAA-only. Doesn't cover OSHA.
Compliancy Group
HIPAA compliance platform — closest analog.
OSAP
Training association, not software.
The consultant who visits once a year
This is how most practices handle it. Pays $2–5K for an annual audit + binder. Ripe for disruption.
Why it scores well No SaaS leader owns dental compliance. Buyer is one person (office manager or dentist-owner). Sales cycle is weeks. Regulatory framework is stable and well-documented. Existing PM systems don't touch this space — so we're not competing, we're filling a gap.
Fastest-growing Regulatory tailwind

Med Spas / Aesthetic Clinics

Market size
~11K US med spas, growing 8–12%/yr. Hot category.
Regulators
State medical boards, state cosmetology boards, FDA (device-specific), OSHA. States are rapidly tightening rules (CA, FL, NV, TX all pushed new med-spa regulations 2024–2026).
Pain
Laser technician certifications, injectable training logs, medical director chart review logs, adverse event reporting, consent forms, before/after photo consent, HIPAA for treatment records.
ACV
$1,500–$5,000/year/location. Cash-rich industry.
TAM math
11K × $3,000/yr = $33M TAM. Smaller, but fast-growing and underserved.

Competitors

PatientNow / RxPhoto
EMR + marketing focused. Not compliance-led.
Nextech
Aesthetic EHR. Compliance is an afterthought.
AestheticsPro / Aesthetic Record
PM-focused.
No dedicated compliance SaaS
This is the gap.

6What to avoid (and why)

Skip for now These verticals look adjacent but the economics or competition kill them as a near-term expansion.
VerticalWhy skip
Home Health / Hospice WellSky, MatrixCare, Homecare Homebase, Axxess, Alora are all dug in. CMS Conditions of Participation require deep clinical integration. You'd need a clinician on the team before you can sell.
Trucking / DOT DQF Motive (formerly KeepTruckin, $2B+) + Samsara + J.J. Keller. The ELD hardware moat means they all offer DQF as a feature. Our wedge isn't differentiated here.
Restaurants / HACCP Jolt is the direct competitor and has ~15K locations. Toast and Square offer adjacent compliance features. Thin margins + crowded = no.
Construction sub prequalification Avetta ($2B+) and ISNetworld (owned by Veritas PE) control this market. Network effect is brutal: GCs make subs use them. We can't break in.
Skilled Nursing (SNF) PointClickCare has 70%+ of the market. F-tag compliance is deeply tied to CMS MDS submissions — requires clinical SME.
Cannabis METRC is the mandatory integration + Dutchie, Flowhub, BioTrack control the seed-to-sale software. HR/staff compliance is a sliver nobody wins on.
Affordable housing / LIHTC RealPage, Yardi, MRI lock this up. Buyers are enterprise-scale PMs. Long sales cycles.
Pharmacies (USP 797/800 compounding) Highly specialized niche. Need pharmacy SMEs. Market is a few thousand compounding pharmacies. Not worth the detour.
Veterinary clinics IDEXX (Cornerstone) + AVImark + ezyVet are dug in. DEA log compliance is real but small per-clinic spend.

The meta-thesis

ComplianceKit isn't a daycare app — it's a state-inspection-ready platform for fragmented, paper-based, regulated small businesses. Every vertical we enter widens the core data model and sharpens the "inspection-ready" narrative. Five years in, we own the category of "software that passes state inspections for you."
