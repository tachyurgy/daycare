# How to Pass Your West Virginia Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In West Virginia, your licensor is a **regional specialist from the Bureau for Family Assistance (BFA), Division of Early Care and Education (ECE)**, inside the **WV Department of Human Services (DoHS)**. Before 2024 this was DHHR/BCF — you'll see both names on old paperwork. Visits are **unannounced**. Rule 78-1-5 says the specialist can show up at any time and look at any part of your operation. Fire Marshal and your county health department also inspect — you need clean reports from both at renewal.

- DoHS Child Care Centers portal: https://bfa.wv.gov/child-care-centers
- Public provider search: https://www.wvdhhr.org/bcf/ece/cccenters/ecewvisearch.asp

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **Statute:** W. Va. Code §49-2-801 et seq. — the law that gives DoHS the power to license you.
- **Regulations:** **78 CSR 1 — Child Care Centers Licensure** — the full rulebook. PDF: https://dhhr.wv.gov/bcf/Childcare/Documents/ChildCareCenterRegulationWeb.pdf
- **Appendix 78-1 E** — the ratio tables. This is the page your inspector will flip to first.
- **Inspection authority:** W. Va. Code §49-2-113 — https://code.wvlegislature.gov/49-2-113/

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From Appendix 78-1 E, Table A (single-age groups):

| Age | Max children per qualified staff | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| 6 weeks – 12 months | 4 | 8 |
| 13 – 24 months | 4 | 12 |
| 25 – 35 months (2 yrs) | 8 | 16 |
| 3 yrs | 10 | 20 |
| 4 yrs | 12 | 24 |
| 5 yrs | 12 | 24 |
| School-age | 16 | 32 |

**Warning:** In mixed-age rooms, the ratio for the **youngest child** governs (78-1-10.5.b.2). Water-activity ratios in Table B are much stricter — for kids under 12 months it's 1:1. **At least 2 qualified staff must always be on duty when children are present.** Cooks, bookkeepers, and lifeguards do NOT count toward ratio.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced, shows ID, says which rule series they're inspecting under (78 CSR 1).
2. Checks that your current license is posted and not expired.
3. Walks every room — counts heads, counts qualified staff, compares against Table A / Appendix 78-1 E.
4. Pulls staff files — looks for WV State Police + FBI fingerprint clearance, WV Child Abuse & Neglect Registry check, Sex Offender Registry check, TB screening, CPR/First Aid, and continuing-ed hours (78-1-8 and 78-1-9).
5. Pulls child files — looks for 78-1-7 required items (enrollment, immunizations, emergency contacts, medication authorizations, health history).
6. Checks fire drill log, evacuation plan, nap-room supervision for infants (78-1-10), medication log.
7. Sits down, writes a non-compliance report if anything is off, gives you a corrective-action deadline.

Non-compliance histories get published on the state search tool under each center's Provider Number (e.g., the sample detail page at http://www.wvdhhr.org/bcf/ece/cccenters/get_details.asp?q=30068285). Parents can see it.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio and group-size violations** — especially in mixed-age rooms where staff forget the youngest-child rule (78-1-10).
2. **Incomplete staff files** — missing the WV Child Abuse & Neglect Registry check or an expired TB screening (78-1-8.5.g.4).
3. **Missing or late CPR/First Aid** — required current for qualified staff, especially at open/close (78-1-9).
4. **Child file gaps** — missing immunization record, allergy list, or parent-authorized medication consent (78-1-7).
5. **Nap-area supervision failures** — for infants under 12 months, a qualified staff must be IN the nap area, able to see and hear every child (78-1-10). Cameras alone do not cut it.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
Required by 78-1-7:
- **Enrollment application & agreement** — child's name, address, DOB, parents' contacts.
- **Emergency contacts and authorized pickup list**.
- **Immunization record** — per WV DHHR schedule, or a signed medical/religious exemption.
- **Health history** — allergies, chronic conditions, physician/clinic contact.
- **Medication authorization form + log** — if you give any medicine.
- **School-age kids:** school name + phone (78-1-7.4.n).
- **Daily attendance record**.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
Required by 78-1-8 and 78-1-9:
- **Employment application, resume, references**.
- **WV State Police criminal background check** (fingerprint).
- **FBI fingerprint clearance**.
- **WV Child Abuse & Neglect Central Registry check**.
- **Sex Offender Registry check**.
- **TB risk assessment** every year, OR a TB screening by a licensed health professional.
- **Current child CPR + age-appropriate First Aid certificate**.
- **Safe Sleep training** (if you serve infants).
- **Medication administration training** (if staff give meds).
- **Prevention of abuse & neglect training**.
- **Signed orientation acknowledgment + annual professional-development log**.

## What must be on your walls
- Current DoHS license.
- Most recent Fire Marshal inspection certificate.
- Most recent County Health / Sanitation inspection.
- Evacuation plan and emergency procedures.
- Parent handbook / policies summary.
- Licensing complaint contact number for parents.
- Menus (if you serve meals).
- Daily schedule.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented (frequency per 78 CSR 1 / local fire code).
- **Severe weather / shelter drills** — documented.
- **Evacuation/emergency plan** on file and practiced.
- **Daily attendance** — every room, every child.
- **Medication administration log**.
- **Accident/incident reports**.
- **Nap-supervision log** (infant rooms especially).

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff in every room. Write it down. Check against Appendix 78-1 E.
2. Confirm at least 2 qualified staff are on duty.
3. Current license and most recent fire/health inspection posted at the entrance.
4. Nap area for infants — qualified staff IN the room, not watching a monitor from the hall.
5. Medication cabinet locked. Log up to date.
6. No bleach, chemicals, or medications reachable by kids.
7. Diaper-change station clean, gloves stocked, handwashing sink works.
8. Playground — no broken fencing, no standing water, gates latch.
9. Staff files — latest CPR/First Aid dates visible; no expired certs.
10. Child files — every enrolled child has a current immunization record or exemption.

## What happens if you fail
78 CSR 1 section 5 gives the inspector the right to cite non-compliance and require a corrective-action plan with a deadline. Repeated or serious non-compliance can lead to **conditions of operation**, **provisional license**, **suspension**, or **revocation**. If the Secretary finds immediate danger to children, the license can be **immediately suspended**. Enforcement is announced via DoHS press releases — see the October 2024 Cross Lanes Child Care provisional-license action as a real-world example: https://dhhr.wv.gov/News/2024/Pages/DoHS-Places-Cross-Lanes-Child-Care-and-Learning-Center-on-Provisional-License-Amid-Investigation.aspx. You have appeal rights under W. Va. Code §49-2-801 et seq.

## The weird West Virginia gotcha nobody tells you about
**Youth apprentices and student interns who are at least 17 years old and in their second year of the WV Department of Education-approved Child Development Specialist program CAN count toward your staff-child ratio — but they can never be left alone with kids.** Most owners either ignore this tool (and short-staff themselves) or abuse it (and get cited for leaving an apprentice solo). Also: if you renew 2 years at a time (which 78-1-4.5.b allows for compliant licensees), your renewal application is due **at least 60 days before expiration**. Miss that date and you're on a 30-day fire drill.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a West Virginia owner
Their fear is the unannounced specialist showing up and pulling five child files where one is missing the current immunization record. The 30-second number: in WV, ratios default to the YOUNGEST child in a mixed-age room, so the moment an infant moves into a toddler room, your whole ratio math flips. WV owners today manage this with paper binders and a calendar taped to the fridge. The DoHS provider search publishes non-compliance history where every parent can see it — that reputational stake is your emotional wedge. Lead with: "One click shows whether every child's immunization record and every staff member's TB + CPR are current, so the next unannounced visit doesn't leave a public mark."
