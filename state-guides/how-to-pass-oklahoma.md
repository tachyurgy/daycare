# How to Pass Your Oklahoma Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector is a **licensing specialist** from **Oklahoma Department of Human Services (OKDHS), Child Care Services (OCCS)**. OCCS is the only agency that licenses daycare in Oklahoma. Inspections are **unannounced**, and most licensed centers see **2–4 monitoring visits per year**. State rules require at least one unannounced monitoring visit every year, but in practice OCCS visits more often. Every visit — and every non-compliance — is published to the public **Oklahoma Child Care Locator**, where any parent can see your compliance history for the last 3 years plus a substantiated complaint summary.

- Agency: https://oklahoma.gov/okdhs/services/child-care-services.html
- Public inspection portal: https://childcarefind.okdhs.org/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **This is the law:** 10 O.S. §§ 401–410 (Oklahoma Child Care Facilities Licensing Act)
- **This is the main rulebook:** Oklahoma Administrative Code (OAC) Title 340, Chapter 110, Subchapter 3
- **This is the handbook:** OKDHS Pub. 14-05 "Licensing Requirements for Child Care Programs" (11/1/2025 edition). https://oklahoma.gov/content/dam/ok/en/okdhs/documents/okdhs-publication-library/14-05_LicensingRequirementsforChildCarePrograms_ccs_07312025.pdf
- **This is the ratio rule:** OAC Appendix GG (ratios and group sizes). http://okrules.elaws.us/oac/340:110-3-286
- **This is the background-check law:** Oklahoma SAFETY Act (mandatory fingerprint + name-based checks)

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

For licensed **Child Care Centers** (OAC 340 Appendix GG):

| Age | One staff for this many kids | Max group |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (0–12 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Toddler (12–24 mo) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Two-year-olds | 1:8 | 16 |
| Three-year-olds | 1:12 | 24 |
| Four-year-olds | 1:15 | 30 |
| Five-year-olds (not in school) | 1:15 | 30 |
| School-age (6–12) | 1:15 | 30 |

**Family Child Care Home:** 1:7 total, with ≤4 under 24 months and ≤2 under 12 months.
**Large Family Home:** 2 adults : up to 12 children.

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Ratios must be met **at all times — including naps, outdoor play, and meals**. Only "teaching personnel" count in ratio — the cook, the director in the office, the bus driver do not count.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced and shows a state badge.
2. Checks your posted license, posted ratios, and the Reaching for the Stars rating card.
3. Counts kids and teachers in every room — writes ratios by age band on the visit form.
4. Pulls staff files: OSBI + FBI fingerprint results under the SAFETY Act, Restricted Registry ("Joshua's List") check, entry-level training, CPR/First Aid.
5. Pulls child files: Form 07LC010E enrollment & emergency card, immunization record, health assessment.
6. Reviews fire drill log (Form 07LC036E), tornado drill log, insurance certificate, fire/health inspection dates, equipment inventory.
7. Goes through the standard 35-item checklist — each item is marked **C** (Compliant), **NC** (Non-compliant), or **NR** (Not Reviewed).
8. Signs the form, gives you a copy, and walks the corrective action with you.

The report is then posted to `childcarefind.okdhs.org` within days.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Oklahoma
1. **Ratio out of balance** (OAC 340 Appendix GG / 340:110-3-286).
2. **CPR or Pediatric First Aid expired** — at least one trained staff must be present **at all times** (340:110-3-287.1).
3. **Background check not current** — SAFETY Act fingerprint must be re-run every 5 years; Restricted Registry checked at each hire.
4. **Tornado drill log missing or not monthly** in March–October — Oklahoma is severe-weather country.
5. **Entry-level training not completed** — 12 clock hours within first 90 days (new staff), pre-service orientation within 14 days.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Form 07LC010E** — Child's Enrollment & Emergency Information
- Immunization record on OSDH Form 216A (or notarized exemption affidavit)
- Health assessment / physical within 30 days of enrollment
- Allergy / special diet / medication authorizations
- Pickup and field-trip authorization
- Signed parent handbook acknowledgment
- Incident / injury reports
- Daily sign-in / sign-out (keep 1 year)

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Completed employment application + ethics/policies acknowledgment
- **OSBI state + FBI fingerprint background check** (SAFETY Act) — every 5 years
- **Restricted Registry / Joshua's List** check at hire
- TB or health statement
- **Form 07LC013E** — Health Assessment for Child Care Employees
- Pre-service orientation within 14 days; 12 clock hours entry-level training
- Annual professional development: **12 hours (aides), 20 (teachers), 30 (directors)**
- CPR + Pediatric First Aid (at least one staff on site at all times)
- ITS-SIDS training (if any infants)
- Medication administration training (if administering)
- Mandated reporter acknowledgment

## What must be on your walls
- Current license
- Ratio chart
- Fire evacuation map (each room)
- Tornado shelter plan and route
- Immunization exemption notice (OSDH required)
- Reaching for the Stars rating (if rated)
- OCCS complaint hotline (1-844-834-8314)

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly (Form 07LC036E)
- **Tornado drills** — monthly **March through October** (climate-specific Oklahoma rule)
- **Daily sign-in / sign-out**
- **Medication log**
- **Injury / incident log**
- **Fire extinguisher** date check
- **Equipment inventory** — updated annually

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids vs. staff in every room. Only teaching staff count.
2. Confirm license, ratios, tornado plan, fire map posted.
3. Check the fire drill log — is this month's drill there?
4. Check the tornado drill log — is this month's in there if it's March–October?
5. Pull three staff files: OSBI/FBI date, CPR date, SIDS training (if infants).
6. Pull three child files: immunization, health assessment within 30 days.
7. Lock up chemicals, medications, and firearms (if any — unloaded, ammo separate).
8. Sleep areas: backs only, firm mattress, no blankets, one infant per crib.
9. Playground: no broken equipment, gates latched.
10. Greet the inspector. Be friendly. Ask which rooms they want to see first.

## What happens if you fail
OCCS records a **non-compliance** on the visit report. Corrective action plan and deadline are set. Repeat or serious violations trigger a **compliance agreement**, probation, civil penalty, suspension, or revocation. Your license can drop from **3-Star → 2-Star → 1-Star** on Reaching for the Stars, which directly cuts your subsidy reimbursement. Every visit is public for **3 years** on `childcarefind.okdhs.org` (older records available through OKDHS in-person file inspection per OAC 340:110-1 Part 14). Appeals go through OKDHS's Hearing Office.

## The weird Oklahoma gotcha nobody tells you about
**Tornado drills must happen every month from March through October** — eight months a year — with the log available on site. Most owners remember fire drills; they forget the tornado one. Oklahoma is the only state where this specific climate-driven drill is enforced this strictly. If an inspector lands in May and your April tornado drill isn't logged, that's an automatic NC. A second one in the same season and you're in corrective action. Pro tip: combine your fire drill and your tornado drill in the same week — different sirens, different routes, same checklist.
