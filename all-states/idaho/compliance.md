# Idaho — Child Care Compliance Overview

## Licensing Authority

**Idaho Department of Health and Welfare (IDHW) — Division of Family and Community Services (Child Care Licensing)**
- Address: PO Box 83720, Boise, ID 83720-0036
- Phone: 208-334-5700
- Web: https://healthandwelfare.idaho.gov/services-programs/child-care

Additional oversight:
- **Local public health districts** (7 regional health districts) conduct inspections — e.g., Eastern Idaho Public Health (EIPH), Central District Health, etc.
- **IdahoSTARS** — quality rating & resource/referral partner (https://idahostars.org)

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Statute:** **Idaho Code §§ 39-1101 – 39-1120** — Basic Day Care License Law.
  - §39-1110 — Health Standards (food prep, diaper changing, sleep/play areas, handwashing, water supply)
- **Regulations:** **IDAPA 16.06.02 — Rules Governing Standards for Child Care Licensing**
  - Note: IDAPA chapter organization was reorganized (16.06.02 was previously Foster Care Licensing and/or Child Care Licensing depending on version); current Child Care Licensing rules are at IDAPA 16.06.02 per EIPH and IdahoSTARS official references.
- **2025 Legislation (HB 243):** Retained the 12-point-per-staff ratio framework but adjusted underlying point values for some age groups.

## Facility Types Regulated

Per Idaho Code §39-1102, three main categories by size:

- **Family Daycare Home** — ≤ 6 children. *Not required to be licensed*, may voluntarily elect licensure.
- **Group Daycare Facility** — 7 – 12 children. Must be licensed.
- **Daycare Center** — ≥ 13 children. Must be licensed.

Some cities (e.g., Boise) impose **additional local licensing requirements** stricter than state minimums.

## Key Licensing Forms

- **Day Care License Application** (IDHW)
- **Enhanced Background Check Request** (department-administered, at least every 5 years for owner/operator)
- **Fire Marshal Inspection Report**
- **Public Health District Inspection Report** (food service + sanitation)
- **Documentation of first aid / CPR certification** (provider level)

## Child-to-Staff Ratios — Point System (IDAPA 16.06.02.335)

Idaho uses a **point-based ratio system**. Each staff member may be responsible for up to **12 points of children**.

| Age Range | Points per Child (current) |
|---|---|
| Birth – under 24 months | **2.000** |
| 24 months – under 36 months | **1.333** |
| 36 months – under 5 years | **0.923** |
| 5 years – under 13 years | **0.480** |

Example: a classroom of 12-point capacity may contain any combination that totals ≤ 12 points — e.g., 6 infants (6 × 2 = 12), or 9 toddlers (9 × 1.333 = 12), or 13 preschoolers (13 × 0.923 = 12), etc.

A 2025 legislative bill (HB 243) adjusted the point values for some groups while retaining the 12-point maximum per staff member.

## Required Child Records

- Enrollment form (name, DOB, parent info, emergency contacts)
- Immunization record (Idaho Immunization Reminder Information System or paper record)
- Health history and allergy/special needs
- Authorization for emergency medical care
- Medication authorization & log
- Authorized pick-up list
- Attendance record
- Parent handbook acknowledgment

## Required Staff Records (EIPH Guidelines / IDAPA 16.06.02)

- Employment application & resume
- **Criminal History & Background Check clearance** — Department of Health and Welfare enhanced check (all owners, operators, staff, and any individual 13 years or older with unsupervised direct child contact). Required to be on-site and shown to inspector during initial and annual inspections.
- Enhanced background check required at least **every 5 years** for owner/operator/applicant
- First Aid, **Infant–Child CPR**, and pediatric rescue breathing (must be from a certified instructor)
- Orientation documentation
- Annual training hours (per IDAPA 16.06.02.525 and related QRIS / subsidy requirements)
- TB risk assessment (if required by local health district)
- SIDS / safe-sleep training (if serving infants)

## Inspection Cadence

- **Initial inspection** required before license issuance.
- **Annual inspections** by local public health district (e.g., EIPH, CDHD) + IDHW.
- Documentation of background check clearance must be available on-site during initial and annual inspections.
- Complaint-based inspections conducted as needed.
- **Playground equipment, kitchen, diaper areas** all inspected per 16.06.02 and §39-1110.
- Inspection reports posted on IdahoChildCareCheck.org (pass/fail history, substantiated incidents).

## Renewal Cadence

- License issued for **one year**; renewed annually.
- Annual renewal inspection required.
- Enhanced background check re-run **at least every 5 years** for owner/operator.
- Local public health district issues sanitation / food-service approval annually.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Point-based ratio system** is unique to Idaho — requires operators to calculate weighted class composition, not simple ratio tables.
- **12-point maximum per staff member** — strictly enforced during inspections.
- **Local jurisdictions (cities)** that require child care licensing may have **more stringent** requirements (e.g., Boise).
- **Family Daycare Homes (≤ 6 children) are exempt** from state licensure but may voluntarily license and are encouraged to participate in ICCP (Idaho Child Care Program / subsidy) which requires licensure.
- **Idaho Child Care Program (ICCP)** subsidy enrollment requires licensure (per IDAPA 16.06.12).
- **Safe sleep mandates:** infants on backs, firm mattress, well-fitting sheet, safety-approved crib/playpen; **no car seats, swings, strollers, infant slings** for sleep; one infant per crib; crib clear of toys/blankets; direct sight/sound observation required at all times.
- **Transportation:** child safety restraints required; state-compliant seats by age.
- **Abuse & neglect reporting:** mandatory reporter obligations apply.
- **HB 243 (2025)** — ongoing deregulation effort; adjustments to underlying point values for some age groups while retaining 12-point cap.
