### A. INSPECTOR-USED EVALUATION FORMS (Used by CCR Licensing staff during inspections)

#### Form 7259 -- Personnel Records Evaluation - Child Care Centers
- **Download**: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/documents/laws-regulations/forms/7259/7259.pdf
- **Who fills it out**: Inspector (Licensing representative)
- **Type**: Checklist / evaluation form
- **What it covers**: Systematic review of whether each staff member's personnel file is complete. Evaluates presence and currency of:
  - Background check clearances
  - Pre-employment affidavits
  - Training documentation
  - Health statements
  - CPR/First Aid certification
  - Professional development records
- **Format**: Interactive PDF (requires Adobe Reader for full functionality)

#### Form 7260 -- Children's Records Evaluation - Child Care Centers
- **Download**: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/documents/7260.pdf
- **Who fills it out**: Inspector
- **Type**: Checklist / evaluation form
- **What it covers**: Reviews whether children's enrollment files contain all required documents:
  - Admission information (Form 2935)
  - Emergency contact info
  - Immunization records
  - Allergy/medical condition documentation
  - Parent authorizations (medication, pickup, field trips)
  - Sign-in/sign-out logs
- **Format**: Interactive PDF

#### Form 7261 -- Center Records Evaluation
- **Download**: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/documents/laws-regulations/forms/7261/7261.pdf
- **Who fills it out**: Inspector
- **Type**: Checklist / evaluation form
- **What it covers**: Reviews overall center-level administrative records:
  - License and permit documentation
  - Plan of operation
  - Liability insurance verification
  - Fire/health/gas inspection records
  - Emergency practices documentation
  - Menu and activity plans
  - Controlling persons documentation
  - Waivers/variances
- **Format**: Interactive PDF

#### Form 7270 -- Children's Records Evaluation LCCH/RCCH
- **Download**: Via HHSC forms page (same base URL)
- **Who fills it out**: Inspector
- **Type**: Checklist
- **What it covers**: Same as 7260 but adapted for Licensed Child Care Homes (LCCH) and Registered Child Care Homes (RCCH)

#### Form 7271 -- Personnel Records Evaluation LCCH/RCCH
- **Download**: Via HHSC forms page
- **Who fills it out**: Inspector
- **Type**: Checklist
- **What it covers**: Same as 7259 but adapted for home-based care operations

### B. PROVIDER-MAINTAINED OPERATIONAL FORMS

#### Form 1100 -- Daily Building and Grounds Checklist
- **Download**: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/documents/laws-regulations/forms/1100/1100.pdf
- **Who fills it out**: Provider (daily by designated staff)
- **Type**: Daily checklist with columns for MON-FRI
- **What it covers** (extracted directly from the form):
  - Environment free of choking objects for children under 3
  - Machine washing cloth toys weekly / when contaminated
  - Linens washed weekly and when soiled
  - Sleeping equipment sanitized between children
  - Garbage inaccessible
  - Floors, ceilings, walls in good repair and clean
  - Areas well heated, lighted, ventilated
  - Table tops and furniture sanitized when soiled
  - Hazardous materials stored per manufacturer instructions
  - Electrical outlets childproofed (under age 5)
  - Glass in sliding doors marked at children's eye level
  - Play materials safe, free from sharp edges, non-toxic
  - Bodies of water inaccessible
  - Active play space inspected for hazards before children present
  - Broken equipment removed/repaired
  - Current menu posted
  - Current activity plans posted
  - Toxic/cleaning supplies out of reach
  - Toilet paper, soap, disposable towels available
  - Naptime lighting appropriate for supervision
  - Adequate space between cribs/cots/mats
  - TVs anchored so they cannot tip

#### Form 7263 -- Emergency Practices
- **Download**: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/documents/laws-regulations/forms/7263/7263.pdf
- **Who fills it out**: Provider (designee documents drills)
- **Type**: Tracking/log form with monthly rows
- **What it covers** (extracted directly):
  - **Fire Drill Log**: Monthly fire evacuation drills (must exit in < 3 minutes); columns for date/time, staff initials, exit time
  - **Sheltering/Severe Weather Drills**: 4x/year minimum; date/time, staff initials, type (Shelter/Severe Weather/Lock-Down)
  - **Lock-Down Drills**: 4x/year minimum
  - **CO Alarm Detector Tests**: Monthly log
  - **Smoke Detector Tests**: Monthly log
  - **Fire Extinguisher Checks**: Monthly log with date fields
  - **Fire Extinguisher Locations**: Fill-in field
  - **Relocation Site**: Post-evacuation assembly location
  - **Battery-Powered Lighting**: Verification field
  - **First Aid Kit Location**: Fill-in field
  - **Operation Inspection Dates**: Fire, Health, Gas inspection dates

#### Form 1099 -- Operational Discipline and Guidance Policy
- **Who fills it out**: Provider
- **Type**: Policy document / narrative form
- **What it covers**: Facility's discipline and guidance practices

#### Form 2550 -- Operational Policy on Infant Safe Sleep
- **Who fills it out**: Provider
- **Type**: Policy document (English and Spanish)
- **What it covers**: Facility's infant safe sleep practices per SIDS prevention guidelines

#### Form 2935 -- Admission Information
- **Who fills it out**: Provider (with parent)
- **Type**: Data entry form (English and Spanish)
- **What it covers**: Child enrollment data -- demographics, emergency contacts, authorized pickup, medical info, allergies

#### Form 2941 -- Child Care Operation Sign-in/Sign-out Log
- **Who fills it out**: Parents (at drop-off/pickup)
- **Type**: Daily log form
- **What it covers**: Time-stamped attendance records for each child

#### Form 7240 -- Monthly Attendance Record
- **Who fills it out**: Provider
- **Type**: Data tracking form
- **What it covers**: Monthly aggregate attendance data

#### Form 2947 -- Child Care Center Personnel Information Record
- **Who fills it out**: Provider (HR/admin)
- **Type**: Data entry form
- **What it covers**: Staff qualifications, training, employment history

#### Form 2948 -- Plan of Operation
- **Who fills it out**: Provider (director/owner)

- **What it covers**: Center operational plan -- hours, ages served, capacity, program description, staffing plan

#### Form 7239 -- Incident/Illness Report
- **Who fills it out**: Provider staff
- **Type**: Incident report / narrative form (English and Spanish)
- **What it covers**: Documentation of injuries, illnesses, or incidents occurring during care

#### Form 7243 -- Emergency Telephone Numbers
- **Who fills it out**: Provider

#### Form 7255 -- Medication Authorization
- **Who fills it out**: Parent (authorization) / Provider (administration log)

- **Who fills it out**: Provider (in response to inspection findings)
- **Type**: Corrective action plan
- **What it covers**: How the provider will correct cited deficiencies and by when

### C. TEXAS FIRE SAFETY FORMS

#### Form 3004 -- Family Foster Home Fire Safety Evaluation Checklist
- **Download**: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/regulations/forms/3000-3999/form-3004-family-foster-home-fire-safety-evaluation-checklist
- **Also at**: https://www.vctx.org/page/open/8192/0/Foster%20Home%20Checklist.pdf
- **Instructions**: https://www.tdi.texas.gov/fire/documents/fmfsifoster.pdf
- **Who fills it out**: Fire inspector or qualified evaluator
- **Type**: Yes/No checklist (21 items)
- **What it covers** (extracted directly from the form):
  1. Fewer than 7 children in home
  2. All children ambulatory and capable of self-preservation
  3. Working smoke detector in each sleeping room
  4. Working smoke detector in hallway near sleeping rooms
  5. Multi-story: smoke detector at top of stairs
  6. Secondary means of escape from each sleeping room/living area (door to outside, window < 44" from floor with 5.7 sq ft opening, or non-locking door to adjoining room with escape)
  7. Travel from any room to exit without passing through lockable interior door
  8. All exits openable from inside without key/tool/special knowledge
  9. Closet doors openable by child from inside
  10. Bathroom locks unlockable from outside in emergency
  11. Bedrooms/living areas above first floor accessed by standard stairs
  12. Gas/wood/fuel heaters vented to outside
  13. Heaters/fireplaces equipped with immovable screens/barriers
  14. Gas appliances with metal tubing and connectors
  15. Gas-fired heaters inspected annually by qualified technician
  16. Combustible items stored away from heat sources
  17. Lighters/matches out of reach of children
  18. Flammable liquids in safety cans, away from heat and children
  19. 5-pound dry chemical fire extinguisher in kitchen and on every level
  20. Home Fire Escape Plan written, practiced, documented
  21. Electrical system in good condition

## PART 3: FIRE SAFETY INSPECTION FORMS (Multi-State / National)

### A. NFPA Forms

#### NFPA 10 Fire Extinguisher Monthly Inspection Form
- **Standard**: NFPA 10 (Standard for Portable Fire Extinguishers), Section 7.2.1.2
- **Frequency**: Monthly (at approximately 30-day intervals)
- **Example form**: https://campserv.emory.edu/_includes/documents/sections/resources/fire-extinguisher-nfpa-10-monthly-check.pdf
- **Type**: Monthly checklist / tag initials
- **Who fills it out**: Facility staff (monthly visual check) or certified fire extinguisher technician (annual)
- **What it covers**:
  - Check service tag for 13 months since last certified inspection
  - Location and visibility (nothing blocking, properly mounted)
  - Seal intact, pin in place
  - Condition: no dents, no leaking, pressure gauge in green
  - Initial the back of service tag monthly
  - If 12 spaces full, extinguisher needs annual certified servicing
- **Additional resources**:
  - NFPA Annex E revisions: https://www.nfpa.org/api/files?path=%2Ffiles%2FAboutTheCodes%2F302%2FNFPA+302+A19+Annex+E+Revisions+82117.pdf
  - NFPA 10 form templates: https://joyfill.io/form-library/nfpa-10-form-download
  - Template marketplace: https://www.buildarray.com/market/forms/nfpa-fire-safety-forms

### B. State-Specific Daycare Fire Safety Forms

#### North Carolina DCD-0303 -- Adult Day Care & Child Care Fire Inspection Report
- **Download**: https://ncchildcare.ncdhhs.gov/Portals/0/documents/pdf/D/DCD-0303-FIREINSPECTIONFORM.pdf
- **Who fills it out**: Fire inspector
- **Type**: Yes/No/N/A checklist (27 items) with approval determination
- **Sections** (extracted from form):
  1. **General Precautions** (items 1-2): Attic/basement/closets clear of combustibles, clearance from ignition sources
  2. **Emergency Planning** (items 3-5): Approved evacuation plan posted, monthly fire drills evidenced, employee training records
  3. **Fire Service Features** (items 6-8): Street number posted (4"+ contrasting color), 20' fire apparatus road, hydrants/FD connections clear by 3'
  4. **Building Services and Systems** (items 9-13): Approved heating, emergency/exit lights, electrical panels clear 30", wiring condition, Type I hood over grease-producing cooking
  5. **Fire Resistance Rated Construction** (items 14-15): Fire rating maintained, door hold-open devices operating
  6. **Interior Decorations & Furnishings** (items 16-19): No corridor storage, max 50% wall coverage decorations, max 20% corridor wall artwork, exits unobstructed
  7. **Fire Protection** (items 20-24): Sprinkler system maintained with annual test reports, smoke/fire alarm with annual test reports, extinguishers mounted and working, cooking suppression maintained, protective guards on fireplaces/furnaces
  8. **Means of Egress** (items 25-27): Exits and corridors unobstructed, approved locking devices, unobstructed yard/fence egress
- **Approval types**: Daytime only / Daytime and nighttime
- **Determination**: Satisfactory / Unsatisfactory

#### Idaho -- Fire Safety Inspection for State Daycare Licensing
- **Download**: https://doi.idaho.gov/wp-content/uploads/SFM/FDProviders/ChecklistsForms/Daycare-Inspection-Form.pdf
- **Who fills it out**: Fire inspector or designee
- **Type**: Yes/No checklist (4 pages)
- **Sections** (extracted from form):
  1. **Exiting Requirements**: Unobstructed exits, exits openable from inside without key, two exits with travel distance < 75' (110' with sprinklers), minimum 32" clear width and 6'8" height, sleeping room egress windows (5.7 sq ft minimum)
  2. **Fire Extinguishers**: Centers: 2A-10BC in kitchen + one other location, max 5' height, max 75' travel; Groups/Homes: 2A-10BC in kitchen; Hood suppression if required; Additional extinguishers in facilities > 3,000 sq ft; Sprinkler system if > 20,000 sq ft or > 100 children under 18 months
  3. **Telephone**: Operable phone on premises
  4. **Smoke Detectors**: Basement stairway detector, detectors outside sleeping areas, detectors in each sleeping room, detectors on each story
  5. **Fire Safety and Evacuation Plan**: Procedures for accounting for all persons, evacuation routes and assembly points, smoke detector/alarm/extinguisher locations, drill schedule with records
  6. **Carbon Monoxide Detectors**: Required where CO sources present

## PART 5: KEY OBSERVATIONS

1. **Texas** uses numbered forms (1000-7999 series) where 7000-series forms are primarily inspector evaluation tools and 1000-2000 series are provider operational forms. The forms are interactive PDFs hosted on hhs.texas.gov.

2. **California** has two layers of inspection tools:
   - **LIC-series forms**: Traditional PDF forms for specific compliance areas (emergency plans, records checklists, personnel records)
   - **CARE Tools**: Comprehensive Excel-based inspection instruments that systematically walk inspectors through every regulatory domain. These are the primary inspection instrument since ~2020.

3. **Fire safety inspections** are typically conducted by a separate authority (local fire marshal or state fire marshal) rather than the childcare licensing agency itself. The childcare licensing agency requires proof of a current fire clearance.

4. **Form 7263 (Texas)** is the single best form for tracking fire safety compliance at a childcare operation -- it combines fire drill logs, shelter/lockdown drill logs, CO/smoke detector tests, and fire extinguisher checks into one monthly tracking document.

5. **NFPA 10** governs fire extinguisher inspections nationally. Monthly visual checks require only initialing the service tag. Annual certified inspections require a qualified technician.
