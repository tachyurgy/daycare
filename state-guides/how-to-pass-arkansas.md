# How to Pass Your Arkansas Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector is a **licensing specialist** from the **Arkansas Department of Human Services (DHS), Division of Child Care and Early Childhood Education (DCCECE), Child Care Licensing Unit**. DCCECE is the state licensor for all Arkansas child care — centers, licensed and registered family homes, out-of-school-time, and night-time care. Expect **1–3 unannounced inspections a year** depending on your facility type and compliance history. Every visit, every citation, and every complaint shows up on the public **DHS eLicensing** portal.

- Agency: https://portal.arkansas.gov/state_agencies/department-of-human-services-dhs/division-of-child-care-and-early-childhood-education/
- Public provider search: https://ardhslicensing.my.site.com/elicensing/s/search-provider/find-providers
- Complaint line: 1-800-582-4887

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **This is the law:** Ark. Code Ann. §§ 20-78-201 through 20-78-223 (Child Care Facility Licensing Act)
- **This is the rulebook:** Arkansas Admin Code 016.22.20-005 — Minimum Licensing Requirements for Child Care Centers, Licensed Family Homes, Out-of-School Time, Registered Family Homes. https://regulations.justia.com/states/arkansas/agency-016/division-22/rule-016-22-20-005/
- **This is the reportable-offense law:** Ark. Code § 20-78-607 (permanent disqualification for certain felonies)
- **This is the mandated reporter law:** Ark. Code § 12-18-402
- **This is the printable PDF of Minimum Licensing Requirements (Dec 2020):** https://licensingregulations.acf.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/licensing_regulation/AR_Center_Dec_2020_508.pdf

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

**Child Care Centers** (Minimum Licensing Requirements, 016.22.20-005):

| Age | One staff for this many kids |
|---|---|
| Under 18 months | 1:5 |
| 18 months – 36 months | 1:8 |
| 36 months – 3 years | 1:12 |
| 4 years | 1:15 |
| 5 years | 1:18 |
| School-age (6+) | 1:18 |

Arkansas **does not set a hard maximum group size** for most age bands in the Minimum Licensing Requirements — compliance relies on the ratio plus square-footage.

**Naptime:** children ≥2½ yr — 50% of required staff may stay in the room; extra staff must be available on-site for emergencies.

**Family Child Care Homes:** up to 10 children (≤5 under 3 years); 2-adult rule kicks in at 7+ children.

**Mixed groups:** ratio of the youngest age band applies during combined lunch/outdoor play.

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up. The naptime 50% rule applies only to children **2½ years and older** — never to infants or toddlers.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced and shows a DHS badge.
2. Checks your license, Better Beginnings level card, and posted ratios.
3. Walks every room, counts kids vs. staff by age band.
4. Pulls staff files: Arkansas State Police criminal check + **Arkansas Child Maltreatment Central Registry** + FBI fingerprint + Adult Maltreatment Registry + Sex Offender Registry, reportable-offense affidavit, mandated reporter training, 15-hour annual training log, CPR + Pediatric First Aid.
5. Pulls child files: enrollment, Arkansas Department of Health immunization record (or Form ID-33 exemption), physical within 60 days, allergy/meds authorization.
6. Reviews fire drill log (monthly), tornado drill log (monthly **March–October**), incident log, fire marshal + health inspection reports.
7. Writes citations referencing the specific 016.22.20-005 sub-rule with corrective action deadlines.
8. Files the report into eLicensing within days.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Arkansas
1. **Ratio not met** (016.22.20-005).
2. **Background check stale** — initial + every 5 years required. Reportable-offense affidavit must be signed at every hire.
3. **Tornado drill log missing** — Arkansas requires **monthly drills March through October**. Climate-specific and easy to skip.
4. **Annual in-service training short** — 15 hours minimum for staff, 30 for directors. Includes Better Beginnings topics, CPR, First Aid, SIDS, mandated reporter.
5. **Physical exam missing** — must be on file within **60 days** of enrollment.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment / emergency form
- **Arkansas Department of Health immunization** record (or Form ID-33 medical/religious/philosophical exemption)
- Physical exam within **60 days** of enrollment
- Allergy, special-diet, and medication authorization
- Pickup authorization
- Daily attendance log
- Incident / injury log
- Parent handbook acknowledgment

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application, references, **reportable-offense affidavit** (Ark. Code § 20-78-607)
- **Arkansas State Police criminal background check** + **Arkansas Child Maltreatment Central Registry** + **FBI fingerprint** + **Adult Maltreatment Registry** + **Sex Offender Registry** — every 5 years
- Health statement (TB risk assessment)
- Orientation within 30 days
- **Annual in-service — 15 clock hours** (directors 30 hours), including Better Beginnings topics, CPR, First Aid, SIDS, mandated reporter
- **CPR + Pediatric First Aid** — for all caregivers with infants/toddlers; current for ≥1 staff at all times
- Mandated reporter training (Ark. Code § 12-18-402)
- Shaken Baby / Safe Sleep training (infant staff)
- Medication administration training
- Food handler certification (if preparing food)

## What must be on your walls
- Current DHS license
- Ratio chart
- Better Beginnings rating card (if rated)
- Evacuation routes each room
- Tornado shelter plan
- Fire drill and tornado drill logs
- Mandated reporter poster
- Complaint hotline 1-800-582-4887

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly, documented
- **Tornado drills** — monthly **March through October** (Arkansas-specific)
- **Annual safe-shelter plan** review
- **Daily attendance**
- **Medication log**
- **Injury / incident log**

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff in every room against 016.22.20-005.
2. Confirm license, ratios, tornado plan, fire map are posted.
3. Fire drill log — this month entered?
4. Tornado drill log — this month entered (if March–October)?
5. Open three staff files: State Police + FBI + Child Maltreatment Central Registry dates, reportable-offense affidavit, 15-hr training, CPR.
6. Open three child files: immunization, physical within 60 days, allergy plan.
7. Lock chemicals, medications, firearms (unloaded + locked + ammo separate).
8. Cribs: backs, firm mattress, no blankets, one baby per crib.
9. Playground: no broken equipment, pool/splash area supervision noted.
10. Greet the inspector. Offer the file drawer key. Keep a calm tone.

## What happens if you fail
DCCECE issues a **compliance report** with citations and corrective action deadlines. Escalation goes **corrective action plan → probationary status → suspension → revocation**. Your status changes publicly on eLicensing from "Active" to "Probationary," "Suspended," or "Revoked." Your **Better Beginnings level can drop** (1 / 2 / 3) — that directly cuts your CCDF subsidy reimbursement and ABC Pre-K eligibility. Civil money penalties may be assessed. Appeals go through a DHS administrative hearing. Complaint investigation files are available via Arkansas FOIA (3-business-day response window).

## The weird Arkansas gotcha nobody tells you about
Arkansas has **two** kinds of background-registry checks most owners confuse: the **Arkansas Child Maltreatment Central Registry** (for children) AND the **Adult Maltreatment Registry** (for dependent adults). You need **both** for every staff member, plus State Police, FBI fingerprints, and sex-offender registry. That's **5 separate checks** at hire and every 5 years — more than most neighboring states. On top of that, Arkansas requires a **reportable-offense affidavit** signed by the employee at hire certifying they haven't been convicted of anything on the Ark. Code § 20-78-607 list. Miss any one of the five checks or the affidavit and you're cited.
