# How to Pass Your Delaware Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In Delaware, your licensor is an **Office of Child Care Licensing (OCCL) specialist** — and, unusually, OCCL is inside the **Department of Education (DOE)**, not a health-and-human-services agency. (It moved from DSCYF in 2015.) You'll get a **pre-license inspection** plus at least **one unannounced annual monitoring visit**. The Division of Public Health inspects if you prepare meals, and the State Fire Marshal inspects at licensure and periodically.

- DOE Office of Child Care Licensing: https://education.delaware.gov/families/birth-age-5/occl/
- Search for Licensed Child Care (public): https://education.delaware.gov/families/birth-age-5/occl/search_for_licensed_child_care/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **Statutes:** 31 Del. C., Chapter 3, Subchapter IV (Child Care); 14 Del. C. §122 (DOE authority); 14 Del. C. §131 (immunizations for entry).
- **Regulations:** **14 DE Admin. Code 101 — DELACARE: Regulations for Early Care and Education and School-Age Centers** (effective 2022-06-01). Center PDF: https://education.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/occl_delacare-regulations-center_2020.pdf
- **DELACARE Regulations for Family and Large Family Child Care Homes** (effective 2022-08-10). PDF: https://education.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/DELACARE-FCCH-LFCCH-Regulations-August-2022.pdf
- **DELACARE Residential Child Care Facilities** (effective Nov 2024). PDF: https://education.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/DELACARE-Requirements-Residential-Child-Care-Facilities-and-Day-Treatment-Programs.pdf
- **Public records:** 29 Del. C. §10002 et seq. (Delaware FOIA).

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
**DELACARE Early Care & Education Centers:**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (birth – 12 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Young toddlers (12 – 24 mo) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Older toddlers (24 – 36 mo) | 1:8 | 16 |
| Young preschoolers (3 – 4 yr) | 1:10 | 20 |
| Older preschoolers (4 – 5 yr) | 1:12 | 24 |
| School-age (5+) | 1:15 | 30 (varies) |

**Warning:** Mixed-age groups follow the **youngest child**'s ratio. **Family Child Care Home:** up to 6; no more than 2 infants <12 months per caregiver. **Large Family Child Care Home:** 7 – 12 kids; no more than 4 under age 2.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced.
2. Confirms current license + Delaware Stars (if participating) posted.
3. Walks every room; counts kids vs. staff; checks ratios against DELACARE.
4. Pulls staff files — DE SBI, FBI fingerprint, Child Protection Registry (CPR), Adult Abuse Registry, sex offender registry, out-of-state checks, **5-year refresh**, qualifications, Delaware First / DIEEC training log, CPR/First Aid, TB-free documentation.
5. Pulls child files — enrollment, **Delaware Immunization Record** per 14 Del. C. §131, Child Health Appraisal, emergency consents.
6. Reviews fire drill log, medication log, attendance, incident reports.
7. Writes deficiencies — **these appear on the OCCL public search for 5 YEARS** (longer than the typical 3-year window in most states).

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
(Backed by the Delaware **Compliance Review Information** dataset, ~12,428 records, and **Complaint Reviews**, ~1,942 records — both published live via Socrata.)
1. **Documentation gaps** — missing Child Health Appraisal, missing immunization, missing emergency consent.
2. **Ratio / group-size breaches** — especially youngest-child rule at transitions.
3. **Background-check misses** — especially the 5-year refresh or missing an out-of-state check for a new hire.
4. **Training log shortfalls** — annual hours via the DIEEC / Delaware First registry.
5. **Facility-record gaps** — missing fire or sanitation documentation at renewal.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment application with parent/guardian contact and authorized pickup.
- **Delaware Immunization Record** (required for entry per 14 Del. C. §131).
- **Child Health Appraisal** (physical).
- Emergency medical authorization.
- Food allergy / dietary restriction documentation.
- Signed authorizations (photos, field trips, transportation).
- Daily attendance.
- Incident / accident reports.
- Records of any non-compliance or complaints (retained 5 years).

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **DE SBI + FBI fingerprint + Child Protection Registry + Adult Abuse Registry + sex offender registry + out-of-state checks** — pre-hire and **every 5 years**.
- **Delaware First / DIEEC** annual professional development registry.
- Evidence of qualifications (CDA or equivalent for lead teachers).
- **Current pediatric CPR and First Aid**.
- Mandated reporter training.
- Orientation records.
- Health screening (TB-free documentation).
- Performance evaluations.
- Training log (hours vary by role).

## What must be on your walls
- Current DELACARE license.
- Delaware Stars rating (if participating).
- Most recent State Fire Marshal inspection.
- Division of Public Health food permit (if meals prepared).
- Evacuation plan / emergency procedures.
- OCCL complaint contact.
- Daily schedule.
- Menu.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented.
- **Emergency / shelter drills** — documented.
- **Daily attendance** by room.
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident / accident reports**.
- **Health & safety reviews** — mandatory for ALL staff (not just lead teachers).

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff per room against the DELACARE table.
2. Post current license + Delaware Stars + latest fire and health inspections.
3. Pull a random staff file: 5-year background refresh current? DIEEC log up to date?
4. Pull a random child file: Delaware Immunization Record current? Health Appraisal on file?
5. Medication locked; log current.
6. Diaper-change stations clean; gloves stocked.
7. Outdoor area fenced; gates latch; no standing water.
8. Fire-drill log — last drill within required interval; evacuation map posted.
9. Health & safety review documentation for ALL staff, not just leads.
10. Any recent parent complaint or injury — is the incident report filed?

## What happens if you fail
OCCL can issue a deficiency finding and require a corrective action plan. Persistent or serious findings escalate to **conditional license**, **suspension**, or **revocation**. The unique feature of Delaware: **every non-compliance and complaint from the past 5 YEARS is published on the OCCL public search** (longer than most states) — parents see them. Delaware also publishes this as open data on **data.delaware.gov**, which means journalists, researchers, and competitors can query it. Appeal rights run under Delaware's Administrative Procedure Act; FOIA appeals go through the Delaware Attorney General under 29 Del. C. §10005.

## The weird Delaware gotcha nobody tells you about
**Delaware's public-search window for your non-compliances is 5 YEARS, not 3.** Most states publish 2 – 3 years of violations; Delaware publishes 5, including **substantiated complaints that did NOT rise to a regulation violation**. And the state publishes it all as **machine-readable open data** (Socrata datasets `wb83-pkcv` for compliance reviews and `pnbd-85r6` for complaints). Anyone can query your history programmatically. There is no hiding behind "they'll never find it." Second gotcha: DE requires **health & safety reviews for ALL staff**, not just lead teachers — so an aide hired last month who skipped training is a citation.
