# Wyoming Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
**Wyoming Department of Family Services (DFS), Division of Early Care and Education (Child Care Licensing Program).** DFS issues certifications (Wyoming's term for license), conducts inspections, and maintains the public facility finder.

- Address: Wyoming DFS, 2300 Capitol Avenue, Cheyenne, WY 82002
- Phone: (307) 777-7564 | Toll-free: 800-457-3659
- Public finder: `childcare.dfs.wyo.gov` (formerly findchildcarewy.org)

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Statute:** **W.S. § 14-4-101 through § 14-4-116** (Child Care Facilities). Also W.S. § 14-4-201+ (Child Care Certification Board where applicable). Authority to promulgate rules is granted in W.S. § 14-4-107.
- **DFS Rules (WY Rules & Regs, DFS, chapter set for child care):**
  - **Chapter 2** — Rules for Licensing Child Care Facilities
  - **Chapter 3** — School-Age Child Care (some jurisdictions)
  - **Chapter 5** — Rules for Certification of a **Family Child Care Home (FCCH)**
  - **Chapter 6** — Rules for Certification of a **Family Child Care Center (FCCC)**
  - **Chapter 7** — Rules for Certification of a **Child Care Center (CCC)**

## Facility Types Regulated
1. **Family Child Care Home (FCCH)** — 3-10 children in the operator's residence, no additional staff. Chapter 5.
2. **Family Child Care Center (FCCC)** — up to 15 children, in the home or another building the operator does not reside in; requires 2 caregivers when >10 children. Chapter 6.
3. **Child Care Center (CCC)** — 16+ children in a non-residential setting. Chapter 7.
4. **School-Age Child Care** — care for K-12 children outside school hours.
5. **Family Friend or Neighbor / In-Home** — subsidy-eligible but not certified/licensed.

## Key Licensing Forms
- Certification Application (DFS child care program)
- Background check packet — WDCI fingerprint + FBI + Central Registry + Sex Offender
- Health Certificate (operator / staff / household members)
- Child Health Report & Immunization (WY DOH schedule)
- Fire inspection certificate (State Fire Marshal or local)
- Environmental / sanitation inspection
- Emergency Preparedness Plan
- Policies: discipline, illness exclusion, safe sleep, medication
- Annual Renewal application

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (Child Care Centers — Chapter 7)
| Age | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Birth – 12 months | 1:4 (2:8, 3:10) | 10 |
| 12 – 24 months | 1:5 (2:10, 3:12) | 12 |
| 2 years | 1:8 | 16 |
| 3 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 1:12 | 24 |
| 5 years | 1:15 | 30 |
| School-age (6+) | 1:17 | 34 |

**Family Child Care Home (Chapter 5):** 3-10 children, solo operator, sub-limits on infants (typically no more than 2 under 12 months).

**Family Child Care Center (Chapter 6):** up to 15 children total; a second caregiver required when more than 10 children are present.

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment form with emergency contacts & authorized pickups
- Immunization record per Wyoming DOH schedule (or valid exemption)
- Health history / physical exam
- Allergy and individual health plan where applicable
- Signed consent: transportation, field trips, photos, emergency medical treatment
- Daily attendance
- Incident / injury reports
- Medication administration authorization and log
- Infant safe-sleep acknowledgment (for facilities caring for infants)

## Required Staff Records
- Application + qualifications documentation
- **Background checks** — state + FBI fingerprint, Central Registry, sex offender registry, within-state and any state of residence in prior 5 years (per federal CCDBG + W.S. 14-4-107 as implemented)
- Health certificate (free of communicable disease) & TB screening
- CPR / First Aid (pediatric) certification
- Mandated reporter training
- Pre-service and annual in-service training (minimum 15 hours/year recommended; varies by role per DFS standards tied to the federal floor)
- Professional development tracked through Wyoming's **Wyoming Early Childhood Professional Learning** system / STAR rating participation if opted in

## Inspection Cadence
- **Initial certification inspection** before issuance.
- **Annual inspection** (announced or unannounced) for every certified provider.
- **Unannounced monitoring** may occur at any time.
- **Complaint-triggered inspections** within statutory response windows.

## Renewal Cadence
- Certifications are issued on **an annual basis** and renewed every 12 months with updated fire/health/background documentation.
- Lapses require re-application, not simple reinstatement, once expired.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Unique terminology — "certification" vs "license":** Wyoming uses "certification" for all regulated child care categories. Functionally equivalent to licensing.
- **Three parallel certifications by setting size (Chapters 5 / 6 / 7):** provider's choice of model drives which chapter governs.
- **Smallest state population:** roughly **500-650 certified child care providers statewide**. Cheyenne, Casper, Gillette, Laramie, Rock Springs, Sheridan, and Jackson are the meaningful clusters; vast geographic gaps in between.
- **findchildcarewy.org / childcare.dfs.wyo.gov** is the canonical facility finder with text and map views; includes ages served, hours, capacity, and certification status.
- **Quality Rating Improvement System (QRIS):** Wyoming operates a voluntary STAR rating (1-4) via DFS — participation tied to subsidy enhancement.
- **Group size vs ratio enforcement:** Wyoming enforces both group size caps and ratios separately; a room may be within ratio but out of compliance on group size.
- **Expulsion/suspension policies:** W.S. and DFS rules require written, published expulsion/suspension policies consistent with federal CCDBG requirements.
- **Rural transportation:** several certified providers operate in frontier counties where Wyoming's ratios for multi-age groupings default to the youngest child's age band.
