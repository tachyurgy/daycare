Missouri

Population rank: 19th–20th (~6.2M) Licensed providers: ~2,700 statewide Report date: 2026-04-18

01 Licensing Authority

Missouri Department of Elementary and Secondary Education (DESE) — Office of Childhood (created 2021) — Section for Child Care Regulation (SCCR). Licensing authority was transferred from DHSS to DESE in 2021; citations migrated from 19 CSR 30-62 → 5 CSR 25-500. Consumer directory still lives on DHSS infrastructure at Show Me Child Care Provider Search.

02 Primary Regulatory Citation

  • 5 CSR 25-500 — Licensing Rules for Centers, Group Homes, Family Homes (effective 2022)
  • 5 CSR 25-400 — Definitions & General Provisions
  • 5 CSR 25-600 — Child Care Subsidy
  • Enabling statute: RSMo Chapter 210 (§§ 210.201–.257)
  • Ratios (5 CSR 25-500.112): Cornell LII
  • Historical 19 CSR 30-62 (archive): SOS archive

Missouri is the hardest state in our target set for programmatic violation harvest because the DHSS search app blocks automation. Fastest path: Sunshine Law request (RSMo §§ 610.010–610.035) to Childcare@dese.mo.gov or via DESE Sunshine portal — 3 business-day acknowledgement, copy fees only.

07 Outreach Signals

  • 2021 agency transition pain. Providers are still confused about DHSS → DESE migration and citation updates (19 CSR → 5 CSR). ComplianceKit can frame itself as a "stay-current" answer.
  • CCLIS rollout June 2026 brings new abbreviated inspections — unfamiliar terrain for directors; content-led SEO around "what to expect under Missouri CCLIS" will rank early.
  • Tornado drill requirement (plus quarterly fire/intruder drills) is a multi-event drill tracker wedge.
  • Family Care Safety Registry is MO-unique — staff onboarding workflow should auto-queue FCSR registration.
  • Religious exemption providers (~700 statewide) must still register with DESE; an underserved segment for minimum-standards compliance tooling.
