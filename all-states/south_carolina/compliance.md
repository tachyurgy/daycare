# South Carolina Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority

**South Carolina Department of Social Services (DSS), Division of Early Care and Education** — operating as "SC Child Care Services" (https://www.scchildcare.org). DSS is the sole state licensing agency for child care.

- Main office: 1535 Confederate Ave., Columbia, SC 29201
- Licensing phone: 803-898-2570
- Public provider search (ABC Quality): https://abcquality.org/families/find-a-provider/

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **S.C. Code Ann. Title 63, Chapter 13, Article 3** (Child Care Licensing Law) — § 63-13-20 through § 63-13-1100. The substantive licensing statute governing centers, group homes, and family homes.
- **S.C. Code of Regulations, Chapter 114, Article 5, Subarticle 1** — administrative rules:
  - **R. 114-500 series** — Licensed Child Care Centers (centers serving 13+ children).
  - **R. 114-510 series** — Group Child Care Homes (7-12 children).
  - **R. 114-520 series** — Family Child Care Homes (up to 6 children).
- Background-check authority: **S.C. Code § 63-13-180** (SLED + FBI + Central Registry checks).

## Facility Types Regulated

1. **Licensed Child Care Center** — 13 or more children; commercial, faith-based or school-based.
2. **Group Child Care Home** — 7 to 12 children in the provider's residence (license required).
3. **Family Child Care Home** — up to 6 children in the provider's home; **registration** is mandatory; licensing is optional.
4. **Approved** facilities — public and non-public schools meeting DSS approval.
5. **Church-Operated** centers — may elect "registered" status but are still subject to health & safety statutes.
6. **License-exempt** — certain summer camps, parent drop-off programs, etc.

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (Regulation 114-503 / 114-504 et seq.)

| Age | Staff-to-Child Ratio |
|---|---|
| Birth – 1 year | 1:5 |
| 1 – 2 years | 1:6 |
| 2 – 3 years | 1:8 |
| 3 – 4 years | 1:12 |
| 4 – 5 years | 1:17 |
| 5 – 6 years | 1:20 |
| 6 – 12 years | 1:23 |

Notes:
- Ratios apply **at all times children are present**, including during activities away from the facility.
- Group size is addressed via physical-space requirements (35 sq ft indoors/child; 75 sq ft outdoors) and supervision standards (114-514) rather than hard numeric group caps, except for infant rooms.
- Water activities, transportation, and sleep periods have additional supervision requirements (R. 114-514).

## Required Child Records

- Completed enrollment form (DSS 3086 or provider equivalent)
- Parent/guardian contact and emergency contact information
- Authorization to release child, transport, seek emergency medical care
- Current immunization certificate (SC DHEC Form 2740 or equivalent) — per S.C. Code § 44-29-180
- Medical/health record and allergies
- Individual medication authorization and administration log
- Daily attendance record (arrival/departure)
- Accident/incident reports
- Written withdrawal documentation

## Required Staff Records

- Application, résumé, education verification (per R. 114-504 qualification tiers)
- **SLED state criminal records check + FBI fingerprint + Central Registry (child abuse/neglect)** clearance — required before unsupervised child contact (S.C. Code § 63-13-180)
- Health Assessment (DSS 2926) — required for operator, all caregivers, and household members in home-based settings
- Current CPR and First Aid certification for at least one staff on duty (center must ensure coverage)
- DSS-approved **pre-service** health and safety training documentation
- Bloodborne-pathogens training
- **Annual 15 hours of in-service training** (child growth, safety, program management)
- Orientation / TB screening records
- Personnel file with employment dates, performance reviews

## Inspection Cadence

- DSS licensing staff conduct at minimum **one unannounced annual inspection** per licensed facility (S.C. Code § 63-13-520; Reg. 114-501 et seq.).
- **Complaint-triggered inspections** conducted unannounced upon receipt of any regulatory complaint.
- Re-inspection required following any written plan of correction tied to a cited violation.
- Fire and health inspections done by State Fire Marshal and SC DHEC annually (DSS 2905).

## Renewal Cadence

- License is issued for a **two-year term** for child care centers in good standing (Reg. 114-502); renewal application must be submitted prior to expiration.
- Registration (family / registered-church) is also **renewed every two years**.
- Provisional license may be issued for up to 6 months for new operators not yet meeting full standards.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **ABC Quality** is the state's voluntary QRIS; subsidy (SC Voucher) providers must participate.
- **Mandatory reporter** status applies to all staff (S.C. Code § 63-7-310).
- **SLED + FBI + Central Registry** check is required in advance of any child-contact role (cannot be conditional).
- **Health Assessment (DSS 2926)** must cover the operator, all staff, and, in home-based settings, every household member age 16+.
- **Safe Sleep** — cribs must meet CPSC standards; documented safe-sleep training for infant-room staff.
- **Religious-exempt** centers retain reporter duties and background-check duties even if exempt from full licensing standards; a list of religiously-exempt centers is maintained by DSS.
- **Drop-off** at religiously-exempt programs requires parental notice of exempt status (S.C. Code § 63-13-60).
- **Transportation**: all vehicles must carry attendance logs; staff must complete transportation-specific training.
- **Posting required**: current license, most recent inspection report, emergency evacuation plan, complaint phone number.
- **Expulsion policy** must be written; use of suspension/expulsion restricted per 2019 BRIGHT Act guidance.
