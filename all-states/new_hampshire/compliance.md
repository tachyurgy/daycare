# New Hampshire — Child Care Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority

**New Hampshire Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS), Office of Program Support, Child Care Licensing Unit (CCLU).**

- Address: 129 Pleasant Street, Concord, NH 03301
- Phone: (603) 271-9025 (main) / (603) 271-4624 (licensing)
- Email: CCLUoffice@dhhs.nh.gov

CCLU licenses, monitors, investigates complaints, and enforces compliance for all non-residential child care programs in New Hampshire.

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **NH Code of Administrative Rules He-C 4002** — NH Child Care Program Licensing Rules (the comprehensive child care rule, most recent readoption 2025-08-26; also readopted 2017 with amendments).
- Enabling statute: **RSA 170-E** — "Child Day Care, Residential Care and Child-Placing Agency Licensing."
- Background-check rules at **He-C 4002.12** aligned with **RSA 170-E:7** and federal CCDBG background-check requirements.

## Facility Types Regulated (He-C 4002)

| Program Type | Description | Section |
|---|---|---|
| Family Child Care | 1 provider, up to 6 children (excluding provider's own) | He-C 4002.34 |
| Family Group Child Care | 1 provider + 1 assistant, up to 12 children | He-C 4002.34 |
| Group Child Care Center | Center-based, age- and capacity-specific | He-C 4002.35 |
| Preschool Program | Half-day program for 2.5+ years | He-C 4002.38 |
| Infant and Toddler Program | Center-based, 6 weeks–35 months | He-C 4002.37 |
| School-Age Program | Before/after school, ages 5–12 | He-C 4002.39 |
| Night Care | Care between 9 p.m. and 5 a.m. | He-C 4002.40 |

## Key Licensing Forms

- **Child Care Licensing Application** (DHHS-CCLU)
- **Personnel Information Form** (per staff — used for background check, qualifications)
- **Child's Application for Enrollment / Emergency Information Form**
- **Health Form** for each child (signed by physician; includes immunization record per RSA 141-C:20-a)
- **Criminal Background Check Release** (state, federal fingerprint, child abuse/neglect registry)
- **Fire Inspection Report** (local fire marshal)
- **Building / Zoning approvals** (municipal level)

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes

**Infant and Toddler Program (He-C 4002.37):**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group Size |
|---|---|---|
| 6 weeks–12 months | 1:4 | 12 |
| 13–24 months | 1:5 | 15 |
| 25–35 months | 1:6 | 18 |

Staffing scales with group size (e.g., 9–12 infants requires one lead teacher + two assistants).

**Preschool (typically ages 3–5) and mixed-age groups:** ratios published in He-C 4002.35 / .38; for preschool (3 years) 1:8, (4-5 years) 1:12 are typical ceilings. Ratios for mixed-age groups use the **average age** of children present (not youngest, as is common in other states).

**Family Child Care (He-C 4002.34):**
- 1 provider, up to 6 children; no more than 3 under age 3; no more than 2 under 18 months.
- Family Group: 1 provider + 1 assistant, up to 12 children.

A second staff person must be in the building whenever 5 or more children are present.

## Required Child Records

- Enrollment application with parent/guardian contact, emergency contacts, authorized pickups
- Child's health form & physical (annual physical for children under 2; every 2 years ages 2–6)
- Immunization record per RSA 141-C:20-a (with religious/medical exemption documentation if applicable)
- Emergency medical consent
- Allergy / medical condition list
- Daily attendance log
- Incident/accident reports
- Transportation / field trip permissions
- For infants: feeding schedule, sleep patterns, developmental milestones (He-C 4002.37)

## Required Staff Records

- Personnel Information Form (name, DOB, qualifications)
- Criminal background check results (state, FBI, child abuse registry) per RSA 170-E:7
- Two references on file
- Health screening statement (physician-signed)
- Evidence of qualifications (degree/CDA/credit hours) per role
- Current CPR and First Aid certification
- Annual in-service training log (minimum 18 hours/year for center-based staff, varies by role)
- Orientation and mandated-reporter training
- Evaluation / supervision records

## Inspection Cadence

- **Pre-license inspection** by CCLU before issuance
- **At least one on-site monitoring visit per license cycle** (cycle = 3 years); unannounced
- **Follow-up / complaint investigations** as warranted (unannounced)
- **Fire marshal** inspection every 2 years (per local jurisdiction)
- **Food service** inspection if meals are prepared (NH DHHS Food Protection Section)

## Renewal Cadence

Licenses are issued for **three (3) years** (per He-C 4002.02). Renewal applications must be submitted before expiration; the department issues a new 3-year license upon satisfactory inspection. There is also a **"permit"** category for short-term or conditional operations.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Three-year license cycle** is unusually long among U.S. states (most are 1–2 years).
- **"Critical Rules and Corrective Action Plans"** — He-C 4002 enumerates specific rules deemed "critical," where any violation automatically triggers a Corrective Action Plan with firm deadlines.
- **Mixed-age ratios use the average age** rather than youngest — this gives NH centers more flexibility than most states but requires careful documentation.
- **Administrative fines** are explicitly authorized (He-C 4002) — CCLU can issue monetary penalties without revoking the license.
- **NH Child Care Search (the DHHS consumer-facing portal)** publishes licensing history including inspection violations and complaint outcomes — providers should assume any critical-rule violation will be visible to parents.
- **Background checks** must be completed every 5 years and include state, FBI, child abuse registry, and any state where the staff member lived in the prior 5 years.
