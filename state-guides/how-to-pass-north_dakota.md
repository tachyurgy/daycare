# How to Pass Your North Dakota Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In North Dakota, your licensor is an **Early Childhood Licensing Unit specialist** inside **ND DHHS Early Childhood Services** (the unit moved from DHS to DHHS in the 2022 reorganization). You'll get **at least two monitoring visits per year** — one **announced/scheduled** and one **unannounced drop-in** — plus complaint-triggered visits. Fire Marshal and local public health must sign off. Everything flows through the **Child Care Licensing (CCL) System** portal that replaced the prior paper process in 2023.

- DHHS Child Care Licensing: https://www.hhs.nd.gov/cfs/early-childhood-services/child-care-licensing
- CCL System (provider portal): https://www.hhs.nd.gov/cfs/early-childhood-services/providers/child-care-licensing-system

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **Statute:** **NDCC Chapter 50-11.1** — Early Childhood Services (licensing authority); **NDCC 50-11.3** (background checks); **NDCC 23-07** (immunization).
- **Regulations:** **NDAC Article 75-03**. Key chapters:
  - **75-03-08** — Family Child Care (up to 7 children in provider's home).
  - **75-03-09** — Group Child Care (up to 18 children).
  - **75-03-10** — Child Care Center (19+ children). Ratio rule: **75-03-10-08**.
  - **75-03-11** — School-Age Child Care.
  - **75-03-12** — Self-Declaration (5 or fewer children, optional).
  - **75-03-14** — Pre-School.
- **Public records:** NDCC Chapter 44-04 (Open Meetings and Records). CPS investigations exempt under NDCC 50-25.1-11.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
**Child Care Centers — NDAC 75-03-10-08:**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (0 – under 12 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| 12 – 23 months | 1:5 | 10 |
| 24 – 35 months | 1:7 | 14 |
| 3 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 1:10 | 25 |
| 5 years (not in K) | 1:12 | 30 |
| School-age (6 – 12) | 1:20 | 40 |

**Group Child Care (75-03-09):** max 18 children; adjusted ratios by age.
**Family Child Care (75-03-08):** max 7 children including provider's own, with sub-limits on infants.

**Warning:** Mixed-age rooms default to the **youngest** child's ratio and group-size cap.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives (one visit per year announced; one unannounced drop-in per year; complaints anytime).
2. Confirms current license posted.
3. Walks rooms; counts children and staff; checks NDAC 75-03-10-08 ratios.
4. Pulls staff files — state BCI + FBI fingerprint per NDCC 50-11.3, Child Protective Services record check, sex offender registry, references, CPR/First Aid/AED, mandated-reporter training, **24 hrs/year** continuing ed (center teachers) or **9 hrs/year** (family/group), TB screening.
5. Pulls child files — enrollment, immunization per NDCC 23-07, physical exam within 90 days of admission (centers), emergency consents.
6. Reviews fire drill log, medication log, incident reports.
7. Issues a **correction order** if applicable — posted on the public search for 3 years.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Background-check gaps** — NDCC 50-11.3 requires state BCI + FBI fingerprint for every person with unsupervised access, **including adult household members** in home-based care. This is easy to miss on a new spouse or roommate.
2. **Ratio / group-size violations** — especially when the youngest-child rule flips a mixed room.
3. **Training shortfalls** — 24 hrs/year for center staff is above the federal floor; family/group providers need 9 hrs.
4. **Missing physical exam** — centers require physical within 90 days of admission per NDAC 75-03-10.
5. **Expired Fire Marshal or local public health inspection**.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment form (parent/guardian contact, authorized pickups, emergency contacts).
- **Immunization record** per NDCC 23-07 and NDDOH schedule.
- **Physician / clinic physical exam within 90 days of admission** (center-based).
- Individual care / special needs plan where applicable.
- Signed consents: transportation, photos, field trips, emergency medical.
- Daily attendance log.
- Incident / injury / illness reports.
- Medication logs.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application / resume with education & training history.
- **Criminal history record check** (state BCI + FBI fingerprint) per NDCC 50-11.3 — required pre-hire.
- **Child Protective Services record check**.
- Sex offender registry verification.
- Reference checks.
- **Current CPR / First Aid / AED** certification.
- Mandated reporter training.
- **24 hours annual continuing education** (center teachers); **9 hours** (family/group).
- TB screening / general health statement.
- Documentation of qualifications for director and lead teacher roles.

## What must be on your walls
- Current DHHS license.
- Most recent Fire Marshal inspection.
- Most recent local public health / sanitation inspection.
- Evacuation plan / emergency procedures.
- Tornado shelter plan.
- Daily schedule.
- Menu.
- DHHS complaint contact.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented.
- **Tornado / severe weather drills** — documented (ND is a tornado and winter-storm state).
- **Daily attendance** by room.
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident / injury reports**.
- **Emergency preparedness plan** on file.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff per room against NDAC 75-03-10-08 ratios and max group sizes.
2. Post current license + latest Fire Marshal and public-health inspections.
3. Pull a random staff file: state BCI, FBI, CPS, sex offender current? 24-hour CE log on track?
4. Same check for any adult household member in a home-based setting.
5. Pull a random child file: immunization per NDCC 23-07? Physical within 90 days of admission?
6. Medication locked; log current.
7. Diaper-change stations clean; gloves stocked.
8. Tornado shelter plan posted; last drill logged.
9. Playground fenced; no hazards.
10. Fire-drill log — last drill within required interval.

## What happens if you fail
DHHS can issue a **correction order** with a deadline; repeated or serious findings can lead to a **conditional license**, **suspension**, or **revocation** under NDCC 50-11.1. Correction orders from the past 3 years are **public** via the DHHS online child care search. Appeal rights under the ND Administrative Agencies Practice Act. For bulk records, a public-records request under NDCC 44-04 goes to the Early Childhood Licensing Unit, 600 E. Boulevard Avenue, Dept. 325, Bismarck, ND 58505-0250.

## The weird North Dakota gotcha nobody tells you about
**ND inspects twice a year, not once — one scheduled visit and one unannounced drop-in.** Owners new to ND or coming from a 1-visit state are caught flat-footed by the drop-in. Second gotcha: the **Self-Declaration tier** (NDAC 75-03-12) lets a micro-provider skip full licensing for 5 or fewer children, but **background checks under NDCC 50-11.3 still apply** to every adult in the home — many self-declared providers think they're fully exempt and get referred for enforcement when a Protective Services complaint shows a household member without a background check. Third gotcha: **physical exam required within 90 days of admission** for center enrollment — pediatricians in Fargo and Bismarck book out well beyond that.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a North Dakota owner
Their fear is the unannounced drop-in visit — the second of two yearly — landing when training logs are behind. The 30-second number: 24 annual continuing-ed hours per center staff member, 9 per family/group provider, and background checks on every adult including household members. ND owners today use the new CCL System portal for applications but still track training and background expirations in paper. Lead with: "Automatic training-hour tracking that shows the DHHS drop-in inspector a live 24-hour log for every staff member — and background-check alerts when a household member turns 18 or moves in." Second hook: the Self-Declaration tier is a trap for tiny operators — background checks still apply.
