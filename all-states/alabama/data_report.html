6.Violations & Inspection Data

Weakest transparency of any state in this batch. DHR's directory returns only name + address + phone + license status. Deficiency reports are posted at the facility, not published online. Compliance history requires a formal Alabama Open Records Act request (Ala. Code § 36-12-40).

Full details: violations.md

  • FOIA turnaround 30-60 business days; electronic CSV extracts supported.
  • 2018 Child Care Safety Act (Act 2018-390) closed the broad church-exempt loophole for facilities receiving federal/state funds — broader licensed population than pre-2018.
  • Enforcement-action press releases are the main public transparency mechanism in absence of online lookup.

7.Outreach Signals

Regulatory pain points

  • Annual license renewal with clean fire + health inspections required.
  • Corporal punishment prohibited; discipline policy must be written + signed by parents.
  • 12 annual in-service training hours per staff.
  • Child-check ("left behind") protocols + annual vehicle inspection for transportation.
  • ABI + FBI fingerprint + CA/N clearance required before unsupervised child contact.

Market opportunity

  • No public compliance portal = facility directors have no visibility into their own record. ComplianceKit can fill this gap.
  • Act 2018-390 brought hundreds of previously-exempt church centers into the licensed universe — net-new market.
  • Alabama Quality STARS participation drives CCDBG bonus payments — SaaS can tie directly to star-level tracking.
  • Biggest metros: Birmingham, Huntsville, Montgomery, Mobile, Tuscaloosa — already in the leads CSV.
