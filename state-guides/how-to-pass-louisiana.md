# How to Pass Your Louisiana Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Louisiana Department of Education (LDE), Division of Early Childhood — Licensing Section**. Licensing moved from DCFS to LDE on **October 1, 2014**, so older web results that send you to DCFS are out of date. You get **at least one unannounced inspection per year**, with intervals not to exceed 12 months (LAC 28:CLXI, Chapter 7). Complaints trigger unannounced visits anytime. Annual Fire Marshal and Louisiana Department of Health (LDH) sanitation inspections run in parallel. Every inspection — with all 43 Bulletin 137 regulations flagged In Compliance or Not — posts publicly on the Louisiana School & Center Finder, with **no rolling drop-off window**. Findings stay visible indefinitely. Public sites:
- LDE Licensing: https://doe.louisiana.gov/early-childhood/child-care-facility-licensing
- Louisiana School & Center Finder (public search + inspections): https://louisianaschools.com/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — La. R.S. 17:407.31 – 17:407.53 (Early Learning Center Licensing Act).
- **The rulebook inspectors use** — **Bulletin 137** (Louisiana Early Learning Center Licensing Regulations), codified at LAC 28:CLXI.101 et seq.
  - Full rule PDF (2024 ACF 508): https://licensingregulations.acf.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/licensing_regulation/LA_CENTER_JUL_2024_508.pdf
- **Bulletin 140** — performance / academic standards for **Type III** centers (subsidy-receiving centers).
- **Bulletin 139** — CCDF subsidy program standards.
- **Ratio rule** — LAC 28:CLXI.1711. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/louisiana/La-Admin-Code-tit-28-SS-CLXI-1711
- **License-type rule** — LAC 28:CLXI.307. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/louisiana/La-Admin-Code-tit-28-SS-CLXI-307

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From LAC 28:CLXI.1711. Mixed-age groups follow the youngest child's ratio. Tightened ratios apply during **water activities** and **field trips**. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age Group | Children per staff | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (< 1 year) | 5 | 15 |
| 1 year | 7 | 21 |
| 2 years | 10 | 20 |
| 3 years | 13 | 26 |
| 4 years | 15 | 30 |
| 5 years | 19 | 38 |
| 6 years and up | 23 | 46 |

Only staff members **directly providing care, supervision, or guidance to children** count in ratios.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced.
2. Checks license is posted, license type matches (Type I, II, or III).
3. Walks every room, counts children, compares to staff present.
4. Pulls staff files — **CCCBC (Child Care Criminal Background Check)** clearance first.
5. Pulls child files — immunizations, physical, allergy plan, attendance.
6. Reviews Orientation Training completion (within 7 days of hire) and LDE Key Orientation Modules 2 and 3 (within 30 days).
7. Reviews emergency plan (includes hurricane evacuation), incident reports, drill logs.
8. Runs through the **43 Bulletin 137 regulations**, flagging each In Compliance or Not.
9. Confirms current Fire Marshal + LDH sanitation inspections are on file.
10. Exit conference. The inspection posts on louisianaschools.com/schools/<your code>/Inspections — typically within 2–3 weeks — with every non-compliance rule cite, narrative, and corrective-action due date.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
Per LDE Bulletin 137 "Hot Topics" and Legislative Auditor data:
1. **CCCBC gaps** — one of the most comprehensive in the U.S. (state + FBI fingerprint + sex offender + state child abuse registry + out-of-state registries for recent residents). Any miss = cite.
2. **Out-of-ratio** (LAC 28:CLXI.1711) — especially during staff breaks and water activities.
3. **Orientation training** not done on time — 15 topics within 7 days of first day, LDE Key Modules 2 & 3 within 30 days.
4. **Critical incident reports not submitted within 24 hours**.
5. **Type III centers** failing Bulletin 140 academic approval / performance-profile standards.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment / application** with contact info and emergency contacts.
- **Signed parent policy acknowledgments** (discipline, release, health).
- **Current immunization record** per LAC 51:II (LDH Form USPHS PHS 731 / IMM-1).
- **Physical health exam / physician's statement**.
- **Allergy / special-needs info**.
- **Authorizations** — emergency medical care, transportation, field trips.
- **Medication consent + log**.
- **Daily attendance** (sign-in/sign-out).
- **Incident / critical-incident reports**.
- **Written enrollment contract** — tuition, policies, withdrawal process.
- Records kept **minimum 2 years after disenrollment**.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **CCCBC clearance** — state + FBI fingerprint + National Sex Offender Registry + state child abuse/neglect registry + out-of-state registry checks for applicants who lived outside LA in the last 5 years. Required **before** unsupervised child contact; refreshed every 5 years.
- **Application, resume, qualifications** per Bulletin 137.
- **Personal history statement, emergency contact**.
- **Current pediatric First Aid + CPR**.
- **Orientation training** — within 7 calendar days of first day, covering 15 topic areas (supervision, discipline, SIDS, shaken baby, food safety, allergies, CPR/first aid, medication, mandated reporter, emergencies, etc.).
- **LDE Key Orientation Training Modules 2 and 3** — within 30 calendar days of first day.
- **Annual training** — minimum **12 clock hours**; more for directors / lead teachers.
- **Mandated reporter acknowledgment** (La. R.S. 14:403).
- **TB screen** as applicable.
- **Employment dates, evaluations, discipline records**.

## What must be on your walls
- Current LDE Early Learning Center license (Type I, II, or III).
- Most recent inspection summary / Bulletin 137 results.
- Emergency Plan (evacuation, shelter-in-place, lockdown, reunification — mandated for Gulf Coast hurricane preparedness).
- Menus.
- Parent complaint contact (ldelicensing@la.gov / 225-342-9905).
- Safe Sleep policy in infant rooms.
- Written abusive-head-trauma / shaken-baby prevention policy.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** — per emergency plan.
- **Hurricane / severe-weather evacuation drill** — per plan (mandatory for LA Gulf Coast).
- **Shelter-in-place and lockdown drill** — per plan.
- **Daily attendance**.
- **Medication log**.
- **Critical Incident Report** — submitted to LDE **within 24 hours** of incident.
- **Annual Fire Marshal inspection** on file.
- **Annual LDH sanitation inspection** on file.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff. Ratios tighten during water play and field trips.
2. Pull 3 random staff files — CCCBC clearance, Orientation Training (within 7 days), LDE Key Modules 2 & 3 (within 30 days), 12-hour annual training log, CPR card.
3. Pull 3 random child files — current LDH immunization, enrollment contract, allergy plan.
4. Infant rooms: safe sleep, shaken-baby prevention policy posted.
5. License (Type I / II / III) posted and correct?
6. Emergency plan includes hurricane evacuation + reunification?
7. Current Fire Marshal inspection on file? Current LDH sanitation inspection on file?
8. Pull up your louisianaschools.com/schools/<your code>/Inspections page — any Not In Compliance finding with an unresolved corrective-action date?
9. Any critical incident in the last 24 hours that still needs reporting to LDE?
10. If Type III, Bulletin 140 academic approval and performance-profile materials on hand?

## What happens if you fail
LDE issues noncompliance findings under specific LAC 28:CLXI.xxxx cites. A corrective-action due date is set for each. Escalation includes probation, suspension, revocation — which LDE and DCFS publish as press releases (see DCFS news archive). Type III centers can lose academic approval and thus CCDF subsidy dollars, which is often more financially damaging than the license action itself. Public inspection pages retain every finding indefinitely — there is no drop-off window like South Carolina's 36-month or Wisconsin's 3-year.

## The weird Louisiana gotcha nobody tells you about
Louisiana has **three license types with completely different rules on top of the same base**: **Type I** (church-affiliated, no state/federal funds — automatic revocation if they take state money), **Type II** (no state or federal funds, or food-only USDA funds), and **Type III** (receives state or federal funds — must also meet Bulletin 140's academic and performance-profile standards). Type III is where the real money and real scrutiny are. Picking the wrong type or accepting the wrong funds can **automatically revoke** your license (LAC 28:CLXI.307). Two more quirks: Louisiana publishes inspection history with **no drop-off window** — findings stay visible on your louisianaschools.com/schools/<code>/Inspections page forever. And the **CCCBC** may be the most thorough background check regime in the country — it pulls the five registries listed above and does out-of-state checks for recent movers; compared to your Alabama or Mississippi neighbor, you're running the gauntlet.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Louisiana owner
Biggest fear: a posted Not-In-Compliance on louisianaschools.com that never drops off (no rolling window), and — for Type III centers — losing academic approval, which cuts CCDF subsidy revenue directly. Drop this in 30 seconds: "LDE inspectors hit 43 Bulletin 137 regulations per visit, your Orientation Training is 15 topics within 7 days and LDE Key Modules 2 and 3 within 30, Critical Incidents report within 24 hours, and CCCBC is five registries. If you're Type III, Bulletin 140 sets your academic approval. Are you tracking CCCBC refresh, orientation timing, and critical-incident SLAs in one system?" Today they're using a mix of the CCCBC portal, paper Bulletin 137 forms, and a binder for critical incidents. The close: "We make every Bulletin 137 regulation a field in your staff and child files — so when inspection day comes, all 43 are green."
