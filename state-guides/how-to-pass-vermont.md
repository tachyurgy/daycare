# How to Pass Your Vermont Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In Vermont, your licensor is a **Child Development Division (CDD) specialist** inside the **Department for Children and Families (DCF)**. You'll get a **scheduled annual monitoring visit** plus **unannounced monitoring** at any time (CDD can visit any operating hour) and **complaint investigations** within statutory windows (usually 14 – 30 days). Fire Marshal and health approvals are required. Everything runs through the **Bright Futures Information System (BFIS)** portal.

- DCF Child Development Division: https://dcf.vermont.gov/cdd
- BFIS (public portal): https://www.brightfutures.dcf.state.vt.us
- Find Child Care: https://dcf.vermont.gov/cdd/families/find-care

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **Statute:** **33 V.S.A. Chapter 35** (Child Care Services), especially **§3502** (CDD licensing authority); **§3511** (Registered Family Child Care Homes); **§4913** (mandated reporter).
- **Regulations (Code of Vermont Rules):**
  - **CVR 13-171-004** — Licensing Regulations for **Center Based Child Care and Preschool Programs (CBCCPP)**.
  - **CVR 13-171-005** — Licensing Regulations for **Registered and Licensed Family Child Care Homes**.
  - **CVR 13-171-006** — After-School Programs.
  - **CVR 13-162-009** — Non-Recurring Clientele (drop-in care).
- **Public records:** 1 V.S.A. §§ 315 – 320.

Center rule PDF: https://outside.vermont.gov/dept/DCF/Shared%20Documents/CDD/Licensing/CC-CenterBased-Regs.pdf

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
**CBCCPP — CVR 13-171-004:**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (6 wk – 12 mo) | **1:4** | 8 |
| Toddler (12 – 24 mo) | **1:4** | 8 |
| Young Toddler (24 – 36 mo) | 1:5 | 10 |
| Preschool (3 yr) | 1:7 | 14 |
| Preschool (4 – 5 yr, not in K) | 1:10 | 20 |
| School-age (K – 12 yr) | 1:13 | 26 |

**Registered Family Child Care Home (CVR 13-171-005):** up to 6 full-time and 4 part-time school-age; no more than 2 under 24 months; no more than 6 under kindergarten age (including your own).

**Warning:** Vermont's **1:4 applies to infants AND toddlers up to 24 months** — tighter than nearly every other state. Only staff working directly with children **≥90% of their duty time** count toward ratio — directors and admins who "also float" do not count.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives (scheduled annual or unannounced).
2. Confirms current license and STARS level posted.
3. Walks rooms; counts kids and staff who meet the **90% direct-care rule**; checks ratios.
4. Pulls staff files — **Background Record Check (BRC)** from Vermont Crime Information Center + FBI fingerprint + Child Protection Registry + Sex Offender Registry + Adult Protective Services; health statement; TB; CPR/First Aid; orientation within 30 days; **Northern Lights** training hours (15 – 24 hrs/year for center teachers).
5. Pulls child files — enrollment, immunization per VT DOH schedule, health history & physical (centers), emergency consents.
6. Reviews emergency preparedness plan, medication log, incident reports.
7. Writes violation with corrective action + deadline; posts to BFIS (public portal) within weeks.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
(Backed by the 2026 VT State Auditor report and VTDigger coverage: 11 of 40 "standard" citations should have been "serious" — VT is tightening enforcement in 2026.)
1. **Ratio violations at the 12 – 24-month band** — VT's 1:4 toddler rule is stricter than NH, NY, or MA.
2. **Staff failing the 90% direct-care rule** — a director who counts toward ratio while handling admin is not actually in ratio.
3. **Background Record Check gaps** — VCIC + FBI + Child Protection + Sex Offender + Adult Protective Services is FIVE separate checks; missing any one is a citation.
4. **Training logged in the wrong place** — hours must be in the **Northern Lights Career Development Center registry**, not in an internal spreadsheet.
5. **Severity misclassification** — post-2026 audit, CDD is reclassifying "standard" citations as "serious" more often.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment form with emergency contacts, authorized pickups.
- **Current immunization record** per Vermont DOH schedule (or signed exemption).
- **Health history & physical** (center-based enrollment).
- Individual health / allergy / special-needs plan where applicable.
- Consents: transportation, photos, field trips, emergency treatment.
- Daily attendance.
- Incident reports.
- Medication authorization & log.
- STARS documentation where participating.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application, resume, qualifications.
- **Background Record Check (BRC)** — VCIC + FBI fingerprint + Child Protection Registry + Sex Offender Registry + Adult Protective Services. Required pre-hire; **re-run every 5 years**.
- Health statement & TB screening.
- **Current CPR / First Aid**.
- **Orientation within 30 days** of hire.
- **Annual training hours tracked in Northern Lights Career Development Center registry** (15 – 24 hrs/year for center teachers).
- **Mandated reporter training** (33 V.S.A. §4913).
- Qualification documentation for director, program leader, teacher.

## What must be on your walls
- Current DCF/CDD license.
- STARS (Step Ahead Recognition System) rating.
- Most recent Fire Marshal inspection.
- Most recent health inspection.
- Evacuation plan / emergency procedures.
- Daily schedule.
- Menu.
- CDD complaint contact.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented per CVR 13-171-004.
- **Severe weather / shelter drills** — documented.
- **Daily attendance** by room.
- **Medication administration log**.
- **Incident / injury reports**.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff per room — enforce 1:4 at infants AND toddlers to 24 months.
2. Confirm every ratio-counted staff is at 90%+ direct-care time.
3. Post current license + STARS + fire + health inspections.
4. Pull a random staff file: all 5 BRC components (VCIC, FBI, CPR-child-protection, Sex Offender, APS) current?
5. Northern Lights registry — are training hours logged there, not a spreadsheet?
6. Pull a random child file: immunization current? Health history and physical on file?
7. Medication locked; log current.
8. Orientation timestamps — every hire oriented within 30 days?
9. Fire-drill log — last drill within required interval.
10. Any recent incident — classified correctly (standard vs. serious) per post-2026 audit guidance?

## What happens if you fail
CDD can issue a violation with a required corrective action and deadline. Severity is classified **"standard"** or **"serious"** — and the **2026 State Auditor report** showed 11 of 40 "standard" citations should have been "serious". Expect more citations to flip "serious" in 2026. Repeated/serious findings escalate to **conditional license**, **suspension**, **revocation**, or **surrender**. Violations appear on BFIS for a **minimum of 5 years**. Appeal rights under VT administrative procedure. Records requests under 1 V.S.A. §§315 – 320 (acknowledgment within 2 business days).

## The weird Vermont gotcha nobody tells you about
**Vermont has the tightest infant/toddler ratios nationally: 1:4 all the way to 24 months, with a hard group-size cap of 8.** Most states relax to 1:5 or 1:6 at 18 months — VT does not. Combined with the **90% direct-care rule**, it means a small center often needs 2 dedicated infant/toddler teachers plus a float, and "the director can cover lunch" is a ratio violation. Second gotcha: after the March 2026 State Auditor report, CDD is under pressure and reclassifying "standard" citations as "serious" — violations that were minor paperwork citations last year may now be serious enforcement events.
