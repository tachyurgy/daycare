# How to Pass Your South Dakota Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In South Dakota, your licensor is a **DSS Division of Child Care Services specialist** — licensing is administered through the **Office of Licensing and Accreditation (OLA)**. You'll get an **initial licensing inspection**, an **annual monitoring inspection** (at least one announced visit per year), and **unannounced monitoring** at any time. All inspection results, corrective action plans, and stipulation / consent agreements are **published on the OLA Constituent Portal** for parents to see.

- DSS Child Care Licensing: https://dss.sd.gov/childcare/licensing/
- OLA Provider Search (public): https://olapublic.sd.gov/child-care-provider-search/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **Statute:** SDCL Title 26, Chapter 26-6 (Child Welfare; Foster Homes, Shelters, and Child Care); **SDCL 26-6-14.4** (background checks); **SDCL 13-28-7.1** (immunization).
- **Regulations:** **ARSD Chapter 67:42** — Child Care Services. Key articles:
  - 67:42:01 — General provisions.
  - 67:42:10 — Registered Family Day Care.
  - 67:42:15 — Before and After School Programs.
  - **67:42:17** — Licensed Child Care Centers and School-Age Programs (ratios, supervision, records).
- **Public records:** SDCL Chapter 1-27 (Open Records).

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
**Child Care Centers — ARSD 67:42:17:18 and 67:42:17:19:**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (under 12 mo) | 1:5 | 10 |
| Toddler (12 – 23 mo) | 1:5 | 10 |
| 2 years | 1:5 | 10 |
| 3 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 5 years | 1:15 | 30 |
| School-age (6+) | 1:20 | 40 |

**Registered Family Day Care (ARSD 67:42:17:21):** max 12 children including provider's own; no more than 4 under 24 months; no more than 2 under 12 months.

**Warning:** Ratios are looser than many states (SD allows 1:5 infants, where most states require 1:4) — but **group size caps are strict** and a mixed-age room is governed by the **youngest** child.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives (annual announced visit scheduled; unannounced monitoring can happen anytime).
2. Confirms current license posted.
3. Walks rooms; counts children and staff; checks ARSD 67:42:17 ratios and group sizes.
4. Pulls staff files — Central Registry check (SD Child Abuse & Neglect), state + FBI fingerprint per **SDCL 26-6-14.4**, sex offender check, references, CPR/First Aid, mandated-reporter training, 20-hour annual in-service log, TB screening.
5. Pulls child files — enrollment, immunization per SDCL 13-28-7.1, medical / health statement, emergency consents, daily attendance.
6. Reviews fire drill log, medication log, incident reports, emergency plan.
7. Writes deficiencies; corrective-action plan uploaded to the OLA Constituent Portal.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Background-check gaps** — SDCL 26-6-14.4 requires state + FBI fingerprint AND Central Registry for every adult in the setting (employees, substitutes, volunteers, adult household members) **before unsupervised access**. Substitutes and volunteers are the most common miss.
2. **Ratio / group-size violations** — SD's ratios are liberal, but **group size caps** (10 for infants/toddlers/2s, 20 for 3s/4s) bite at enrollment growth.
3. **Missing or expired immunization record** — per SDCL 13-28-7.1 and the SDDOH schedule.
4. **Training log shortfalls** — 20 hrs/year is the operating expectation for center staff.
5. **Central Registry miss** — easy to overlook alongside the fingerprint check.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment application** with emergency contacts and authorized pickups.
- **Current immunization record** meeting SDCL 13-28-7.1 and SDDOH schedule (or a signed religious/medical exemption affidavit).
- **Medical / health statement**.
- Individual child care plan (for children with special needs or allergies).
- Written consents: transportation, field trips, photography.
- Daily attendance.
- Incident / accident reports.
- Medication administration logs (when applicable).

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application and resume with education / experience.
- **Central Registry** check (SD Child Abuse & Neglect).
- **State + FBI fingerprint-based criminal background check** (SDCL 26-6-14.4).
- Sex offender registry check.
- Written reference checks.
- **Current pediatric CPR and First Aid**.
- Mandated reporter training acknowledgment.
- **20 hours/year** in-service training log.
- TB screening / health statement.

## What must be on your walls
- Current DSS / OLA license.
- Most recent Fire Marshal inspection.
- Evacuation plan / emergency procedures.
- Daily schedule.
- Menu.
- DSS complaint contact.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented.
- **Severe weather / tornado drills** — documented (South Dakota is a tornado state).
- **Daily attendance** by room.
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident / accident reports**.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff per room — watch group size caps (10 for infants/toddlers/2s, 20 for 3s/4s).
2. Post current license + fire marshal + sanitarian inspection.
3. Pull a random staff file — Central Registry, state + FBI, sex offender all current? CPR/First Aid current?
4. Same check for substitutes and volunteers in the building today.
5. Pull a random child file: immunization per SDCL 13-28-7.1? Medical statement on file?
6. Medication locked; log current.
7. Diaper-change stations clean; gloves stocked.
8. Tornado shelter plan posted; last drill logged.
9. Playground — fence intact; no standing water.
10. Attendance — live, accurate, by room.

## What happens if you fail
OLA can issue a non-compliance finding, require a corrective action plan, or escalate to a **stipulation / consent agreement**, **provisional license**, **suspension**, or **revocation**. All findings and CAPs are **posted on the OLA Constituent Portal for 3+ years** and visible to every parent. South Dakota doesn't have a general-purpose FOIA statute, but SDCL 1-27 makes records presumptively open; child abuse investigation records are exempt under SDCL 26-8A-13. Appeals go through DSS administrative hearings and ultimately state court.

## The weird South Dakota gotcha nobody tells you about
**"Registration" vs. "Licensing" is a trap.** Family day cares are **registered** (ARSD 67:42:10), not licensed — but the Central Registry, state + FBI fingerprint, and sex offender checks under SDCL 26-6-14.4 still apply to every adult in the home. Owners treat "registered" as a lighter category and skip the full background package. OLA does not. Second gotcha: SD **group size caps** (10 for infants/toddlers/2s, 20 for 3s/4s, 30 for 5s, 40 for school-age) trip owners at enrollment growth — you can be inside your ratio and still out of compliance on the group size.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a South Dakota owner
Their fear is the OLA Constituent Portal — every inspection, CAP, and stipulation agreement sits next to their name where parents can read it. The 30-second number: SDCL 26-6-14.4 requires Central Registry + state + FBI fingerprint + sex-offender check for **every** adult in the building — employees, substitutes, volunteers, household members — before any unsupervised access. SD owners today manage this on a spreadsheet and forget substitutes. Lead with: "One place where every adult in your building — employees, subs, volunteers, household — has current Central Registry, FBI, and sex-offender clearances, and expirations alert you 60 days out." SD is a small market (~700 – 750 providers), so one strong reference in Sioux Falls or Rapid City goes far.
