# How to Pass Your Wyoming Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In Wyoming, your regulator is the **Department of Family Services (DFS), Division of Early Care and Education, Child Care Licensing Program**. Important terminology quirk: Wyoming calls it **certification**, not licensing — but functionally it's the same thing. You'll get an **initial certification inspection**, an **annual inspection** (may be announced or unannounced), **unannounced monitoring** at any time, and **complaint-triggered inspections**. Fire Marshal and environmental health must sign off.

- WY DFS Child Care: https://dfs.wyo.gov/providers/child-care-2/
- Find Child Care WY (public finder): https://childcare.dfs.wyo.gov/home/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **Statute:** **W.S. § 14-4-101 through § 14-4-116** (Child Care Facilities); **§14-4-107** grants rule-making authority.
- **DFS Rules (child-care chapters):**
  - **Chapter 2** — Rules for Licensing Child Care Facilities.
  - **Chapter 5** — Rules for Certification of a **Family Child Care Home (FCCH)**.
  - **Chapter 6** — Rules for Certification of a **Family Child Care Center (FCCC)**.
  - **Chapter 7** — Rules for Certification of a **Child Care Center (CCC)**.
- **Public records:** W.S. § 16-4-201 through § 16-4-205 (Wyoming Public Records Act).

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
**Child Care Centers — DFS Chapter 7:**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Birth – 12 months | 1:4 (2:8, 3:10) | 10 |
| 12 – 24 months | 1:5 (2:10, 3:12) | 12 |
| 2 years | 1:8 | 16 |
| 3 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 1:12 | 24 |
| 5 years | 1:15 | 30 |
| School-age (6+) | 1:17 | 34 |

**Family Child Care Home (Ch. 5):** 3 – 10 kids, solo operator, sub-limit of 2 under 12 months.
**Family Child Care Center (Ch. 6):** up to 15 kids; second caregiver required when >10 kids.

**Warning:** Wyoming **enforces ratio AND group size separately**. A room can be within ratio but still over group size — a common citation.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives (initial by appointment; annual announced or unannounced; complaint anytime).
2. Confirms current certification posted.
3. Walks rooms; counts kids and staff; checks Chapter 7 (or 5/6) ratios AND group sizes.
4. Pulls staff files — **WDCI fingerprint + FBI + Central Registry + Sex Offender registry** per W.S. 14-4-107; health certificate; TB; pediatric CPR/First Aid; mandated-reporter training; ~15-hour/year in-service log.
5. Pulls child files — enrollment, immunization per WY DOH schedule, physical, emergency consents, safe-sleep acknowledgment (infants).
6. Reviews fire drill log, medication log, incident reports, emergency plan.
7. Writes findings; substantiated complaints are **publicly posted on Find Child Care WY** from 2019 onward.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Group-size violations with ratio still met** — WY enforces both; a 1:4 infant room with 11 infants is still cited for group size.
2. **Background-check gaps** — WDCI fingerprint + FBI + Central Registry + sex offender, including any state of residence in the prior 5 years per federal CCDBG.
3. **Missing safe-sleep acknowledgment** — required for facilities caring for infants per DFS Chapter 7.
4. **Missing fire or environmental inspection** at annual renewal.
5. **Expired CPR / First Aid** — pediatric certification must be current.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment form with emergency contacts and authorized pickups.
- **Immunization record** per Wyoming DOH schedule (or valid exemption).
- **Health history / physical exam**.
- Allergy and individual health plan where applicable.
- Signed consents: transportation, field trips, photos, emergency medical treatment.
- Daily attendance.
- Incident / injury reports.
- Medication administration authorization and log.
- **Infant safe-sleep acknowledgment** (for facilities caring for infants).

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application + qualifications documentation.
- **WDCI fingerprint + FBI + Central Registry + sex offender registry** — includes any state lived in during the prior 5 years (W.S. 14-4-107 + federal CCDBG).
- Health certificate (free of communicable disease) + TB screening.
- **Current pediatric CPR and First Aid**.
- Mandated reporter training.
- Pre-service and annual in-service training (minimum ~15 hours/year recommended; varies by role per DFS standards tied to federal floor).
- Professional development tracked through Wyoming Early Childhood Professional Learning / STAR rating (if opted in).

## What must be on your walls
- Current DFS certification.
- Most recent Fire Marshal inspection.
- Most recent environmental / sanitation inspection.
- Evacuation plan / emergency procedures.
- Daily schedule.
- Menu.
- DFS complaint contact.
- **Expulsion / suspension policies** (W.S. and DFS rules require written, published policies consistent with federal CCDBG).

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented.
- **Severe weather / tornado drills** — documented (WY has both tornado and winter-blizzard exposure).
- **Daily attendance** by room.
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident / injury reports**.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff per room — check **both ratio and group size** against Chapter 7.
2. Post current certification + latest fire and environmental inspections.
3. Pull a random staff file: WDCI + FBI + Central Registry + sex offender registry current? Includes any out-of-state residency in prior 5 years?
4. Pediatric CPR / First Aid — current for every caregiver?
5. Pull a random child file: immunization current? Infant file includes safe-sleep acknowledgment?
6. Medication locked; log current.
7. Diaper-change stations clean; gloves stocked.
8. Playground fenced; gates latch; winter ice cleared.
9. Fire-drill log — last drill within required interval.
10. Expulsion/suspension policy — written and posted.

## What happens if you fail
DFS can issue findings and require a corrective action plan; persistent/serious findings can escalate to a **probationary certification**, **suspension**, or **revocation** of certification. Substantiated complaints from **2019 to present** are published on **Find Child Care WY**. Lapses require **re-application**, not simple reinstatement. Appeal rights under WY administrative procedure. Records requests under W.S. §16-4-201 through §16-4-205.

## The weird Wyoming gotcha nobody tells you about
**Wyoming uses the word "certification" instead of "license" — but it is functionally the same thing**, and you can lose it the same way. New operators moving from CO or ID sometimes ignore compliance because "we're just certified, not licensed" — DFS citations don't care. Second gotcha: **Wyoming enforces ratio AND group size as two separate rules** — a 1:4 infant room with 11 infants is ratio-compliant but group-size-violating. Third gotcha: WY requires background checks in **any state the hire lived in during the prior 5 years** (federal CCDBG) — a new hire who just moved from another state without out-of-state clearance is an automatic citation.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Wyoming owner
Their fear is Find Child Care WY publishing a substantiated complaint — the rancher-town rumor economy does the rest. The 30-second number: every hire needs WDCI + FBI + Central Registry + sex offender checks, **plus out-of-state clearance** for any state they lived in during the prior 5 years. WY owners today track certifications (yearly) and background checks (5-year refresh) on paper because the DFS rules span 4 separate chapters. Lead with: "One compliance log that covers Chapters 2, 5, 6, or 7 depending on your setting, alerts on annual certification lapse, and flags any new-hire out-of-state residency that needs clearance." Small state (500 – 650 providers) — one strong Cheyenne or Casper reference lands the next five.
