1. Licensing Authority

Hawaii Department of Human Services (DHS), Benefit, Employment & Support Services Division (BESSD), Child Care Program — Child Care Licensing Units. DHS contracts with PATCH (People Attentive to Children) Hawaii as the statewide Child Care Resource & Referral agency and administers the DHS Early Childhood Registry through PATCH.

  • Oahu Unit 2 (Waipahu / West Oahu): (808) 675-0470

6. Violations & Inspection Data

Transparency gap. Hawaii is an outlier: the DHS consumer portal publishes license status only — not inspection findings, deficiencies, or complaint outcomes. Per Honolulu Civil Beat's 2024 reporting, Hawaii is the only state out of compliance on three CCDBG transparency categories and has posted no serious-incident data online since 2016.

Data source

  • Primary (consumer-facing, license status only): DHS Child Care Provider Search
  • Statutory basis: HRS §346-153 (requires DHS to retain current + 2 prior years of inspection, deficiency, and complaint records, and make them available for public review).
  • Open-records path: Hawaii UIPA (HRS Chapter 92F); appeals via Office of Information Practices.
  • Federal oversight backdrop: HHS-OIG A-09-19-02006 (2020).

Access strategy

  • No bulk API or CSV for inspection findings — file a UIPA request to DHS BESSD for CSV/PDF export of inspection reports + deficiencies + CAPs + complaint findings for the last 3 years.
  • For single-facility enrichment before a sales call, contact the appropriate island licensing unit directly (see phone numbers above).
  • Do not scrape the consumer portal for violation analytics — it doesn't publish them.

7. Outreach Signals

  • Infant/toddler specialty license (HAR 17-895.1) — a center cannot legally serve <24-month-old children under a Group Center license. Providers who pitch expansion into infant/toddler must secure a separate license.
  • PATCH Registry gatekeeping — staff cannot count toward ratio until PATCH evaluates and listings are current. A center with expiring registry entries hits an operational cliff. High-urgency sales hook.
  • TB clearance (staff + children) — recurring annual/biennial workflow unique to Hawaii.
  • Annual renewal for first 4 years, biennial thereafter — a predictable annual trigger for compliance-product outreach.
  • Island logistics — DHS inspection response times vary by island; neighbor-island operators over-index on self-serve readiness tools.
