Tennessee — Child Care Compliance Data Report

Population rank: 15 · ~7.2M residents · Prepared 2026-04-18 for ComplianceKit

1. Licensing Authority

Tennessee Department of Human Services (TDHS), Child Care Licensing. Public-school-run child care is licensed by Tennessee Department of Education (TDOE); TDHS covers private, nonprofit, and for-profit providers.

  • Agency: tn.gov/humanservices
  • Provider portal (eLicensing): tdhs.service-now.com/tncc
  • Consumer locator: Find Child Care → ServiceNow map
  • Complaint hotline: 1-800-462-8261 / 615-313-4820

2. Primary Regulatory Citation

  • Statute: Tennessee Child Care Licensing Act of 2000 — T.C.A. § 71-3-501 et seq.; enforcement: § 71-3-509
  • Rules: Chapter 1240-04-01 — Licensure Rules for Child Care Agencies (effective Nov 20, 2025)
  • Chapter 1240-04-07 Report Cards & Rated Licensing: Cornell LII

6. Violations & Inspection Data new

Since July 2014, TDHS has published a per-facility compliance history alongside the Star-Quality Report Card. Every licensed provider receives four visits per year (three unannounced monitoring + one annual evaluation), so the compliance page refreshes ~quarterly.

Freshness

  • Compliance history refreshes quarterly (4 visits/year mandatory).
  • Star-Quality Report Cards refresh annually after the evaluation closes.
  • Digital Tennessee FFY rollups refresh annually at fiscal-year boundary (Sep 30).

Access Strategy

  • SWORPS ArcGIS is the bulk-friendly primary; already used for roster. Provides identifiers + star ratings.
  • Playwright against the ServiceNow CSP (onedhs.tn.gov/csp?id=tn_cc_prv_maps) for per-facility Compliance History + Star Report Card navigation. Agency code from ArcGIS feed = join key.
  • Star Report Cards are PDFs hosted on the SWORPS webapp; two-step flow — browse index → click agency → fetch PDF.
  • Digital Tennessee is simple static HTML; scrape per FFY URL.
Records-law backup. Tennessee Public Records Act (T.C.A. § 10-7-503 et seq.) — 7 business days acknowledgment required. TDHS front: dhs.customerservice@tn.gov. Oversight / appeals: Office of Open Records Counsel.

7. Outreach Signals (Hot-Lead Detection)

  • High-risk violation in past 90 days — 5-day follow-up revisit creates urgency; owner actively correcting.
  • Star rating dropped year-over-year — subsidy revenue is at risk; ERS-related deficiencies are addressable with process tools.
  • 0-star agencies — bottom-of-market; often the most exposed operators but also most receptive to a low-cost fix.
  • Annual license (12-month term) — every TN facility renews yearly, so 1/12 of the universe is in "renewal mode" in any given month.
  • Digital Tennessee FFY rollup spikes — counties with higher injury/abuse counts year-over-year surface as likely high-risk markets for outreach.
  • Nov 2025 rule rewrite — 1240-04-01 revised in November 2025; centers still operating under prior rule interpretations are a pre-qualified audience.
