# How to Pass Your Wisconsin Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector is a **Child Care Licensing Specialist** from the **Wisconsin Department of Children and Families (DCF), Bureau of Early Care Regulation (BECR)**. Group centers get **at least one unannounced routine licensing inspection per year**. Family child care gets visited at least once per 2-year license term. Complaints trigger visits anytime. Everything within the last **3 years** is published on the **Child Care Finder** public site, pulled from DCF's internal WISCCRS database (updated within 24 hours of entry). Public sites:
- DCF Child Care Licensing: https://dcf.wisconsin.gov/cclicensing
- Public search (Child Care Finder): https://childcarefinder.wisconsin.gov/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — Wis. Stat. ch. 48.65 (licensed child care).
- **The rulebook for group centers (9+ kids)** — DCF 251. https://docs.legis.wisconsin.gov/code/admin_code/dcf/201_252/251
- **The rulebook for family centers (4–8 kids)** — DCF 250.
- **Day camps** — DCF 252.
- **County-certified small care** — DCF 202.
- **Ratio rule** — DCF 251 Table 251.055.
- **Most-Frequently-Cited list (from DCF itself)** — https://dcf.wisconsin.gov/ccregulation/providers/most-freq-cited
- **Serious Violations list** — https://dcf.wisconsin.gov/cclicensing/seriousviolations

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From DCF 251 Table 251.055 (group centers). Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age band | One staff for every... | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Birth – 12 months (infant) | 4 | 8 |
| 12 – 24 months (toddler) | 4 | 8 |
| 2 years | 6 | 12 |
| 2 yr 9 mo – 3 years | 8 | 16 |
| 3 years | 10 | 20 |
| 4 – 5 years | 13 | 24 |
| School age (5+ attending K or above) | 17 | 32 |

Mixed-age infant/toddler groups are capped at 8. **Family Child Care (DCF 250):** 1 provider with up to 8 children under age 7, no more than 3 under 2 and no more than 6 from one family. *(Wisconsin has revised infant/toddler rules scheduled to take effect summer 2026 — verify the current edition before you change anything.)*

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced.
2. Checks license is posted and current.
3. Walks every room, counts children, compares to staff present. Uses **Form DCF-F-0078 — Staff-to-Child Ratio Worksheet**.
4. Pulls staff files — Background Record Check (BRC, Form DCF-F-CFS0062), Registry entry, 25-hour annual CE log.
5. Pulls child files — Form CFS0055 (health history), immunization, medication consent CFS0056.
6. Reviews noncompliance correction plans from prior visits.
7. Looks at drill logs, safe sleep setup, biting policy, and any required written policies.
8. Exit conference. Noncompliance Statement is posted to Child Care Finder right after the visit, with the provider's Correction Plan posted once submitted.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
DCF publishes its own Most-Frequently-Cited list. The top categories are:
1. **Background checks** — BRC not current; fingerprint or registry check missing.
2. **Physical exams** on staff and/or children — expired or missing.
3. **Abuse/neglect training** — not completed or documented.
4. **Child tracking / attendance / supervision** (DCF 251) — kids not tracked in/out properly.
5. **CPR certification** — not current, or not enough certified staff on the floor.

Source: https://dcf.wisconsin.gov/ccregulation/providers/most-freq-cited

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment form** with emergency contacts, physician, authorized pickups.
- **Immunization record** per Wisconsin Immunization Law (DHS 144).
- **DCF-F-CFS0055** — Health History and Emergency Care Plan. Within 30 days.
- **Physical exam** — within 3 months after enrollment; annual for under 2, biennial for 2+.
- **Allergy / medical action plans**.
- **DCF-F-CFS0056** — Medication Consent.
- **Authorizations** for transportation, photos, emergency treatment.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **DCF-F-CFS0062** — Background Record Check request. Initial fingerprint + state/national; rechecks every **4 years**. DOJ, Sex Offender Registry, DCF Child Abuse/Neglect Registry.
- **Entry on The Registry (wiregistry.org)** — mandatory WI professional registry.
- **Child Care Teacher credential** or equivalent Registry-approved coursework (80-hour Intro to Child Care Profession / Skills & Strategies).
- **Current pediatric First Aid + CPR**.
- **25 hours annual continuing education** for lead teachers; **15 hours** for assistants — all logged via The Registry.
- **Shaken Baby Syndrome Prevention** training (Wis. Stat. § 48.982).
- **Mandated Reporter training**.
- **TB screening** where applicable.
- **Wisconsin Child Care Administrator Credential** (director).

## What must be on your walls
- Current DCF license.
- Most recent Noncompliance Statement / Correction Plan summary.
- **YoungStar rating**.
- Written Safe Sleep (ABCs) policy in infant rooms.
- Written biting policy, discipline policy, health & safety policy, emergency plan.
- Menus.
- License capacity + ages served.
- Shaken Baby Syndrome prevention notice.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** — per emergency plan.
- **Severe weather / tornado drill** — per plan.
- **Lockdown drill** — per plan.
- **Staff-to-Child Ratio Worksheet** (Form DCF-F-0078) kept current.
- **Daily attendance / child tracking**.
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident reports (Form CFS0082)**.
- **No wading pools allowed.** Swimming pools require a lifeguard.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Fill in the Ratio Worksheet for every group right now.
2. Pull 3 random staff files — BRC (under 4 years old?), Registry membership, 25-hour CE log, CPR card.
3. Pull 3 random child files — immunization, CFS0055 health history.
4. Infant rooms: ABCs of sleep, no loose bedding.
5. Written biting policy posted? Written safe sleep policy? Written discipline policy? All required.
6. License + YoungStar rating posted?
7. Medication cabinet — locked, in-date, matching CFS0056.
8. Walk the playground — no wading pool; swimming pool has a lifeguard on duty.
9. Open your Child Care Finder page — any posted Noncompliance Statement without a Correction Plan yet?
10. Shaken Baby Syndrome prevention notice visible (Wis. Stat. § 48.982)?

## What happens if you fail
DCF issues a **Noncompliance Statement**. You submit a **Correction Plan** with target dates. A repeat or "serious" violation (see the DCF Serious Violations list) leads to warnings, license conditions, probationary status, suspension, or revocation. Providers who have lost a license or are operating illegally are listed at https://dcf.wisconsin.gov/ccregulation/illegal-revoked. Critical Incident reports are published at https://dcf.wisconsin.gov/cps/incidents. Wisconsin Shares subsidy is tied to compliance — losing standing can cut your reimbursement.

## The weird Wisconsin gotcha nobody tells you about
Wisconsin uses **continuous licensure** — there's no set renewal date. You pay the fee annually, maintain compliance, and the license just keeps going until a change in ownership or a revocation. Great in theory, brutal in practice: there's no built-in "reset" to clean up records each year, so lapses (an expired CPR card, a staff file missing a signature) just accumulate. And the WISCCRS data system pushes every finding to Child Care Finder within 24 hours — parents see your Noncompliance Statement before you've even filed the Correction Plan. Also uniquely Wisconsin: **The Registry (wiregistry.org)** is the mandatory workforce registry for every staff member, and your ongoing 25-hour annual continuing ed for lead teachers must log there — not in your own records. If it's not in The Registry, for licensing purposes it didn't happen.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Wisconsin owner
Biggest fear: a Noncompliance Statement posted to their Child Care Finder page within hours of a visit — and the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel's decade-old "Cashing In On Kids" coverage still casts a long shadow. Drop this in 30 seconds: "Your BRC is a 4-year clock, your lead teachers owe 25 CE hours a year in The Registry, and DCF 251 Table 251.055 sets infant ratio at 1:4. Are you tracking every BRC expiration, Registry training hour, and Correction Plan due date in one place?" Today they are juggling The Registry, paper Noncompliance Statements, and a binder of Correction Plans. The close: "We turn every DCF 250/251 cite into a file-level task so you never get surprised by a Correction Plan deadline or a Registry hour gap."
