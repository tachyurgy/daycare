# How to Pass Your Illinois Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the Illinois Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS), Division of Licensing. Annual monitoring visits are **unannounced** (required under Procedures 383). Complaint visits are always unannounced. A new **Illinois Department of Early Childhood (IDEC)** is set to take over licensing during 2026–2027, but as of today DCFS still issues the license under 89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 407.

- Agency: https://dcfs.illinois.gov/
- Public inspection search (DCFS Sunshine): https://sunshine.dcfs.illinois.gov/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — Child Care Act of 1969, 225 ILCS 10. This is the enabling statute.
- **The binding rulebook for day-care centers** — 89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 407. [https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/illinois/title-89/part-407](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/illinois/title-89/part-407)
- **Rule 407 PDF (DCFS)** — https://dcfs.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/dcfs/documents/about-us/policy-rules-and-forms/documents/rules/rules-407.pdf
- **Day Care Homes (3–8 children)** — 89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 406.
- **Group Day Care Homes (up to 12 children)** — 89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 408.
- **Background checks** — 89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 377.
- **Licensing Compliance and Monitoring Procedures** — 89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 383 and **Procedures 383** (PDF): https://dcfs.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/dcfs/documents/about-us/policy-rules-and-forms/documents/procedures/procedures-383.pdf
- **Childcare immunization code** — 77 Ill. Adm. Code 665.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (6 weeks – 14 months) | 1:4 | 12 |
| Toddlers (15 – 23 months) | 1:5 | 15 |
| Two-year-olds | 1:8 | 16 |
| Three-year-olds | 1:10 | 20 |
| Four-year-olds | 1:10 | 20 |
| Five-year-olds | 1:20 | 20 |
| School-age (kindergartners present) | 1:20 | 30 |

Inspectors count heads on the spot. Mixed-age groups follow the ratio of the youngest child. Source: § 407.190.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive unannounced and sign in.
2. They check the license is posted.
3. They walk every classroom and count children against staff.
4. They pull staff files and check DCFS CANTS clearance, Illinois State Police, FBI, and sex offender registry checks.
5. They pull child files — physical, immunizations, lead screening, emergency contacts.
6. They review the training log for each staff — 15 hours per year minimum.
7. They check fire-drill records, radon test, and the annual fire-marshal inspection.
8. They close out with any violations, which get posted to the DCFS Sunshine portal as a Monitoring Report.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio violations** under § 407.190.
2. **Background-check gaps** — CANTS, ISP fingerprint, FBI, or sex offender registry missing or expired (Part 377).
3. **15-hour annual training not met** for any staff member (§ 407.120).
4. **Missing lead-screening documentation** for children under 6.
5. **Missing radon test** (required every 3 years) or the test result not posted next to the license.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment form** — parent/guardian, emergency contacts, pickup list.
- **Current physical exam** signed by a physician (within 6 months at admission; updated per DPH schedule).
- **Immunization record** meeting 77 Ill. Adm. Code 665.
- **Lead screening documentation** for children under 6.
- **2+ authorized pickup contacts.**
- **Consent for emergency medical treatment.**
- **IEP/IFSP and allergy records** as applicable.
- **Transportation and field-trip permissions.**
- **Daily attendance log** (retained 1 year).
- **Incident / accident reports.**
- **Medication administration records** with written parent authorization.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **CFS 718-A** — Applicant Personal Information.
- **CFS 718-D** — CANTS / SACWIS background check request.
- **Illinois State Police fingerprint-based criminal history check.**
- **FBI national fingerprint check.**
- **Sex Offender Registry check** (state and federal).
- **Physical exam + TB screening** at hire.
- **Orientation training** before working alone with children.
- **15 clock hours of in-service training per year** (directors more).
- **DCFS mandated reporter training** — every 3 years.
- **Infant/child CPR + first aid** — at least one certified staff on site at all times.
- **Radon safety training.**
- **Director qualifications** matching § 407.140 tiers.
- **ExceleRate Illinois / Gateways Registry** enrollment.

## What must be on your walls
- **Current license** — posted in a public area.
- **Capacity statement.**
- **Most recent inspection summary.**
- **Radon test result** next to the license (most recent).
- **Emergency exit plan** in each room.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Monthly fire drill** — self-documented; annual fire-marshal inspection on top.
- **Daily attendance log** — retained 1 year.
- **Incident / accident reports** same-day.
- **Medication administration records** per dose.
- **Tummy time** required daily for non-walking infants (Part 407).

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Is the license posted? Is the radon test result posted next to it?
2. Count each room. Ratio good?
3. Pull three staff files. CANTS, ISP, FBI, sex offender check — all clear and current?
4. Pull three child files. Physical within 6 months? Immunizations? Lead screening for under-6?
5. Is the 15-hour training log current for every staff?
6. Is this month's fire-drill record written up?
7. Lock medication and cleaning-chemical cabinets.
8. Outlet covers in infant / toddler rooms?
9. Is the DCFS mandated reporter training current (within 3 years) for everyone?
10. Is every non-walking infant getting documented tummy time daily?

## What happens if you fail
DCFS Sunshine codes each finding as **CAN** (child abuse/neglect reported), **COR** (violations corrected), **ENR** (enforcement — referred for revocation), **MON** (monitoring), or **NFA** (no findings). Findings post to the public Sunshine portal within 48–72 hours. A pattern can lead to a license revocation referral and publication on the Investigation Actions tracker. Source: DCFS Sunshine portal and Procedures 383.

## The weird Illinois gotcha nobody tells you about
Illinois runs a dedicated public transparency portal called **DCFS Sunshine** at sunshine.dcfs.illinois.gov — a statutory response to the Child Care Act transparency mandate that went live for anything dated January 1, 2010 or later. Every violation, every enforcement action, every substantiated child abuse report is searchable by name. Parents use it. Reporters use it. On top of that, **radon testing is required every 3 years** in applicable Illinois zones, and the result has to be posted next to the license. Newer state standards also require daily **tummy time** for non-walking infants under Part 407. These two items are the most-missed by operators moving in from out of state.
