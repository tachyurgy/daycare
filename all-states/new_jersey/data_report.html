6. Violations & Inspection Data new

Every licensed center and registered family home has a detail page on Child Care Explorer that includes every inspection report (CCC-108) and every OOL Complaint Investigation Summary Report produced since the center was licensed (DCF maintains at minimum a 3-year rolling archive; older reports are usually still accessible).

Sources

  • Child Care Explorer portal: root · per-facility URL pattern /portal/provider-details/<facility_id> (examples: 700361, 702715).
  • ChildCareNJ.gov Provider Search: parent-facing front door — same data model, different skin.
  • OOL program page: centers.html — describes publication policy.
  • Complaint intake: public complaint form.

Freshness

  • Inspection reports post ~10 business days after onsite visit.
  • Complaint investigation summaries post at investigation close (~45–60 day median).
  • Roster (ArcGIS Hub) refreshes monthly (2026-04-08 snapshot reflected in leads CSV).

Access Strategy

  • Playwright against childcareexplorer.njccis.com/portal/provider-details/<id>. Enumerate integer IDs in the observed 700000–900000 window.
  • Intercept the page's XHR calls to capture the NJCCIS JSON API response (contains a documents[] array with signed PDF URLs for each inspection). This turns the scrape into a JSON harvest instead of DOM parsing.
  • Cloudflare is present but tolerant of normal headers; use standard UA + referer.
OPRA backup. Open Public Records Act, N.J.S.A. 47:1A-1 et seq. — 7-business-day initial response. DCF OPRA hub: link. Check OPRAmachine for prior request precedents before filing. Reasonable ask: "all inspection reports and complaint investigation summaries issued by DCF OOL between <start> and <end>, in electronic format."

7. Outreach Signals (Hot-Lead Detection)

  • Provisional license on Explorer page — provider has up to 6 months to achieve substantial compliance. High-intent buyer.
  • Repeat citation at 3A:52-4.3 (ratios) across two inspections — chronic ratio violations mean the provider is understaffed or miscoding ages; ComplianceKit's ratio calculator is a direct fix.
  • NJDEP environmental-site proximity — centers at or near listed contaminated sites (the NJDEP SRWMP pipeline is why the roster lives in the NJDEP portal) have mandatory periodic re-testing (N.J.A.C. 3A:52-7.11) — compliance overhead creates pull.
  • FOIPS flag = true — these are programs in public schools; generally lower priority (not private operators).
  • Recent license change (renewal date in last 90 days) — the 3-year cycle renewal is the highest intent purchase window.

8. Next-Step Data Acquisition

  • Playwright target: Enumerate Explorer IDs 700000–900000 (sparse; est. 6,000–8,000 active centers + family homes). Harvest JSON + PDFs. Budget ~15 worker-hours at 3 concurrent browsers.
  • Sockrata probe: Monitor pdn3-t238 for public release; if opened, bulk-pull supersedes the ArcGIS CSV for freshness.
  • County CCR&R: Registered family child care providers (5 children max) are registered through 22 county CCR&R agencies, not DCF directly. For a full family-home list, scrape the childcareexplorer.njccis.com FCC filter rather than the ArcGIS centers-only feed.
  • OPRA for aggregate enforcement: File scoped requests for adverse-action orders (revocation, denial, non-renewal) per fiscal year to build a timeline.
  • Email append: Leads CSV already has phone + email for most centers; enrich owner/director titles via CCC-21 application data (in PDFs on Explorer).
