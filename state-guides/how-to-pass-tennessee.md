# How to Pass Your Tennessee Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Tennessee Department of Human Services (TDHS), Child Care Licensing**. They are titled "Child Care Licensing Consultants." Tennessee is aggressive about visits — **four per year** per facility: three unannounced monitoring visits, plus one annual evaluation. Complaints trigger additional unannounced visits on top of that. Fire and environmental/sanitation are separate annual inspections run by TN SFMO and the local health department. Public portals:
- TDHS Child Care Services: https://www.tn.gov/humanservices/for-families/child-care-services.html
- Public provider locator: https://onedhs.tn.gov/csp?id=tn_cc_prv_maps

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — Tennessee Child Care Licensing Act of 2000, T.C.A. § 71-3-501 et seq.
- **The rulebook inspectors use** — Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. Chapter **1240-04-01**, last revised November 20, 2025. https://publications.tnsosfiles.com/rules/1240/1240-04/1240-04-01.20251120.pdf
- **Ratio rule (for centers)** — 1240-04-01-.22, Chart 1. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/tennessee/Tenn-Comp-R-Regs-1240-04-01-.22
- **Enforcement rule** — T.C.A. § 71-3-509. https://law.justia.com/codes/tennessee/title-71/chapter-3/part-5/section-71-3-509/

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From 1240-04-01-.22, Chart 1. Tennessee is strict: **for infants and toddlers the 10% variance does NOT apply**. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age band | One staff for every... | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (6 weeks – 15 months) | 4 | 8 |
| Young Toddlers (12 – 30 months) | 6 | 12 |
| Older Toddlers (24 – 35 months) | 7 | 14 |
| 3-year-olds | 9 | 18 |
| 4-year-olds | 13 | 20 |
| 5-year-olds | 16 | 20 |
| School-age | 20 | — |

A second adult must be on the premises whenever more than 12 children are present. Food preparers do not count in ratio while they are in the kitchen.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced — one of three annual monitoring visits, or the annual evaluation.
2. Checks your license is posted and current.
3. Walks every room, counts children, compares against staff present right then.
4. Pulls staff personnel files (per 1240-04-01-.10).
5. Pulls child enrollment files — looking for the **HS-3041 Immunization Certificate** and emergency info.
6. Checks your drill logs (fire monthly, severe-weather quarterly).
7. Reviews medication log, incident reports, daily attendance.
8. Annual evaluation adds an **ERS observation** that feeds your **Star-Quality Report Card** — the public rating that drives your subsidy reimbursement rate.
9. Exit conference. Anything cited as High-Risk triggers a **5-day follow-up revisit**.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Out-of-ratio** (1240-04-01-.22) — especially in infant/toddler rooms, where there is no variance allowance.
2. **Personnel file gaps** (1240-04-01-.10) — missing background check refresh, training record, or annual evaluation.
3. **Supervision lapses** (1240-04-01-.13) — kids out of sight, or a single adult left alone with more than 12 kids.
4. **Immunization records not on the HS-3041 form** — must be the official Tennessee certificate.
5. **Drill log blanks** — monthly fire and quarterly severe-weather (tornado) drills missed or not signed.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **HS-3035** — Child's Record / Enrollment Form. Emergency contacts, authorized pickups, parent signature.
- **HS-3041** — official Tennessee Certificate of Immunization. Must be this form.
- **Physical exam** — within 30 days of enrollment.
- **Parent consents** — emergency medical, field trips, transportation, water activities.
- **Allergy / special-needs care plan**.
- **Daily sign-in/sign-out** signed by parent.
- **HS-3033** — Medication Administration Log / Authorization.
- **Incident reports** (copies given to parents same day).
- Records kept **at least 1 year** after a child disenrolls.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **HS-0166** — Personnel Information Form.
- **HS-2985** — Criminal Background Check Request. Fingerprint-based TBI + FBI, TN Sex Offender Registry, TN Felony Offender database, DCS Abuse Registry. Out-of-state registry check if the person lived out of TN in the last 5 years. Refreshed every 5 years.
- **Qualifications** — director must have CDA, associate's in ECE, or equivalent (1240-04-01-.05(5)).
- **Orientation** — within first month of hire.
- **18 clock hours annual training** via TECTA-approved courses.
- **Pediatric CPR + First Aid** — current, at least one certified adult on-site at all times.
- **TB test** at hire.
- **Annual Mandatory Reporter training**.
- **Annual performance evaluation** documented.

## What must be on your walls
- Current TDHS license.
- Most recent inspection report in a conspicuous place (rule requires it).
- Star-Quality Report Card.
- Written Safe Sleep policy in every infant room.
- **Child abuse summary** — fiscal-year counts of abuses, injuries, and deaths per TDHS Digital Tennessee requirement.
- Emergency evacuation route per room.
- Menus.
- Complaint hotline: 1-800-462-8261.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill — monthly**, logged.
- **Severe-weather (tornado) drill — quarterly**, logged.
- **Active-threat / lockdown drill** — per your emergency plan.
- **Daily attendance sign-in/out** signed by parent.
- **Medication log** (HS-3033).
- **Vehicle use log** — any vehicle used for transport must be inspected and driver must be 21+ with valid license (CDL / commercial endorsement for 10+ passengers).
- **Serious injury / death** — report to TDHS within 24 hours; written report within 48 hours (HS-0222).

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff on the floor right now. Fix any ratio gap immediately.
2. If more than 12 kids are present, is a second adult on the premises?
3. Walk the infant room — everyone on back, no loose bedding, safe-sleep policy posted.
4. Pull 3 random staff files — background check date, training hours, CPR card.
5. Pull 3 random child files — HS-3041 on top.
6. Fire drill log — initialed for this month?
7. Tornado drill log — initialed this quarter?
8. License and most recent inspection report posted and visible?
9. Medication cabinet locked; each bottle matches a current parent authorization.
10. Walk the playground — fence, gate latch, no hazards.

## What happens if you fail
T.C.A. § 71-3-509 is the enforcement ladder: from written warning, to probation, to civil penalty, to summary suspension, to denial or revocation. High-risk findings require a 5-day follow-up revisit. Your star rating can drop, which directly cuts your subsidy reimbursement rate. Failure to report an abuse allegation to DCS immediately (T.C.A. § 37-1-403) is itself grounds for revocation. Every monitoring visit is posted to the public compliance history within about 2 weeks.

## The weird Tennessee gotcha nobody tells you about
Your **Star-Quality rating is a public report card tied to real money**. The annual evaluation uses the Environment Rating Scale (ERS) and the result lands on the public Report Card at the SWORPS/UTK portal. Parents see it. More importantly, your reimbursement rate from the state subsidy program is directly tied to stars — a one-star drop can cost a mid-size center tens of thousands of dollars a year. A single "High Risk" citation (say, a supervision lapse) can knock you down a rung at renewal time. So in Tennessee, a compliance visit isn't just pass/fail — it's a pricing event.

Biggest fear: a one-step drop in their public Star-Quality rating at the annual evaluation — because it's a direct pay cut on their CCDF subsidy reimbursement. Drop this in 30 seconds: "You get four TDHS visits a year — three unannounced monitoring plus the annual evaluation — and your Report Card posts to SWORPS. A single high-risk citation can ding your stars and your rate. Are you tracking the 18-hour TECTA training deadline and the 5-year background-check refresh per teacher?" Today they're juggling TECTA printouts, paper HS-3041 immunization forms, and a shared binder for drill logs. The close: "We keep your Star rating clean by catching every expiring credential and missed drill before your Consultant walks in."
