6. Violations & Inspection Data

Primary source

eLicense CT is the state system of record: structured license metadata + PDF inspection reports keyed by DocumentIdnt. Example: a 7-page Division of Licensing inspection PDF.

PDF heavy ASP.NET VIEWSTATE No Socrata — extractable via Playwright session + PDF parse.

Secondary source

The 211 Child Care search tool is the consumer-friendly mirror: per-facility inspection cards + substantiated complaint history for the last 5 years. Listing pages: https://www.211childcare.org/listings/{ID}/inspections.

Aggregate reports

OEC publishes one PDF per year: "Number of Deaths, Serious Injuries, Substantiated Child Abuse & Neglect in Child Care Settings". Available 2016 → 2025 at 211 Child Care Other Reports. Latest direct link: 2025 report PDF.

Independent archive

CT Mirror Day Care Inspector — investigative-nonprofit searchable archive of fiscal-2017 inspection reports obtained via records request. Useful for pre-2019 context. Launch article: CT Mirror 2018-10-22.

Freshness

eLicense updated 24–72 hrs after licensor filing. 211 nightly sync. Annual reports Q1 of following year. Background-check 2-yr currency drives many mid-cycle re-inspections.

Freedom-of-information path

CGS §§ 1-200 et seq. — OEC responds in 4 business days (acknowledgment); 10–60 days for bulk. Complaint files subject to CGS § 17a-28 DCF abuse-and-neglect confidentiality; redacted summaries releasable. Denials appealable to CT Freedom of Information Commission.
