# How to Pass Your Michigan Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Michigan Department of Lifelong Education, Advancement, and Potential (MiLEAP)**, in the **Child Care Licensing Bureau (CCLB)**. Until late 2023 this lived under LARA/BCHS, but it was moved to MiLEAP in December 2023. The person at your door is a CCLB **licensing consultant**. They come out at least once a year, usually unannounced, plus a renewal inspection at the end of your 2-year license term, plus complaint visits any time. Every inspection report gets posted publicly in the CCHIRP portal within 1–3 business days of supervisor sign-off.

- Agency: https://www.michigan.gov/mileap/early-childhood-education/cclb
- Public inspection portal (CCHIRP): https://cclb.my.site.com/micchirp/s/statewide-facility-search

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The law** — Child Care Organizations Act, 1973 PA 116, MCL 722.111–722.128. https://www.legislature.mi.gov/documents/mcl/pdf/mcl-Act-116-of-1973.pdf
- **Center rules** — Mich. Admin. Code R 400.8101 through R 400.8840 (2025 revision). https://www.michigan.gov/mileap/-/media/Project/Websites/mileap/Documents/Early-Childhood-Education/CCLB/Licensing-Rules-and-Statutes/Center-Rules/R-4008101-to-4008840-1Rules-2025.pdf
- **Family and group home rules** — R 400.1901 through R 400.1963. https://ars.apps.lara.state.mi.us/AdminCode/DownloadAdminCodeFile?FileName=R+400.1901++to+400.1963.pdf
- **Ratio rule (centers)** — R 400.8182. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/michigan/Mich-Admin-Code-R-400-8182
- **Heads up** — the rules were **rewritten and renumbered in May 2025**. If your binder is older than that, you have the wrong rule numbers.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age band | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (birth – 30 months) | 1:4 | 12 |
| Toddler (30 months – 3 years) | 1:8 | 16 |
| Preschool (3 – 4 years) | 1:10 | 30 |
| Preschool (4 years – school age) | 1:12 | 36 |
| School-age | 1:18 | 36 |

Mixed-age rooms follow the **youngest child's** ratio and group size. Family Home license (DF): 1 caregiver, max 6 kids including own under 6. Group Home license (DG): caregiver plus 1 approved assistant, max 12. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive unannounced and sign in.
2. They check that your license is posted and your Great Start to Quality rating is visible.
3. They walk every classroom and count children against staff.
4. They observe infant rooms for safe sleep (R 400.8305).
5. They pull staff files looking for background checks, credentials, and training logs.
6. They pull child files looking for immunization records and physicals.
7. They review your BCAL-3300 incident reports and BCAL-1248 serious incident reports.
8. They close out with a Licensing Study Report (BCAL-5245) or a Special Investigation report. The report is uploaded as a PDF attachment on your CCHIRP facility page.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio violations** — cited as R 400.8182.
2. **Caregiver qualification gaps** — cited as R 400.8135 through R 400.8138.
3. **Safe sleep** — cited as R 400.8305 (infant on back, approved crib, no blankets).
4. **Missing or late 16 annual training hours** — cited as R 400.8142.
5. **Late or missed 24-hour serious-incident report** — cited as the BCAL-1248 reporting rule (death, hospitalization, abuse allegation, missing child).

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **BCAL-3730** — Child Information Record (parent contact, physician, insurance, authorized pickups).
- **Immunization record** — BCAL-4584 or through the Michigan Care Improvement Registry (MCIR). Waivers (medical or nonmedical) must be signed by the county health department.
- **Physical / health appraisal** — within 30 days of enrollment for centers, within 6 months for family homes.
- **Allergy plan** and **special diet** notes if applicable.
- **BCAL-1326** Medication Authorization — one per medication.
- **Daily attendance record.**
- **BCAL-3300** Incident/Accident Reports.
- **USDA CACFP** records if you participate.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **Background checks through CCHIRP** — fingerprint-based state and FBI criminal history, sex offender registry, Central Registry (child abuse/neglect). Must be clear before unsupervised contact. Renewed at least every 5 years (federal CCDBG floor) plus with any reported status change.
- **Credential documentation** — director/lead caregiver qualifications per R 400.8135–8138.
- **16 clock hours of approved professional development per year** — R 400.8142. Must cover first aid, CPR, medication admin, SIDS, shaken baby, food allergy, and mandated reporter at the federal floor.
- **Pediatric CPR and First Aid** cards — all caregivers within 90 days of hire.
- **Orientation within 4 weeks of hire** — must include SIDS, shaken baby, mandated reporter, and emergency preparedness.
- **TB screening** per local health.

## What must be on your walls
- Your **current CCLB license**.
- **Your Great Start to Quality star rating.**
- **Most recent inspection report** (or clear signage of where parents can view it).
- **Emergency evacuation plan** posted in every room.
- **Safe sleep policy** in every infant room.
- **Menu** (current week).
- **Daily schedule** by classroom.
- **USDA non-discrimination poster** (if CACFP).

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly (standard CCLB rule).
- **Tornado and shelter-in-place drills** — seasonally, per your written emergency plan.
- **Daily attendance** — maintained in real time.
- **Medication administration log** — every dose.
- **Incident/accident log** (BCAL-3300).
- **Serious incident report (BCAL-1248)** within 24 hours to CCLB for death, hospitalization, abuse allegation, or missing child.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Stand in each classroom and count. Use the youngest child's ratio.
2. Look at the license on the wall. Current?
3. Infant room: every baby on back, no blankets, cribs meet CPSC standards?
4. Open the emergency folder. Fire drill logged this month?
5. Pull 3 random child files. Immunizations current? BCAL-4584 or MCIR printout in file?
6. Pull 3 random staff files. CCHIRP background check stamp visible? 16 hours of PD recorded this year?
7. Walk the playground. Anything broken, sharp, or a climbing hazard near the fence?
8. Check cleaning supplies. Locked and out of child reach?
9. Confirm your CCHIRP profile has the updated staff list. Inspectors cross-check what's in CCHIRP against who's in the building.
10. Make sure you filed any BCAL-1248 serious incident within the 24-hour window.

## What happens if you fail
CCLB can issue a **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)**, move you to a **Provisional License** (6 months, non-renewable), or pursue **Summary Suspension**, **Non-Renewal**, or **Revocation**. Each violation is cited to a specific R-rule in the inspection report. Repeat violations carry more weight and are flagged in the report. Summary suspensions are announced in an individual MiLEAP press release. You have administrative appeal rights under the Michigan Administrative Procedures Act. The full **Closed/Suspended Due to Disciplinary Action** list runs on a rolling 5-year window here: https://www.michigan.gov/en/mileap/Early-Childhood-Education/cclb/parents/panel-collapse/provider-choose/child-care-providers-closed-or-suspended-due-to-disciplinary-action

## The weird Michigan gotcha nobody tells you about
Michigan runs its entire licensing life on **CCHIRP** — a Salesforce-based portal. Applications, renewals, staff lists, background checks, and incident reports all flow through CCHIRP. Two things trip new owners up: (1) the **2025 rule rewrite** (effective May 2025) **renumbered the entire R 400.8101 series**. If your binder, policies, or training materials quote pre-2025 rule numbers, inspectors will flag them. (2) Michigan requires **serious incident reports within 24 hours** on BCAL-1248 — that's tighter than the 48-hour window most states use. A death, hospitalization, abuse allegation, or missing child reported at hour 25 is a violation on top of whatever happened.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Michigan owner
MI owners' #1 fear is a **CCHIRP-posted inspection** that permanently lives on their public profile — parents Google the name, find the PDF, and enrollment drops. Drop this line: "Michigan's serious incident clock is 24 hours on BCAL-1248 — half what most states give you. One missed deadline = a violation on top of the incident itself." Also reference the **May 2025 rule renumbering** — many owners are still working off old binders and don't know their rule citations are stale. Today they track compliance in Excel or CCHIRP's own minimal dashboard, which doesn't warn them before renewal or training deadlines lapse. The 2-year license cycle is also shorter than neighboring states, so they feel the renewal crunch twice as often.
