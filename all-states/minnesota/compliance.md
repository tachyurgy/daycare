# Minnesota Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority

**Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF), Licensing Division.** Licensing authority for child care centers transitioned from the Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS) to DCYF following the 2023-2024 reorganization. Public records and inspection history remain mirrored on the DHS Licensing Information Lookup (LIL) at `licensinglookup.dhs.state.mn.us`.

- DCYF: https://dcyf.mn.gov/
- DHS Licensing Information Lookup: https://licensinglookup.dhs.state.mn.us/
- License types recognized: **Child Care Center** (Rule 3), **Certified Child Care Center**, **Family Child Care** (Rule 2).

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Minnesota Rules Chapter 9503** — Child Care Center Licensing (full chapter at https://www.revisor.mn.gov/rules/9503/).
- **Minnesota Statutes Chapter 245A** — Human Services Licensing Act (general licensing statute).
- **Minnesota Statutes Chapter 142B** — Licensing of Programs for Children (DCYF-era statute).
- **Minnesota Rules 9502** — Family Child Care Licensing.
- Health and background check cross-references: **Minn. Stat. 245C** (background studies) and **Minn. Rule 4605** (reportable disease / immunization).

## Facility Types Regulated

1. **Child Care Center (Rule 3)** — non-residential, typically 11+ children; Chapter 9503.
2. **Certified Child Care Center** — a streamlined pathway for smaller non-residential programs outside of Rule 3 full licensure (often used by faith-based or multi-purpose facilities).
3. **Family Child Care (Rule 2)** — in the provider's home, county-licensed under Chapter 9502.
4. **Drop-In and School-Age programs** — special sub-parts under 9503.0075.
5. **License-exempt** providers (e.g., certain public schools, tribal programs, parent co-ops meeting statutory exemptions).

| Form | Purpose |

| Applicant Agreement, Acknowledgment, and Verification (DHS-5148) | Required notarized form for any new license application. |
| Child Care Center License Application (DHS-4477) | Initial application for Rule 3 centers. |
| Change Request (DHS-5148-B or -F series) | Used to request a change in capacity, age served, or location. |

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (Rule 9503.0040)

Age categories under Minnesota Rule:

- **Infant** — 6 weeks to 16 months
- **Toddler** — 16 months to 33 months
- **Preschooler** — 33 months to first day of kindergarten
- **School-age** — kindergarten through 12

| Age Group | Minimum Staff:Child Ratio | Maximum Group Size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (6 wk – 16 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Toddler (16 – 33 mo) | 1:7 | 14 |
| Preschooler (33 mo – K) | 1:10 | 20 |
| School-age | 1:15 | 30 |

Notes:
- A child may be flex-designated an "infant" up to 18 months, a "toddler" up to 35 months, or a "preschooler" at 31 months if parent, teacher and director agree it is in the child's best interests (Rule 9503.0040).
- **At least two staff persons must be present at the center whenever it is operating**, even if ratios would only require one.
- **Drop-in programs** (9503.0075) use a different scale: 1:4 for infants (6 wk – 16 mo), 1:7 for 17–29 mo, 1:10 for 30 mo – 12 yr.
- Ratios must be maintained at all times including outdoor play and transitions. Maximum group size has defined exceptions for meals, outdoor activity, field trips, naps, and special activities.

## Required Child Records

Per Chapter 9503 and background-study cross-references, each child's file must contain:

- Enrollment application (name, DOB, address, parent contact, emergency contacts)
- Signed parent-provider agreement / policies acknowledgement
- Current immunization record (or conscientious / medical exemption) — per Minn. Stat. 121A.15 and Minn. Rule 4605
- Health record and/or physician's statement, allergy information
- Authorization for emergency medical care
- Written authorization for transportation, field trips, photos
- Individual Child Care Program Plan (ICCPP) documenting developmental goals and any accommodations
- Authorization and log for any medication administered
- Daily attendance record (arrival/departure times)
- Special health-care needs plan, if applicable
- Incident / injury reports when applicable

## Required Staff Records

- Application and resume documenting qualifications (Teacher, Assistant Teacher, Aide qualifications under 9503.0032 – 0034)
- **Background study clearance** via NETStudy 2.0 (Minn. Stat. 245C) — required before unsupervised access to children
- Reference checks
- Current CPR and Pediatric First Aid certifications (for required staff positions)
- Annual in-service training log (minimum ongoing training hours required by rule)
- Orientation training log (including mandatory reporter, safe sleep, SUIDS, abusive head trauma, shaken baby syndrome)
- TB screening as required by health department
- Documentation of Safe Sleep training for infant-room staff
- Supervisory performance reviews / evaluations
- Emergency contact information

## Inspection Cadence

- **Unannounced annual inspection** of every licensed child care center once each calendar year by DCYF Licensing (formerly DHS).
- **First-year "Early and Often" monitoring:** four center visits in the first year — first visit is a scheduled technical-assistance visit approximately three months post-licensure; remaining three are unannounced.
- **Complaint-triggered** visits can be initiated at any time; all complaint investigations are unannounced.
- Inspection results (violations and corrections) are published on the public Licensing Information Lookup.

## Renewal Cadence

- Licenses are issued on a **calendar-year basis** and expire **December 31** of their year (initial license is 12 months or less).
- License is **renewed annually** contingent on compliance, timely fee payment, and successful inspection cycle.
- Background studies must be kept current for all staff, owners and household members 13+.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Minimum two staff on site at all times** (9503.0090), regardless of ratio requirements.
- **Safe Sleep** rules (9503.0155) aligned with AAP guidance; specific documentation required for infants.
- **Parent Aware** quality rating program (voluntary) is the state's QRIS — several grants are tied to Parent Aware rating.
- **Early and Often** licensing model (post-2019) — extra support visits in year 1.
- **NETStudy 2.0 background studies** processed by DHS/DCYF; fingerprint-based; required for all child-contact staff and household members 13+.
- **All centers must have a written Risk Reduction Plan** (9503.0140), updated annually and after any significant incident.
- **Immunization compliance** — Minn. Stat. 121A.15 applies; centers must follow DHS immunization schedule or accept notarized exemption.
- **Mandated reporter training** required at orientation and ongoing.
- **Shaken Baby Syndrome / Abusive Head Trauma training** required annually for any staff working with children under age 5.
- Public inspection data (LIL) is fully indexed and open data, making customer-facing transparency a key compliance concern.
