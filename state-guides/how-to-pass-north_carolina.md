# How to Pass Your North Carolina Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **North Carolina Department of Health and Human Services (NCDHHS)**, in the **Division of Child Development and Early Education (DCDEE)**. The person at your door is a DCDEE licensing consultant. They visit at least once a year, unannounced, and can show up any time during operating hours. Complaint visits happen on top of that. NC is unusual: your license itself carries a **star rating from 1 to 5 stars**, and the inspector is also the one judging that rating.

- Agency: https://ncchildcare.ncdhhs.gov/
- Public inspection portal: https://ncchildcare.ncdhhs.gov/childcaresearch

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The law** — N.C. Gen. Stat. Chapter 110, Article 7 (the Child Care Facilities Act).
- **The operating rulebook** — 10A N.C. Admin. Code, Chapter 09. Effective November 1, 2024 version: https://ncchildcare.ncdhhs.gov/Portals/0/documents/pdf/C/Chapter_9_Child_Care_Rules_effective_November_1_2024.pdf
- **Ratio rule** — 10A NCAC 09 .0713. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/north-carolina/10A-N-C-Admin-Code-09-0713
- **Enhanced ratio rule for 4- and 5-star centers** — 10A NCAC 09 .2818.
- **Staff qualification and background check rule** — 10A NCAC 09 .2703.
- **Star rating rule series** — 10A NCAC 09 .2800 series (this controls your license).

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age group (single-age) | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (0–12 months) | 1:5 | 10 |
| Toddlers (12–24 months) | 1:6 | 12 |
| 2–3 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 3–4 years | 1:15 | 25 |
| 4–5 years | 1:20 | 25 |
| School-age (5+ years) | 1:25 | 25 |

If you mix ages in one group, you must use the ratio for the **youngest child** in the group. 4- and 5-star centers must meet **tighter "enhanced" ratios** under 10A NCAC 09 .2818. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive unannounced and sign in.
2. They check the license is posted with the star rating visible.
3. They walk every classroom and count children against staff.
4. They pull child files looking for immunization records and health assessments.
5. They pull staff files looking for background checks, NC Early Educator Certification, and training records.
6. They check the Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan and drill log.
7. They verify required wall postings and daily attendance.
8. They close out a **DCDEE Visit Report**. The report is then posted to your public page on the legacy Visits portal: https://ncchildcaresearch.dhhs.state.nc.us/Visits.asp?Facility_ID=YOUR_ID

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio violations** — cited as 10A NCAC 09 .0713.
2. **Missing or stale Criminal Background Check (CBC)** — re-check required every 3 years per 10A NCAC 09 .2703.
3. **Missing NC Early Educator Certification** for lead teachers.
4. **Missing ITS-SIDS training** for any staff assigned to an infant/toddler room (required annually).
5. **Missing Responsible Individuals List (RIL) check** — this is a NC-only child abuse registry check that people forget.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment application** with parent/guardian, 2+ emergency contacts, and authorized pick-up list.
- **Immunization record** meeting the NC Immunization Rules (DTaP, polio, MMR, Hib, hep B, varicella, PCV, flu).
- **Medical/health assessment** signed by a physician, completed within 30 days of enrollment.
- **Allergy action plan** with a photo of the child if applicable.
- **Care plan** for any special needs or chronic condition.
- **Permissions** — transportation, field trips, water activities, photography.
- **Daily attendance record.**
- **Medication authorizations** for every medication, signed by parent.
- **Incident/accident reports** for anything that happened in care.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **Criminal Background Check (CBC)** — fingerprint-based, state SBI plus FBI. Submitted through NCABCMS: https://ncabcms.nc.gov/DCDEE/Applicant/
- **Responsible Individuals List (RIL)** check — the NC child abuse registry.
- **Sex offender registry** check.
- **NC Early Educator Certification** — EEC 5 or higher for lead teachers.
- **Pediatric CPR and First Aid** cards.
- **ITS-SIDS training** (annual) for any infant/toddler room staff.
- **Shaken baby / abusive head trauma** training (annual).
- **Child abuse recognition and reporting** training.
- **Emergency preparedness** training.
- **Annual in-service training hours** (15+ for staff; administrators above 3-star have their own minimum).
- **Staff health assessment** with TB screen at hire.

## What must be on your walls
- Your **Star-Rated License** (visible, current).
- **Most recent inspection summary.**
- **Emergency evacuation plan and routes.**
- **USDA non-discrimination poster** (if you serve CACFP meals).
- **Menu** (current week).
- **Daily schedule** by classroom.
- **Staff qualifications/credentials** posted for parents to see.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly (cite 10A NCAC 09).
- **Shelter-in-place and lockdown drills** — as required in the Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan, reviewed annually.
- **Daily attendance** — maintained in real time.
- **Daily health check** log for each child at arrival.
- **Medication log** when any med is given.
- **Incident/accident log.**
- **Diaper and sleep logs** for infant/toddler rooms.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Stand in each classroom and count. Do the ratio for the youngest child.
2. Look at the license on the wall. Is it current? Is the star rating visible?
3. Open the emergency folder. Is the fire drill log signed for this month?
4. Pull 3 random child files. Immunizations current? Health assessment signed by a doctor?
5. Pull 3 random staff files. CBC stamp not older than 3 years? CPR/First Aid current?
6. Walk the playground. Remove any broken or hazardous equipment.
7. Check cleaning supplies. Locked and out of reach of children?
8. Check cribs. Infants on their backs? No blankets, bumpers, or inclined sleepers?
9. Check the diapering area. Sanitizer within reach but not accessible to children?
10. Check that the CCHC review report is on file (NC requires a Child Care Health Consultant contract for centers with 6+ children).

## What happens if you fail
DCDEE can issue a **Written Warning**, move you to a **Special Provisional License**, hit you with a **Summary Suspension**, or **Revoke or Deny** your license. Each violation gets cited to a specific rule (like 10A NCAC 09 .0713). You get a corrective action period. Serious ratio, supervision, or abuse findings can trigger immediate closure. You have appeal rights, but the public Visits page shows every violation. Administrative Actions are posted in the monthly Statistical Detail report. Overview of penalties: https://www.northstatelawfirm.com/blog/penalties-for-childcare-facility-license-violations-in-north-carolina/

## The weird NC gotcha nobody tells you about
In North Carolina, **your star rating IS your license**. The 1–5 stars are printed right on the license certificate. You re-earn it every 3 years through an Environment Rating Scales (ERS) assessment — ITERS-3 for infants/toddlers, ECERS-3 for preschool, SACERS-U for school-age. On top of that, **any center with 6 or more children must contract with a Child Care Health Consultant (CCHC)** — a registered nurse, registered dietitian, or MD who conducts onsite reviews. Inspectors will ask to see that CCHC agreement. Most new owners don't know the CCHC contract is required until they fail on it.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a NC owner
NC owners' #1 pain is the **star rating re-assessment every 3 years** — it's both their license and their marketing. Losing a star publicly tanks enrollment. Drop this line: "Your CBC re-check is on a 3-year rolling clock per 10A NCAC 09 .2703 — one missed recheck is a violation posted to your public Visits.asp page." Also mention the **CCHC contract requirement for 6+ child centers** — a large share of NC owners don't realize it's mandatory and get cited for it. Today they're tracking all this in spreadsheets or paper binders, and the DCDEE portal shows every failed inspection forever. NC buyers are risk-averse because the public shaming is brutal.
