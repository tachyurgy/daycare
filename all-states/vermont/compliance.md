# Vermont Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
**Vermont Department for Children and Families (DCF), Child Development Division (CDD).** CDD operates the Bright Futures Information System (BFIS) as the public-facing registry and the provider/parent portal.

- Address: Vermont DCF, Child Development Division, 280 State Drive, Waterbury, VT 05671-1040
- Phone (Child Care Helpline): 800-649-2642
- Provider / parent portal: **BFIS** — https://www.brightfutures.dcf.state.vt.us

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Statute:** **33 V.S.A. Chapter 35** (Child Care Services), especially **33 V.S.A. § 3502** (CDD licensing authority) and **33 V.S.A. § 3511** (registered family child care homes). Related: 33 V.S.A. § 105(b), § 306(b) (DCF licensing authority).
- **Code of Vermont Rules (CVR):**
  - **CVR 13-171-004** — Licensing Regulations for **Center Based Child Care and Preschool Programs (CBCCPP)**
  - **CVR 13-171-005** — Licensing Regulations for **Registered and Licensed Family Child Care Homes**
  - **CVR 13-171-006** — After-School Programs
  - **CVR 13-162-009** — Children's Day Care Licensing Regulations for Non-Recurring Clientele (drop-in care)

## Facility Types Regulated
1. **Center Based Child Care and Preschool Program (CBCCPP)** — group care in a non-residential setting; CVR 13-171-004.
2. **Registered Family Child Care Home (RFCCH)** — up to 6 children full-time and 4 part-time (school-age), operator's residence; CVR 13-171-005.
3. **Licensed Family Child Care Home (LFCCH)** — larger home-based care with an assistant; up to 12 children with restrictions.
4. **After-School Program** — school-age only; CVR 13-171-006.
5. **Non-Recurring Drop-In Care** — occasional-use settings; CVR 13-162-009.

## Key Licensing Forms
- Application for Child Care License / Registration (in BFIS)
- Background Records Check (BRC) request — Vermont Crime Information Center (VCIC) + FBI fingerprint + Child Protection Registry + Sex Offender Registry + Adult Protective Services
- Health History & Immunization form (VT DOH approved or physician equivalent)
- Staff / Home Member Health Statement & TB screening
- Emergency Preparedness Plan template
- Medication authorization form
- Nutrition / meal plan
- Floor plan & exit map

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (CBCCPP — CVR 13-171-004)
| Age | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (6 wk – 12 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Toddler (12 – 24 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Young Toddler (24 – 36 mo) | 1:5 | 10 |
| Preschool (3 yr) | 1:7 | 14 |
| Preschool (4-5 yr, not in K) | 1:10 | 20 |
| School-age (K – 12 yr) | 1:13 | 26 |

Only staff working directly with children ≥90% of their assigned duty time count toward ratio.

**Registered FCCH (CVR 13-171-005):** max 6 children full-time + 4 part-time school-age, with sub-limits: no more than 2 under 24 months; no more than 6 children under kindergarten age including the provider's own.

**Licensed FCCH:** up to 12 children with a qualified assistant, same age sub-limits scaled.

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment form with emergency contacts, authorized pickups
- Current immunization record per Vermont DOH schedule (or signed exemption)
- Health history & physical (for center-based enrollment)
- Individual health / allergy / special needs plan where applicable
- Consent forms: transportation, photos, field trips, emergency treatment
- Daily attendance
- Incident reports
- Medication authorization & log
- STARS (Vermont's quality recognition system) documentation where participating

## Required Staff Records
- Application, resume, qualifications
- **Background Record Check (BRC)** — VCIC + FBI fingerprint + Child Protection Registry + Sex Offender Registry + Adult Protective Services. Required pre-hire; re-run every 5 years
- Health statement & TB screening
- CPR / First Aid certification (valid)
- Orientation documentation (within 30 days)
- Professional development: minimum annual hours tracked in the **Northern Lights Career Development Center registry**; hours required scale with role (center teachers typically 15-24 hrs/year)
- Mandated reporter training (VT 33 V.S.A. § 4913)
- Qualification documentation for director (education + experience), program leader, teacher

## Inspection Cadence
- **Initial licensing visit** prior to issuance.
- **Scheduled annual monitoring visit** (CBCCPP and LFCCH).
- **Unannounced monitoring visits** — CDD may visit at any time during operating hours.
- **Complaint investigations** initiated within statutory windows (typically 14-30 days depending on severity).
- RFCCH — unannounced monitoring on a rotating schedule (generally annually).

## Renewal Cadence
- Licenses/registrations are issued for **three (3) years** for centers and registered homes per CDD policy (subject to ongoing monitoring and compliance). Renewal applications submitted via BFIS with updated BRCs, fire, and health documentation.
- Some historical references cite annual renewal; current practice under CDD is triennial for compliant programs.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **STARS — STep Ahead Recognition System:** Vermont's tiered Quality Recognition & Improvement System (1-5 STARS). Participation is voluntary but tied to subsidy reimbursement rates. STARS ratings appear on every provider profile in BFIS.
- **Bright Futures Information System (BFIS):** the canonical data system for the state — handles licensing applications, renewals, subsidy administration (Child Care Financial Assistance Program / CCFAP), STARS, and the public provider directory. Integration with BFIS is effectively required for market participation.
- **Northern Lights Career Development Center:** runs the Vermont Early Childhood Career Registry — the state-recognized system of record for professional development hours and practitioner credentials.
- **Child Care Contribution (Act 76, 2023):** a 0.44% payroll tax funding Vermont's universal child care expansion; affects provider reimbursement structure.
- **Strictest infant ratio nationally:** 1:4 for infants AND toddlers (up to 24 months) is tighter than most states.
- **90% rule:** only staff spending ≥90% of assigned time directly with children count in ratio — eliminates floating admin / director ratio-shopping.
- **Relatively small market:** approximately **1,000-1,350 regulated programs** statewide (CBCCPP + registered/licensed family homes + after-school). Chittenden County (Burlington, S. Burlington, Essex, Winooski) dominates; Rutland, Washington (Montpelier/Barre), Windsor, and Franklin counties are secondary.
