# How to Pass Your Iowa Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector is a **licensing consultant** from the **Iowa Department of Health and Human Services (Iowa HHS), Bureau of Child Care**. Iowa HHS is the only agency that licenses child care centers and registers child development homes statewide. Iowa requires **an annual unannounced on-site visit** at every licensed center (IAC 441-109.3), plus complaint-driven follow-ups. Every visit, every complaint, and every corrective action is posted on the public **Iowa HHS Titan Compliance Report** portal and mirrored on **Iowa Child Care Connect**.

- Agency: https://hhs.iowa.gov/programs/programs-and-services/child-care
- Public compliance search: https://secureapp.dhs.state.ia.us/dhs_titan_public/ChildCare/ComplianceReport
- Parent-facing portal: https://iachildcareconnect.org

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

**Child Care Centers** (IAC 441-109.8):

| Age | One staff for this many kids |
|---|---|
| 0–12 months | 1:4 |
| 13–23 months | 1:4 |
| 2-year-olds | 1:6 |
| 3-year-olds | 1:8 |
| 4-year-olds | 1:12 |
| 5-year-olds | 1:15 |
| School-age (6+) | 1:15 |

**Mixed-age rule:** youngest child's ratio applies. **Combined 3–5 yr groups: 1:12.**

**Opening / closing two-hour exception:** one staff may care for up to 7 children if ≤4 are under 2.

**When >8 children are present (including in a transport vehicle), at least 2 adults must be on premises.**

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Ratios apply at all times. Do not round up.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced and shows Iowa HHS ID.
2. Checks your posted license, Iowa Quality for Kids (IQ4K) rating, and emergency plan.
3. Walks each room and counts kids vs. staff against IAC 441-109.8.
4. Pulls staff files: **Iowa Centralized Employee Registry (CER)**, Iowa criminal + child abuse + sex-offender + dependent adult abuse registry checks, **FBI fingerprint** for hires after 9/30/2018, mandated-reporter training, annual 10-hour training log, CPR + Pediatric First Aid.
5. Pulls child files: enrollment, Iowa-compliant immunization record (Iowa Code 139A), physical exam within 6 months, allergy/diet plan, emergency authorization.
6. Reviews fire drill, tornado drill, and shelter-in-place logs.
7. Writes cited rules (IAC 441-109.x) with finding narrative and corrective action deadline.
8. Files everything into Titan within 72 hours. It's public immediately.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Iowa
1. **Ratio not met** (IAC 441-109.8), especially during open/close and transport.
2. **FBI fingerprint missing** for new hires after 9/30/2018 — this is a newer rule that catches owners off guard.
3. **Mandated reporter training** not refreshed — newer rule under HF 2502 (2019) requires 1 hour at hire + 2 hours every 3 years.
4. **Tornado drill log** — required **monthly March–November** in Iowa; frequently forgotten.
5. **Annual training hours** short — 10 hours/year for all staff (with ≥2 in Iowa's Early Learning Standards); directors 12.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment information & emergency contacts
- **Immunization record** per Iowa Code 139A (or religious/medical exemption via Form 470-0603)
- Physical exam within **6 months** of enrollment
- Allergy & diet statement; **Form 470-0720 Physical Care Plan** if applicable
- Authorization for emergency care & pickup
- Daily attendance
- Incident / injury report log
- Parent acknowledgment of child-abuse reporting policy

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application & reference checks
- **Iowa Centralized Employee Registry (CER)** enrollment on hire
- Iowa DCI criminal history + Iowa child abuse registry + Iowa sex offender registry + dependent adult abuse registry
- **FBI fingerprint** (hires after 9/30/2018)
- Out-of-state child-abuse & criminal checks if applicant lived outside Iowa in past 5 years
- Health statement; TB if risk factors
- **Mandatory reporter training** within 6 months; refreshed every 3 years (Iowa Code 232.69)
- Universal precautions / bloodborne pathogens — initial + annual
- Orientation before unsupervised contact
- **Annual training — 10 hours** (≥2 in Iowa's Early Learning Standards); directors 12 hours
- CPR + Pediatric First Aid — renewed every 2 years
- SIDS + safe sleep training (infant staff)
- Medication administration training (if administering)

## What must be on your walls
- Current license
- IQ4K rating card (if rated)
- Ratio chart (by age)
- Evacuation routes (each room)
- Tornado shelter plan and route
- Menu (current week)
- Iowa HHS Complaint Hotline: 866-448-4605

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly
- **Tornado drills** — monthly **March through November** (Iowa-specific)
- **Shelter-in-place / disaster drill** — annual
- **Daily attendance**
- **Medication log**
- **Injury / incident log**
- **Pesticide / radon** notification to parents if applicable

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff in every room. Check the 2-adult rule if >8 kids.
2. Confirm license, ratios, tornado plan, fire map posted.
3. Fire drill log: this month logged?
4. Tornado drill log: is this month in there if it's March–November?
5. Open three staff files: CER, Iowa registry checks, FBI print (post-9/30/2018 hires), mandated reporter, 10-hour training.
6. Open three child files: immunization, physical within 6 months, allergy plan.
7. Kitchen clean, allergy plan visible, chemicals locked.
8. Sleep area: backs only, firm mattress, no soft bedding, one infant per crib.
9. Playground: no broken equipment, standing water removed.
10. Greet the inspector. Offer a seat. Be factual.

## What happens if you fail
Iowa HHS writes a **Compliance Evaluation** showing non-compliant items and corrective-action due dates. Serious or repeat issues escalate through **corrective action → sanction → enforcement (suspension / revocation)**. Substantiated complaints list as "substantiated," "unsubstantiated," or "partially substantiated" and are public on Titan. Licenses can be issued for up to **2 years**, with annual monitoring; a provisional 6-month license is used for new facilities. Appeals go through the Iowa HHS Hearing Unit. Every entry is public on Titan and may appear on `data.iowa.gov`.

## The weird Iowa gotcha nobody tells you about
**Tornado drills must happen every month from March through November** — nine months a year — and each one must be logged with date and time. Iowa is the only state that stretches the tornado-drill season that long (most Midwest states cap at October; a few like Oklahoma stop in October too). Combine it with the **2-adult rule for any group >8 children, including in transport vehicles** — which means if you're a small center running a 10-child school-pickup van, one driver is not enough. Most owners don't know about the in-vehicle adult rule until they get cited.
