# Alaska Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
**Alaska Department of Health (DOH), Division of Public Assistance (DPA), Child Care Program Office (CCPO).** CCPO licenses and monitors child care facilities outside the Municipality of Anchorage. **The Municipality of Anchorage Child Care Licensing Program** is a delegated local authority that licenses facilities inside Anchorage municipal boundaries under parallel regulations.

- State CCPO address: 619 E. Ship Creek Ave., Suite 230, Anchorage, AK 99501
- State CCPO phone: (907) 269-4500 | Toll-free: 888-268-4632
- Email: CCPO@alaska.gov / doh.ccpo.info@alaska.gov
- Provider portal: **AKCCIS** (Alaska Child Care Information System) — https://akccis.com

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Statute:** **AS 47.32** (Centralized Licensing of Entities That Provide Care) and **AS 47.25.001–47.25.095** (Child Care Grant Program / subsidy authority).
- **Administrative Code:** **7 AAC 57** — Child Care Facilities Licensing. Key articles:
  - 7 AAC 57.010–.050 — Application & licensing
  - 7 AAC 57.200–.299 — Administration (records, insurance, policies)
  - 7 AAC 57.300–.399 — Facility & environment
  - 7 AAC 57.500–.599 — Care and Services (ratios, supervision, nutrition)
  - 7 AAC 57.900 — Definitions
- **Municipal Anchorage:** Anchorage Municipal Code Chapter 16.55 mirrors and in some areas exceeds 7 AAC 57.

## Facility Types Regulated
1. **Child Care Home** — in provider's residence, up to **8 children** under age 13, with no more than 3 under 30 months (7 AAC 57.900 definition).
2. **Group Child Care Home** — in a residence, up to **12 children** with additional staffing.
3. **Child Care Center** — non-residential facility, 13+ children.
4. **School-Age Program** — care for children 5-12 before/after school.
5. Legally exempt / Approved Relative / In-Home / Tribal-approved / DoD-certified providers — may participate in CCAP (Child Care Assistance Program) without full licensure.

## Key Licensing Forms
- CC-501 — Application for Child Care Facility License
- CC-35 / CC-36 — Background Check Request (ABI fingerprint + FBI)
- CC-61 — Parent Guide / "Teddy Bear Letter" (required posting)
- Staff health statement, TB screening
- Child enrollment form with emergency contacts
- CC-80 series — Incident / serious injury report
- Fire Marshal inspection
- Environmental health inspection (DEC or local)

## Child-to-Caregiver Ratios & Group Sizes (7 AAC 57.505 — centers)
| Age | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (birth – 18 mo) | 1:5 | 10 |
| Toddler (19 – 36 mo) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Preschool (3 & 4 yr) | 1:10 | 20 |
| Kindergarten / older | 1:14 | 28 |
| School-age (6+) | 1:18 | 36 |

**Mixed-age grouping rule:** ratio and group size default to the youngest child's age band.

**Home settings (7 AAC 57.405 / .900):** no more than 8 children total under age 13, of which no more than 3 may be under 30 months. Group Home: up to 12 children with a second qualified caregiver.

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment application with emergency contacts, authorized pickups
- Immunization record per Alaska DOH schedule (or valid exemption)
- Health history and physical exam
- Written permissions (transportation, outings, photos)
- Allergy / medical / individual care plan where needed
- Daily attendance and sign-in/out with caregiver verification
- Incident / injury logs (serious incidents reported to CCPO within 24 hours)
- Medication administration record

## Required Staff Records
- Application with education, training, experience
- **Background check:** Alaska Barrier Crimes check (AS 47.05.300), State ABI fingerprint + FBI, sex offender registry, central child abuse registry — required before unsupervised contact
- CPR / First Aid / (pediatric) certification
- TB screening and health statement
- Mandated reporter training acknowledgement
- Orientation within 90 days (per 7 AAC 57.535)
- Continuing professional development hours (center staff must obtain **ongoing annual training** per 7 AAC 57.220 — minimum 12-24 hours depending on role, tracked in the SEED Alaska Early Childhood Registry)
- Documented qualifications for director, lead teachers, aides

## Inspection Cadence
- **Initial licensing inspection** prior to license issuance.
- **Annual renewal inspection** (announced) by CCPO or Municipality of Anchorage licensor.
- **Random unannounced monitoring visits** during the license term.
- **Complaint investigations** as received; serious incident reports within 24 hrs.

## Renewal Cadence
- Licenses are issued for **two (2) years** (biennial). Providers submit renewal packet with updated fire / environmental / background check documentation at least 30 days before expiration.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Alaska Barrier Crimes Act (AS 47.05.310):** a specific, enumerated list of crimes that are disqualifying for child care employment; variance requests go to DOH Background Check Unit.
- **SEED Registry:** Statewide early childhood professional registry (`seedalaska.org`) is the canonical location for tracking provider training hours. Many PD requirements are verified through SEED.
- **Tiered geographies:** CCPO licenses statewide; Municipality of Anchorage is a delegated licensor with concurrent jurisdiction inside city limits. Providers in Anchorage operate under both 7 AAC 57 and AMC 16.55.
- **Unique facility categories:** Alaska recognizes Tribal-approved, Approved Relative, DoD/Coast Guard certified, and In-Home CCAP-enrolled providers — legally exempt from licensing but tracked by CCPO for subsidy purposes.
- **AKCCIS Provider Portal:** akccis.com is the current online intake/renewal/monitoring system (replaces the older ICCIS database).
- **Small state scale:** roughly **500-700 licensed child care facilities** statewide. Anchorage, Fairbanks, Mat-Su Valley (Wasilla/Palmer), Juneau, and Kenai Peninsula are the five primary markets.
- **Serious incident reporting:** deaths, hospitalizations, medication errors, allegations of abuse must be reported to CCPO within 24 hours per 7 AAC 57.545.
- **Posting requirements:** Parent Guide (CC-61), license, fire inspection, most recent monitoring report must be visibly posted.
