1. Licensing Authority

  • Agency: https://www.michigan.gov/mileap/early-childhood-education/cclb

6. Violations & Inspection Data new

Michigan publishes inspection reports, special investigation reports, and disciplinary actions for every licensed provider through the MiLEAP-operated Salesforce portal and a legacy LARA archive that remains live.

Sources

  • CCHIRP Statewide Facility Search — link. Salesforce Experience Cloud; inspection reports render as PDFs inside the facility detail pane.
  • Legacy LARA search — link. ASP.NET. PDFs available for every inspection since July 1, 2002 via GetDocument.aspx?DocID=<int>.
  • Disciplinary-action list — revoked / suspended / non-renewed (5-year rolling).
  • MiLEAP press feed — summary-suspension announcements (e.g. Washtenaw County 2025-02-12, Calhoun County 2026-04-08).

Freshness

  • CCHIRP: near-real-time; consultant uploads visible within 1–3 business days.
  • LARA legacy: mirrored daily from same back-end.
  • Disciplinary-action list: updated as adverse actions become final.

Access Strategy

  • Playwright (Chromium) against cclb.my.site.com/micchirp/s/ — search, click through facilities, download PDFs rendered in the Licensing History component. Rate ≤ 1 req/sec per worker; 3–5 concurrent workers.
  • Requests + BeautifulSoup for the legacy LARA search — facility pages at Profile.aspx?FacilityId=<int> and PDFs at GetDocument.aspx?DocID=<int>.
  • Single-page scrape for the disciplinary list.
FOIA backup. Michigan FOIA (MCL 15.231 et seq.) — 5 business days initial response, 10-day extension. MiLEAP FOIA office: FOIA@michigan.gov; CCLB: MiLEAP-CCLB-Help@michigan.gov. Use for records older than the 3-year rolling public archive and for structured CSV exports.

7. Outreach Signals (Hot-Lead Detection)

  • Repeat violations on the CCHIRP page for the same R-number across two consecutive monitoring cycles — signals a compliance gap the owner knows about. Trigger a targeted outreach referencing the specific rule.
  • License on provisional status (6 months, non-renewable) — owner has an active to-do list from CCLB and a hard countdown. High willingness to pay for compliance tooling.
  • Recent appearance on the disciplinary-action list — any provider whose license was suspended/revoked and who is attempting a fresh application under a new entity (common pattern; searchable by address match).
  • Great Start to Quality below "Empowered" — subsidy reimbursement gated at 3-star-equivalent; facilities below that line lose revenue and may invest in QRIS improvement tools.
  • Press-release summary suspensions — every MiLEAP press release names a facility and gives enough detail to cold-call the owner or their successor within 24–48 hours.
