# Oklahoma — Child Care Licensing Compliance Reference

_Compiled 2026-04-18 for ComplianceKit. All citations are to publicly-available primary sources — verify before relying in production._

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Oklahoma Child Care Facilities Licensing Act**, 10 O.S. §§ 401–410
- **Oklahoma Administrative Code (OAC) Title 340, Chapter 110, Subchapter 3** — Licensing Standards for Child Care Facilities
  - Part 15 (§§ 340:110-3-281 to 340:110-3-310) — Centers, Day Camps, Drop-Ins, Out-of-School Time, Part-Day, Sick-Child programs
  - Part 3/5 — Family Child Care Homes and Large Family Child Care Homes
  - **OAC 340 Appendix GG** — Ratios and Group Sizes
- Program-wide rulebook: OKDHS Pub. 14-05 "Licensing Requirements for Child Care Programs" (current edition 11/1/2025)

## Facility Types Regulated

| Type | Capacity / Notes |
|------|-------------------|
| Family Child Care Home | 1 primary caregiver, up to 7 children (incl. provider's own <5) |
| Large Family Child Care Home | 2 caregivers, up to 12 children |
| Child Care Center | Any program serving 30+ hrs/week, in non-residential setting |
| Part-Day Program | <30 hrs/week, typically preschool |
| Out-of-School Time Program | School-age before/after-school |
| Day Camp | Seasonal, <30 consecutive days |
| Drop-In Program | Occasional, short-duration care |
| Program for Sick Children | Specialized center for ill/mildly-ill kids |

Roughly **3,728 licensed programs** statewide (OKDHS figure, 2025).

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (OAC 340 Appendix GG)

**Center ratios / max group size:**

| Age Band | Ratio | Max Group |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Infant (0–12 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Toddler (12–24 mo) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Two-year-olds | 1:8 | 16 |
| Three-year-olds | 1:12 | 24 |
| Four-year-olds | 1:15 | 30 |
| Five-year-olds (not in school) | 1:15 | 30 |
| School-age (6–12) | 1:15 | 30 |

**Family Child Care Home:** 1:7 total, of which ≤4 may be under 24 months and ≤2 may be under 12 months.

**Large Family Home:** 2 caregivers : up to 12 children, with sub-limits on under-2s.

Ratios must be maintained **at all times** (including nap, outdoor, meals); only "teaching personnel" count in ratio.

## Required Child Records (center file per child)

- Enrollment & emergency information (Form 07LC010E)
- Parent/guardian authorization (pickup, field trips, medical)
- **Immunization record** per Okla. State Dept. of Health schedule (or signed exemption)
- Health assessment / physical within 30 days of enrollment
- Allergy / special diet / medication authorization
- Signed parent handbook acknowledgment
- Incident / injury reports
- Daily sign-in/sign-out (retained 1 year)

## Required Staff Records (per employee)

- Employment application & signed ethics / policies acknowledgment
- **OSBI + national fingerprint background check** (SAFETY Act, renewed every 5 yrs)
- Child Care Restricted Registry check (initial + at each hire)
- TB screening / health statement
- **Pre-service orientation (within 14 days)** + 12 clock hours Entry-Level Training (new staff)
- Annual professional development: **min. 12 clock hours for aides, 20 for teachers, 30 for directors**
- CPR + Pediatric First Aid (required for ≥1 person on premises at all times)
- ITS-SIDS training (infant rooms)
- Medication administration training (if administering)
- Mandated reporter acknowledgment

## Inspection Cadence

- **Unannounced monitoring visits at least 1× per year** (typically 2–4 for centers)
- Additional visits for complaints, Reaching for the Stars QRIS assessments, and initial/renewal
- Inspection reports & history are public at the OK Child Care Locator (childcarefind.okdhs.org)

## Renewal Cadence

- **Annual renewal**
- Fee schedule tied to capacity (center $25–$50 typical, family home ~$10)
- Renewal requires: current immunization records, staff trainings up to date, passed most-recent inspection, paid fee

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Reaching for the Stars** QRIS — voluntary 1-Star / 2-Star / 3-Star rating tied to subsidy reimbursement rates. Most marketed centers are Star-rated.
- **Oklahoma SAFETY Act** — mandatory fingerprint + name-based background check for every adult on premises (including household members in home-based).
- **Safe Sleep & ITS-SIDS** — required training; infant room posting; no blankets/soft bedding.
- **Weapons restriction** — firearms must be unloaded, locked, inaccessible, ammunition stored separately (OAC 340:110-3-283).
- **Tornado drills** — monthly March–October (climate-specific).
- **Immunization** — follows Oklahoma State Department of Health childhood schedule; philosophical & religious exemptions permitted with notarized affidavit.
- **Transportation** — child-transportation-specific rules (driver MVR check, 15-passenger van ban for under-5s, arrival/departure check-off).
