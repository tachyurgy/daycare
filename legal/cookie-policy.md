## 1. What Is a Cookie?

A cookie is a small text file stored on your device by your browser when you visit a website. Cookies allow sites to remember information about your visit, such as your login state and preferences. Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, and pixel tags; in this policy, we refer to them collectively as "cookies."

We use only two categories of cookies: **strictly necessary** and **analytics**. We do **not** use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

## 3. What We Do Not Use

We do not use:

- Advertising cookies.
- Third-party social-media tracking pixels.
- Cross-site tracking cookies.
- Fingerprinting libraries whose primary purpose is tracking.
- Data brokers of any kind.

We also do **not** sell or share personal information, including information collected via cookies, as those terms are defined under CCPA/CPRA, TDPSA, and FDBR.

## 4. Your Choices

### 4.1 Cookie Banner

On your first visit, you will see a cookie banner. You can choose to accept or reject analytics cookies. Your choice is stored in a first-party preference cookie (`ck_consent`) and respected on subsequent visits.

### 4.2 Browser Controls

Most browsers let you block or delete cookies via their settings. If you block strictly necessary cookies, parts of the Site and Service may not function.

### 4.3 Global Privacy Control (GPC)

We recognize the [Global Privacy Control](https://globalprivacycontrol.org/) signal as a valid opt-out of sale or sharing of personal information for California residents. Because we do not sell or share personal information, the GPC signal is logged for audit purposes and confirms that analytics cookies will not be set if you have signaled a preference.

### 4.4 Do Not Track

We do not respond to browser "Do Not Track" signals because there is no industry consensus on interpretation. However, we do not track users across third-party sites regardless of DNT.

### 4.5 Opt-Out of Analytics

To opt out of analytics cookies, you can:

1. Reject analytics in our cookie banner.
2. Enable GPC in your browser (recommended).
3. Use your browser's Do Not Track setting (honored on a best-effort basis).
4. Block third-party cookies in your browser.
