# Alabama Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority

**Alabama Department of Human Resources (DHR), Child Care Services Division.**

- Website: https://dhr.alabama.gov/child-care/
- Phone: (334) 242-1425 or (866) 528-1694
- Public directory: https://apps.dhr.alabama.gov/daycare/daycare_search (centers) and https://apps.dhr.alabama.gov/Child_Care_Services/Lic_Day_Care_Homes (homes)

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Ala. Code § 38-7-1 et seq.** — Alabama Child Care Act of 1971 (licensing statute).
- **Ala. Admin. Code Chapter 660-5-26** — Minimum Standards for Day Care Centers and Nighttime Centers.
- **Ala. Admin. Code Chapter 660-5-27** — Minimum Standards for Family Day Care Homes and Family Nighttime Homes / Group Day Care Homes and Group Nighttime Homes.
- **Ala. Admin. Code r. 660-5-20-.04** — Child Care Program rules.
- **Ala. Code § 38-13** — Background Checks (SCAN / child abuse & neglect registry).
- **2018 "Child Care Safety Act"** (AL Act 2018-390) — expanded licensing to many previously-exempt religious-affiliated day cares that receive state/federal funds.

## Facility Types Regulated

1. **Day Care Center** — 13+ children in non-residential setting.
2. **Nighttime Center** — center providing care between 9 p.m. and 5 a.m.
3. **Family Day Care Home** — up to 6 children (including provider's own), in the provider's home.
4. **Group Day Care Home** — 7-12 children, in the provider's home (or in a non-residential setting if 7-12 children).
5. **Family / Group Nighttime Homes** — corresponding home-based nighttime care.
6. **License-Exempt** — church-affiliated centers that elect exemption **and do not receive state/federal funds**; exempt centers must still file notice and meet health and safety basics (Act 2018-390).

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (Rule 660-5-26)

Centers may not exceed the following minimum staff-to-child ratios:

| Age Group | Staff-to-Child Ratio |
|---|---|
| 0 – 18 months (infants) | 1:5 |
| 18 months – 2½ years | 1:7 |
| 24 – 36 months | 1:8 |
| 2½ – 4 years | 1:11 |
| 4 years – school-age | 1:18 |
| School-age | 1:21 (commonly applied; verify in current handbook) |

Rules require:
- Ratios maintained **at all times**, including during nap time, transport, and water/swimming activities (which require tightened ratios).
- **No mixed-age group may exceed the ratio for the youngest child** in the group.
- For swimming or wading, additional watchers are required (typically an additional adult plus CPR-certified lifeguard for pools > 3 ft).
- Maximum group size is not a separate numeric cap in Alabama rule; square footage (35 sq ft indoor / 75 sq ft outdoor per child) and supervision govern group structure.

## Required Child Records

- Completed enrollment form (child name, DOB, parent info, emergency contacts, authorized pick-ups)
- Current Alabama Certificate of Immunization (blue card, IMM 50/51) or religious/medical exemption — per Ala. Admin. Code 420-4-3
- Physical/health examination records as required
- Medication authorization and administration logs
- Signed authorization for transportation, field trips, and emergency medical care
- Daily attendance sign-in/sign-out
- Incident/accident reports
- Allergy / special-needs plan
- Written notice of discipline policy receipt

## Required Staff Records

- Application / résumé, references, qualification documentation (director and caregiver qualifications per 660-5-26)
- **ABI + FBI fingerprint clearance** — required prior to unsupervised contact with children
- **Central Registry on Child Abuse/Neglect (SCAN / CA/N) clearance** — required for all staff and volunteers
- Current infant-child First Aid and CPR certification (at least one certified staff on duty at all times)
- TB screening / health statement
- Mandatory orientation and at least **12 clock hours of annual in-service training** (per 660-5-26)
- Documentation of Safe Sleep, Shaken Baby Syndrome, and mandated-reporter training
- Performance evaluations / disciplinary actions

## Inspection Cadence

- **At least one unannounced annual inspection** of each licensed day care center (per 660-5-26 and DHR policy).
- **Monitoring visits** may be conducted quarterly for newer centers or those with prior deficiencies.
- **Complaint-based** inspections are unannounced and have no frequency cap.
- Fire Marshal and Health Department conduct separate annual inspections; reports must be current and on file with **no violations cited** at time of licensing review.

## Renewal Cadence

- Day care center license is issued for **one year** and must be renewed annually (Ala. Admin. Code 660-5-26).
- Renewal requires current fire inspection, current health (environmental / sanitation) inspection, and full clearance of any outstanding corrective action plan.
- Background checks must be renewed per 38-13-7 (typically every 5 years, or on triggering events).

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Child Care Safety Act (2018-390)** — requires previously-exempt church child care facilities that receive state or federal funds (including CCDBG subsidies) to be licensed and follow minimum standards.
- **Nighttime care** has distinct rule Chapter 660-5-26 (Nighttime Centers) — includes supervision during sleeping hours.
- **Alabama Quality STARS** — voluntary QRIS; tied to CCDBG bonus payments.
- **Mandated reporter** duties apply to all staff, per Ala. Code § 26-14.
- **Transportation**: child-check ("child left behind") protocols and annual vehicle inspection required.
- **Outdoor play** — 75 sq ft per child, fenced area, shade required.
- **Medication administration** requires written parent authorization, original container, log; only trained staff may administer.
- **Corporal punishment is prohibited**; discipline policy must be written and provided to parents.
- Exempt church-operated day cares must still post notice of exempt status and provide parents written disclosure.
