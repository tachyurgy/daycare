# How to Pass Your California Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the California Department of Social Services (CDSS), Community Care Licensing Division (CCLD). They are called a **Licensing Program Analyst**, or **LPA**. LPAs come from 21 regional offices across the state. They must visit each licensed center **at least once every three years**. Risky sites and complaint calls get visits more often. Routine visits are always unannounced.

- Agency: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/child-care-licensing
- Public inspection search: https://www.ccld.dss.ca.gov/carefacilitysearch/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — California Health & Safety Code § 1596.70 and after. This is the Child Day Care Facilities Act. [https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displayText.xhtml?lawCode=HSC&division=2.&title=&part=&chapter=3.4.&article=](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displayText.xhtml?lawCode=HSC&division=2.&title=&part=&chapter=3.4.&article=)
- **The binding rulebook** — Title 22 of the California Code of Regulations, Division 12. Every rule the LPA cites comes from here. [https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/community-care-licensing/policy/child-care-regulations](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/community-care-licensing/policy/child-care-regulations)
- **The inspector's framework** — the CARE Tools (Compliance and Regulatory Enforcement). This is what every LPA uses in the field.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age | Ratio |
|---|---|
| Infant (0–2 years) | 1 staff for every 4 children |
| Toddler / Preschool (2–6 years) | 1 staff for every 12 children |
| School-Age (6–14 years) | 1 staff for every 15 children |

Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive unannounced and ask to see the director.
2. They check that your license is posted where parents can see it.
3. They walk every classroom and count children against adult staff.
4. They check exits, cleaning chemicals, medications, and outdoor play spaces.
5. They pull a sample of child files and check each document.
6. They pull a sample of staff files and check Live Scan clearances and training.
7. They review sign-in/sign-out logs, incident reports, and medication logs.
8. They close with an exit interview, list any findings, and post a LIC 9213 Notice of Site Visit at the facility.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio slips.** Too few adults for the number of infants or preschoolers in the room. Title 22 Div. 12.
2. **Missing Live Scan or background clearance for a staff member.** A Type A citation every time.
3. **Incomplete child files** — a missing immunization record, missing LIC 701, or no LIC 702 consent for emergency care.
4. **Locked-cabinet failures** — cleaning chemicals, medications, or sharps not stored out of reach.
5. **Missing or expired CPR / pediatric First Aid** for the staff on duty.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **LIC 700** — Identification and Emergency Information. Basic contacts and emergency pickups.
- **LIC 701** — Physician's Report for the child. A doctor signs off on health.
- **LIC 702** — Consent for Emergency Medical Treatment.
- **Current immunization record** per the California Department of Public Health schedule, or a valid exemption.
- **TB test clearance.**
- **Signed consents** for transportation, field trips, and photography.
- **LIC 9221** parent consent for any medication + **LIC 622** medication administration log.
- **Daily sign-in / sign-out log.**
- **Infant center only:** a quarterly needs/services plan, a feeding plan, a toilet-training plan, and **LIC 9227** individual infant sleeping plan.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **LIC 508** — Criminal Record Statement.
- **Live Scan fingerprint clearance** from CA DOJ and FBI. This must clear **before** the person has contact with children.
- **Child Abuse Central Index (CACI) check.**
- **Sex offender registry check.**
- **Health screening + TB clearance.**
- **CPR and pediatric First Aid card** — current.
- **Mandated reporter training** certificate (AB 1207).
- **Role qualification documents** from the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing Child Development Permit Matrix.

## What must be on your walls
- **Current license** — posted where parents can see it.
- **LIC 9213 Notice of Site Visit** after an inspection — leave it up.
- **Emergency exit plan** in each room.
- **Daily schedule and menu** visible to parents.
- **Personal Rights form (LIC 613A)** posted in a common area.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Daily attendance** — every child, every day, signed in and out.
- **Incident and injury reports** — written up the day it happens.
- **Medication log (LIC 622)** — every dose, every time.
- **Fire and disaster drills** — Title 22 requires documented drills and a written emergency plan on file.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Check the license is posted and current.
2. Count each room. Do the numbers match staff on the floor right now?
3. Lock the cleaning-chemical cabinet. Lock the medication cabinet.
4. Are all outlets covered in the rooms where children under 3 play?
5. Is the fire exit path clear?
6. Is every infant sleeping on their back, in a crib, with no blankets or soft items?
7. Pull three random child files. Is each one complete?
8. Pull three random staff files. Live Scan cleared? CPR current?
9. Is the sign-in/sign-out sheet current for today?
10. Is the LIC 9213 from your last visit still posted?

## What happens if you fail
CCLD classifies violations as **Type A** (immediate health, safety, or personal-rights risk) or **Type B** (affect operations but no immediate risk). The LPA writes a Corrective Action Plan with a due date. Serious or repeat findings can trigger a civil penalty assessment on form **LIC 421**, which you must post at the facility. CCLD can also move to suspend or revoke a license. Every inspection and every finding is public on the CCLD Facility Search (April 2015 forward). Source: Title 22 Div. 12 and the CCLD Facility Search.

## The weird California gotcha nobody tells you about
CCLD runs a **public inspection transparency website** that shows every deficiency and every correction plan for every licensed facility in the state, going back to April 2015. Parents use it. Competitors use it. Reporters use it. You cannot hide a bad visit. If you are in San Francisco or Berkeley, you also stack under **municipal child-care regulations** on top of Title 22 — a second health inspection and a separate business license. Most new owners miss this and get a surprise visit from the city.
