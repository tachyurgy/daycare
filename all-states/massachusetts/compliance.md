# Massachusetts Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
- **Agency:** Massachusetts Department of Early Education and Care (EEC)
- **Parent agency:** Executive Office of Education
- **URL:** https://www.mass.gov/orgs/department-of-early-education-and-care

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **606 CMR 7.00** — Standards for the Licensure or Approval of Family Child Care; Small Group and School Age and Large Group and School Age Child Care Programs
- **606 CMR 14.00** — Criminal and Background Record Checks (BRC)
- **Enabling statute:** M.G.L. c. 15D

## Facility Types Regulated
- **Family Child Care (FCC)** — home-based care for up to 6 children (or 10 with assistant)
- **Small Group and School Age** (formerly "Small Group") — up to 9 children in small settings
- **Large Group and School Age** — centers serving 10+ children (traditional center-based care)
- **Residential child care** (separately regulated)

## Key Licensing Forms
- **Application for a License** — EEC application for family, small group, or large group license
- **Background Record Check (BRC) Authorization Form** — required for all staff and household members 15+
- **Professional Qualifications Registry / EEC Certification** packets (Teacher, Lead Teacher, Director I/II)
- **Transportation Plan / Vehicle Inspection Form** (when transporting children)
- **Health Care Policy** template (required by 606 CMR 7.11)
- **Individual Health Care Plan (IHCP)** for children with chronic conditions/allergies
- **Injury and Incident Report**
- **Emergency Evacuation / Shelter-in-Place Plan**

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (per 606 CMR 7.10 — center-based)
| Age band | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (birth–15 months) | 1:3 | 7 |
| Toddlers (15–33 months) | 1:4 | 9 |
| Preschool (33 months–Kindergarten) | 1:10 | 20 |
| Kindergarten | 1:13 | 26 |
| School age (Grade 1–14 yrs) | 1:13 | 26 |

Mixed-age groups: ratio defaults to that of the youngest child.

**Family Child Care:** Up to 6 children under age 14, of whom no more than 3 can be under 2, and no more than 2 under 15 months. With an approved assistant, up to 10.

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment application with emergency contacts
- Physical exam within 12 months of enrollment, then annually for under-2 and biennially for 2+
- Lead screening documentation (MA DPH requires at 9–12 months and at 2, 3, 4 years)
- Immunization records per MA DPH schedule (MMR, DTaP, polio, Hep B, varicella, Hib, PCV, Hep A, influenza, rotavirus per age)
- Individual Health Care Plan (if applicable)
- Authorizations: emergency medical, photo, transportation, pick-up list
- Parent/guardian signed parent handbook acknowledgment

## Required Staff Records
- **Background Record Check (BRC)** — every 3 years (CORI, SORI, DCF, fingerprint-based national check)
- **EEC Professional Certification** (Teacher, Lead Teacher, Director) through EEC Professional Qualifications Registry
- **First Aid & CPR** — current certification (infant/child)
- **20 hours annual professional development** (Teacher/Lead Teacher)
- **Annual Orientation** for new staff (606 CMR 7.09(22))
- **Medication Administration Program (MAP)** certification if administering meds
- **Mandated Reporter training**
- Physical health statement / TB screening (where applicable)

## Inspection Cadence
- **Routine licensing visits:** typically every 2 years for license renewal; unannounced monitoring visits occur in-between
- **Complaint investigations** and **CPS investigations** as triggered
- Family Child Care visits: at least once every 2 years; Group/School Age: same cycle

## Renewal Cadence
- License is valid for **up to 2 years**; renewal requires an on-site visit
- Provisional license (new programs) typically 6 months; converts to a full license after compliance review

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Quality Rating and Improvement System (QRIS):** EEC-operated 4-level system — Level 1 (met licensing) through Level 4 (accredited / high quality). Tied to subsidy rate differentials and CFCE grants.
- **Massachusetts Commonwealth Cares for Children (C3) grant program** — stabilization funding requires attestation and compliance.
- **Massachusetts Universal Pre-K (UPK)** — optional program with additional standards.
- **DPH regulations 105 CMR 430.00** — sanitation and health code overlaid on EEC licensing
- **Lead paint inspection** required for programs in buildings constructed before 1978 serving children under 6
- **Staff-to-child ratio posted** and visible at each group area
- **Mandatory reporter** status for all early educators (M.G.L. c. 119, § 51A)
- **Transportation:** any vehicle used requires written plan, annual inspection, and driver BRC
- **C3 and EEC data reporting:** monthly enrollment and compliance attestations
- **Sleep safety** — ABCs (Alone, on Back, in Crib) for infants; soft bedding prohibited
