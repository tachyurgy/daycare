# How to Pass Your Pennsylvania Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the Pennsylvania Department of Human Services (DHS), Office of Child Development and Early Learning (OCDEL), Bureau of Certification Services. Your annual renewal inspection is announced. You can also get an **unannounced monitoring inspection at any time** under 55 Pa. Code § 3270.24(b). Complaint inspections are unannounced. Every inspection writes up on a Certification Inspection Instrument (CII) and gets posted publicly.

- Agency: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dhs/resources/for-providers/child-care-for-providers
- Public inspection search (COMPASS): https://www.compass.dhs.pa.gov/providersearch/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — Human Services Code, Articles IX and X (62 P.S. §§ 901–922 and 1001–1087).
- **Child Care Centers rulebook** — 55 Pa. Code Chapter 3270. [https://www.pacodeandbulletin.gov/Display/pacode?file=/secure/pacode/data/055/chapter3270/chap3270toc.html](https://www.pacodeandbulletin.gov/Display/pacode?file=/secure/pacode/data/055/chapter3270/chap3270toc.html)
- **Group Child Care Homes (7–12 children)** — 55 Pa. Code Chapter 3280.
- **Family Child Care Homes (4–6 children)** — 55 Pa. Code Chapter 3290.
- **Cross-cutting licensure procedures** — 55 Pa. Code Chapter 20.
- **Act 73 of 2007** — the Child Protective Services Law (CPSL) that sets background-check rules.
- **Quarterly Licensing Report** (public) — https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dhs/resources/data-reports/quarterly-licensing-report

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (birth – 1 year) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Young toddler (1 – 2 years) | 1:5 | 10 |
| Older toddler (2 – 3 years) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Preschool (3 years – kindergarten) | 1:10 | 20 |
| Young school-age (K – 3rd grade) | 1:12 | 24 |
| Older school-age (4th grade – 15 years) | 1:15 | 30 |

Inspectors count heads on the spot. Mixed-age groups follow the ratio of the youngest child. You must have **at least 2 staff on site whenever a child is present** — 55 Pa. Code § 3270.13.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive and sign in with the director.
2. They check the Certificate of Compliance is posted.
3. They walk each room and count children against staff.
4. They review child files — enrollment, immunizations, physical, emergency contacts.
5. They pull staff files and check PA Child Abuse History (CY 113), PA State Police, and FBI fingerprint clearances.
6. They review training records in the PA Key / Pennsylvania Professional Development Registry.
7. They check fire drills, fire-safety training, and the annual fire-marshal inspection certificate.
8. They close out with a CII. Violations go on a Plan of Correction (POC) with a due date.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Background-check gaps** — CY 113, SP4-164, or FBI fingerprint past the 60-month renewal. CPSL / Act 153.
2. **Ratio violations** — 55 Pa. Code § 3270.51.
3. **Missing the 2-staff-person rule** under § 3270.13 at opening or closing.
4. **Staff training hours below the 6-hour annual minimum** — § 3270.31.
5. **Mandated reporter training** not renewed (Act 31 of 2014 — 3 hours at hire, 2 hours every 2 years).

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **CD 51** — Child Enrollment / Health Record signed by the parent.
- **CD 54** — Emergency Contact & Authorization for Medical Care.
- **Immunization record** per the PA School Code (DTaP, polio, MMR, Hib, hep B, varicella, PCV, flu).
- **Physical exam / health assessment** at admission, updated annually.
- **Allergy and chronic-condition care plan.**
- **Transportation and field-trip permission slips.**
- **Authorization for emergency medical treatment.**
- **Medication administration log** when meds are given.
- **Daily attendance record.**
- **Accident / incident report log.**

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **CY 113 — PA Child Abuse History Clearance** (renewed every 60 months).
- **SP4-164 — PA State Police Criminal Record Check** (renewed every 60 months).
- **FBI fingerprint-based federal criminal check** (renewed every 60 months).
- **CD 94** — Staff Application and Orientation Record (orientation done within 90 days).
- **CD 95** — Staff Medical Assessment + TB screening within 60 days of hire, updated every 2 years.
- **6 hours approved child-care training per year** under § 3270.31 (directors have higher requirements).
- **First Aid + infant/child CPR** certification — at least one current staff on duty.
- **Annual fire safety training.**
- **Mandated reporter training** under Act 31 — 3 hours at hire, 2 hours every 2 years.
- **Pediatric abusive head trauma training** for staff working with infants / toddlers.
- **PA Key / PD Registry** profile.

## What must be on your walls
- **Certificate of Compliance** — posted in a public area.
- **Most recent inspection summary** (parents must be able to see it).
- **Fire evacuation plan** in each room.
- **Emergency contact / poison control** numbers.
- **Radon test result** posted next to the license (in radon-risk zones).

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Monthly fire drill** — documented.
- **Daily attendance log.**
- **Medication administration log** for every dose.
- **Accident / incident reports** same-day, parent signature.
- **Fire-safety inspection** annually by a local or state fire authority.
- **Radon test** every 3 years in applicable zones; posted with the license.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Is the Certificate of Compliance posted?
2. Count each room. Ratios good? Two staff on site?
3. Pull three staff files. CY 113, SP4-164, FBI — all within the 60-month window?
4. Pull three child files. Immunizations, physical, CD 51, CD 54 — all in?
5. Is the current month's fire-drill log filled in?
6. Is this year's mandated reporter training up to date?
7. PA Key / PD Registry up to date for every staff?
8. Lock the medication and cleaning-chemical cabinets.
9. Are outlet covers in the infant / toddler rooms?
10. If radon required — is the most recent test result posted?

## What happens if you fail
The inspector writes a Plan of Correction with a due date. Repeated or serious non-compliance can trigger a **Provisional certificate** — a short-term license under a formal correction plan. Further failure can mean denial, revocation, or surrender. Every adverse action is listed in the **DHS Quarterly Licensing Report**. Source: 55 Pa. Code Chapter 20 and the DHS Quarterly Licensing Report page.

## The weird Pennsylvania gotcha nobody tells you about
Everything rides on the **60-month background-check clock**. CY 113, SP4-164, and the FBI fingerprint each have to be renewed every 5 years — and **every** person 14 years or older in the home (if you run a Group or Family home) is included under Act 153. One household member whose CY 113 expired is enough to get you cited. Another surprise: under the Pennsylvania radon-zone rules, some counties require a radon test **every 3 years** and the result has to be posted next to your license. Operators coming from a non-radon state almost always miss this.
