# West Virginia — Child Care Compliance Overview

## Licensing Authority

**West Virginia Department of Human Services (DoHS) — Bureau for Family Assistance (BFA) — Division of Early Care and Education (ECE)**
*(formerly WV DHHR Bureau for Children and Families, BCF)*
- Address: 350 Capitol Street, Room 730, Charleston, WV 25301
- Web: https://bfa.wv.gov/child-care-centers (center licensing) and https://dhhr.wv.gov/bcf/Childcare (legacy portal)

Licensing work is performed by regional specialists under DoHS BFA Division of Early Care and Education, reflecting a 2024 reorganization splitting the former DHHR into separate Health and Human Services departments.

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Statute:** W. Va. Code §49-2-801 et seq. (Child Care; licensing authority).
- **Regulations:** **Title 78, Code of State Rules (CSR), Series 1 — "78 CSR 1"** — Child Care Centers Licensure.
  - Key sections: 78-1-3 (definitions), 78-1-4 (licensure procedures), 78-1-5 (inspection and investigation), 78-1-6 (licensee requirements), 78-1-7 (children's records), 78-1-8 (staffing), 78-1-9 (personnel training), 78-1-10 (group size / staff:child ratios), Appendices (including Appendix 78-1 E: Staff/Child Ratio Tables).
- **Child Care Centers** fall under 78 CSR 1. Family Child Care Homes & Family Child Care Facilities are governed by related series (not this document's scope).

## Facility Types Regulated

Per 78 CSR 1 definitions:

- **Child Care Center** — facility serving more children than a Family Child Care Home/Facility; staffing determined by staff:child ratio tables.
- **Family Child Care Facility** — smaller out-of-home program (separate rule series).
- **Family Child Care Home** — in-home, limited capacity.
- **Informal / Relative** care and **School-Age-Only** programs have separate provisions.
- **Day Camp** — school-age program when school not in session; not exceeding a defined duration.

## Key Licensing Forms

- **Center Licensure Application** (initial / renewal)
- **License Amendment Request**
- **Fire Marshal Inspection Certificate**
- **Health / Sanitation Inspection** (OEHS — Office of Environmental Health Services)
- **ECE-CC-14A** Home Information Packet (family child care related)

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (Appendix 78-1 E)

### Table A — Single-Age Groups

| Age of Children | Max Children per Qualified Staff | Max Group Size |
|---|---|---|
| 6 weeks – 12 months | 4 | 8 |
| 13 months – 24 months | 4 | 12 |
| 25 – 35 months (2 yrs) | 8 | 16 |
| 36 – 47 months (3 yrs) | 10 | 20 |
| 48 – 59 months (4 yrs) | 12 | 24 |
| 60 months – school-age (5 yrs) | 12 | 24 |
| School-age | 16 | 32 |

### Table B — Water Activities Ratios (Single-Age)

| Age | Max Children | Staff Required |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ 12 months | 1 | 1 |
| 13 – 24 months | 2 | 1 |
| 25 – 59 months | 4 | 1 |
| ≥ 60 months | 8 | 1 |

- At least **2 staff members must be on duty** when children are present.
- In **mixed-age groups**, the ratio for the **youngest child** governs (78-1-10.5.b.2).
- Evening/night care adds requirements for in-room staff supervising sleep.
- Preschool/5-and-under must not be grouped with children > 24 months in certain configurations (78-1-10.2.b).

## Required Child Records (78-1-7)

- Child's name, address, gender, date of birth (7.4.a)
- Parents' or guardians' names, addresses, phone numbers
- Emergency contacts and authorized pick-up
- Enrollment application & agreement
- Health history, allergies, chronic conditions
- **Immunization record** per WV DHHR schedule or medical/religious exemption
- Physician / clinic contact information
- Medication authorization and log
- School-age children: name/phone of school (7.4.n)
- Daily attendance record

## Required Staff Records (78-1-8 / 78-1-9)

- Employment application, resume, references
- **Criminal background check clearance** — WV State Police + FBI fingerprint + WV Child Abuse & Neglect Central Registry + Sex Offender Registry
- **TB risk assessment** repeated annually OR TB screening by licensed health care professional (8.5.g.4)
- **Orientation records** and signed acknowledgment of center policies
- **Annual professional development** (continuing ed hours)
- **Medication administration training** (from approved source, if staff administers meds) — may count toward annual PD
- **Prevention of abuse & neglect** training (may also count toward annual PD)
- **Child First Aid** certification & **age-appropriate CPR** certification (current; required for qualified staff member when only one qualified staff is on duty at open/close)
- **Safe sleep practices** training (for programs serving infants)
- Qualified staff member who works at least half the operating hours of any infant/toddler program
- Director qualifications documentation (9.1.h — early childhood education or child development degree / certification)
- **Annual evaluation of director** by governing body or licensee (6.1.a)

## Inspection Cadence (78-1-5)

- **Unannounced inspections** of all aspects of center operation and premises, at any time.
- Inspections are performed by **DoHS/BFA Division of Early Care and Education** licensing specialists.
- **Fire Marshal inspection** required following physical plant changes and periodically per AHJ policy.
- **County/local health department inspections** for food service and sanitation — positive report required for renewal.
- Non-compliance findings trigger corrective action; repeated/significant non-compliance may result in suspension, revocation, or conditions of operation.

## Renewal Cadence (78-1-4)

- Renewal application must be submitted **≥ 60 days prior to expiration** of current license (4.2.c).
- **Regular or renewal license:** issued for a period of **up to two (2) years** for a licensee in compliance (4.5.b).
- Center must submit positive fire marshal inspection + county health department inspection + food service inspection report with renewal application.
- **Immediate suspension** possible if Secretary finds center operation constitutes an immediate danger to children.
- Licensee/Secretary may impose **conditions of operation** as alternative to suspension.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Water-activity ratios** are separately tabulated in Appendix 78-1 E Table B — much stricter than routine ratios.
- **Nap/sleep supervision:** For children ≤ 12 months, each qualified staff required to meet ratio must be **in the nap area** and able to see and hear all children at all times. For children > 12 months in nap program, at least one qualified staff member must be in each area visually supervising.
- **Evening/nighttime care:** additional requirements — one qualified staff in each sleeping room hourly checks on each sleeping child.
- **Transportation:** no child may be left unattended in a vehicle at any time.
- **Qualified staff definition** excludes staff performing cooking, bookkeeping, or lifeguarding duties from counting toward ratio.
- **Youth Apprentice / Student Intern (≥ 17 yrs)** in second year of Child Development Specialist program (approved by WV DoE) may count in staff:child ratio but may not work alone.
- **Person-in-charge** must be designated to perform director duties during all hours of operation if director is absent.
- **Support staff** not counted in ratio, but may be reassigned to supervise children only in emergencies.
- **Substitute plan** must be documented — qualified substitute available if a staff absence exceeds 2 consecutive weeks.
- **Infant feeding:** breast milk recommended birth through 11 months; formula preparation standards per 78 CSR 1 Appendix.
