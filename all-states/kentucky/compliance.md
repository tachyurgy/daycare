# Kentucky Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority

Kentucky splits child-care oversight between two agencies of the **Cabinet for Health and Family Services (CHFS)**:

- **Office of Inspector General (OIG), Division of Regulated Child Care (DRCC)** — issues and monitors licenses (centers and certified homes).
  - Web: https://www.chfs.ky.gov/agencies/os/oig/drcc/Pages/default.aspx
  - Email: chfsoigrccportal@ky.gov
  - Fax: (502) 564-9350
- **Department for Community Based Services (DCBS), Division of Child Care (DCC)** — child care subsidy (CCAP), quality (KY All STARS), training registry.
  - Web: https://www.chfs.ky.gov/agencies/dcbs/dcc/Pages/find-care.aspx

Public provider search: **kynect** at https://kynect.ky.gov/benefits/s/child-care-provider

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **KRS Chapter 199** (Children's Services) and **KRS 199.898 – 199.899** — Child-Care Licensing statute.
- **922 KAR 2:090** — Child-Care Center Licensure (license types, application, inspections, renewal).
- **922 KAR 2:110** — Child-Care Center Provider Requirements (operational standards).
- **922 KAR 2:120** — Child-Care Center Health and Safety Standards (including ratios, records, physical plant).
- **922 KAR 2:100** — Certification of Family Child-Care Homes.
- **922 KAR 2:280** — Child-Care Background Checks (CCBC program).
- **922 KAR 2:270** — Kentucky All STARS quality-based graduated early childhood rating system.

## Facility Types Regulated

1. **Type I Child-Care Center** — license issued to regularly provide child care for 4 or more children in a **non-residential** setting, OR 13 or more children in a **residential** setting with designated space separate from the primary residence.
2. **Type II Child-Care Center** — primary residence of the licensee in which child care is regularly provided for at least 7 but not more than 12 children (including children related to the licensee).
3. **Certified Family Child-Care Home** — home-based provider for up to 6 children (922 KAR 2:100), voluntarily certified.
4. **Registered providers** — subsidy-registered in-home or relative caregivers (CCAP only).
5. **License-exempt** — certain public-school and short-hour programs (subject to statutory exemptions in KRS 199.8941).

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (922 KAR 2:120)

| Age Group | Staff:Child Ratio | Maximum Group Size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (< 12 months) | 1:5 | 10 |
| Toddler 12 – 24 months | 1:6 | 12 |
| Toddler 24 – 36 months | 1:10 | 20 |
| Preschool 3 – 4 years | 1:12 | 24 |
| Preschool 4 – 5 years | 1:14 | 28 |
| School-age 5 – 7 years | 1:15 | 30 |
| School-age 7+ (before/after school) | 1:25 | 30 |
| School-age 7+ (full-day) | 1:20 | 30 |

Notes:
- Ratios apply **at all times**, including during supervision of sleeping children.
- Group size is enforced per classroom; mixed-age groups must meet ratio for youngest child.
- **35 sq ft indoor / 60 sq ft outdoor** per child.

## Required Child Records (922 KAR 2:110 / 2:120)

- Written enrollment agreement with parent name, address, emergency contacts
- Child's name, DOB, home address
- Health status, medical history, allergies, special needs plan
- **Immunization certificate** submitted within **30 days of enrollment** (Kentucky Immunization Certificate)
- Physical examination record
- Signed authorization for emergency medical care, field trips, transportation, photos
- Medication administration log (time, date, amount, staff name, child name)
- Daily attendance record (arrival/departure)
- Incident / injury reports
- Weekly meal menus (retained 30 days)
- Transportation logs (child names, on/off times — retained 5 years)
- Fire / emergency drill documentation (date, time)

## Required Staff Records (922 KAR 2:110 / 2:280)

- Name, address, DOB, date of employment
- Educational qualifications / training documentation
- **Child-care background check (CCBC)** — KSP state check, **FBI fingerprint**, **Child Abuse/Neglect Registry (CA/N)**, **National Sex Offender Registry**, **state-of-residence checks** for out-of-state applicants — required **before** unsupervised child contact (922 KAR 2:280)
- **TB screening** prior to employment and every 2 years thereafter
- Pediatric First Aid and CPR certifications
- Orientation training documentation (pre-service health & safety + mandated reporter)
- **Annual written performance evaluation** of each staff by director
- Annual in-service training (minimum 15 hours for center staff; 6 hours for CFCCH)
- Background check recertification (every 5 years unless triggered sooner)

## Inspection Cadence

- Licensed centers are **inspected on a quarterly basis** for first 2 years (probationary period after certain violations), and **at least once annually** thereafter per 922 KAR 2:090.
- A center with a license granted after the 3-year or 7-year probationary period triggers serves probationary inspections semi-annually or quarterly.
- **Unannounced** complaint inspections have no fixed frequency.
- Fire Marshal and health-department inspections required annually; reports submitted to OIG-DRCC.

## Renewal Cadence

- **Regular license** expires one year from effective date or last renewal; must be renewed annually (922 KAR 2:090).
- **Preliminary license** expires six months from issuance — used during initial provisional operation.
- Renewal requires use of form **OIG-DRCC-06**.
- Background checks recertified every 5 years.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Kentucky All STARS** (922 KAR 2:270) — mandatory quality rating system; all licensed centers and certified family homes have a STARS rating (Levels 1-5).
- **CCBC (Child-Care Background Check)** is comprehensive: 5-component check that any new hire must clear before starting.
- **Immunization** certificate must be on file within 30 days — Kentucky Certificate of Immunization Status.
- **Mandated reporter** (KRS 620.030) — all staff; in-service training required.
- **Shaken Baby Syndrome / Abusive Head Trauma / Safe Sleep** — infant-room staff must complete state-approved training prior to unsupervised infant contact.
- **Medication administration** — Kentucky requires specific written authorization and training; staff must complete a DPH-approved training module.
- **Transportation log retention** is unusually long (5 years) — compliance software advantage.
- **Serious Incident Reports** must be submitted to OIG-DRCC promptly (typically within 24 hours).
- **Discipline** — corporal punishment, abusive language, and discipline linked to rest/toileting/food are all prohibited.
