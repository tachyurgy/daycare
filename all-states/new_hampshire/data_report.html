6. Violations & Inspection Data

He-C 4002: Statements of Findings and CAPs become public on or after the CAP due date (typically 30–45 days after issuance). The NH Child Care Search exposes the last 3 years of inspection reports per facility but does not offer bulk export.

Sources

  • Consumer-facing (per-facility, Salesforce): NH Child Care Search
  • CCLU News & Updates (ad-hoc enforcement notices): CCLU News & Updates
  • Inspection process explainer: HoLu Child Care Licensing Inspections PDF
  • Open-records path: RSA 91-A (Right-to-Know) — 5 business-day response window.

Access strategy

  • No anonymous bulk endpoint. Salesforce Visualforce remote actions (fetchAccountList, fetchProviderList) require session tokens.
  • High-volume option: Puppeteer/Playwright scraper with Salesforce session handling.
  • Clean option: RSA 91-A request to CCLU (CCLUoffice@dhhs.nh.gov) for provider roster + SoFs + CAPs + fines, last 36 months.
  • Ad-hoc: monitor CCLU News & Updates for enforcement announcements.

7. Outreach Signals

  • 3-year license cycle — unusually long; creates windows where compliance drift can accumulate undetected. Strong pitch for continuous-compliance software.
  • "Critical rules" trigger auto-CAP — He-C 4002 enumerates specific rules where any violation auto-triggers a Corrective Action Plan with firm deadlines. Miss the CAP deadline and administrative fines follow. Classic pain point.
  • Background checks every 5 years including any state the staff member lived in during prior 5 years — operationally complex for small centers.
  • Mixed-age ratios use average-age (unusual) — requires careful documentation; easy to get wrong.
  • Publicly visible deficiency history — any critical-rule violation appears on the parent-facing NH Child Care Search. Reputational cost hook.
