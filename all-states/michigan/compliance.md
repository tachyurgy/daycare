# Michigan — Child Care Licensing Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority
- **Agency:** Michigan Department of Lifelong Education, Advancement, and Potential (MiLEAP) — Child Care Licensing Bureau (CCLB). Prior to 2023, licensing was administered by Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA) / Bureau of Community and Health Systems (BCHS); CCLB was transferred to MiLEAP in December 2023.
- **URL:** https://www.michigan.gov/mileap/early-childhood-education/cclb
- **Public search portal:** https://cclb.my.site.com/micchirp/s/statewide-facility-search (CCHIRP)

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Statute:** Child Care Organizations Act, 1973 PA 116, MCL 722.111–722.128.
- **Administrative Rules for Centers:** Mich. Admin. Code R 400.8101 – R 400.8840 (Licensing Child Care Centers; effective 2025 revision).
- **Administrative Rules for Family / Group Homes:** R 400.1901 – R 400.1963 (Licensing Family and Group Child Care Homes).

## Facility Types Regulated
- **Child Care Center (CCC)** — non-residential, 1+ children away from parents, licensed for capacity.
- **Family Child Care Home (DF, 1–6)** — provider's residence, ≤6 unrelated minor children.
- **Group Child Care Home (DG, 7–12)** — provider's residence, 7–12 children with at least one additional adult.
- **Before/After School Program (school-age)** — distinct license subclass under center rules.
- **Tribal and license-exempt** (e.g., Head Start alone, drop-in during parent activity) per MCL 722.111.

## Key Licensing Forms (MiLEAP / CCLB)
- **BCAL-3730** — Child Information Record.
- **BCAL-4616** — Child Care Application (initial license).
- **BCAL-4617** — Request for Renewal of Child Care License.
- **BCAL-4584** — Child and Adolescent Health Center Immunization Record / Minimum Immunization Record.
- **BCAL-1326** — Medication Authorization.
- **BCAL-3300** — Incident/Accident Report.
- **BCAL-5245** — Licensing Study Report (completed by licensing consultant).
- **BCAL-1248** — Notice of Serious Incident/Injury.
- **Great Start to Quality** registration (quality rating) — separate but required for subsidy eligibility.

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (Centers — R 400.8182, Table 4)
| Age band | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (birth – 30 months) | 1:4 | 12 |
| Toddler (30 months – 3 years) | 1:8 | 16 |
| Preschool (3 – 4 years) | 1:10 | 30 |
| Preschool (4 years – school age) | 1:12 | 36 |
| School-age | 1:18 | 36 |

Mixed-age rooms: ratio and group size are determined by the age of the **youngest** child.

**Family Home (1–6):** one caregiver, max 6 minor children incl. caregiver's own under 6.
**Group Home (7–12):** caregiver + one approved assistant, max 12.

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment form with emergency contact, authorized pickups, allergies, dietary restrictions.
- Immunization record (Michigan Care Improvement Registry MCIR or BCAL-4584); compliance with MDHHS schedule; waiver (medical/nonmedical) with signed county health-department certificate if claimed.
- Physical exam / health appraisal (within 30 days of enrollment for centers; within 6 months for family homes).
- Written child information record (parent contact, physician, insurance).
- Daily attendance.
- Medication authorization form per med (BCAL-1326).
- Incident/accident reports.
- USDA CACFP records if participating.

## Required Staff Records
- **Background checks:** Fingerprint-based FBI/state criminal history via CCHIRP; sex-offender registry; Central Registry clearance (child abuse/neglect) — required prior to unsupervised contact. Must be renewed per federal CCDBG (every 5 years) and rechecked with state changes.
- **Education/credential:** Program director / lead caregiver qualifications per R 400.8135–8138 (CDA, associate's, or ECE coursework depending on role).
- **Training:** Minimum **16 clock hours of approved professional development per year** for caregivers and program directors (R 400.8142). Includes health/safety topics required by CCDBG (first aid, CPR, medication admin, SIDS, shaken baby, food allergy, mandated reporter).
- **CPR/First Aid:** At least one adult trained in pediatric CPR and first aid present at all times; all caregivers within 90 days of hire.
- **TB screening** per local health requirements.
- **Orientation** within 4 weeks of hire (Michigan-specific — SIDS, shaken baby, mandated reporter, emergency preparedness).

## Inspection Cadence
- **Annual licensing inspection** by CCLB consultant (largely unannounced for ongoing centers).
- **Complaint investigations** conducted on receipt of credible allegation.
- **Renewal inspection** immediately prior to license term expiration.
- Random unannounced monitoring visits during the license term.
- Fire/environmental inspections on the cycle required by the state fire marshal and local health department (typically annual).

## Renewal Cadence
- Regular (non-provisional) child care center and home license: **2-year term**, renewed by filing BCAL-4617 and passing renewal inspection.
- Provisional license: 6 months, non-renewable, issued while applicant finishes unmet requirements.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **CCHIRP portal** is mandatory for all licensees — applications, renewals, staff lists, incident reports, and background-check status are submitted electronically.
- **Great Start to Quality (GSQ)** star rating (1–5): required publication on the license record; required at a minimum of "Empowered" (previously 3-star) for most state subsidy payments.
- **Safe Sleep** — infants must be placed on backs in cribs meeting federal CPSC standards; written safe-sleep policy signed by parents (R 400.8305).
- **Medication administration** requires written authorization and annual medication-administration training.
- **Serious incidents** (death, hospitalization, abuse allegation, missing child) must be reported to CCLB within 24 hours on BCAL-1248.
- **Statewide Longitudinal Data System (MSDS)** — centers receiving subsidy must roster children.
- **2025 rule rewrite** restructured center rules (R 400.8101 series) — effective May 2025; providers must verify compliance against the current table of rules (see "Center Rules" PDF under MiLEAP CCLB).
