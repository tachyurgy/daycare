01 Licensing Authority

Colorado Department of Early Childhood (CDEC) — created 2022, taking over licensing from CDHS. Division of Early Learning Licensing and Administration (DELLA). Consumer portal: Colorado Shines. Licensing hub: CDEC Licensing.

02 Primary Regulatory Citation

  • 12 CCR 2509-8 § 7.702 — Child Care Centers (< 24-hour care) — Cornell LII
  • § 7.707 — Large Family Child Care Homes
  • § 7.708 — Family Child Care Homes (regular)
  • § 7.712 — School-Age Child Care Centers
  • § 7.701 — General Rules for Child Care Facilities
  • Enabling statute: C.R.S. § 26.5-5-301 et seq.
  • Agency migration 2022: regulations administered by CDEC DELLA; legacy CCR path (12 CCR 2509-8) retained for continuity.

CDEC recommends emailing the CDEC Legal Team before filing a formal CORA — routine violation extracts are often produced informally, faster. CORA: first hour free, $41.37/hr thereafter; 3-working-day response. Colorado moved to continuous licensure, so there's no renewal deadline pressure — monitor through annual unannounced inspections.

07 Outreach Signals

  • Universal Preschool (UPK) contracting pain. Since 2023, universal pre-K has shifted administration to CDEC; providers need aligned compliance tracking for both licensing AND UPK contracts — double paperwork, single pain point.
  • Two-year agency migration recency. CDEC was created in 2022; directors are still adjusting. Content-led SEO around "Colorado child care licensing 2026" has low competition and high intent.
  • PDIS professional registry is mandatory statewide — ComplianceKit staff-file sync to PDIS is a wedge.
  • Altitude-specific requirements (Rocky Mountain programs) create a niche sub-segment for content.
  • CCCAP Fiscal Agreement separates subsidy providers — higher ARPA, more willing to pay for compliance.
