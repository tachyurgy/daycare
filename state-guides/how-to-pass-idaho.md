# How to Pass Your Idaho Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector could be from the **Idaho Department of Health and Welfare (IDHW) — Division of Family and Community Services (Child Care Licensing)** *and/or* from your **regional public health district** (there are 7: EIPH, Central District Health, Southwest District Health, South Central, Public Health, North Central, Panhandle). IDHW sets the rules; your regional health district does most of the boots-on-the-ground inspections. Expect **annual inspections** + **complaint follow-ups**. Every inspection — pass or fail — publishes on **idahochildcarecheck.org** as a numbered record with pass/fail counts, per-criterion findings, and inspector comments. Records go back to **October 2017**.

- Agency: https://healthandwelfare.idaho.gov/services-programs/child-care
- Public inspection site: https://www.idahochildcarecheck.org/
- File a complaint: https://healthandwelfare.idaho.gov/services-programs/children-families-older-adults/file-child-care-complaint

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **This is the law:** Idaho Code §§ 39-1101 – 39-1120 (Basic Day Care License Law). https://legislature.idaho.gov/statutesrules/idstat/title39/t39ch11/
- **This is the health-standards law:** Idaho Code § 39-1110 (food prep, diaper changing, sleep/play areas, handwashing, water supply)
- **This is the rulebook:** IDAPA 16.06.02 — Rules Governing Standards for Child Care Licensing. https://adminrules.idaho.gov/rules/current/16/160602.pdf
- **This is the ratio rule (unique point system):** IDAPA 16.06.02.335. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/idaho/IDAPA-16.06.02.335
- **This is the 2025 legislative update:** HB 243 — adjusted point values for some groups while retaining 12-point cap. https://legislature.idaho.gov/wp-content/uploads/sessioninfo/2025/legislation/H0243E1.pdf

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

Idaho is different from every other state. It uses a **12-point-per-staff** system. Each staff member is responsible for **up to 12 points of children**. Each age group is a point value:

| Age | Points per child |
|---|---|
| Birth – under 24 months | **2.000** |
| 24 months – under 36 months | **1.333** |
| 36 months – under 5 years | **0.923** |
| 5 years – under 13 years | **0.480** |

Source: IDAPA 16.06.02.335.

**Examples** (each totals 12 points per staff member):
- 6 infants (6 × 2.000 = 12)
- 9 toddlers aged 24–36 months (9 × 1.333 ≈ 12)
- 13 preschoolers (13 × 0.923 ≈ 12)
- 25 school-age (25 × 0.480 = 12)

Or any **combination** that adds up to 12 points. So: 1 infant (2) + 4 preschoolers (3.69) + 10 school-age (4.80) = 10.49 points — that's fine for one staff member.

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot AND do the math on the spot. Do not round up. Do not assume a simple ratio. **Know your class math before walking into the room.** HB 243 (2025) adjusted some point values — make sure you're using current IDAPA 16.06.02 values.

**Facility types** (Idaho Code § 39-1102):
- **Family Daycare Home** (≤6 children) — **not required** to be licensed; may license voluntarily.
- **Group Daycare Facility** (7–12 children) — must be licensed.
- **Daycare Center** (≥13 children) — must be licensed.

**City rules:** Some cities — Boise notably — have **additional local licensing requirements** stricter than the state. Check city hall.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives (typically unannounced) and shows ID.
2. Checks posted license and posted ratio/point rules.
3. Walks each room. Counts kids by age and runs the 12-point math.
4. Pulls staff files: **IDHW enhanced background check** (at least every 5 years for owner/operator; required for all staff and any individual 13+ with unsupervised direct child contact), First Aid, **Infant–Child CPR**, pediatric rescue breathing (from a certified instructor), orientation, annual training hours.
5. Pulls child files: enrollment, **Idaho immunization record** (paper or via Idaho Immunization Reminder Information System), health history, emergency authorization, medication authorization.
6. Walks through the 31-item inspection criteria list (idahochildcarecheck.org uses the same 31 numbered items): supervision, CPR, ratios, food safety, emergency planning, sleep, diaper area, kitchen, playground.
7. Writes per-criterion findings and inspector comments. Determines pass or fail.
8. Posts the full inspection to `idahochildcarecheck.org` — usually within days. Any substantiated incident also publishes there.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Idaho
1. **Ratio math wrong** — a classroom adds up to more than 12 points per staff member (IDAPA 16.06.02.335).
2. **Enhanced background check expired** — required at least every 5 years for owner/operator, and for any individual ≥13 years old with unsupervised contact.
3. **Infant–Child CPR instructor not certified** — Idaho specifically requires the CPR card come from a certified instructor.
4. **Safe sleep violation** — infants on backs, firm mattress, safety-approved crib. No car seats, swings, strollers, or slings for sleep. One infant per crib.
5. **Direct sight/sound observation missing** during naps or diapering.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment form (name, DOB, parent info, emergency contacts)
- **Idaho immunization record** (paper OR via Idaho Immunization Reminder Information System)
- Health history and allergy / special needs
- Authorization for emergency medical care
- Medication authorization and log
- Authorized pick-up list
- Attendance record
- Parent handbook acknowledgment

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Employment application & résumé
- **IDHW enhanced background check clearance** — all owners, operators, staff, and any individual 13+ with unsupervised direct child contact. Required to be on-site and shown to inspector during initial and annual inspections.
- Enhanced background check re-run at least every 5 years for owner/operator
- First Aid, **Infant–Child CPR**, and pediatric rescue breathing — from a **certified instructor**
- Orientation documentation
- Annual training hours (per IDAPA 16.06.02.525 and QRIS/subsidy requirements)
- TB risk assessment (if required by local health district)
- SIDS / safe-sleep training (if serving infants)

## What must be on your walls
- Current license
- Ratio / point system chart
- Evacuation routes each room
- Emergency contact list
- Menu (current week)
- Complaint number (Idaho 211 CareLine / IDHW)
- Posted safe-sleep policy in infant rooms

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — per emergency plan, documented
- **Daily attendance**
- **Medication log**
- **Injury / incident log**
- **Playground equipment** inspected per § 39-1110 rules
- **Kitchen / diaper area** disinfection log

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids by age in every room. Multiply by point values. Total must be ≤12 per staff.
2. Post the math sheet on the wall of any mixed-age room.
3. Confirm license, ratios, safe-sleep policy posted.
4. Fire drill log — this month entered?
5. Open three staff files: enhanced background check date, certified-instructor CPR card, First Aid, training log.
6. Open three child files: immunization, emergency authorization, allergy plan.
7. Cribs: backs only, firm mattress, no soft objects, one infant per crib, **no car seat / swing / stroller / sling sleep**.
8. Direct sight/sound on napping infants at all times.
9. Playground: no broken equipment.
10. Greet the inspector. Offer the file drawer key. Keep it simple.

## What happens if you fail
A failed inspection shows on `idahochildcarecheck.org` as a "failed" inspection with per-criterion findings and inspector comments. **Resolution status** updates as you correct. Repeat or serious findings trigger license suspension or revocation through IDHW's administrative process. Regional health districts also pull their sanitation or food-service approvals if needed. ICCP (Idaho Child Care Program subsidy) enrollment requires licensure — losing the license kills subsidy participation. Appeals go through IDHW's hearing office. Public records requests under Idaho Code § 74-101 give 3–10 business days to produce.

## The weird Idaho gotcha nobody tells you about
**Idaho is the only state with a point-based ratio system.** Every other state publishes a simple "one teacher to X children by age" table. Idaho gives you a **12-point budget per staff**, and the age-band point values got adjusted in 2025 (HB 243). A classroom with a mix of ages looks different here than anywhere else — you need to do the multiplication. The other gotcha: **family daycare homes with ≤6 kids are exempt from state licensure**, but if you want to accept ICCP (subsidy) funding, you MUST be licensed. And several cities (Boise in particular) add **local licensing on top of state** — you'll face two sets of rules. If you open a home in Boise, check city code first.

## For Magnus — how to sell to an Idaho owner
Their fear is a **"failed" inspection showing publicly on idahochildcarecheck.org** — Idaho's transparency is aggressive and it stays up. Drop the **12-point math** in 30 seconds — most owners carry a mental simple-ratio table and don't realize they're miscounting their mixed-age rooms. Also mention the **certified-instructor CPR** nuance (Idaho enforces that specifically, some states don't). Most owners run the 7-health-district inspection scramble on paper, a laminated ratio card, and manual 5-year background renewal reminders. Say "I'll run the 12-point math automatically for every room combination and alert you before a class goes over." Boise, Meridian, Nampa, Idaho Falls, Coeur d'Alene are the targets; Boise adds city-level rules so that metro is the most complex — and willing to pay.
