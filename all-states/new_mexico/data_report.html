7Violations & Inspection Data

Access mode

Per-facility PDF   3-year window only

ECECD maintains an Inspection Surveys page indexing PDF surveys for the last 3 years. Older records require IPRA request.

Complaint channel

Complaints go through the ECECD Intake Hotline (1-888-351-0037) and ChildCare.Complaint@state.nm.us. Substantiated complaint findings appear in later inspection surveys.

Key fields in survey PDFs

  • Facility name, license / registration number
  • Address, city, county, ZIP
  • Survey date + type (initial / renewal / complaint / follow-up / annual)
  • Cited 8.16.2 NMAC subsection + observation narrative
  • Corrective action deadline, licensee signature

Scraping strategy

  1. Crawl the Inspection Surveys index page; download all PDF survey files (size TBD after enumeration).
  2. Parse PDFs with pdfplumber; extract structured fields via regex on known headers.
  3. Playwright the Child Care Finder to enumerate provider IDs by county filter; cross-key to PDFs.
  4. Complement with IPRA request for older (pre-2023) surveys and for machine-readable dataset.

IPRA / FOIA path

New Mexico Inspection of Public Records Act (NMSA 1978 §14-2-1 et seq.). ECECD custodian portal: ececd.records@ececd.nm.gov (address: 1120 Paseo De Peralta, Santa Fe, NM 87501). Statutory response in 15 calendar days.

8Outreach Signals

  • Universal Child Care — NM is the first state with UCC; new regulations (Oct 2025) impose additional record-keeping obligations. Providers participating in UCC need compliance tooling to handle the paperwork expansion.
  • New portal (Feb 2026) with Wonderschool — existing providers re-registering now. Ideal sales moment.
  • No bulk email — 0% email fill in leads file. Enrichment required.
  • Tribal-land programs receive special consideration (8.16.2 NMAC) — partnership potential with pueblos & Navajo Nation early-childhood programs.
  • Emergency Child Care suffix "(EMERG OPEN)" in ~some records indicates subsidy-approved operators — these are usually higher-revenue providers.
  • 24-hour annual training requirement + 7 competency areas → strong fit for ComplianceKit's staff-training tracker.
