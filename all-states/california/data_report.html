1. Licensing Authority

California Department of Social Services (CDSS), Community Care Licensing Division (CCLD) — Child Care Program Office. Operates through 21 Regional Offices statewide. Licensing Program Analysts (LPAs) conduct all inspections using the CARE (Compliance and Regulatory Enforcement) framework.

Headquarters 744 P Street, Sacramento, CA 95814
Phone (916) 651-8848 | Complaint hotline: 844-LET-US-NO
Facility search ccld.dss.ca.gov/carefacilitysearch
CDSS CCL cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/child-care-licensing

2. Primary Regulatory Citation

Statute: California Health & Safety Code § 1596.70 et seq. (California Child Day Care Facilities Act).

Administrative Code: Title 22, California Code of Regulations, Division 12 — the binding rule set.

Enforcement framework: CCLD CARE Tools — standardized inspection & deficiency framework.

Citations: Type A (immediate health/safety/personal-rights risk) and Type B (operational but not immediate risk). LIC 421 civil penalty assessment follows.

6. Violations & Inspection Data

Rich per-facility transparency; no public bulk feed. CCLD Facility Search publishes full inspection PDFs Apr 2015-present for every licensed facility. There is no Socrata / JSON / ArcGIS deficiency export. Bulk acquisition = (1) scrape facility-by-facility + OCR PDFs, or (2) CPRA request for the LIS extract.

CCLD Facility Search

Per-facility HTML + PDF. April 2015 - present. Excel export of facility roster available; deficiency detail requires per-PDF extraction.

CHHS Open Data (roster)

CCL Facilities dataset — statewide roster (data.chhs.ca.gov/dataset/ccl-facilities). Name, address, status, license number. No deficiencies.

Citations

Type A (immediate risk) / Type B (operational). LIC 421 civil penalty assessment. 9 inspection domains for centers, 6 for FCCH.

Records-request path

California Public Records Act (Gov. Code § 7920 et seq.) to CDSS. 10-calendar-day initial determination. Target: full LIS extract.

Full analysis: violations.md

7. Outreach Signals

  • Biennial (2-year) renewal — half the market in renewal at any time. Persistent buying trigger.
  • 9 inspection domains for centers — one of the most detailed inspection frameworks nationally. Self-inspection simulator value prop is high.
  • Title 22 depth — infant-center plans (quarterly needs/services, toilet-training, feeding, individual sleeping) are ongoing paper-intensive asks that map 1:1 to ComplianceKit document modules.
  • CTC Permit Matrix — staff qualification tracking is genuinely hard in CA; lead-teacher permit levels + ongoing PD = ComplianceKit Staff File winner.
  • LA + Bay Area + San Diego + Orange + Inland Empire + Sacramento — six MSAs likely cover ~75% of the center-based market. Concentration-first outreach.
  • Municipal overlays (SF, Berkeley) — stacked requirements increase willingness to pay.
  • Public inspection transparency since 2015 — operators keenly aware their deficiencies are online; reputation-management angle opens sales conversations.
  • $99 Professional tier aligns with CA center economics — typical ARPA is comfortable for a 40-60 child facility even in high-cost markets.

8. Next-Step Data Acquisition

  1. Pull CHHS Open Data ccl-facilities dataset as the canonical statewide roster. Join to existing california_leads.csv to de-dup and fill missing license numbers.
  2. Scrape CCLD Facility Search incrementally by county (58 counties). Priority order: LA, San Diego, Santa Clara, Alameda, Orange, San Francisco, Riverside, San Bernardino, Sacramento, Contra Costa.
  3. Download + OCR inspection PDFs — normalize to (license_number, visit_date, visit_type, citation, Type_A_B, narrative, CAP_due, civil_penalty). Plan for millions of PDFs across 10+ years × 28k facilities.
  4. File CPRA request to CDSS for full LIS extract — request as outlined in violations.md (license rosters, visits, deficiencies, civil penalties, complaints, 2016-present). Initial determination in 10 days; production on reasonable-time basis.
  5. Enrich leads with emails via the facility search (contact info on each profile), CACFP sponsor listings, CA QualityStartCA child care quality-rating registry, regional R&R network partners.
  6. Integrate CA QRIS (Quality Rating & Improvement System) / Quality Counts California as an additional signal layer — QRIS-participating centers have evidence-collection pain that aligns with the Documents module.
