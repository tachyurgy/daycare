# How to Pass Your Maryland Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector is an **MSDE Licensing Specialist** from the **Maryland State Department of Education (MSDE), Division of Early Childhood (DEC), Office of Child Care**. Maryland has **13 regional licensing offices**, and each has a designated specialist who is assigned to your program. Visits are **annual and unannounced**. Complaints trigger additional unannounced visits. Every finding posts publicly on **CheckCCMD.org** — Maryland has required public publication of inspection findings since **January 2011**. Public sites:
- MSDE DEC licensing: https://earlychildhood.marylandpublicschools.org/child-care-providers/licensing
- Public search (CheckCCMD): https://www.checkccmd.org/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — Md. Code, Education Article, Title 9.5.
- **The rulebook for centers** — COMAR 13A.16.
- **Family Child Care Home rules** — COMAR 13A.15.
- **Large Family Child Care Home rules** — COMAR 13A.18.
- **Letters of Compliance (public-school / recreational programs)** — COMAR 13A.17.
- **Ratio rule** — COMAR 13A.16.08.03. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/maryland/COMAR-13A-16-08-03
- **Regulations landing** — https://earlychildhood.marylandpublicschools.org/regulations

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From COMAR 13A.16.08.03 (centers). Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age band | One staff for every... | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (6 weeks – 18 months) | 3 | 6 |
| Toddler (18 months – 2 years) | 3 | 9 |
| 2 years | 6 | 12 |
| 3 years | 10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 10 | 20 |
| 5 years (Kindergarten) | 12 | — |
| School age (6+ years) | 15 | 30 |

Mixed-age groups with infants/toddlers follow the youngest-child ratio. With preschoolers and school-agers combined, 1:10 applies, and no more than 6 two-year-olds in a group of 13–20. **Family Child Care** is max 8 children with 1 provider; **Large Family** is up to 12 with 2 adults.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced.
2. Checks license is posted and current.
3. Walks every classroom, counts children, compares to staff present.
4. Pulls staff files — looking first for the **Criminal Background Check (CBC)** via OCC 1231/1203.
5. Pulls child files — health inventory (OCC 1214), immunizations, emergency form (OCC 1205).
6. Reviews emergency preparedness plan, fire drill log, lead screening compliance.
7. Exit conference and then files a report into the CheckCCMD database. Inspection report posts within ~30 days of the visit. CheckCCMD also lists verified complaints and any License Actions.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
Based on COMAR 13A.16 enforcement patterns and CheckCCMD public violation categories:
1. **Out-of-ratio, especially infant 1:3** — tight ratios, very little slack.
2. **CBC lapses (OCC 1203/1231)** — fingerprint-based state + FBI not renewed every 5 years; CPS clearance missing.
3. **Staff qualifications short** — lead teachers need the 45-hour preschool/school-age course or CDA; directors need the 90-hour Child Care Course per COMAR 13A.16.06.
4. **Lead screening** not documented for kids 1 and 2 in targeted ZIPs (Maryland law).
5. **Annual 12 clock hours** of continuing training not logged.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Application & emergency contact info** (OCC 1205 Emergency Form).
- **OCC 1214** — Health Inventory. Within 60 days of enrollment.
- **Immunization record** (MDH Form 896 equivalent) per COMAR 10.06.04.03.
- **Lead testing documentation** (Maryland law requires screening at ages 1 and 2 in targeted ZIPs).
- **OCC 1215** — Individual Child Care Plan for children with special needs/allergies.
- **OCC 1216** — Medication Authorization.
- **Signed parent handbook + discipline policy acknowledgment**.
- **Daily attendance record**.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **OCC 1203 / OCC 1231** — Criminal Background Clearance. Fingerprint-based state + FBI. Renewed every **5 years**. Includes CPS clearance and Sex Offender Registry check.
- **45-hour Preschool** or **45-hour School Age** course (depending on role).
- **90-hour Child Care Course** (senior staff/director) plus administrative training per COMAR 13A.16.06.
- **CDA** or equivalent ECE credential for lead teachers.
- **Current pediatric First Aid + CPR**.
- **12 clock hours annual continuing training** across core knowledge areas.
- **Medication Administration Training (MAT)** if dispensing meds.
- **Communicable disease training** (SIDS/safe sleep, shaken baby, emergency preparedness).
- **TB screening** per local requirement.
- **Listed in the Continuing Professional Development Registry (CPDR)**.

## What must be on your walls
- Current MSDE license.
- Most recent inspection summary.
- Emergency Preparedness & Disaster Plan.
- Safe Sleep Policy (written) posted in infant rooms.
- Menus.
- Parent complaint / licensing specialist contact.
- Breastfeeding-friendly accommodations notice.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** — per emergency plan (monthly is standard practice).
- **Emergency Preparedness & Disaster Plan** with annual drills documented.
- **Daily attendance log**.
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident / injury reports**.
- **Fire Marshal inspection** — annually.
- **Environmental Health inspection** — as required by local health.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff on the floor. Infant 1:3 is where most people lose.
2. Pull 3 random staff files — CBC renewal date (under 5 years?), 45/90-hour course certificate, 12-hour training log, CPR card.
3. Pull 3 random child files — OCC 1214 health inventory, immunization, lead screening.
4. Infant rooms: safe sleep, no bumpers or loose bedding.
5. Fire drill log — current month initialed?
6. Maryland EXCELS rating documents on hand (for subsidy reimbursement).
7. License posted and current.
8. Emergency preparedness plan posted and up to date.
9. Medication cabinet — locked, in-date, matching OCC 1216.
10. Quick scan of your CheckCCMD.org record — any open finding from last visit not yet marked corrected?

## What happens if you fail
MSDE issues findings and a plan-of-correction requirement. The finding posts publicly on CheckCCMD.org. Serious or uncorrected noncompliance leads to License Action — probation, denial, suspension, revocation — and also posts. Appeals go through the MSDE administrative process. Remember: CheckCCMD is mandatory public publication; there is no hiding from it.

## The weird Maryland gotcha nobody tells you about
**CheckCCMD.org has been live since January 2011 — earlier than most states.** That means Maryland parents have more than a decade of habit checking public inspection history before they visit. Your reputation is literally a public URL: `https://www.checkccmd.org/ProviderInformation.aspx?ProvNum=<your license number>`. Every verified complaint, every area of noncompliance, every license action is right there with your COMAR cite, inspector region, and resolution status. The other Maryland quirk: you have **13 different regional offices**, each with its own specialist. Which specialist you get assigned to shapes how strictly some ambiguous rules are interpreted — especially around COMAR 13A.16.06 director qualifications and the 90-hour course.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Maryland owner
Biggest fear: the CheckCCMD.org public record. Every parent in Maryland learns to type in the provider number before they tour. A single area-of-noncompliance shows up with the exact COMAR cite and stays visible. Drop this in 30 seconds: "You've had CheckCCMD since 2011, the CBC renewal is a 5-year clock, and your lead teachers owe 12 hours of training a year logged in CPDR. Are you tracking every staff CBC date and every open finding on your CheckCCMD page in one system?" Today they are bouncing between OCC 1203/1231 paperwork, CPDR logins, and a spreadsheet for training hours. The close: "We'll keep your CheckCCMD page clean — because in Maryland, your page is your brand."
