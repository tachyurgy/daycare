# Louisiana Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority

**Louisiana Department of Education (LDE), Division of Early Childhood — Licensing Section.** Authority was transferred from the Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS) to LDE on October 1, 2014.

- Website: https://doe.louisiana.gov/early-childhood/child-care-facility-licensing
- Licensing phone: 225-342-9905
- Email: ldelicensing@la.gov
- Public facility search: https://louisianaschools.com/ (School & Center Finder)

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **La. R.S. 17:407.31 – 17:407.53** — Early Learning Center Licensing Act.
- **Bulletin 137 — Louisiana Early Learning Center Licensing Regulations** — codified at **Louisiana Administrative Code Title 28, Part CLXI** (LAC 28:CLXI.101 et seq.). Adopted by the Board of Elementary and Secondary Education (BESE); administered by LDE.
- **Bulletin 139 — Louisiana Child Care and Development Fund Programs** (CCDF / subsidy standards).
- **Bulletin 140 — Louisiana Early Childhood Care and Education Network** (performance / academic standards for Type III centers).
- Background checks: **Child Care Criminal Background Checks (CCCBC)** per 17:407.71 and ACF CCDBG requirements.

## Facility Types Regulated

All non-exempt centers serving 7+ unrelated children must hold an **Early Learning Center License** in one of three types (LAC 28:CLXI.307):

1. **Type I License** — Church or religious tax-exempt organization under IRC §501(c); receives **no** state or federal funds. Automatic revocation if state/federal funds are received.
2. **Type II License** — Center receives no state or federal funds, **or** only receives USDA food-nutrition program funds (CACFP). Not eligible for CCDF subsidy payments.
3. **Type III License** — Center that receives state or federal funds (CCDF/LA 4, NSECD, etc.) beyond food-program funds. Must meet Bulletin 140 Early Childhood Care and Education Network performance standards and kindergarten-readiness requirements.

Separately regulated:
- **Family Child Care Homes** (Registered Family Child Care Providers / RFCCP) — DCFS-era program under LAC 67:V; currently registered with LDE for subsidy purposes.
- **In-home and in-relative care** through CCAP — Bulletin 139 only; no Bulletin 137 license.

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Maximum Group Sizes (LAC 28:CLXI.1711)

| Age Group | Child:Staff Ratio | Maximum Group Size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (< 1 year) | 5:1 | 15 |
| 1 year | 7:1 | 21 |
| 2 years | 10:1 | 20 |
| 3 years | 13:1 | 26 |
| 4 years | 15:1 | 30 |
| 5 years | 19:1 | 38 |
| 6 years and up | 23:1 | 46 |

Notes:
- **Only staff members directly providing care, supervision, or guidance to children are counted** in ratios.
- **Mixed-age groups** must meet the ratio of the youngest child in the group.
- Tightened ratios apply during **water activities** and **field trips**.
- Ratios must be met **at all times**, including outdoor play, meals, naps, and during staff breaks.

## Required Child Records (LAC 28:CLXI, Chapter 17)

- Child's application / enrollment form with contact info and emergency contacts
- Signed parental policy acknowledgements (discipline, release, health)
- Current immunization record (per LAC 51:II)
- Physical health exam / physician's statement
- Allergy and special-needs information
- Authorization for emergency medical care, transportation, field trips
- Medication consent and administration log
- Daily attendance log (sign in/out)
- Incident / critical-incident reports
- Records retention: minimum **2 years after termination of enrollment** (recent revision from 3 years)

## Required Staff Records

- Application, résumé, qualifications documentation (director and staff qualifications under Bulletin 137)
- **CCCBC clearance** (state + FBI fingerprint + National Sex Offender Registry + state child abuse/neglect registry + out-of-state checks for recent residents) — required **prior** to unsupervised child contact
- Personal history statement, emergency contact info
- Current pediatric First Aid and CPR certification
- **Orientation training** — completed within 7 calendar days of first day of work, covering 15 topic areas (supervision, discipline, SIDS, shaken baby, food safety, allergies, CPR/first aid, medication, mandated reporter, emergencies, etc.)
- LDE Key Orientation Training Modules 2 and 3 completed within 30 calendar days of first day
- **Annual training** (minimum 12 clock hours; more for directors / lead teachers)
- Mandated reporter acknowledgement; TB screen as applicable
- Employment dates, evaluations, discipline records

## Inspection Cadence

- **At least one unannounced inspection per year** (intervals not to exceed 12 months) after initial licensure (LAC 28:CLXI, Chapter 7, §703 and §705).
- **Initial licensing inspection** completed by LDE before license issues.
- **Fire Marshal** and **Louisiana Department of Health** sanitation inspections required annually (and for initial licensure).
- **Complaint-based** unannounced inspections conducted as needed.
- **Critical incident reports** must be submitted to LDE within 24 hours.

## Renewal Cadence

- License is issued for a **one-year** term and must be renewed annually.
- Renewal application due **at least 30 days** prior to expiration.
- Renewal requires current Fire Marshal and LDH sanitation inspection reports and satisfactory compliance history.
- CCCBC clearances must be maintained and refreshed every 5 years (or at triggering events).

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Child Care Criminal Background Check (CCCBC)** — Louisiana is one of the most comprehensive in the U.S.: state + FBI fingerprint + sex offender registry + state child abuse registry + out-of-state checks for applicants who have resided outside LA in last 5 years. Issued via LDE's CCCBC portal.
- **Type III centers** must meet Bulletin 140 kindergarten-readiness / CLASS observation performance standards and participate in LA's community networks.
- **Early Childhood Community Networks** — all publicly-funded early childhood seats are coordinated via 65 community networks statewide.
- **Louisiana Pathways / LA Quality Start** — QRIS rebranded as "Act 3 / Performance Profile" under Bulletin 140.
- **Mandated reporter** status for all staff (La. R.S. 14:403); training required at orientation.
- **Shaken Baby / Abusive Head Trauma prevention**, **SIDS / Safe Sleep**, and **Pediatric CPR** training required.
- **Emergency Preparedness Plan** must include evacuation, shelter-in-place, lockdown, reunification (mandated for hurricane-prone Gulf Coast).
- **Academic Approval (Type III only)** — additional BESE endorsement required for centers receiving public education funds.
- **Abusive Head Trauma / Shaken Baby** — a written prevention policy is required.
- **Critical Incident reporting** must go to LDE within 24 hours and be logged.
- **Written enrollment contract** required — must specify tuition, policies, withdrawal process.
