# North Dakota Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
**North Dakota Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS), Early Childhood Services — Early Childhood Licensing Unit** (formerly the Department of Human Services Early Childhood Services division before the 2022 DHHS reorganization).

- Address: 600 E. Boulevard Avenue, Dept. 325, Bismarck, ND 58505-0250
- Phone: (701) 328-3541 | Toll-free: 800-472-2622
- Provider portal: Child Care Licensing (CCL) System — applications, renewals, monitoring uploads, correction orders, incident reports

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Statute:** **NDCC Chapter 50-11.1** — "Early Childhood Services" — authorizes DHHS to license early childhood programs and promulgate minimum standards.
- **Administrative Code:** **NDAC Article 75-03**, specifically:
  - **75-03-08** — Family Child Care (up to 7 children in provider's home)
  - **75-03-09** — Group Child Care (up to 18 children in provider's home or another building)
  - **75-03-10** — Child Care Center (19+ children)
  - **75-03-11** — School-Age Child Care
  - **75-03-12** — Self-Declaration (5 or fewer children, optional)
  - **75-03-14** — Pre-School (half-day / educational)

## Facility Types Regulated
1. **Self-Declared Providers** — care for 5 or fewer children, maximum 3 under 24 months; registration is optional (required to participate in subsidy).
2. **Family Child Care** — up to 7 children, operator's home; NDAC 75-03-08.
3. **Group Child Care** — up to 18 children; NDAC 75-03-09.
4. **Child Care Center** — 19+ children; NDAC 75-03-10.
5. **School-Age Programs** — NDAC 75-03-11.
6. **Pre-School** — primarily educational, part-day; NDAC 75-03-14.

## Key Licensing Forms
- Application for Early Childhood License (submitted via the Child Care Licensing portal)
- SFN 847 / SFN 530 series — Child health history & immunization
- SFN 1259 — Self-Declaration registration
- Criminal History Record Check authorization (FBI fingerprint via BCI)
- Child Protection Services (CPS) record check request
- Sex Offender Registry check acknowledgment
- Fire Marshal inspection certificate
- Environmental / sanitation inspection (local public health)
- Health Policy, Discipline Policy, Emergency Plan templates

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (Child Care Centers, NDAC 75-03-10-08)
| Age | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (0 - under 12 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| 12 - 23 months | 1:5 | 10 |
| 24 - 35 months | 1:7 | 14 |
| 3 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 1:10 | 25 |
| 5 years (not in K) | 1:12 | 30 |
| School-age (6-12) | 1:20 | 40 |

**Group Child Care (75-03-09):** max 18 children; adjusted ratios by age. **Family Child Care (75-03-08):** max 7 children including provider's own, with sub-limits on infants. The state publishes ratio worksheets on the DHHS Early Childhood Services provider page.

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment form (parent/guardian contact, authorized pickups, emergency contacts)
- Health history & immunization record per NDCC 23-07 and NDDOH schedule
- Physician/clinic physical exam within 90 days of admission (center-based)
- Individual care / special needs plan where applicable
- Signed consents: transportation, photography, field trips, emergency medical
- Daily attendance log
- Incident / injury / illness reports
- Medication logs

## Required Staff Records
- Application / resume with education & training history
- **Criminal history record check** (state BCI + FBI fingerprint) — required pre-hire per NDCC 50-11.3
- **Child Protective Services record check**
- Sex offender registry verification
- Reference checks
- CPR / First Aid / AED certification (maintained current)
- Mandated reporter training
- 24 clock hours of continuing education / professional development annually (center teachers); Family/Group providers 9 hours
- TB screening / general health statement
- Documentation of meeting qualification requirements (director, lead teacher)

## Inspection Cadence
- **At least 2 monitoring visits per year** per licensed facility:
  - **1 announced / scheduled** visit
  - **1 unannounced drop-in** visit
- **Initial licensing inspection** before issuance.
- **Complaint investigations** as received.
- Monitoring results and any correction orders from the past 3 years are public via the online child care search tool.

## Renewal Cadence
- Licenses are issued for **two (2) years** for centers and group/family homes (biennial renewal) with annual monitoring in between.
- Self-declared registrations renew annually.
- Renewal paperwork submitted via the CCL portal; background checks re-run per statutory schedule.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Public posting of monitoring results:** DHHS operates the online child care search tool that displays monitoring results and correction orders from the preceding 3 years. Parents can see compliance history in real time.
- **Self-Declaration tier** is unusual — it's a no-license option for micro-providers that still enables subsidy participation, requiring background checks and a safety orientation but not full licensing.
- **Background check statute (NDCC 50-11.3):** every person with unsupervised access, including adult household members in home-based care, must clear state + FBI fingerprint-based checks before that access.
- **Training hours:** 24 hrs/year for center staff (above the federal CCDBG floor of 10-12), 9 hrs/year for family/group home providers.
- **Pre-licensure orientation:** required new applicant training offered regionally.
- **Rural coverage:** ND has roughly 1,100-1,400 licensed providers (including self-declared). Fargo/West Fargo is the single largest cluster; Bismarck/Mandan, Grand Forks, Minot, and Williston are the secondary markets.
- **Fire Marshal + Local Health** inspections must be current; the state licensing action depends on both.
