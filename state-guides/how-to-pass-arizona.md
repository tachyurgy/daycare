# How to Pass Your Arizona Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Arizona Department of Health Services (ADHS), Bureau of Child Care Licensing**. If you run a center, they own you. They do one full annual visit and it is **unannounced** — you will not get a heads-up. They can also show up any time a parent calls a complaint in. Every finding they write lands on the public **AZ Care Check** portal, usually within a day or two after a supervisor signs off. (Group homes of 5–10 kids are different — those are done by DES, inspected twice a year, both unannounced.) Bookmark these two:
- ADHS licensing home: https://www.azdhs.gov/licensing/childcare-facilities/index.php
- Public inspection portal (AZ Care Check): https://azcarecheck.azdhs.gov/s/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — A.R.S. Title 36, Chapter 7.1 (§§ 36-881 to 36-897.01). https://www.azleg.gov/arsDetail/?title=36
- **The rulebook inspectors use** — 9 A.A.C. 5, Articles 1–8 (R9-5-101 through R9-5-809). Big rewrite went into effect **July 2024**. https://www.azdhs.gov/documents/policy-intergovernmental-affairs/administrative-counsel-rules/rules/rulemaking/child-care/9-aac-5-draft-july-2024.pdf
- **The inspection form the inspector actually carries** — Center Rules Instrument Checklist. https://www.azdhs.gov/documents/licensing/childcare-facilities/providers/forms/center-rules-instrument-checklist.pdf
- **Ratio rule (R9-5-404)** — https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/arizona/Ariz-Admin-Code-SS-R9-5-404
- **Staff file rule (R9-5-402)** — https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/arizona/Ariz-Admin-Code-SS-R9-5-402

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From R9-5-404. **The ratio is set by the youngest kid in the room.** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age of youngest child in group | One staff for every... |
|---|---|
| Under 1 year (infants) | 5 children |
| 1-year-olds | 6 children |
| 2-year-olds | 8 children |
| 3-year-olds | 13 children |
| 4-year-olds | 15 children |
| 5-year-olds (not yet in school) | 20 children |
| School-age | 20 children |

Indoor space rule: 35 sq ft per child. Outdoor space: 75 sq ft per child. Student aides and lifeguards do **not** count in ratio.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Walks in unannounced. You sign them in.
2. Checks your license is posted and valid.
3. Walks every classroom. Counts children in each room. Compares to the staff on the floor right then.
4. Pulls a sample of staff files — looking first for the **Level I Fingerprint Clearance Card**.
5. Pulls a sample of child files — looking for immunization record and emergency contacts.
6. Reviews your fire evacuation drill log and emergency/disaster plan.
7. Looks at medication log, incident reports, staff daily sign-in/out log.
8. Exit conference — tells you what they're citing. A written Statement of Deficiencies lands on AZ Care Check a day or two later. You have **10 days** to submit a Plan of Correction.

The whole thing is graded on the Center Rules Instrument Checklist linked above.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Out-of-ratio** (R9-5-404) — a teacher steps out to the bathroom, the room goes over. That's a finding.
2. **Missing or expired Level I Fingerprint Clearance Card** (R9-5-402) — you cannot even have the person on the floor without one.
3. **Staff daily attendance log not filled in** — R9-5-402 requires arrival/departure times for every staff member every day.
4. **Safe sleep violations in the infant room** (R9-5-509) — blankets in the crib, bumpers, inclined sleepers, babies on their stomachs. Automatic cite.
5. **Medication administration errors** (R9-5-507) — no written parent consent for the dose, expired meds, or no log entry.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment form** — emergency contacts, authorized pickups, allergies, medical conditions.
- **Arizona School Entry Immunization Record (Blue Card)** — per A.R.S. § 36-883.02. Or a signed medical/religious/personal-belief exemption on the ADHS form.
- **Parent handbook acknowledgment** — signed.
- **Safe sleep acknowledgment** (infants).
- **Field trip / transportation consent forms**.
- **Medication authorization** — separate one per dose, per day.
- **Daily attendance sign-in/out**.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **Level I Fingerprint Clearance Card** (from Arizona DPS, current).
- **DCS Central Registry check** — pulled before hire.
- **Resume / qualifications** per R9-5-401 (director) or R9-5-403 (staff).
- **TB screening / health declaration** at hire.
- **Orientation checklist** — covered within 30 days of hire (emergencies, CPR/first aid, mandated reporter, meds, abuse signs).
- **Annual continuing education** — moving to **24 hours/year** under the 2024 rule (phase-in through August 2026; was 18 hours before).
- **Pediatric CPR + First Aid** (at least one certified staff on-site at all times).
- **Medication administration training** (for anyone who gives meds).
- **Annual written evaluation**.

## What must be on your walls
- Current ADHS license.
- Most recent inspection report.
- Emergency evacuation plan and evacuation map per room.
- Fire drill log.
- Menus.
- Parent handbook / complaint phone number.
- Hand-washing and diaper-changing steps (where applicable).

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Monthly fire/evacuation drill**, logged (R9-5-405 family).
- **Emergency and disaster plan** including Arizona-specific wildfire and monsoon/flood scenarios.
- **Daily staff sign-in/out log** (R9-5-402).
- **Daily child attendance**.
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident reports** (R9-5-407).
- **Extreme heat protocol** — indoor temperature and outdoor heat-index rules unique to Arizona.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff. Fix any ratio gap **now**.
2. Check the fingerprint card date on everyone on the floor today.
3. Sweep the infant room: back-sleeping, no bumpers, no blankets, no inclined sleepers.
4. Confirm today's signature on the staff sign-in/out sheet.
5. Eyeball child files — is there a current immunization blue card in every one?
6. Confirm the license on the wall is current and visible.
7. Pull the fire drill log — is last month's drill initialed?
8. Medication cart: locked, in-date, matching parent authorization slips.
9. Outside: fence intact, gate latches, no heat-exposure hazards.
10. Walk your emergency plan binder — current, posted, everyone can find it.

## What happens if you fail
ADHS issues a written Statement of Deficiencies. You submit a Plan of Correction within **10 days**. They come back for a revisit. Enforcement escalates from a notice, to a civil money penalty, to suspension, to revocation — see A.R.S. § 36-882/883 and the AZ Care Check enforcement-action pages. Revocation appeals go to an administrative hearing. Everything posts publicly on AZ Care Check for a rolling **three-year window**.

## The weird Arizona gotcha nobody tells you about
Arizona has **two** regulators. ADHS licenses centers (5+ kids, non-residential). DES certifies group homes (5–10 kids in a house) and family homes (≤4 kids). They use different rules, different inspectors, and different public portals (AZ Care Check vs. https://azchildcareprovidersearch.azdes.gov/). If you're a home-based provider looking up your own "ADHS report," you're looking in the wrong place. And the 2024 rule rewrite phases training hours to 24/year through **August 2026** — older handbooks still say 18 and will get you cited if you rely on them.

## For Magnus — how to sell to an Arizona owner
Biggest fear: an unannounced visit while a teacher is out on break and the room goes over ratio — it happens almost every week and lands on AZ Care Check within 48 hours. Drop this in 30 seconds to prove you know their world: "Your 2024 9 A.A.C. 5 rewrite pushed training from 18 to 24 hours a year, phased in through August 2026 — are you tracking the new hours on every teacher's file yet?" That single sentence signals you know the rule rewrite, which most vendors don't. Today they're using paper files plus a Google Sheet for fingerprint card expirations and CE hours, or a subsidy-facing system like Procare that doesn't model the Level I card as a gating credential. The close: "I'll keep you out of Care Check — nobody should be able to Google your name and see a ratio violation."
