## 1. You Must Comply With Law

You must comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and licensing requirements in your use of the Service, including:

- State child care licensing and regulatory requirements.
- State and federal privacy, health, and employment laws.
- Breach notification statutes.
- Export controls and sanctions laws.

---

## 2. Data Handling Restrictions

The Service is designed to handle specific categories of child care compliance data. You must **not** upload or process the following in the Service:

- **Unmasked Social Security numbers.** Mask to last 4 digits if necessary for your records; do not upload full SSNs.
- **Full payment card numbers or card verification values.** Payment data is handled only through Stripe.
- **Raw background check reports** provided by consumer reporting agencies. You may record the fact that a background check cleared and the date; you must not upload the underlying report or detailed results.
- **Protected Health Information (PHI)** as defined under HIPAA, in contexts where HIPAA applies to you. (Most daycares are not HIPAA-covered entities; confirm your status.)
- **Biometric identifiers** (fingerprints, facial scans, voice prints) and biometric identifier templates.
- **Driver's license or passport scans** beyond what is strictly required for a specific compliance purpose.
- **Bank account and routing numbers.**
- **Government-issued IDs of minors,** except where explicitly required by a regulator.

If you must process any of the above, you must first obtain our written consent and implement appropriate safeguards agreed between us.

## 3. Prohibited Conduct

You may not:

- **Access without authorization.** Use or attempt to use another user's account, session token, or magic link without explicit permission.
- **Circumvent security.** Attempt to bypass, probe, scan, or test the vulnerability of the Service or network, or breach any authentication or security measure, without our prior written consent (including a written penetration-testing authorization).
- **Abuse magic-link portals.** Share, forward, reuse, or otherwise misuse parent- or staff-facing magic links.
- **Reverse engineer.** Decompile, disassemble, or reverse engineer any component of the Service, except to the extent such restriction is prohibited by applicable law.
- **Resell or repurpose.** Use the Service to provide services to third parties outside your own licensed child care operations. In particular, do not resell access, wrap the Service in a white-labeled product, or provide consulting services that use the Service as the product without our prior written consent.
- **Impersonate.** Impersonate any person or entity, or falsely state or otherwise misrepresent an affiliation.
- **Harass or harm.** Use the Service to harass, threaten, or defame any person, including staff, parents, children, or other users.
- **Upload malware.** Upload, transmit, or distribute any file containing viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, spyware, or other malicious code.
- **Scrape or overload.** Use automated means to scrape, index, or harvest data from the Service, except as expressly permitted by our documented API. Do not subject the Service to volumes of requests that degrade performance for other users.
- **Infringe IP.** Upload content that infringes any third party's intellectual property or proprietary rights.
- **Violate privacy.** Upload personal information of any individual without the authority to do so (for example, parent data without the necessary authorization from the daycare to hold that information).
- **Tamper with audit trails.** Attempt to alter, falsify, or delete audit-trail records or metadata that the Service generates.
- **Deceptively e-sign.** Use the e-signature functionality to sign in the name of another person without that person's authorization, or to manipulate a document's contents after signing.
- **Circumvent metering.** Exceed or evade plan limits (e.g., number of sites, users, record counts) through artifice.
- **Misuse AI features.** Use AI-assisted features (OCR, extraction, summaries) to produce content you know or reasonably should know is false, and then rely on that output as an authoritative record.

## 4. Content Standards

Uploaded content must:

- Relate directly to your licensed child care compliance obligations.
- Be accurate to the best of your knowledge at the time of upload.
- Not contain unlawful, defamatory, obscene, or intentionally harmful material.
- Not include non-consensual imagery of any person, including children.
- Not contain images or videos of children in any context that could reasonably be considered sensitive beyond what is strictly required for compliance.

## 5. Security Obligations

- Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication where offered.

- Not share login credentials between individuals. Each Authorized User should have their own account.

3. We may report violations to law enforcement and cooperate with investigations.
4. We may terminate your account for material or repeated violations.
5. We may preserve evidence of violations as required by law or reasonably necessary to defend our rights.
