# How to Pass Your Minnesota Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector now works for the **Minnesota Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF), Licensing Division**. Licensing authority for child care centers moved from DHS to DCYF in the 2023–2024 reorganization. You get **one unannounced inspection every calendar year**. New centers get four visits their first year under the "Early and Often" model — one scheduled at the 3-month mark, then three unannounced. Complaint visits come with no warning, any time. Every monitoring visit posts to the public **Licensing Information Lookup (LIL)** within **3 days** of the visit, and correction orders, licensing actions, and maltreatment findings stay up for **4 years**. Public sites:
- DCYF: https://dcyf.mn.gov/
- Licensing Information Lookup (public inspection portal): https://licensinglookup.dhs.state.mn.us/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main statute (current)** — Minn. Stat. Chapter 142B (DCYF-era licensing of programs for children).
- **The legacy statute** — Minn. Stat. Chapter 245A (Human Services Licensing Act).
- **The rulebook for centers** — Minnesota Rules Chapter 9503. https://www.revisor.mn.gov/rules/9503/
- **The rulebook for family child care** — Minnesota Rules 9502.
- **Ratios + group size** — Rule 9503.0040. https://www.revisor.mn.gov/rules/9503.0040/
- **Drop-in / School-age** — Rule 9503.0075.
- **Background studies (NETStudy 2.0)** — Minn. Stat. 245C. https://www.revisor.mn.gov/statutes/cite/245C

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From Minn. Rule 9503.0040. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age band | One staff for every... | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (6 weeks – 16 months) | 4 | 8 |
| Toddler (16 – 33 months) | 7 | 14 |
| Preschooler (33 months – Kindergarten) | 10 | 20 |
| School-age (K – age 12) | 15 | 30 |

**Drop-in programs** (9503.0075) use different numbers: 1:4 for 6 wk–16 mo, 1:7 for 17–29 mo, 1:10 for 30 mo–12 yr. **Minimum two staff must be present whenever the center is operating** (Rule 9503.0090), even if ratios would allow only one. Ratios must be maintained during outdoor play and transitions.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced.
2. Checks license is posted and current.
3. Walks every room, counts children, compares to staff present.
4. Pulls staff files — NETStudy 2.0 background study clearance first.
5. Pulls child files — immunization (or exemption), health record, ICCPP.
6. Reviews written plans: **Risk Reduction Plan** (Rule 9503.0140), Emergency Plan, Health & Safety Plan.
7. Reviews drill logs.
8. Exit. Findings post to LIL in **3 days**. Correction orders, licensing actions, and maltreatment memoranda remain public for **4 years**.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
Per Chapter 9503 and Star Tribune coverage of MN correction-order patterns:
1. **Out-of-ratio or fewer than 2 staff on site** (Rule 9503.0090 — two-staff floor is strict).
2. **NETStudy 2.0 background study** not current, or a staff member on the floor before clearance.
3. **Risk Reduction Plan** missing, outdated, or not updated after a significant incident.
4. **Immunization record** missing or exemption not properly notarized under Minn. Stat. 121A.15.
5. **Mandated reporter / Shaken Baby / SUIDS** training not documented at orientation.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment application** (name, DOB, address, parent contact, emergency contacts).
- **Signed parent-provider agreement**.
- **Current immunization record** — per Minn. Stat. 121A.15 and Minn. Rule 4605 — OR a notarized conscientious / medical exemption.
- **Health record** / physician's statement, allergy information.
- **Authorization for emergency medical care**.
- **Written authorizations** for transportation, field trips, photos.
- **Individual Child Care Program Plan (ICCPP)** — developmental goals and accommodations.
- **Medication authorization + log** for any medication administered.
- **Daily attendance record** (arrival/departure times).
- **Special health-care needs plan**, if applicable.
- **Incident / injury reports** when applicable.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **NETStudy 2.0 background study clearance** (Minn. Stat. 245C) — required **before** unsupervised access to children. Also required for owners, board members, and household members 13+.
- **Application + resume** documenting qualifications per Rule 9503.0032–0034 (Teacher, Assistant Teacher, Aide).
- **Reference checks**.
- **Current CPR and Pediatric First Aid certifications** for required positions.
- **Annual in-service training log** (minimum ongoing hours required by rule).
- **Orientation training log** — must include mandated reporter, safe sleep, SUIDS, abusive head trauma, Shaken Baby Syndrome.
- **TB screening** as required by health department.
- **Safe Sleep training** specifically for infant-room staff.
- **Supervisory performance reviews**.

## What must be on your walls
- Current DCYF license.
- Most recent inspection / correction order summary.
- Written Risk Reduction Plan, Emergency Plan, and Health & Safety Plan.
- Safe Sleep policy in infant rooms.
- Menus.
- Mandated reporter notice.
- Parent complaint contact.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** — per emergency plan.
- **Severe weather / tornado drill** — per plan.
- **Lockdown drill** — per plan.
- **Daily attendance** (arrival/departure).
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident reports**.
- **Two-staff-on-site log** — whenever open.
- **Annual update to the Risk Reduction Plan** and after any significant incident.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff. Is there a second adult on the premises? (Rule 9503.0090.)
2. Pull 3 random staff files — NETStudy 2.0 clearance on top, CPR card, annual training log.
3. Pull 3 random child files — immunization or notarized exemption, ICCPP, health record.
4. Risk Reduction Plan — when was it last updated?
5. Infant rooms: safe sleep posture, training documented.
6. Fire drill log — current month initialed?
7. Tornado drill log — this season?
8. License posted and current (issued by DCYF, not DHS — newer licenses should show DCYF).
9. Medication cabinet — locked, in-date, matching consent.
10. Pull your LIL detail page — any correction order from the last 4 years not marked resolved?

## What happens if you fail
DCYF issues a **correction order** with a category of violation and a deadline. The order posts to your LIL detail page. Serious or uncorrected issues lead to **conditional license, fine, suspension, or revocation** — all posted publicly for 4 years. Maltreatment investigations get their own investigative memorandum on LIL with a finding (determined / not determined / inconclusive). Appeals go through the state administrative process.

## The weird Minnesota gotcha nobody tells you about
Minnesota licensing just went through a **major agency move** (DHS → DCYF, 2023–24), and public records still live on the old DHS-branded **Licensing Information Lookup** at `licensinglookup.dhs.state.mn.us`. Your license number is the permanent key — a simple URL like `Details.aspx?l=<your_license_number>` pulls your full record. That means parents (and journalists, after 2026's viral Star Tribune coverage of alleged fraud) can type your license number into Google and see **every correction order from the last 4 years** plus any maltreatment findings. Second gotcha: **two staff on premises at all times**, regardless of how few kids are there — so that early-morning or late-afternoon slot where ratios would let a single teacher work alone still requires a second adult under Rule 9503.0090. Third: Minnesota licenses expire **December 31** each year, so renewal is a fixed annual calendar event, not a rolling one like some states.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Minnesota owner
Biggest fear: a correction order or maltreatment memo on their LIL page — 4-year retention means a bad call in 2026 still shows up in 2030, and Star Tribune is in heavy rotation on MN child-care fraud stories. Drop this in 30 seconds: "LIL posts your correction orders within 3 days of the visit and keeps them for 4 years. Rule 9503.0090 says two staff on site at all times. Are you tracking NETStudy 2.0 clearance, your Risk Reduction Plan version, and your two-staff log in one place?" Today they are using NETStudy for background, Parent Aware for QRIS, and a binder for the Risk Reduction Plan — DCYF doesn't give them one system. The close: "We catch every NETStudy expiration and flag the two-staff window before an LIL post can hurt you."
