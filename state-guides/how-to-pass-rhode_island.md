# How to Pass Your Rhode Island Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In Rhode Island, your licensor is a **DHS Office of Child Care** specialist. (Before 2019, DCYF handled this; DCYF now only licenses foster homes, child-placing agencies, and residential child care and handles enforcement referrals for unlicensed operators.) You'll get **at least TWO unannounced monitoring visits per year** — stricter than most neighboring states. Complaint-driven visits can happen anytime. Fire Marshal inspects at initial licensure and periodically. Food-service permits come from the Department of Health if you prepare meals.

- DHS Office of Child Care: https://dhs.ri.gov/programs-and-services/child-care/child-care-providers
- RISES Early Learning Programs portal (public): https://earlylearningprograms.dhs.ri.gov/

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
**Child Care Centers (218-RICR-70-00-1):**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Younger Infants (6 wk – 12 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Older Infants (12 – 18 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Toddlers (18 – 36 mo) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Preschool 3 (3 yr) | 1:9 | 18 |
| Preschool 4 (4 yr) | 1:10 | 20 |
| Preschool 5 – 6 (not in K) | 1:12 | 24 |
| School-Age (K – gr 7, under 16) | 1:13 | 26 |

**Family Child Care Home (218-RICR-70-00-2):** up to 6 kids total; no more than 3 under 2; no more than 2 under 18 months.

**Warning:** Group size is set by the **youngest child** in the room. **No exceptions to infant ratios** under any circumstance — no averaging, no combining, ever. At nap time, a single staff per toddler/preschool group may supervise.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced (twice yearly baseline per 218-RICR-70-00-1).
2. Confirms current license + BrightStars rating are posted.
3. Walks rooms; counts children and staff; checks ratios against the table above.
4. Measures or spot-checks floor space (45 sq ft of usable floor space per infant/toddler — 218-RICR-70-00-1.8).
5. Pulls staff files — comprehensive background checks (state BCI + FBI + child abuse/neglect + sex offender), 5-year refresh, qualifications, CPR/First Aid, PD log.
6. Pulls child files — enrollment, immunization per R23-1-IMM, health exam, emergency consents.
7. Reviews daily reports for infants/toddlers (feeding, sleep, communication notes).
8. Writes deficiencies; reports post on **RISES** for parents to see.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
(Based on the RI Auditor General's 2025 audit — 60% of facilities had significant noncompliance; 232 facility findings, 920 documentation findings.)
1. **Infant ratio violations** — the zero-exception rule is inflexible; even 30 seconds of 1:5 is a citation.
2. **Background check gaps** — especially missing the every-5-year refresh for assistants, household members 18+, and volunteers.
3. **Documentation failures** — missing immunization record, missing health exam, missing daily infant reports.
4. **Floor space shortage** — 45 sq ft per infant/toddler is measured; cribs and furniture can push a room out of compliance.
5. **BrightStars / licensing misalignment** — declared practices on BrightStars don't match actual operations at inspection.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Application form** (name, DOB, parent/guardian contact, authorized pickup).
- **Health exam** and **current immunization record** per R23-1-IMM.
- **Emergency medical treatment authorization**.
- Documentation of injuries, accidents, illnesses.
- Progress and development notes.
- Field trip authorizations.
- Parental photo/video consent.
- **Infants/toddlers:** developmental + health history, feeding habits, sleep patterns, communication methods, **daily reports**.
- Daily attendance / sign-in & sign-out.
- **Retain minimum 3 years**.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Personal data and job description.
- **Comprehensive background checks** — state, federal, child abuse & neglect, sex offender — **refreshed every 5 years**.
- Employment verification and references.
- Health records per state immunization rules.
- Performance evaluations.
- Professional development certification and training logs.
- Orientation documentation.
- **Current CPR and pediatric First Aid**.
- Evidence of education qualifications per role.
- **Retain 3 years** after employee leaves.

## What must be on your walls
- Current DHS license.
- BrightStars rating.
- Most recent Fire Marshal inspection.
- DOH food service permit (if meals prepared).
- Evacuation plan / emergency procedures.
- DHS Office of Child Care complaint contact.
- Daily schedule.
- Menu.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented.
- **Emergency / shelter drills** — documented.
- **Daily attendance** with sign-in/sign-out.
- **Infant daily reports** (feeding, sleep, communication).
- **Medication administration log**.
- **Incident / accident reports**.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count infants — if you have 5 on 1 staff for a single second, you will be cited. Split or add staff.
2. Count toddlers and preschoolers; verify ratios per 218-RICR-70-00-1.
3. Measure or estimate floor space — 45 sq ft per infant/toddler (rule 1.8).
4. Post license + BrightStars rating + latest fire inspection.
5. Pull a random staff file: 5-year comprehensive background refresh current?
6. Pull a random child file: immunization per R23-1-IMM, health exam, emergency consent?
7. Infant room — daily report for every infant filled in today?
8. Medication locked; log current.
9. Diaper-change stations clean; gloves stocked.
10. Outdoor area fenced, no hazards.

## What happens if you fail
DHS can issue a deficiency, require a corrective-action plan, downgrade you to **conditional** status, or **suspend / revoke** under 218-RICR-70-00-1. Initial licenses are **Provisional for 6 months** until a monitoring visit confirms compliance. Running a **Family Child Care Home without a license** triggers a DCYF referral to the **RI Attorney General** for prosecution (per DCYF Child Care Licensing Unit). Findings appear on **RISES** so parents see them. Appeal through DHS administrative hearing; APRA appeals go through the Attorney General's Office of Open Government under §38-2-8.

## The weird Rhode Island gotcha nobody tells you about
**Two unannounced monitoring visits per year** — stricter than New Hampshire, Massachusetts, or Connecticut (218-RICR-70-00-1). Owners used to other NE states relax after visit #1 and get caught on visit #2. Second gotcha: **RI has ZERO exceptions on infant ratios**. No averaging, no "just for 5 minutes while I answer the door." The rule is absolute; a single moment of 1:5 at infants is a full citation that ends up on RISES. Third gotcha: the 2025 RI Auditor General's audit of 50 facilities found **60% had significant noncompliance** — DHS is under public pressure right now, so expect tighter visits through 2026.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Rhode Island owner
Their fear is the RI Auditor General's 2025 audit (60% noncompliance) turning into a statewide crackdown — and every deficiency landing on RISES where parents see it. The 30-second number: 2 unannounced monitoring visits per year, zero infant-ratio exceptions, and 45 sq ft per infant. RI owners today live between DHS, DCYF (enforcement-side), and BrightStars, with separate paper trails each. Lead with: "One place where DHS, BrightStars, and your background-check refreshes all live, so when the DHS specialist walks in twice a year you're clean on the first visit and the second." Reinforce: two audits just hit RI's child care sector in 2025 — the heat is on.
