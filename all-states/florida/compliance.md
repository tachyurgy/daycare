# Florida Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
**Florida Department of Children and Families (DCF), Office of Child Care Regulation.** DCF Licensing Counselors conduct inspections. Some counties (including Hillsborough, Broward, Palm Beach, Pinellas, Sarasota) operate **local licensing agencies** delegated by DCF that enforce DCF standards plus any county-specific enhancements.

- DCF address: 2415 N. Monroe Street, Suite 400, Tallahassee, FL 32303-4190
- Helpline: (855) 776-2729
- Public search: **CARES Public Search** — https://caressearch.myflfamilies.com/PublicSearch
- Child Care Licensure landing: https://www.myflfamilies.com/services/licensing/child-care-licensure

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Statute:** **Florida Statutes Chapter 402** (Health and Human Services — Social and Economic Assistance), especially **§ 402.301 - § 402.319** (Child Care Facilities).
- **Administrative Code:** **Florida Administrative Code Chapter 65C-22** (Child Care Standards) — the binding rule set.
- **Incorporated by reference:** the **Child Care Facility Handbook (October 2021)** is incorporated by reference into 65C-22, making its requirements legally binding. The Handbook is the single source of truth inspectors consult in the field. Companion: **School-Age Child Care Handbook**.
- **Inspection form:** **CF-FSP 5316** — Standards Classification Summary — the form inspectors fill out during the visit, keyed directly to Handbook sections.
- **Health &amp; Safety Checklist:** **CF-FSP 5274** — used during health &amp; safety inspections.

## Facility Types Regulated
1. **Licensed Child Care Facility** — 2+ unrelated children; 65C-22.001.
2. **Licensed Family Day Care Home (FDCH)** — up to 10 children in provider's residence (age sub-limits); 65C-20.
3. **Licensed Large Family Child Care Home (LFCCH)** — up to 12 children with substitute / employee; 65C-20.
4. **Registered Family Day Care Home** — in counties that permit registration vs. licensing.
5. **School-Age Child Care Program** — covered by School-Age Child Care Handbook.
6. **Public School VPK (Voluntary Pre-K)** sites — DOE-overseen but often co-located with DCF-licensed programs.

## Key Licensing Forms (CF-FSP series)
- **CF-FSP 5081** — Application for a License to Operate a Child Care Facility
- **CF-FSP 5131** — Application for Family Day Care Home
- **CF-FSP 5274** — Health &amp; Safety Checklist (daily operational checklist + inspection)
- **CF-FSP 5316** — Standards Classification Summary (the inspection form)
- **CF-FSP 5337** — Background Screening and Personnel File Requirements
- **CF-FSP 5131A** — Child Care Application for Enrollment
- **CF-FSP 5075** — Physician's Statement
- **CF-FSP 5025** — Notice of Deficiency Form

## Child-to-Staff Ratios &amp; Group Sizes (F.S. § 402.305(4))
| Age | Ratio | Class if violated |
|---|---|---|
| Birth - 1 year | **1:4** | Class 2 |
| 1 - under 2 years | 1:6 | Class 2 |
| 2 - under 3 years | 1:11 | Class 2 |
| 3 - under 4 years | 1:15 | Class 2 |
| 4 - under 5 years | 1:20 | Class 2 |
| 5 years and older | 1:25 | Class 2 |

Ratio violations are **Class 2** (serious) — inspectors count heads on-the-spot.

## Required Child Records (per 65C-22 + CF-FSP 5316)
- Enrollment application (CF-FSP 5131A)
- Current immunization record per Florida DOH 680 or 681 (or valid exemption)
- Physician's statement / physical (CF-FSP 5075)
- Health history, allergy &amp; special needs plan
- Signed consents: transportation, field trip, photography, emergency medical
- Daily attendance / sign-in / sign-out
- Incident reports
- Medication authorization + administration log
- VPK paperwork (if a VPK site)

## Required Staff Records (per CF-FSP 5337 + 65C-22)
- Application + personal history
- **Level 2 background screening** (Florida Department of Law Enforcement + FBI fingerprint via Clearinghouse, local law enforcement, sex offender registry, juvenile records, reference checks) — required **pre-hire**; cleared via the **Care Provider Background Screening Clearinghouse**
- **Florida DCF 40-Hour Introductory Child Care Training** — completed within first 90 days; 30 hours core + 10 hours specialization
- **10 hours annual in-service training** per staff member
- **Florida Child Care Staff Credential** — required for lead teachers
- **Florida Director Credential** — required for directors
- CPR / First Aid certification — at least one staff member on-site at all times must be current
- TB screening / health statement

## Inspection Cadence
- **Minimum 2 unannounced inspections per year** for licensed facilities — the most aggressive routine schedule of any major state.
- Licensed family and large family day care homes: **2 inspections per year**.
- Some documentation cites 3 inspections/year (~every 4 months) for facilities; baseline statutory minimum is 2.
- **Complaint investigations** unannounced, within statutory response windows.
- County health department + local fire marshal inspections required as separate clearances.

## Renewal Cadence
- Licenses are issued on an **annual cycle**. Renewal packet with updated fire/health/background documentation submitted to DCF Licensing Counselor.
- 40-hour DCF training must be completed within 90 days of hire; 10 hours/year thereafter.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Third-largest state market in the US:** roughly **11,000-12,000+ licensed child care providers** (facilities + family homes combined). Major MSAs: Miami/Fort Lauderdale, Orlando, Tampa/St. Petersburg, Jacksonville, Cape Coral/Fort Myers.
- **Child Care Facility Handbook** is the single-source inspector reference — incorporated by reference into 65C-22. Every inspector uses it in the field. ComplianceKit's self-inspection simulator should mirror its Table of Contents.
- **CF-FSP 5316 Standards Classification Summary** = the inspection form. **32 inspection categories** on the form for facilities (the published product spec identifies these).
- **Violation classification:** Class 1 (most serious, immediate risk) / Class 2 (serious, e.g., ratio violations) / Class 3 (less serious administrative). Enforcement escalates by class and repeat-offense history.
- **Care Provider Background Screening Clearinghouse** — statewide portal where background-check results live; employer rosters must be maintained in the Clearinghouse for portability across providers.
- **40-hour DCF introductory training** — the strictest introductory-training requirement among the big three states (CA: no mandated intro hours of similar structure; TX: 24 hours).
- **Florida Child Care Staff Credential + Director Credential** — formal credentialing requirements; maps directly to Staff File credential-tracking module.
- **County-level variance:** local licensing agencies in ~5 counties impose additional requirements on top of DCF statewide standards.
- **VPK integration** — many providers also operate Voluntary Pre-K sites under DOE oversight; dual-reporting is a pain point.
- **5 years of continuously updated child fatality data** published publicly by DCF.
