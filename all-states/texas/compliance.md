# Texas Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
**Texas Health and Human Services Commission (HHSC), Child Care Regulation (CCR) Division.** (CCR transferred from DFPS to HHSC on September 1, 2017.) CCR licenses, monitors, and inspects all child care operations statewide.

- HHSC address: 4900 North Lamar Blvd., Austin, TX 78751
- Child Care Regulation program: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/services/family-safety-resources/child-care
- Public search: https://childcare.hhs.texas.gov/ (Search Texas Child Care)

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Statute:** **Texas Human Resources Code, Chapter 42** (Regulation of Certain Facilities, Homes, and Agencies That Provide Child-Care Services).
- **Administrative Code:** **Texas Administrative Code (TAC), Title 26, Chapters 744-749**, the most widely cited being:
  - **26 TAC Chapter 744** — Minimum Standards for School-Age and Before/After-School Programs
  - **26 TAC Chapter 745** — Licensing (procedural rules covering applications, deficiencies, enforcement)
  - **26 TAC Chapter 746** — Minimum Standards for Child-Care Centers
  - **26 TAC Chapter 747** — Minimum Standards for Child-Care Homes (Registered / Licensed / Listed)
  - **26 TAC Chapter 748** — Minimum Standards for General Residential Operations
  - **26 TAC Chapter 749** — Minimum Standards for Child-Placing Agencies

## Facility Types Regulated
1. **Licensed Child-Care Center** — 13+ children; Chapter 746.
2. **Licensed Child-Care Home (LCCH)** — 7-12 children in the provider's residence; Chapter 747.
3. **Registered Child-Care Home (RCCH)** — up to 6 children (can include 6 additional school-age); Chapter 747.
4. **Listed Family Home** — care for 1-3 unrelated children; must be listed with HHSC.
5. **School-Age and Before/After-School Program** — Chapter 744.

## Key Licensing Forms (CCR forms)
CCR uses numbered **Form** series. Core:
- **Form 2936** — Application for License / Certification
- **Form 2935** — Admission Information (per-child)
- **Form 2941** — Sign-In / Sign-Out Log
- **Form 2948** — Plan of Operation (operational blueprint — hours, services, policies, procedures)
- **Form 1100** — Daily Building &amp; Grounds Checklist (24 items, daily Mon-Fri)
- **Form 7239** — Incident / Illness Report
- **Form 7259** — Personnel Records Evaluation (inspector uses at visit)
- **Form 7260** — Children's Records Evaluation (inspector uses at visit)
- **Form 7261** — Center Records Evaluation (inspector uses at visit)
- **Form 7263** — Emergency Practices

## Child-to-Staff Ratios &amp; Group Sizes (Chapter 746, Child-Care Centers)
| Age | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| 0 - 11 months | **1:4** | 10 |
| 12 - 17 months | 1:5 | 13 |
| 18 - 23 months | 1:9 | 18 |
| 2 years | 1:11 | 22 |
| 3 years | 1:15 | 30 |
| 4 years | 1:18 | 35 |
| 5 years | 1:22 | 35 |
| 6 - 8 years | 1:26 | 35 |
| 9 - 12 years | 1:26 | 35 |

Ratios must be maintained **at all times** — during transitions, outdoor play, field trips.

## Required Child Records (per Chapter 746 + Form 7260)
- Admission Information (Form 2935) — parent/guardian, emergency contacts, authorized pickups
- Current immunization record per Texas Department of State Health Services schedule (or valid exemption)
- Health statement / physical
- Hearing &amp; vision screening records (when age-required)
- Individual health / allergy / special needs plan where applicable
- Signed consents: transportation, field trips, photography, emergency treatment
- Daily sign-in / sign-out (Form 2941)
- Incident / illness reports (Form 7239)
- Medication authorization + administration log

## Required Staff Records (per Chapter 746 + Form 7259)
- Personal history statement
- **Background check** — Texas DPS criminal history, FBI fingerprint, Central Registry check (per federal CCDBG + Chapter 745 Subchapter F). Mandatory for all employees, volunteers, and household members with unsupervised access; **pre-service** clearance required.
- Health statement / TB screening
- **Pre-service training: 24 hours** — 8 hours must be completed before working directly with children
- CPR / First Aid / pediatric AED certification
- **Annual in-service training: 24 hours/year** for full-time caregivers (center staff)
- SIDS prevention training (facilities serving infants)
- Mandated reporter training
- Qualification documentation for director, assistant director, caregivers (education + experience per Chapter 746 Subchapter D)

## Inspection Cadence
- **Initial licensing inspection** pre-license plus additional visits in the first year of operation.
- **Routine annual inspection** — typically a full-day visit; inspector uses a laptop / tablet running the **CLASS system** to record deficiencies.
- **Complaint investigations** — always unannounced.
- **Follow-up** visits after deficiency finding.
- **Inspectors:** HHSC state employees.

## Renewal Cadence
- Licenses do not "expire" on a fixed renewal calendar the way CA's do. Instead, HHSC conducts periodic monitoring and re-evaluation; operators maintain compliance continuously. Annual fees apply (Chapter 745 fee schedule).

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Second-largest state market in the US:** roughly **16,000-18,000 regulated operations** (centers + registered / licensed homes + listed family homes + school-age programs) with wide geographic distribution. Major MSAs: Houston, Dallas-Fort Worth, San Antonio, Austin, El Paso.
- **Deficiency weight system** — each Minimum Standard carries a deficiency weight: **High / Medium-High / Medium / Medium-Low / Low**. Weights drive enforcement decisions and administrative penalty amounts.
- **Public transparency** — every deficiency is posted on the operation's public online record at childcare.hhs.texas.gov within 14 days of the visit. This is one of the most transparent state datasets in the country.
- **CLASS system** — HHSC's inspector-facing case management system. The public search is the external face of CLASS data.
- **Administrative penalties** — progressive enforcement: deficiency → evaluation → probation → suspension → revocation. 30-day hearing window after penalty notice (Chapter 745 Subchapter M).
- **Plan of Operation (Form 2948)** — a distinctive TX requirement; comprehensive document covering every operational aspect, updated at least annually.
- **Daily Building &amp; Grounds Checklist (Form 1100)** — 24-item daily checklist that is the most operationally intensive recurring TX compliance task.
- **Listed Family Home tier** — unique sub-tier for micro-providers caring for 1-3 unrelated children; minimal compliance but still HHSC-listed.
- **Chapter 745 Subchapter F (background checks)** — prescriptive, one of the strictest in the country; ongoing re-check schedule.
