# Illinois — Child Care Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority
- **Agency:** Illinois Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS)
- **Division:** Division of Licensing (Office of Child Welfare)
- **URL:** https://dcfs.illinois.gov/
- Portal for public info (sunshine): https://sunshine.dcfs.illinois.gov/
- Note: The **Illinois Department of Early Childhood (IDEC)**, created in 2024, is assuming responsibilities for ECE programs in a phased transition; as of 2026 DCFS still issues day-care licenses under Part 407.

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Child Care Act of 1969** (225 ILCS 10) — enabling statute
- **89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 407** — Licensing Standards for Day Care Centers (most relevant)
- **89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 406** — Licensing Standards for Day Care Homes (3–8 children in residence)
- **89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 408** — Licensing Standards for Group Day Care Homes (up to 12 children)
- **89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 377** — Background checks / CANTS clearances
- **89 Ill. Adm. Code Part 383** — Licensing Compliance and Monitoring Procedures
- **Immunization Code** — 77 Ill. Adm. Code 665 (childcare immunization)

## Facility Types Regulated
- **Day Care Center (DCC)** — 15+ children, non-residential; subject to Part 407
- **Day Care Home (DCH)** — 3–8 children in operator's residence (Part 406)
- **Group Day Care Home (GDCH)** — up to 12 children in operator's residence, with an assistant (Part 408)
- **Part-day preschools / nursery schools** — typically licensed as DCCs
- **School-age programs** — licensed as DCCs when serving 15+ kids outside school
- **License-exempt programs** — certain school district, faith-based, and short-duration programs

## Key Licensing Forms
- **CFS 388** — Application for License to Operate a Day Care Facility
- **CFS 718-D** — Background Check Request (CANTS / SACWIS)
- **CFS 718-A** — Applicant Personal Information (staff & household)
- **CFS 600-9** — Medical Report for Day Care Home Provider (adult)
- **CFS 600** — Medical Report on Child
- **CFS 410-P** — Physical Examination for Child Care Provider
- **CFS 1050-52** — Summary of Licensing Standards (notice to providers)
- **CFS 437** — Emergency Preparedness Plan template
- **Certificate of Fire Inspection** — from local/state Fire Marshal
- **Certificate of Occupancy** — from local building authority
- **Department of Public Health certificate** — for food-service centers

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (§ 407.190)
| Age band | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (6 wks – 14 mo) | 1:4 | 12 |
| Toddlers (15–23 mo) | 1:5 | 15 |
| Two-year-olds | 1:8 | 16 |
| Three-year-olds | 1:10 | 20 |
| Four-year-olds | 1:10 | 20 |
| Five-year-olds (preschool) | 1:20 | 20 |
| School-age (kindergartners present) | 1:20 | 30 |

- Combined-age groups use ratio of the youngest child
- Exception: teacher + assistant may supervise up to 30 children if ALL are 5+

## Required Child Records (§ 407.360)
- Child's name, date of birth, home address, parent/guardian names & contacts
- Current physical exam signed by physician (within last 6 months at admission; updated per DPH schedule)
- Immunization record compliant with 77 Ill. Adm. Code 665 (DTaP, polio, MMR, Hib, hep B, varicella, PCV, flu)
- Lead screening documentation (for children under 6)
- Emergency contacts (2+ authorized persons)
- Authorized emergency medical consent form
- Special-needs, allergies, and IEP/IFSP records as applicable
- Transportation and field-trip permissions
- Daily attendance log retained 1 year
- Incident/accident reports
- Medication administration records (parent written authorization required)

## Required Staff Records (§§ 407.90–407.120; Parts 383/377)
- **CANTS / State central child-abuse register clearance** (DCFS background check)
- **Illinois State Police fingerprint-based criminal history check**
- **FBI national fingerprint check**
- **Sex Offender Registry** check (state & federal)
- Staff application (CFS 718-A) and references
- Physical exam / TB screening at hire; repeat per DPH guidance
- **Orientation training** before working alone with children
- **15 clock hours of in-service training per year** (Directors higher)
- **Mandated reporter (DCFS) training** — every 3 years
- **Infant/child CPR & first aid** — at least one certified staff on-site at all times
- **Radon safety training** (facility-level)
- **Director qualifications** meeting Section 407.140 education/experience tiers
- **ExceleRate Illinois registry** (Gateways Registry) enrollment for ECE staff
- Annual fire safety training
- Annual **monthly fire drill** documentation (Rule 407)

## Inspection Cadence
- **Annual unannounced monitoring inspection** — required (Procedures 383)
- **60-day post-license inspection** — within 60 days of initial license
- **Permit monitoring** — at least monthly during permit phase (pre-full license)
- **Complaint-driven inspection** — any time
- **Fire safety inspection** — monthly (facility self-inspection) + annual by Fire Marshal
- **Radon test** — every 3 years (report posted next to license)
- **Health inspection** — annually by local health department for centers preparing food

## Renewal Cadence
- A **day care center license is valid for three (3) years** (§ 407.40)
- Renewal application is mailed by DCFS; must be completed, signed, and returned by stated deadline (§ 407.55)

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **ExceleRate Illinois** — state QRIS with Licensed + Bronze/Silver/Gold Circles of Quality and Great Start credential tiers
- **Gateways to Opportunity registry** — mandatory professional-development registry for directors, teachers, assistants
- **Radon testing** every 3 years, report posted with license
- **Illinois Head Start State Collaboration** programs overlay
- **Pre-K for All** / Illinois Early Childhood Block Grant programs
- **Birth-to-Five Illinois** regional councils oversee ECE planning (post-IDEC transition)
- Every center and home must display the license in a public area
- **Play-based curriculum & screen-time limits** specified
- **Tummy time** required daily for non-walking infants (Part 407, recent addition)
- Chicago Public Schools Pre-K partners have additional collaborative agreements
- CPS (Chicago) and Cook County may layer additional health-department requirements
- **Illinois Department of Early Childhood transition** — expect transfer of licensing function during 2026–2027
