01 Licensing Authority

Massachusetts Department of Early Education and Care (EEC), a cabinet-level agency under the Executive Office of Education. Created under M.G.L. c. 15D; operates the LEAD (Licensing Education Analytic Database) system statewide. Consumer portal: childcare.mass.gov/findchildcare. Provider portal: childcare.mass.gov/providerlicensing.

02 Primary Regulatory Citation

  • 606 CMR 7.00 — Standards for Family, Small Group and School Age, and Large Group and School Age Child Care Programs — full PDF

  • 105 CMR 430.00 (DPH) — sanitation/health overlay
  • Enabling statute: M.G.L. c. 15D

Findings and Corrective Action Plans are posted to the public search within 90 days of case closure and remain visible for up to 5 years. Investigative coverage of EEC oversight gaps appeared in a 2024–2025 Boston 25 News "25 Investigates" series (family-childcare red flags) and a 2024 State Audit of EEC (OSA press release). FOIA path: File a Public Record Request with EEC, eec.rao@mass.gov — first 4 hours free.

07 Outreach Signals

  • Compliance anxiety is fresh. 2024 State Auditor report documented systemic oversight lapses — directors are being told by parents & C3 reviewers to raise their own compliance game.
  • C3 attestation burden is monthly. Every licensed provider drawing C3 stabilization funds must attest to compliance and submit enrollment monthly — a recurring, painful admin workflow ComplianceKit can eliminate.
  • MA QRIS has stalled at Levels 2–3. Providers want to move up but lack bandwidth to assemble evidence binders — a natural upsell for automated document archiving.
  • BRC re-checks every 3 years (606 CMR 14) is a frequently-missed deadline — staff-file tracking is a high-value wedge.
  • Boston-metro FCC density means a highly clustered, word-of-mouth market — one referring champion in Cambridge/Somerville unlocks 10 more.
