# New York — Child Care Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority
- **Agency:** New York State Office of Children and Family Services (OCFS)
- **Division:** Division of Child Care Services (DCCS)
- **URL:** https://ocfs.ny.gov/programs/childcare/
- Regulatory oversight for all New York State is by OCFS, except that **New York City** child day care centers are regulated by the **NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DOHMH), Bureau of Child Care** under NYC Health Code Article 47 (NYC-licensed centers).

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- Social Services Law § 390 (enabling statute — "Child Day Care; License or Registration Required")
- **18 NYCRR Part 418** — Day Care Centers (the operating regulation)
  - **Subpart 418-1** — Child Day Care Centers (13+ children)
  - **Subpart 418-2** — Small Day Care Centers (fewer children, group-size threshold)
- **18 NYCRR Part 416** — Group Family Day Care Homes (7–12 children, plus school-agers)
- **18 NYCRR Part 417** — Family Day Care Homes (3–6 children, plus school-agers)
- **18 NYCRR Part 414** — School-Age Child Care Programs
- **18 NYCRR Part 413** — Definitions, Enforcement, and Hearings (cross-cuts all program types)
- NYC: **NYC Health Code Article 47** (in addition to state regs, for NYC-sited centers)

## Facility Types Regulated
- **Child Day Care Center (DCC)** — 13+ children, non-residential
- **Small Day Care Center (SDCC)** — 3–12 children at a non-residential site that is not a home
- **Group Family Day Care (GFDC)** — operated in a residence, 7–12 children + up to 4 school-agers
- **Family Day Care (FDC)** — operated in a residence, 3–6 children + up to 2 school-agers
- **School-Age Child Care (SACC)** — kindergarten through 12 years, before/after school
- **Legally Exempt** — relative/informal care, registers separately

## Key Licensing Forms
(Most are filed via the OCFS CCFS e-licensing system.)
- **OCFS-LDSS-4699** — Application for a License/Registration to Operate a Child Day Care Program
- **OCFS-LDSS-4700** — Day Care Services Application
- **OCFS-6001** — Staff/Volunteer Application and Medical Statement
- **OCFS-6002** — Staff Medical Statement
- **OCFS-6003** — Request for Criminal History Information (fingerprints)
- **OCFS-4930** — SCR (Statewide Central Register of Child Abuse) database check authorization
- **OCFS-LDSS-4443** — Child Abuse/Maltreatment reporting form
- **OCFS-LDSS-7002** — Child's Medical Statement
- **OCFS-5188** — Child Enrollment / Emergency Information Record
- **OCFS-LDSS-4441** — Record of Medication Administration
- **CCFS-Inspection-Checklist-DCC** — OCFS inspector's compliance checklist
- **OCFS-4420** — Child Care Subsidy (CCAP) Application

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (Subpart 418-1, § 418-1.8)
| Age band | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Under 6 weeks | 1:3 | 6 |
| 6 weeks – 18 months (infant) | 1:4 | 8 |
| 18 – 36 months (toddler) | 1:5 | 12 |
| 3 years (preschool) | 1:7 | 18 |
| 4 years (preschool) | 1:8 | 21 |
| 5 years (preschool/kindergarten) | 1:9 | 24 |
| 6 – 9 years (school-age) | 1:10 | 20 |
| 10 – 12 years (school-age) | 1:15 | 30 |

- Children must be within teacher's line of sight at all times
- No one under 18 may be left alone to supervise children
- Personal-device entertainment while supervising is prohibited

## Required Child Records
- Signed enrollment form with child's full legal name, DOB, address, parent/guardian contacts
- Emergency contact information (min. 2 local contacts authorized to pick up)
- Signed medical statement / physical exam within 30 days of admission and updated annually for children under 6
- Immunization record per **Public Health Law § 2164** (DTP/DTaP, polio, MMR, varicella, Hib, PCV, hep B, influenza for preschoolers)
- Signed authorization to consent to medical treatment in emergency
- Signed permission slips for transportation, field trips, photography, web use
- Written parent handbook acknowledgement
- Daily attendance log
- Incident/accident reports with parent signature
- Medication administration records (if applicable)
- Allergy & special-needs plans

## Required Staff Records
- **Background check** (SSL § 390-b): fingerprint-based criminal history check via OCFS, plus SCR database check (state child abuse registry) — required before unsupervised contact
- Staff medical statement / TB screening within 30 days of employment, updated biennially
- **Mandated reporter training** (Social Services Law § 413) — completed prior to unsupervised contact
- **Child abuse & maltreatment identification** training
- **Health & safety 15-hour pre-service + 30 hours every 2 years** for all child-care staff (per 18 NYCRR § 418-1.14)
- **CPR & first aid certification** — at least one current-certificate holder on premises at all times
- Higher-education / credential records matching role (Director: Bachelor's + 2 yrs experience OR CDA + 3 yrs; Teacher: CDA or equivalent with plan of study)
- OCFS Staff/Volunteer application (OCFS-6001) including 3 references
- Staff orientation record

## Inspection Cadence
- **Annual unannounced inspection** — required under the federal CCDBG Act and implemented through OCFS monitoring
- **Monitoring inspection at least once per licensing period** — typically announced/scheduled
- **Complaint inspection** — performed any time OCFS receives a credible complaint
- **Fire-safety inspection** — annual, performed by local fire authority or Office of Fire Prevention and Control
- **Self-certification / renewal inspection** — performed during the renewal application window

## Renewal Cadence
- Initial and renewal licenses/registrations are issued for **up to 4 years** (maximum statutory term), provided substantial compliance
- Centers must submit a renewal application at least 60 days before expiration

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **QUALITY stars NY** — voluntary quality rating and improvement system (QRIS), 1–5 stars (overlay, not required)
- **Aspire registry** — mandatory state professional-development registry for all child-care staff (OCFS portal)
- **Lead-poisoning screening** — centers in Part 67 "high-risk" zip codes must provide parents with lead-prevention materials
- **Radon testing** for some program types in certain regions
- **Shaken Baby Syndrome training** — required for staff who work with children under 3
- **Pre-service orientation** — 15 hours before working with children
- **NY's "Common Core" 9 training topics** govern all 30-hour biennial continuing-education requirements
- **Enforcement Action Registry** — public search of violations at OCFS CCFS site
- Higher OCFS fees and NYC DOHMH Article 47 dual-regulation for NYC providers (NYC Permit is separate from state)
- **Minimum wage applies** plus hazardous-occupation restrictions for staff under 18
