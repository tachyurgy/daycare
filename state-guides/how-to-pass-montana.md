# How to Pass Your Montana Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In Montana, your licensor is a **DPHHS Child Care Licensing Program specialist** inside the **Early Childhood and Family Support Division (ECFSD), Early Childhood Services Bureau (ECSB)**. You'll face a **pre-license inspection**, an **annual renewal inspection**, and **unannounced monitoring and complaint visits** at any time. The **State Fire Marshal** (or local fire authority) must sign off annually. Your **local health department sanitarian** handles food-service and sanitation inspections (separate from DPHHS licensing).

- DPHHS Child Care Licensing: https://dphhs.mt.gov/ecfsd/childcare/childcarelicensing/
- Licensed Provider Search (SansWrite): https://webapp.sanswrite.com/MontanaDPHHS/ChildCare

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **Statute:** MCA Title 52, Chapter 2, Part 7 (Day Care Facilities); MCA §52-2-704 (licensing authority).
- **Regulations:** **ARM Title 37, Chapter 95** — Licensing of Day Care Facilities. Key rules:
  - ARM 37.95.141 — Facility records.
  - ARM 37.95.172 — Supervision at all times.
  - ARM 37.95.205 – 227 — Center rules.
  - ARM 37.95.623 — **Child-to-staff ratios** (current, post-HB 422 2023).
- **HB 422 (2023)** loosened several ratios — make sure you're following current rules, not pre-2023 ones. Text: https://archive.legmt.gov/bills/2023/billhtml/HB0422.htm
- **Public records:** MCA Title 2, Chapter 6 and Montana Constitution Art. II §9.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From **ARM 37.95.623** (post-HB 422):

| Age | Ratio |
|---|---|
| Birth – 12 months | 1:4 |
| 13 – 23 months (1-yr-olds) | 1:6 |
| 2 years | 1:8 |
| 3 years | 1:10 |
| 4 – 5 years | 1:12 |
| 6 years and over | 1:20 |

Ratios can be doubled at nap time for children 2+ when specific conditions are met.

**Family/Group Home:** Family home 6 kids (including your own under 6); group home up to 12 with a second caregiver. For under-2-year-olds: no more than 3 when only one caregiver is present; under-2-only facilities keep 1:4.

**Warning:** The youngest child in a mixed-age room sets the ratio. HB 422 **loosened** 1-year-olds from 1:4 to 1:6 and 6+ year-olds from 1:14 to 1:20 — any old binder printed before 2023 has stale numbers.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives (pre-license by appointment; annual renewal scheduled; unannounced monitoring at DPHHS discretion).
2. Confirms current license is posted.
3. Walks every room; counts kids vs. ratio staff per ARM 37.95.623.
4. Pulls staff files — Practitioner Registry verification, state + FBI fingerprint + CPS registry background, CPR/First Aid (within 30 days of hire), Infant Safety Essentials (within 30 days), Health & Safety Orientation (within 90 days), Together We Grow (within 90 days), 16-hour annual continuing education.
5. Pulls child files — ARM 37.95.141 requires enrollment, immunizations, physician statement, emergency consents.
6. Reviews fire drill log, medication log, incident reports.
7. Writes deficiencies — posted within days on the SansWrite public portal for anyone to see.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Stale ratio math** — still running 1-year-olds at 1:4 or 6+ at 1:14 (pre-HB 422). Even the updated ratios are easy to violate at pickup/drop-off transitions.
2. **Staff not on Early Childhood Practitioner Registry** — ARM requires all ratio-counted staff to have a current Registry level (exempt if working <500 hours/year or facility serves only 5+).
3. **Orientation training shortfalls** — CPR/First Aid not within 30 days, Health & Safety Orientation past 90 days.
4. **Missing fire marshal or local sanitarian approval** — annual fire marshal report is required at renewal.
5. **Supervision lapses** — ARM 37.95.172 requires supervision at all times; napping kids, bathroom runs, and playground transitions are where citations happen.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
Required by ARM 37.95.141:
- Enrollment form with emergency contacts and authorized pickup.
- Immunization record per DPHHS school immunization schedule.
- Physician's statement / health history.
- Medication administration authorization (if applicable).
- Emergency medical consent.
- Food allergy / dietary restriction notes.
- Daily attendance.
- Accident / incident reports.
- Field trip, transportation, photo consents.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application with work history.
- **Early Childhood Practitioner Registry** level verification.
- **State + FBI fingerprint + CPS registry** background check (via Centralized Background Check Unit).
- Health screening.
- **CPR/First Aid** within 30 days of hire.
- **Infant Safety Essentials** (2 hours, within 30 days).
- **Health & Safety Orientation** (6 hours within 90 days).
- **Together We Grow** (3 hours within 90 days).
- **16 hours annual continuing education** (before Practitioner Registry level expires).
- **Health & Safety Review** (6 hours every 3 years).
- Performance evaluations.

## What must be on your walls
- Current DPHHS license.
- Most recent State Fire Marshal (or local) inspection.
- Most recent local health / sanitarian inspection.
- Evacuation plan / emergency procedures.
- Daily schedule.
- Menu.
- DPHHS complaint contact.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented.
- **Severe weather / shelter drills** — documented.
- **Daily attendance** by room.
- **Medication log**.
- **Incident / injury reports**.
- **Supervision at all times** — ARM 37.95.172.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff per room against ARM 37.95.623 (post-HB 422).
2. Pull the Practitioner Registry list — is every ratio-counted staff current?
3. Post current license + fire marshal + sanitarian inspection.
4. Pull a random staff file: orientation timestamps within 30/90-day windows? 16-hour annual log on track?
5. Pull a random child file: immunizations, physician statement, emergency consent?
6. Medication cabinet locked; log current.
7. Diaper-change stations clean; gloves stocked.
8. Playground gates latch; fence intact; no standing water.
9. Fire-drill log — last drill within required interval.
10. Supervision check — no kid alone, no staff on phone, napping kids in view.

## What happens if you fail
DPHHS can cite non-compliance, require a corrective action plan, issue a **provisional license**, **suspend**, or **revoke**. Findings appear on the **SansWrite public portal within days** — any parent, reporter, or competing owner can see them (the portal note: *"Complaints with no deficiencies cited are not posted."* but every cited visit is). Serious or repeated findings escalate through DPHHS administrative procedure. Appeals run through DPHHS hearings and ultimately Montana district court under MCA §2-6-1009 for records disputes.

## The weird Montana gotcha nobody tells you about
**Your staff training clock starts on day one of hire, not day one of on-the-floor work.** ARM 37.95 requires CPR/First Aid within 30 days of hire, Infant Safety Essentials within 30 days, Health & Safety Orientation within 90 days, and Together We Grow within 90 days. Owners in Bozeman or Missoula who bring on a seasonal hire for the summer think the clock is forgiven — it's not. Second gotcha: HB 422 changed ratios in 2023, and **owners running pre-2023 binders are both out of compliance with supervision staffing AND running short-staffed financially**. Re-print your ratio chart.

## For Montana — how to sell to a Montana owner
Their fear is the SansWrite portal publishing a cited visit that Missoulian or MTN News picks up. The 30-second number: 30-day CPR, 30-day Infant Safety Essentials, 90-day Health & Safety Orientation, 90-day Together We Grow, 16-hour annual CE — and the Practitioner Registry must be current for every ratio-counted staff. Montana owners today track orientation deadlines on paper or a whiteboard. Lead with: "One dashboard that shows exactly which new hires are approaching their 30- or 90-day training deadlines, and whether every ratio-counted staff is on the Practitioner Registry."
