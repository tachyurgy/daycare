# How to Pass Your Georgia Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Georgia Department of Early Care and Learning (DECAL)**, branded as "Bright from the Start." DECAL's Child Care Services division conducts licensing. Routine visits are unannounced. DECAL must do **at least one unannounced inspection per year**. Higher-risk or complaint-triggered visits come more often.

- Agency: https://www.decal.ga.gov/
- Public inspection search: https://families.decal.ga.gov/ChildCare/Search
- Enforcement Actions tracker: https://www.decal.ga.gov/EnforcementActions/Default.aspx

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — O.C.G.A. Title 20, Chapter 1A (the DECAL enabling statute). O.C.G.A. § 20-1A-2 has the core definitions.
- **Child Care Learning Centers rulebook** — Ga. Comp. R. & Regs., Rule Chapter 591-1-1. [https://rules.sos.state.ga.us/gac/591-1-1](https://rules.sos.state.ga.us/gac/591-1-1) (or the DECAL PDF: https://www.decal.ga.gov/documents/attachments/CCLCRulesandRegulations.pdf)
- **Family Child Care Learning Homes rulebook** — Rule Chapter 591-1-2.
- **Staff-to-child ratios** — Rule 591-1-1-.32.
- **Inspector's policy for annual licensing studies** — DECAL CCS-1700. [https://www.decal.ga.gov/documents/attachments/CCS-1700LicensingStudiesPolicy.pdf](https://www.decal.ga.gov/documents/attachments/CCS-1700LicensingStudiesPolicy.pdf)

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (< 18 months) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Walking 1-year-olds | 1:8 | 16 |
| 2-year-olds | 1:10 | 20 |
| 3-year-olds | 1:15 | 30 |
| 4-year-olds | 1:18 | 36 |
| 5-year-olds | 1:20 | 40 |
| 6+ years (school-age) | 1:25 | 50 |

Inspectors count heads on the spot. Staff must be seated within **arm's length** of children under 36 months during meals.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive unannounced and sign in.
2. They check the license is posted and the required notices are up.
3. They walk every classroom and count children against staff.
4. They watch infant and toddler meal time for the arm's-length rule.
5. They pull child files — enrollment, Form 3231 (immunization certificate), Form 3300 (hearing/vision/dental), physical.
6. They pull staff files — DECAL Comprehensive Records Check (CRC), GBI/FBI fingerprint check, training log.
7. They check the Operation Log, drill records, and emergency preparedness plan.
8. They close out with a Visit Report, posted as a PDF to the provider's public page.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio violations** — Rule 591-1-1-.32.
2. **Missing or expired background check** — DECAL CRC or GBI/FBI fingerprint past the 5-year re-check window.
3. **Annual training hours not met** — 10 clock hours per year (20 for directors).
4. **Missing Form 3231 (immunization certificate) or Form 3300 (ear/eye/dental)** in a child file.
5. **Emergency drills not documented quarterly** per the evacuation plan.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment form** with parent/guardian, pickup authorization, and emergency contacts.
- **Form 3231** — Georgia Certificate of Immunization (per Ga. DPH schedule).
- **Form 3300** — Ear, Eye, and Dental Certificate.
- **Child's physical examination / medical statement.**
- **Allergy, chronic-condition, and medication plan.**
- **Behavioral / developmental assessment records.**
- **Daily attendance record.**
- **Field trip and transportation permission slips.**
- **Incident / accident report file.**
- **Medication administration log** with written parent authorization.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **DECAL Comprehensive Records Check (CRC)** — combines child abuse registry, sex offender, and criminal.
- **GBI / FBI fingerprint-based background check** (through IdentoGO).
- **5-year re-check** of CRC and fingerprints.
- **Staff application with references and orientation record.**
- **40 hours of orientation training within first 90 days** for new center staff in covered roles.
- **TB screening** at hire.
- **Infant / child / adult CPR + First Aid** — all staff with direct child contact.
- **10 clock hours of annual ongoing training** (20 for directors).
- **Annual safe-sleep training.**
- **Shaken baby syndrome / abusive head trauma training.**
- **Annual mandated reporter training.**
- **Georgia Professional Development System (GaPDS)** profile.

## What must be on your walls
- **Current license** — posted.
- **Capacity** — posted.
- **Emergency plan** — posted.
- **Menu** — posted.
- **Immunization Certificate (Form 3231) master posting** — required.
- **Background Check Certificate** — posted.
- **Sanitary Practices and Illness Exclusion Policy** — posted in each room.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Quarterly emergency / evacuation drills** — documented.
- **Daily attendance record.**
- **Operation Log** — retained 1 year.
- **Incident / accident reports** same-day.
- **Medication administration log** per dose.
- **Pesticide application notice** — 24 hours before any application.
- **Annual fire marshal inspection** and **annual health department inspection** if serving food.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Is the license posted? Is Form 3231 posted? Is the background check certificate posted?
2. Count each room. Ratio good?
3. Are staff within arm's length of under-36-month-olds at meal time?
4. Pull three staff files. CRC + GBI/FBI within 5 years? 10-hour annual training on track?
5. Pull three child files. Form 3231, Form 3300, physical — all in?
6. Is this quarter's emergency drill logged?
7. Is the Operation Log current?
8. Lock the medication cabinet. Lock cleaning chemicals.
9. Outlet covers in infant and toddler rooms?
10. Is any scheduled pesticide application on the 24-hour parent notice?

## What happens if you fail
DECAL can issue **Fines or Restricted Licenses (Enforcement Actions type=3)**, **Revocations or Refusals to Renew (type=2)**, or **Denials (type=1)**. Each adverse action is published on the DECAL Enforcement Actions page with the Initial and Final Adverse Action dates. Inspection history and a **Compliance at a Glance** zone (Excellent / Support / Deficient) is visible on every provider's public page. Source: DECAL Enforcement Actions — https://www.decal.ga.gov/EnforcementActions/Default.aspx.

## The weird Georgia gotcha nobody tells you about
DECAL publishes a parent-facing **"Compliance at a Glance"** zone for every provider — Excellent, Support, or Deficient — based on a rolling 12 months of violations. A streak of minor findings can push you into "Support" or "Deficient" and parents will see that on your public page. On top of that, Georgia has a distinctive **arm's-length-at-meal-time rule** for children under 36 months that trips up operators from other states. And Georgia is one of the few states where **residential child-care** (24-hour group homes and shelters) is regulated by a different agency entirely — DHS, not DECAL. If you run both a center and a group home, you have two regulators.
