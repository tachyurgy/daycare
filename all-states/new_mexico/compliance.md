# New Mexico — Child Care Compliance Overview

## Licensing Authority

**New Mexico Early Childhood Education and Care Department (ECECD) — Regulatory Oversight Unit (Child Care Services Bureau)**
- Address: PO Drawer 5619, Santa Fe, NM 87502-5619
- Phone: 1-800-832-1321
- Email: child.care@state.nm.us
- Web: https://www.nmececd.org/child-care-licensing-and-registered-homes/
- Online portal (as of Feb 14, 2026): New Mexico Child Care Licensing and Registration Portal (in partnership with Wonderschool)

**Historical note:** Child care licensing was transferred from CYFD (Children, Youth, and Families Department) to ECECD when ECECD was established as a cabinet-level agency.

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Regulations:** 8.16.2 NMAC — Child Care Licensing (Title 8 Social Services, Chapter 16, Part 2).
- Authority derives from statutes granted to ECECD by the New Mexico Legislature (formerly administered by CYFD under Children's Code provisions).
- **Universal Child Care regulations** finalized October 2025; took effect with the opening of the Universal Child Care provider portal.

## Facility Types Regulated

- **Child care center** (licensed, serving > 5 unrelated children)
- **Licensed child care home** (larger home-based programs)
- **Registered family child care home** (smaller home-based; registered rather than licensed)
- **Out-of-school-time (OST) program** (school-age program at a school or other site)
- Special consideration for homes on tribal lands.

## Key Licensing Forms

- **Notarized Licensing/Registration Application** (initial and renewal)
- **DoD certification letter** (for on-base / military-affiliated programs)
- **Background Check Request** (FBI fingerprint + child abuse/neglect registry + sex offender registry)
- **Fire inspection report** from AHJ
- **Department of Health facility inspection** report
- **Food Service Inspection Report**
- **Annual signed staff statement** (per §8.16.2 NMAC; each staff person certifies no conviction requiring disclosure; guardian signs for teen staff)

## Staff/Child Ratios & Group Sizes (8.16.2.23 NMAC)

### Centers where children are grouped by age

| Age Group | Adult : Child Ratio | Max Group Size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (6 wks – 12 mo) | 1 : 6 | 12* |
| Toddlers (12 – 24 mo) | 1 : 6 | 12* |
| 2 years | 1 : 10 | 20 |
| 3 years | 1 : 12 | 24 |
| 4 years | 1 : 12 | 24 |
| 5 years | 1 : 15 | 30 |
| 6 years and older | 1 : 15 | 30 |

### Centers where age groups are combined

| Age Group | Adult : Child Ratio | Max Group Size |
|---|---|---|
| 6 weeks – 24 months | 1 : 6 | 12* |
| 2 – 4 years | 1 : 12 | 24 |
| 3 – 5 years | 1 : 14 | 28 |
| 6 years and older | 1 : 15 | 30 |
| 18–24 mo combined with 24–35 mo | 1 : 6 | 12* |

*Providers licensed before the current rule's effective date are grandfathered on group size but must maintain ratios at all times; new providers and capacity changes after the effective date must meet the stated maximum group size.

Ratios/group sizes must be maintained **based on the youngest child present**. A minimum of two staff members must be on-site at all times (one must be an educator); if fewer than 7 children, the second staff may perform support duties.

## Required Child Records

- Enrollment application (name, DOB, parent contacts, emergency contact)
- Immunization record (complies with NMDOH schedule) or approved exemption
- Health history & special needs documentation
- Authorization for emergency medical treatment
- Medication authorization and administration log
- Allergy / special diet / special needs plan
- Signed acknowledgment of parent handbook
- Daily attendance record
- Records must be maintained on-site and available for licensing inspection

## Required Staff Records (8.16.2 NMAC Subsection B)

- Application, employment history, references
- Background check clearance (FBI NGI + state registry + NM child abuse/neglect + NM sex offender)
- Health certificate (TB screening, basic fitness-for-duty)
- Signed acknowledgment of orientation
- **Director qualifications** documentation (early childhood credential, administrative coursework)
- **24 hours of annual training** for each staff > 20 hrs/week (including the director), covering 7 competency areas over 2 years
- 45-hour entry-level ECE course or approved 3-credit course within 6 months of hire
- CPR & First Aid awareness (with pediatric component)
- SIDS / shaken baby / safe sleep training
- Infectious disease prevention & medication administration training
- **Annual signed statement** certifying criminal history and continued eligibility
- Training log with dates, clock hours, competency area, source

## Inspection Cadence

- **Pre-licensure / on-site survey** required before initial license or renewal.
- **Annual fire inspection** required from AHJ (center must request and document if AHJ does not provide).
- **Playground equipment weekly inspections** must be documented (8.16.2.29 NMAC).
- **Most recent inspection reports** (fire, sanitation, state/local) must be posted and available on-site.
- Facility and registered home provider surveys for the last 3 years are published online by ECECD.
- Unannounced inspections/complaint investigations permitted at any time.

## Renewal Cadence

- Standard annual license; CYFD/ECECD-approved nationally accredited centers may receive multi-year terms.
- Renewal application must be **submitted prior to current license expiration**; applications postmarked < 30 days prior incur a **$25 late fee**.
- After on-site survey, a new license is issued effective the day following expiration of the current license.
- **Automatic expiration** at midnight on the expiration date if renewal is not processed, unless suspension/revocation governs.
- Conditions of operation, sanctions (per 7.1.8 NMAC), or monetary penalties may attach to initial/renewal decisions.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Universal Child Care:** New Mexico is the first state offering universal child care; providers accepting UCC funding have additional contractual obligations phased in 2025–2026.
- **Posted ratios:** Centers must post classroom capacities, ratios, and group sizes in an area of the facility.
- **Primary educator rule:** A child may have no more than three primary, consecutive educators in any day; same educator supervises children < 2 years as much as possible.
- **Director on-site presence:** Director must be on-site for at least 50% of the center's daily core hours of operation.
- **Substitute list:** Program must keep a list of at least two qualified substitutes (names, phone, background check, orientation records).
- **Emergency operations plan** must cover continuity of operations; special needs, infants/toddlers, children with chronic conditions; approved annually by licensing authority.
- **Tribal consideration:** Special consideration may be made for homes located on tribal lands (8.16.2 NMAC).
