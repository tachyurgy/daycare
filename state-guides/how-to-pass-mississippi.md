# How to Pass Your Mississippi Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector is an **agency representative** from the **Mississippi State Department of Health (MSDH), Child Care Facilities Licensure Branch**. Unlike most states, Mississippi's child-care licensor sits inside the Department of Health — not a human services agency. Mississippi law requires **at least two inspections per year**, and MSDH reserves the right to make **unannounced inspections at any time**. Every inspection result — including **Class I violations and monetary penalties** — is published on the public **MSDH Child Care Facility Search**.

- Agency: https://msdh.ms.gov/page/30,0,183.html
- Public inspection search: https://www.msdh.provider.webapps.ms.gov/
- Regulatory violations page: https://msdh.ms.gov/page/30,0,183,707.html

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **This is the law:** Miss. Code Ann. § 43-20-1 et seq. (Mississippi Child Care Licensing Law); rulemaking authority at § 43-20-8
- **This is the rulebook for centers (13+ kids):** 15 Miss. Admin. Code Part 11, Subpart 55, **Chapter 1**. https://msdh.ms.gov/msdhsite/_static/resources/78.pdf
- **This is the rulebook for home programs (12 or fewer):** 15 Miss. Admin. Code Part 11, Subpart 55, **Chapter 2**
- **This is the inspection form:** MSDH Child Care Facility Inspection Report — **Form #281**
- **This is the fire-inspection form:** MSDH Form #333 (signed by local fire authority)
- **This is the food-service inspection form:** MSDH Form #301 / #302

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

From Rule 1.8.2 / 1.8.3:

| Age | One staff for this many kids | Max group | Min sq ft per child |
|---|---|---|---|
| Infants (0–12 mo) | 1:5 | 10 | 40 |
| Toddlers (12–24 mo) | 1:9 | 18 | 45 |
| 2 years | 1:12 | 24 | 35 |
| 3 years | 1:14 | 28 | 35 |
| 4 years | 1:16 | 32 | 35 |
| 5–9 years | 1:20 | 40 | 35 |
| 10–12 years | 1:25 | 50 | 35 |

**Mixed-age groups:** the **youngest child's ratio governs**. Preschool and school-age children may NOT be combined during normal programming.

**Naptime (≥2 yr):** facility may operate at 50% of required staff in the same room **provided the overall staff:child ratio is maintained on-premises**. Children under 2 must always have full ratio in-room.

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up. Mississippi also enforces square-footage rules per age band — not just ratios.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced (or for scheduled renewal). Shows MSDH ID.
2. Checks posted license, MSDH Form #333 fire approval, Form #301/302 food approval, and posted ratios.
3. Walks each room, counts kids vs. staff, measures square-foot compliance.
4. Pulls staff files: **FBI/MCIC fingerprint** criminal history check + child abuse central registry + sex offender registry (processed through MSDH Records Check Unit), **Letter of Suitability for Employment**, current TB screening, CPR + First Aid, orientation.
5. Pulls child files: enrollment, Form 121 immunization certificate (or exemption), health exam, allergy & medication authorizations.
6. Walks through the 35-item **MSDH Form #281** checklist — every item marked compliant or cited.
7. Writes findings on Form #281, leaves a copy with you at the end of the visit.
8. Assesses **monetary penalties** for Class I violations — **$500 first occurrence, $1,000 for subsequent occurrences** (Miss. Code Ann. § 43-20-8).

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Mississippi
1. **Ratio not met** (Rule 1.8.2 / 1.8.3) — ratios apply at all times including arrival/departure, field trips, and water activities.
2. **Letter of Suitability for Employment missing** — every staff must hold this before working with children.
3. **TB screening missing or expired** — current TB must be on file for every staff.
4. **Fire-inspection (Form #333) expired** — often skipped during renewal.
5. **Food manager certification missing** (ServSafe, TummySafe, or National Registry) when the facility prepares food.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment form (name, DOB, parent info, emergency contacts, authorized pickups)
- **Immunization certificate** (Form 121 equivalent) or medical/religious exemption
- Health examination / medical history
- Allergies, special diet, medication authorization
- Authorized pick-up list
- Parent handbook acknowledgment
- Daily attendance with arrival/departure times, signed by parent or staff
- Records retained for the period of enrollment

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application & employment history
- **FBI/MCIC fingerprint** + child abuse central registry + sex-offender registry
- **MSDH Letter of Suitability for Employment**
- Current TB screening
- Orientation documentation (within 1 month of hire)
- **Adult, Child, & Infant CPR + First Aid** (required for staff on premises at all times)
- **ServSafe / TummySafe / National Registry** food manager certificate (if applicable)
- Annual training log (continuing education hours)
- Date of employment & separation

## What must be on your walls
- Current MSDH license
- MSDH Form #333 fire-inspection approval
- Ratio chart (by age band)
- Evacuation routes each room
- Menu (current week)
- Sun-safety programming notice (Rule 1.9.1)
- Parent complaint line: 1-866-489-8734

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — per inspection schedule, documented
- **Severe weather / tornado drills** — documented
- **Daily attendance** signed by parent or staff
- **Medication log**
- **Incident / injury log**
- **Food service temp logs** (if on-site food prep)

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff in every room against Rule 1.8.2.
2. Confirm license, Form #333, ratios, evacuation routes posted.
3. Fire drill log — this month entered?
4. Open three staff files: Letter of Suitability, TB screening date, fingerprint clearance, CPR.
5. Open three child files: Form 121 immunization, health exam, allergy plan, pickup list.
6. Kitchen: milk — **fat-free/1% for ≥2 years, whole milk for <2 years**. No raw vegetables for <2 year olds (choking hazard).
7. Sun-safety plan visible.
8. Cribs: backs only, firm mattress, one infant per crib (SIDS rule).
9. Lock chemicals and medications.
10. Greet the inspector. Offer the file drawer key. Be friendly.

## What happens if you fail
MSDH records each finding on **Form #281** with class-based severity. **Class I violations carry monetary penalties — $500 first occurrence, $1,000 for subsequent occurrences** (Miss. Code Ann. § 43-20-8). Repeat or serious violations escalate to suspension or revocation. License lapses: you can reinstate within one month by paying the renewal + reinstatement fees; after that, you must file a new initial application. Late renewal incurs a $25 late fee. Appeals go through MSDH administrative hearings. Every inspection result publishes to the MSDH Child Care Facility Search.

## The weird Mississippi gotcha nobody tells you about
Mississippi has a **food-specific rule that nobody else has this exact way**: **children age 2 and older must be served fat-free or 1% milk — whole milk is only for children under 2**. Raw vegetables cannot be served to children under 2 (choking hazard). Plus there's a **sun-safety programming requirement** (Rule 1.9.1) — you must build sun-safety education into your activities for all age groups. These are **Form #281** checklist items. If your menu shows whole milk for your 3-year-olds, that's a citation. If your lesson plans don't mention sun safety for your preschool room, that's a citation. Most owners don't realize milk-type is a licensing rule in Mississippi.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Mississippi owner
Their fear is a **Class I violation with a $500 or $1,000 fine** showing up publicly on the MSDH Facility Search, plus the Letter of Suitability revoked for a staff member. Drop the **fat-free/1% milk for ≥2 years rule** in 30 seconds — nobody sells them software that knows this specific menu rule, and it's a real Form #281 line item. Most MS owners are juggling the Letter of Suitability paperwork + TB screening renewals + Form #333 fire inspection expiration on sticky notes. Say "I'll show every staff Letter of Suitability and TB date on one screen, and flag any menu entry that violates the age-based milk rule." Jackson, Gulfport, Southaven, and Tupelo are the targets; many MS centers participate in CACFP so menu compliance and licensing compliance overlap — one sale supports both.
