# How to Pass Your Connecticut Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Connecticut Office of Early Childhood (OEC), Division of Licensing**. OEC is the one state agency that licenses all Connecticut child day care — centers, group homes, and family homes. State law requires **unannounced inspections at least every 2 years**; in practice OEC visits most centers annually. They also come out any time a complaint is filed. Every inspection report, consent order, and substantiated complaint is posted on **eLicense CT** (the public license-lookup portal) and on the parent-facing **211 Child Care** site.

- Agency: https://www.ctoec.org/
- Public inspection portal (eLicense): https://www.elicense.ct.gov/Lookup/LicenseLookup.aspx
- Parent-facing portal: https://www.211childcare.org/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **This is the law:** Connecticut General Statutes Chapter 368a, §§ 19a-77 through 19a-87e. https://www.cga.ct.gov/current/pub/chap_368a.htm
- **This is the center/group-home rulebook:** Regulations of Connecticut State Agencies (RCSA) §§ 19a-79-1a through 19a-79-13
- **This is the family-home rulebook:** RCSA §§ 19a-87b-1 through 19a-87b-23
- **This is the key statute — CGS § 19a-79** (2024 text): https://law.justia.com/codes/connecticut/title-19a/chapter-368a/section-19a-79-formerly-sec-19-43d/
- **This is the mandated reporter law:** CGS § 17a-101

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

**Centers** (RCSA 19a-79-4a, -8):

| Age | One staff for this many kids | Max group |
|---|---|---|
| Under 3 (infant/toddler) | 1:4 | 8 |
| 3-year-olds | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4+ (preschool) | 1:10 | 20 |
| School-age (5–12) | 1:10 | 20 |

**Group Day Care Home (7–12 children):** 1 adult + 1 aide; max 2 children under 24 months.
**Family Child Care Home:** up to 6 children, including the provider's own under age 6.

Source: RCSA § 19a-79-4a. Staffing ratios explained further in CGA OLR 2022-R-0212. https://www.cga.ct.gov/2022/rpt/pdf/2022-R-0212.pdf

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Group size is capped at **8 for under-2** and **10 for age 2** indoors and outdoors. Do not round up.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced and shows OEC ID.
2. Checks your posted license and the posted ratios.
3. Walks each classroom. Counts kids vs. staff.
4. Pulls staff files: background check (DCF CPS + state criminal + FBI fingerprint + sex-offender + DMV), **Early Childhood Professional Registry** ID, CPR, pediatric first aid, director qualifications, annual training log.
5. Pulls child files: enrollment, DPH-compliant immunization record, **HAR-3 health assessment**, emergency authorizations, medication authorization.
6. Reviews fire drill log, medication log, injury log, and evacuation/security plan.
7. Writes up any citation on OEC letterhead, citing the specific RCSA section (e.g., `RCSA 19a-79-4a(e)` for staffing ratios).
8. Posts the full inspection report as a PDF on eLicense, typically within a week.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Connecticut
1. **Ratio not met** (RCSA 19a-79-4a). Most common citation statewide.
2. **Background check expired** — CT requires re-check **every 2 years** (shorter than most states). If the 2-year anniversary passed, staff is technically out of compliance.
3. **Early Childhood Professional Registry ID missing** — every staff in a licensed program must be registered.
4. **HAR-3 health assessment missing or not refreshed** — must be within 60 days of enrollment and updated per DPH schedule.
5. **Annual flu vaccine missing** for ages 6 months – 5 years (CT requires flu each Sept 1 – Jun 30) unless exempted.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment / emergency-contact form (contents spelled out in RCSA 19a-79-7a)
- Connecticut DPH-compliant **immunization record** (or medical/religious exemption under CGS 10-204a)
- **HAR-3 health assessment** within 60 days of enrollment, updated per DPH schedule
- Emergency medical authorization, pickup list, field trip authorization
- Allergy and special-needs plan; medication administration authorization
- Daily attendance (sign-in / sign-out)
- Incident / injury log
- Annual flu vaccine record (ages 6 months – 5 years)

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application / résumé / references
- **OEC-approved background check** — state DCF CPS, state criminal, FBI fingerprint, DMV, sex-offender — **re-done every 2 years**
- **Early Childhood Professional Registry** entry
- Health assessment with TB risk assessment
- Orientation within first 30 days
- **Annual continuing education** — ≥15 hours for staff, ≥30 hours for directors
- CPR + Pediatric First Aid (at least one site-wide; infant rooms must have trained staff)
- Medication administration training (where applicable)
- Mandated reporter training (CGS 17a-101)
- Director qualifications per 19a-79-4a(f) — Bachelor's + 12 ECE credits or equivalency

## What must be on your walls
- Current license
- Staff-to-child ratio posting (by room)
- Evacuation route (each room)
- Allergy emergency plan (if severe-allergy child enrolled)
- Mandated reporter poster
- OEC complaint phone: 1-800-282-6063

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly, documented
- **Lockdown / shelter-in-place** — per evacuation plan
- **Daily attendance**
- **Medication log**
- **Injury / incident log**
- **Radon test** — every 2 years for basement or grade-level rooms
- **Water lead test** — for water served to children

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff in every room against the ratio chart.
2. Confirm license, ratio chart, mandated reporter poster, and evacuation routes are up.
3. Pull this month's fire drill log.
4. Open three staff files: background-check date (≤2 yrs), Professional Registry ID, CPR, training log.
5. Open three child files: immunization, HAR-3 health assessment, emergency authorization.
6. Lock chemicals, medications, sharps.
7. Cribs: flat back, firm mattress, no blankets, one child per crib.
8. Playground: no broken equipment, gates latched.
9. Food area: cleaned, allergy plan visible.
10. Greet inspector warmly. Have the file cabinet unlocked and ready.

## What happens if you fail
OEC documents each violation in the PDF inspection report posted to eLicense. Follow-up inspections verify corrective action. Serious or repeat non-compliance triggers **consent orders, summary suspensions, or revocation** — these appear as "Discipline / enforcement actions" on your eLicense record and stay there permanently. The statewide **annual death/injury/substantiated-abuse report** is published each year on 211 Child Care. Appeals go through OEC's hearing process; ultimate review sits with the Freedom of Information Commission if records disputes arise.

## The weird Connecticut gotcha nobody tells you about
**Background checks must be re-run every 2 years** — Connecticut is one of the strictest states in the country on re-check cadence. Most states say every 5 years, or on a triggering event. CT says every 2 years, no exceptions. This means your compliance tool must not only track the initial background check date, but also the **2-year rolling deadline for every single staff member**. Miss it by one day and you have a non-compliance. Also: CT requires a separate **Professional Registry** ID for every staff member — it's two things, the background check AND the Registry, and inspectors check both.
