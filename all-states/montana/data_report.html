6. Violations & Inspection Data

Montana is the clearest bulk-data win among assigned states. The SansWrite X portal exposes 3 years of inspection history + deficiencies per facility via a JSON backend already used for the leads pull. Per-facility endpoints follow the SansWrite X pattern (list-completed-inspections, get-inspection-details) and can be reverse-engineered from a single devtools session.

7. Outreach Signals

  • HB 422 (2023) loosened ratios — 1-year-olds 1:4 → 1:6; 6+ 1:14 → 1:20. Cross-check a provider's pre-vs-post-2023 license history for historical compliance confusion.
  • Annual renewal with fire marshal inspection required at each renewal — predictable annual trigger.
  • Practitioner Registry gatekeeping — staff qualifications verified annually; expired registry = staff can't count toward ratio = service disruption. Identical pain to Hawaii's PATCH.
  • Drop-in care special rules (ARM 37.95.1105+) — common in ski/tourism zones (Big Sky, Whitefish, Bozeman). Distinct ICP.
  • Massive geography + low density — DPHHS inspection travel drag; complaint response occasionally slow in rural counties. Self-serve readiness software is disproportionately valuable.
  • Tribal jurisdiction overlap — seven reservations; some programs state-licensed, others tribal. Segment carefully.
