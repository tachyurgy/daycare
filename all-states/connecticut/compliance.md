# Connecticut — Child Care Licensing Compliance Reference

_Compiled 2026-04-18 for ComplianceKit. All citations are to publicly-available primary sources — verify before relying in production._

## Licensing Authority

- **Connecticut Office of Early Childhood (CT-OEC) — Division of Licensing**
- 450 Columbus Blvd, Suite 302, Hartford, CT 06103
- Phone: 1-800-282-6063 / (860) 500-4450
- Statewide portal: https://www.ctoec.org / https://portal.ct.gov/oec
- Provider verification: https://www.elicense.ct.gov (eLicense lookup)

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **Connecticut General Statutes (CGS) Chapter 368a**, §§ 19a-77 through 19a-87e — Child Day Care
- **Regulations of Connecticut State Agencies (RCSA) §§ 19a-79-1a through 19a-79-13** — Child Day Care Centers & Group Child Care Homes
- **RCSA §§ 19a-87b-1 through 19a-87b-23** — Family Child Care Homes
- Additional: **CGS § 10-16p et seq.** — School Readiness / OEC programs funding compliance

## Facility Types Regulated

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Child Care Center | 13+ children, non-residential, regular schedule |
| Group Child Care Home | 7–12 children, in residence or other setting |
| Family Child Care Home | Up to 6 children (incl. provider's own <6) in the provider's residence |
| Youth Camp | Seasonal (regulated by OEC) |

Roughly **4,000+ licensed programs** statewide (OEC figure).

## Key Licensing Forms

- **Initial License Application (Center / Group Home)** — OEC Center-Group-Initial-Application
- **Family Child Care Home Initial Application** — FCCH-IN
- **Background Check Authorization** (fingerprint + state/federal + DCF abuse registry)
- **Director Qualifications Form** (minimum bachelor's + ECE credits, or equivalency)
- **Health Assessment for Child Day Care** (DPH / OEC HAR-3)
- **Immunization Certificate** — state DPH form tied to Connecticut's required schedule
- **Early Childhood Professional Registry** ID (required for all staff)
- **Emergency Evacuation & Security Plan**
- **Child's Enrollment Record** — required contents spelled out in 19a-79-7a

Apply **≥60 days before opening**; submit to OEC Licensing with fee.

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (RCSA 19a-79-4a, -8)

**Centers:**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|-----|-------|-----------|
| Infant / Toddler (under 3) | 1:4 | 8 |
| 3-year-olds | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4 & older (preschool) | 1:10 | 20 |
| School-age (5–12) | 1:10 | 20 |

CT regulations require "at least one program staff qualified under 19a-79-4a(e) for every four children or fraction thereof" under age 2, and 1:5 for age 2. Group sizes capped at **8 for under-2** and **10 for age 2** indoors/outdoors.

**Group Day Care Home:** 7–12 children max; 1 adult + 1 aide; max 2 children under 24 months.

**Family Child Care Home:** Up to 6 children, including the provider's own children under age 6.

## Required Child Records (RCSA 19a-79-7a)

- Enrollment / emergency contact form
- Connecticut DPH-compliant **immunization record** (or medical/religious exemption per CGS 10-204a)
- Physical health assessment (HAR-3) within 60 days of enrollment, updated per DPH schedule
- Authorization for emergency medical care, pickup list, field trips
- Allergy & special-needs plan; medication administration authorization
- Daily attendance record (sign-in/out)
- Incident / injury log
- Child's individual file retained per OEC retention schedule

## Required Staff Records

- Application / resume / references
- **CT OEC-approved background check:** state CPS (DCF), state criminal, FBI fingerprint, DMV, sex-offender registry — redone every 2 years
- **Early Childhood Professional Registry** entry (mandatory for staff in licensed programs)
- Health assessment including TB risk assessment
- Orientation within first 30 days
- **Annual continuing education:** ≥15 hours (director 30 hrs)
- CPR + Pediatric First Aid (at least 1 person per site trained; infant rooms must have trained staff)
- Medication administration training where applicable
- Mandated reporter training (required by CGS 17a-101)
- Director qualifications per 19a-79-4a(f): Bachelor's + 12 ECE credits, or equivalent

## Inspection Cadence

- **Unannounced inspections at minimum every 2 years**; in practice OEC targets more frequent review (often annual) for centers
- Additional for complaints / licensing actions / school readiness audits
- History publicly searchable via **eLicense CT** (inspection history, violations, corrective action plans)

## Renewal Cadence

- Connecticut child care center licenses are issued for **up to 4 years** (typical 2-year renewal for many programs); renewal application due prior to expiration
- Family Child Care Home licenses renew on similar cycle
- Continuous monitoring tied to background check renewals (2 yrs) and registry updates

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Early Childhood Professional Registry** — every staff member must be registered; used for QRIS & subsidy eligibility.
- **CT DPH Immunization Schedule** — enforced through OEC inspections; includes annual flu (Sept 1–Jun 30) for ages 6 mo – 5 yr.
- **Lead Paint / Environmental** — pre-1978 facilities must have RRP-compliant renovation; water testing for lead if served to children.
- **Radon Testing** — required every 2 years for facilities in basements or grade-level rooms.
- **NAEYC or Head Start accreditation** carries waiver weight for certain re-inspection items.
- **Schooling & Smart Start / School Readiness** programs: additional OEC funding-program monitoring.
- **Bus / transportation** — State Board of Education regs apply; carriers require CT DOT/DMV permit.
- **Quality Rating: Connecticut Quality Recognition System (in development)** — voluntary.
- **No corporal punishment** (statutory ban, CGS 19a-79-6).
