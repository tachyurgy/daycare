# How to Pass Your Nevada Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **State of Nevada, Division of Supportive Services (DSS), Child Care Licensing Program (CCL)**. Nevada consolidated child-care licensing into DSS on **July 1, 2024** — before that it was split between DPBH and DWSS. Nevada law requires **at least 2 unannounced inspections per 12-month licensing period** — roughly every 6 months — plus complaint inspections on top of that (NRS 432A.180 / NAC 432A.190). Every inspection and every "Regulatory Action" shows up publicly on the **Aithent ALiS Licensee Search** and the parent-facing **findchildcare.nv.gov**.

- Agency: https://www.dss.nv.gov/programs/child-care-licensing-ccl/
- Public provider search: https://www.dpbh.nv.gov/regulatory/child-care-facilities/media/find-child-care/
- License lookup (Aithent): https://nvdpbh.aithent.com/Protected/LIC/LicenseeSearch.aspx?Program=HF&PubliSearch=Y&returnURL=~/Login.aspx?TI=2

**Local note:** Clark County and Washoe County have **local licensing partners** for family child care homes — if you run an in-home program in Clark or Washoe, you may have both state and county oversight.

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **This is the law:** NRS Chapter 432A (Services and Facilities for Care of Children). https://www.leg.state.nv.us/nrs/nrs-432a.html
- **This is the rulebook:** NAC Chapter 432A. https://www.leg.state.nv.us/nac/NAC-432A.html
- **This is the ratios rule:** NAC 432A.5205. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/nevada/NAC-432A-5205
- **This is the inspection law:** NRS 432A.180
- **This is the annual training law:** NRS 432A.1775 (24 hours/year for every caregiver)

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

**Daytime (6:30 AM – 9:00 PM)** from NAC 432A.5205:

| Age | One staff for this many kids | Max group |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (0–12 mo) | 1:4 | 12 (up to 3 caregivers) |
| Toddler (1–2 yr) | 1:6 | 12 |
| 2-year-olds | 1:8 | 16 |
| 3-year-olds | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4-year-olds | 1:13 | 26 |
| 5-year-olds | 1:13 | 26 |
| School-age | 1:15 | 30 |

**Night (9:00 PM – 6:30 AM):** different reduced ratios, **and all night staff must remain awake** — not "dozing" or "resting." This is strictly enforced.

**Mixed-age groups:** the ratio and group size follow the **youngest** child present.

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced and shows a CCL badge.
2. Checks your posted license, ratios, and evacuation/emergency plan.
3. Walks every room and counts kids vs. staff.
4. Pulls staff files: **Nevada DPS Records & ID Services** criminal check + FBI fingerprint + Central Registry (child abuse) + sex-offender registry (done BEFORE unsupervised contact), **Nevada Registry** enrollment, 24-hour annual training documentation, CPR + Pediatric First Aid, SIDS/safe-sleep training for infant staff.
5. Pulls child files: enrollment, Nevada Division of Public Health immunization record, health statement, allergy plan.
6. Reviews fire drills (monthly), earthquake/shelter-in-place and evacuation drills (annual), medication log, injury log.
7. Writes any citation on the inspection report referencing the specific NAC 432A sub-rule.
8. Exit interview: corrective action plan + due date. Any civil money penalty shows as a dollar amount on the report.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Nevada
1. **Ratio not met** (NAC 432A.5205) — especially when a staff leaves the room.
2. **Background check gap** — every person must clear DPS + FBI + Central Registry + sex-offender **before** unsupervised child contact. Working even one day before the clearance lands is an automatic non-compliance.
3. **Annual training short** — NRS 432A.1775 requires **24 hours/year** for every caregiver (except sick-child facility staff). Most states only require 10–15. Nevada is a big outlier.
4. **Night staff asleep** in night-care facilities (NAC 432A.5205).
5. **Nevada Registry not enrolled** — licensed-staff enrollment in the Nevada Registry is mandatory.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment & emergency contact form
- **Nevada Division of Public Health immunization** certificate (or valid exemption)
- Physical / health statement
- Authorizations: emergency medical, pickup, field trip, photography, diaper cream
- Allergy / special-needs plan
- Medication authorization
- Daily attendance (sign-in / sign-out)
- Incident / injury log
- Food allergy / diet plan

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application / references
- **Nevada DPS + FBI fingerprint + Central Registry (CANS) + sex-offender registry** — cleared BEFORE unsupervised contact
- Health statement including TB screening per Nevada DPBH guidance
- **Nevada Registry** enrollment (career ladder)
- Pre-service orientation
- **24 hours of annual training** (NRS 432A.1775)
- CPR + Pediatric First Aid (current)
- Infant safe sleep / SIDS training for infant rooms
- Medication administration training
- Mandated reporter acknowledgment (NRS 432B.220)

## What must be on your walls
- Current DSS license
- Ratio chart
- Evacuation route (each room)
- Emergency / disaster plan summary
- Complaint hotline (DSS CCL)
- Posted menu
- Heat / air-quality advisory protocol (Nevada-specific for desert heat and Western wildfire smoke)

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly
- **Earthquake / shelter-in-place** — annual
- **Evacuation** — annual
- **Daily attendance**
- **Medication log**
- **Injury / incident log**
- **Outdoor-play guidance** on smoke and heat days

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff. Check night-ratio plan if you run night care.
2. Confirm license, ratios, and emergency plan posted.
3. Fire drill log — this month entered?
4. Open three staff files: DPS/FBI clearance date, Nevada Registry ID, 24-hour training log, CPR.
5. Open three child files: immunization, health statement, allergy plan.
6. If you run night care, confirm the night staff stay awake policy is written and acknowledged.
7. Pool, fountain, or open-water trip plans — confirm consent on file and life-safety equipment.
8. Vehicle / transport seats sized correctly; drivers have MVR check.
9. Sleep areas: backs only, firm mattress, one infant per crib.
10. Greet the inspector. Offer the staff file drawer key.

## What happens if you fail
Every inspection records **citations to specific NAC 432A sub-rules** on your Aithent licensee record. Corrective action is required by the deadline. Repeat or serious violations trigger **civil money penalties (CMP)**, **probation**, **suspension**, or **revocation**. Your license status changes to "Active / Expired / Suspended / Revoked / Voluntarily Surrendered" publicly. Nevada publishes a **Statewide Annual Licensing Report** (deaths, serious injuries, substantiated abuse counts). Appeals go through DSS hearings under the Nevada Administrative Procedure Act.

## The weird Nevada gotcha nobody tells you about
**Every caregiver — not just director, not just lead teacher — must complete 24 hours of training per year** under NRS 432A.1775. Most states require 10 to 15 hours. Nevada requires 24. That's roughly 2 hours per month. Owners who came from California or Arizona get hit on this constantly because they're used to lower hour counts. The other one: **if you run a night-care program, all night staff must remain awake.** No couches to "rest." No dozing. Inspectors specifically check this on overnight visits. Build "awake-staff rotation" into your schedule and document it.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Nevada owner
Their fear is a **Regulatory Action posted on Aithent** that any parent browsing `findchildcare.nv.gov` will see — plus a civil money penalty as a visible dollar amount. Drop the **24-hour annual training rule (NRS 432A.1775)** in 30 seconds; most Vegas-based owners underestimate it because neighboring states require less. Most are using the Nevada Registry in one tab + a Google Sheet + a paper fire drill log. Say "I'll show every caregiver's 24-hour training progress and every background-check clearance date in one screen, with the Clark County overlay if you need it." Las Vegas, Henderson, Reno, and Carson City are the targets; focus on centers that opened 2023–2026 (post-consolidation) — they're still figuring out which portal their inspection reports land in.
