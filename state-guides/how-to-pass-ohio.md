# How to Pass Your Ohio Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Ohio Department of Children and Youth (DCY)**, Bureau of Child Care Licensing and Monitoring. DCY took over from the Ohio Department of Job and Family Services (ODJFS) in January 2025. All licensing inspections are **unannounced**, per Ohio's 2011 policy and OAC 5180:2-12-03. Complaint visits are always unannounced.

- Agency: https://childrenandyouth.ohio.gov/
- Public inspection search: https://childcaresearch.ohio.gov/
- Provider portal (OCLQS): https://oclqs.my.site.com/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — Ohio Revised Code Chapter 5104 (Child Day-Care Licensing). [https://codes.ohio.gov/ohio-revised-code/chapter-5104](https://codes.ohio.gov/ohio-revised-code/chapter-5104)
- **Statutory ratios** — ORC § 5104.033. [https://codes.ohio.gov/ohio-revised-code/section-5104.033](https://codes.ohio.gov/ohio-revised-code/section-5104.033)
- **The binding rulebook for centers** — Ohio Administrative Code Chapter 5180:2-12 (renumbered from 5101:2-12 effective January 2, 2025). [https://codes.ohio.gov/ohio-administrative-code/rule-5180:2-12-18](https://codes.ohio.gov/ohio-administrative-code/rule-5180:2-12-18)
- **Compliance inspection rule** — OAC 5180:2-12-03.
- **Family Child Care (Type A / Type B)** — OAC Chapter 5180:2-13.
- **Step Up To Quality (QRIS)** — OAC Chapter 5180:2-14.
- **DCY plain-English guide: "What to expect during a licensing inspection"** — https://dam.assets.ohio.gov/image/upload/jfs.ohio.gov/ofam/What%20to%20expect%20during%20a%20center%20licensing%20inspection%20UPDATED%20082023.pdf

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (< 12 months) | 1:5 (or 2 staff : 12 in same room) | 12 |
| 12 – 18 months | 1:6 | 12 |
| 18 – 30 months | 1:7 | 14 |
| 30 – 36 months | 1:8 | 16 |
| 3-year-olds | 1:12 | 24 |
| 4 – 5 years | 1:14 | 28 |
| Kindergarten – 10 years | 1:18 | 36 |
| 11 – 14 years | 1:20 | 40 |

Inspectors count heads on the spot. Appendix A (the state-issued ratio chart) must be posted in the center. At least **2 employees** must be on site any time 7 or more children are in the building.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive unannounced and sign in.
2. They check that the license is posted.
3. They walk every classroom and count children against staff.
4. They pull staff files and check BCI, FBI, APS, and Central Registry clearances.
5. They review each staff's 20-hours-every-2-years training log and the OPR (Ohio Professional Registry) record.
6. They pull child files — enrollment, medical statement, immunizations.
7. They check safe-sleep and meal-time practices in the infant room.
8. They close out with findings, rated as SRNC, MRNC, or Low risk. The report posts to childcaresearch.ohio.gov within 24 hours.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio violations** under ORC § 5104.033 / OAC 5180:2-12-18 — commonly Serious Risk Non-Compliance (SRNC).
2. **Background-check gaps** — BCI or FBI past the 5-year re-check under OAC 5180:2-12-09.
3. **20-hour biennial training not complete** for a staff member.
4. **Safe-sleep violations** in the infant room (blankets, side-sleeping, non-crib sleep surface).
5. **Missing daily attendance or sign-in/sign-out** records.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment / admission form** with parent info and pickup list.
- **JFS 01297 / medical statement** signed by a physician within 30 days of enrollment.
- **Immunization record** per Ohio Department of Health schedule.
- **Emergency transportation plan** signed by parent.
- **Allergy and special-needs plan.**
- **JFS 01305 incident / injury reports.**
- **Medication administration records** with written parent and physician authorization.
- **Daily attendance record.**
- **Permission for water activities, field trips, and photography.**

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **BCI fingerprint check** (Ohio Bureau of Criminal Investigation) — at hire.
- **FBI fingerprint check** — at hire (required for anyone not an Ohio resident the prior 5 years).
- **5-year re-check** of BCI / FBI.
- **Sex offender registry check.**
- **APS / Central Registry check.**
- **Child medical statement + TB screening.**
- **Staff application, references, and orientation documentation.**
- **Annual communicable disease training** + **annual child abuse recognition training**.
- **Pediatric CPR and First Aid** — at least one staff on site.
- **20 clock hours of approved training every 2 years** (plus first-year add-on topics).
- **Ohio Professional Registry (OPR)** profile — required for every staff member.
- **Annual safe sleep training.**

## What must be on your walls
- **Current license** — posted in a public area.
- **Appendix A** — the state-issued ratio and group-size chart — posted.
- **Most recent inspection** — posted near the license.
- **Emergency exit plan** in every room.
- **Menu and daily schedule** visible to parents.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** — per OAC requirements, documented.
- **Daily attendance and sign-in/sign-out.**
- **Incident/injury reports (JFS 01305)** same-day.
- **Medication log** per dose.
- **Annual fire marshal inspection** and **annual food-service inspection** (local health department) if you prepare food.
- **SRNC parent notice** — if DCY cites a Serious Risk Non-Compliance, you must send a **written notice to parents within 15 business days** and keep a copy on file for 1 year.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Is the license posted and Appendix A posted?
2. Count each room. Ratio good? At least 2 staff on site if 7+ kids?
3. Is today's attendance current?
4. Pull three staff files. BCI and FBI within 5 years? OPR current?
5. Pull three child files. Medical statement within 30 days of enrollment? Immunizations?
6. Infant room: every baby on their back, in a crib, no blankets or soft items?
7. Lock the cleaning-chemical and medication cabinets.
8. Is the 20-hour biennial training log up to date for every staff?
9. Outlet covers in infant and toddler rooms?
10. Has every SRNC in the last year been noticed to parents in writing?

## What happens if you fail
DCY classifies findings as **SRNC (Serious Risk Non-Compliance, 6 points)**, **MRNC (Moderate Risk, 3 points)**, or **Low risk (1 point)**. A program's total point balance feeds the public compliance score at childcaresearch.ohio.gov. An SRNC requires written parent notice within 15 business days. Serious or repeat findings can lead to revocation. Source: OAC 5180:2-12-03 and DCY Licensing Compliance Overview at https://childrenandyouth.ohio.gov/for-providers/licensing-compliance.

## The weird Ohio gotcha nobody tells you about
Ohio assigns every non-compliance a **point value** — 6 for SRNC, 3 for MRNC, 1 for Low risk — and the total is visible publicly as the program's "compliance score." Parents see it. A single SRNC requires you to send a **written notice to every enrolled family within 15 business days** — most operators forget this and get a second citation for failing to notify. On top of that, the licensing agency itself changed — **ODJFS transferred licensing to the new Ohio Department of Children and Youth in January 2025** — so the rule numbers moved from OAC 5101:2-12 to **5180:2-12**. Anything you Google more than a year old may show the old numbers.
