# How to Pass Your Virginia Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Virginia Department of Education (VDOE)**, in the **Division of Early Childhood Care and Education, Office of Child Care Licensing (OCCL)**. Licensing moved from VDSS to VDOE on July 1, 2021 under HB 2299. OCCL does both announced and unannounced inspections. The informal target is **at least one unannounced inspection per licensed center per year** — plus complaint visits any time and a renewal inspection at license expiration. Every inspection gets posted publicly to the legacy `dss.virginia.gov` lookup tool, which has kept every report since July 1, 2003.

- Agency: https://www.doe.virginia.gov/teaching-learning-assessment/early-childhood-care-education
- Public inspection portal (legacy cc2.cgi): https://dss.virginia.gov/facility/search/cc2.cgi
- Consumer-facing finder: https://www.childcare.virginia.gov/find-care

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The law** — Code of Virginia § 22.1-289.010 et seq.
- **Center standards** — 8VAC20-780. https://law.lis.virginia.gov/admincode/title8/agency20/chapter780/
- **Family day home standards** — 8VAC20-790. https://law.lis.virginia.gov/admincode/title8/agency20/chapter790/
- **General licensure procedures and enforcement** — 8VAC20-820. https://law.lis.virginia.gov/admincodefull/title8/agency20/chapter820/
- **Background checks (new combined rule, effective February 1, 2026)** — 8VAC20-821. https://register.dls.virginia.gov/details.aspx?id=9691
- **Ratio rule** — 8VAC20-780-350. https://law.lis.virginia.gov/admincode/title8/agency20/chapter780/section350/

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age band | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Birth up to 16 months (infant) | 1:4 | 12 |
| 16 months up to 24 months (toddler) | 1:5 | 15 |
| 2 years old | 1:8 | 24 |
| 3 years – school-age eligible | 1:10 | 30 |
| School-age up to 9 years | 1:18 | — |
| 9 through 12 years | 1:20 | — |

Mixed-age groups use the ratio and group size for the **youngest** child. Group-size limits do **not** apply during outdoor play, meals, transportation, or the first/last hour of operation. Family Day Homes (8VAC20-790) allow up to 12 children (including provider's own children under school age), with no more than 4 under 2 years, and 1:4 under 16 months. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive (often unannounced) and sign in.
2. They verify your license is posted with the expiration date.
3. They walk every classroom and count children against staff.
4. They observe meals, transitions, and infant safe-sleep setup.
5. They pull child files looking for **Form 032-05-0075 (Physical Examination Report)** and immunization records.
6. They pull staff files looking for background checks (8VAC20-821 starting 2026-02-01), qualifications, and the 16-hour PD log.
7. They check drill logs — monthly fire drills, plus 2 each of evac, lockdown, and shelter-in-place per year.
8. They close out with a **cc2.cgi inspection report** listing each citation to 8VAC20-780 rule, classified by Virginia's **Risk Matrix** (Low / Medium / High / Key Core).

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio and supervision violations** — cited as 8VAC20-780-350.
2. **Background check gaps** — especially under the new 8VAC20-821 (effective Feb 1, 2026) retroactive recheck requirement.
3. **Missing 16 annual hours of professional development** — cited as 8VAC20-780-270.
4. **Missing physical examination report (Form 032-05-0075) or immunization record** in a child file.
5. **Medication without MAT-trained staff** — VDOE-approved Medication Administration Training is required for any staff giving prescription meds (8VAC20-780 MAT provisions).

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment form** with emergency contacts (8VAC20-780-60).
- **Form 032-05-0075 — Child's Physical Examination Report**, within 30 days of enrollment (or prior physical up to 12 months old).
- **Immunization record** per § 22.1-271.2 (Virginia School Entry Schedule), harmonized with the Virginia Department of Health schedule.
- **Allergy and chronic-condition care plan** where applicable.
- **Authorized-pickup list.**
- **Consent to emergency medical treatment.**
- **Daily attendance record.**
- **Written care plan** for any child on medication, therapy, or special diet.
- **Medication administration log.**

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **Background checks under 8VAC20-821** (effective February 1, 2026): fingerprint-based state and FBI criminal history, Virginia CPS Central Registry search, sex offender registry, out-of-state CPS checks if the person lived out of state in the prior 5 years. **Refreshed every 5 years — and as of Feb 1, 2026, a retroactive recheck is required for any staff whose last check is older than 5 years.**
- **Sworn Disclosure Statement (Form 032-05-0029)** — self-disclosure of convictions/findings, prior to hire.
- **Central Registry Release of Information (Form 032-05-0030).**
- **Program director qualifications** per 8VAC20-780-240; lead program staff per 8VAC20-780-245.
- **16 hours per year of professional development** — 8VAC20-780-270. At least half must be on CCDBG topics (health, safety, child development).
- **Orientation training within 90 days of hire** — 11 required topics.
- **Pediatric CPR and First Aid** for any staff counted in ratio.
- **VDOE-approved Medication Administration Training (MAT)** — 6–8-hour course for any staff giving prescription meds.
- **Mandated reporter training** on hire and annually.
- **TB screening** per Dept. of Health guidance.

## What must be on your walls
- Your **current VDOE license** with expiration date.
- **Most recent inspection summary** and any compliance plan.
- **Emergency preparedness plan** and evacuation routes.
- **Daily schedule** by classroom.
- **Menu** (current week).
- **Staff qualifications** visible to parents.
- **USDA non-discrimination poster** (if CACFP).
- **Information to Parents / licensing information.**

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly (8VAC20-780 emergency preparedness rule).
- **Evacuation, lockdown, and shelter-in-place drills** — **2 of each per year**.
- **Annual practice of the full emergency plan.**
- **Daily attendance.**
- **Daily health observation.**
- **Medication administration log** (requires MAT-trained staff).
- **Serious injury reporting (Form 032-05-0024)** within 2 business days; **death/loss** within 24 hours.
- **Annual self-inspection checklist.**

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Stand in each classroom and count. Use the youngest child's ratio.
2. License on the wall? Expiration visible?
3. Infant room: every baby on back, crib-safe, no inclined sleepers?
4. Pull 3 random child files. 032-05-0075 physical signed by a doctor? Immunizations current?
5. Pull 3 random staff files. Background check under 8VAC20-821 current (or retroactively refreshed per the Feb 1, 2026 rule)? MAT card visible if they give meds?
6. Open the emergency folder. Monthly fire drill logged? 2 evac/lockdown/shelter drills logged year-to-date?
7. Walk the playground for CPSC-compliance issues.
8. Confirm daily health observation was documented for every child today.
9. Confirm Form 032-05-0024 was filed for any serious injury in the last 2 business days.
10. If on subsidy, confirm your **Child Care PASS** (Provider Attendance Submission System) is current — PASS launched Dec 1, 2025.

## What happens if you fail
OCCL can issue corrective actions, move you to a **provisional license** (up to 6 months), a **conditional license** (up to 1 year), **deny renewal**, **suspend**, or **revoke**. Each violation is cited to an 8VAC20-780 standard and classified on the Risk Matrix as Low, Medium, High, or **Key / Core** (highest severity). Enforcement orders are announced on the VDOE "What's New" page and posted to the facility's cc2.cgi record. The risk rating is public — parents see it. You have appeal rights under the Administrative Process Act. Public records search: https://dss.virginia.gov/facility/search/cc2.cgi

## The weird Virginia gotcha nobody tells you about
**8VAC20-821 became effective February 1, 2026** and it is the single biggest compliance change in Virginia child care in years. The new rule consolidates all background-check requirements — and it is **retroactive**. Any staff member whose most recent fingerprint-based state/FBI check is older than 5 years must be re-fingerprinted and re-checked now. This includes your most tenured teachers, long-time directors, and anyone whose initial check was done under the old 8VAC20-770 framework. Owners who passed every inspection under the old rule can fail under the new one simply because they never refreshed a 2018 background check. Second quirk: Virginia's **Risk Matrix** publishes each violation as Low, Medium, High, or **Key/Core** — and parents can see the classification. A single Key/Core citation is a marketing disaster even if the license stays active.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Virginia owner
VA owners' #1 pain **right now** (2026) is the **8VAC20-821 retroactive background check rule** that went live February 1, 2026. Every longtime staff member whose last check is older than 5 years has to be re-fingerprinted. Lead with this line: "Under 8VAC20-821, every teacher with a background check older than 5 years needs to be re-done — retroactively. If you haven't audited that list, your next inspection finds it first." Also mention the **Risk Matrix**: any citation rated Key/Core shows up publicly on cc2.cgi and kills enrollment. Today VA owners track all this in binders or OneDrive folders, and the cc2.cgi public search is brutal — every report since 2003 stays online forever. They're sitting ducks for 8VAC20-821.
