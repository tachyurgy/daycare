# How to Pass Your Indiana Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Indiana Family and Social Services Administration (FSSA), Office of Early Childhood and Out-of-School Learning (OECOSL), Bureau of Child Care**. Indiana runs **at least one unannounced monitoring inspection per year** (the federal CCDBG minimum). Complaints trigger visits anytime. If a finding lands in a "critical" category, a **red Critical Health and Safety Violation flag** goes on your public provider page until you correct it. Roughly 28,000 monitoring inspections and 3,000 complaint investigations statewide each year. Public sites:
- FSSA Child Care Finder: https://www.in.gov/fssa/carefinder/
- Public provider search: https://secure.in.gov/apps/fssa/providersearch/home/category/ch

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main statute** — Indiana Code 12-17.2.
- **The rulebook for centers** — 470 IAC 3-4.7. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/indiana/title-470/article-3/rule-470-IAC-3-4.7
- **The rulebook for licensed homes** — 470 IAC 3-1.1.
- **Registered Ministry rule** — 470 IAC 3-4.5 (faith-based, registered not licensed).
- **Inspection interpretive guide** (the one inspectors actually use) — FSSA Centers Interpretive Guide. https://www.in.gov/fssa/carefinder/files/Centers_Interpretive_Guide.pdf
- **Critical Violation categories (explainer)** — https://www.in.gov/fssa/carefinder/critical-health-and-safety-violations-qa/

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From 470 IAC 3-4.7-47 (centers). Mixed-age groups default to the ratio of the youngest child, and the max group size is the lowest allowed for that age. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age band | One staff for every... | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (under 16 months) | 4 | 8 |
| Toddler (16 – 23 months) | 5 | 10 |
| 2 years | 5 | 10 |
| 3 years | 10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 12 | 24 |
| 5 years (Kindergarten) | 15 | 30 |
| School-age (6 – 14 years) | 15 | 30 |

**Class I Home:** up to 12 children with 1 adult (more adults for infants/toddlers). **Class II Home:** 13–16 children.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced.
2. Checks license is posted and current.
3. Walks every room, counts children, compares to staff present right now.
4. Pulls staff files — background check, caregiver qualifications (Form 44129), annual training hours.
5. Pulls child files — immunizations, health record (Form 53466), medication consents (Form 53455).
6. Reviews emergency plan, drill logs (tornado season matters here), and incident reports.
7. Exit conference. The report lands on your provider search page in near-real-time after the consultant finalizes it. Any finding in a critical category flips a **red Critical Health and Safety Violation flag** on the public page until corrected.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
Per FSSA's published critical-violation categories and 470 IAC 3-4.7 enforcement patterns:
1. **Out-of-ratio / out of supervision** — most common "critical" flag.
2. **Background check gaps** — fingerprint-based FBI + state, CPS index (Child Protection Index), and national Sex Offender Registry (NSOR) not all on file, or not refreshed at 5 years.
3. **Caregiver qualifications not documented** (Form 44129) — HS diploma/GED for adults counted in ratio; lead caregivers need ECE credential under 470 IAC 3-4.7-24.
4. **Pre-service health & safety training incomplete** — the 10 federal CCDBG topics must be done before counting in ratio.
5. **Tornado drill logs missing** during March–June season.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment form** with emergency contacts and authorized pickups.
- **Form 53466** — Child's Health Record, including a health/physical statement within 1 year of enrollment.
- **Immunization record** per the Indiana State Department of Health schedule.
- **Individual care plan** for children with special medical needs.
- **Form 53455** — Medication Administration Consent.
- **Allergy / dietary restriction documentation**.
- **Signed parent handbook / policies acknowledgment**.
- **Transportation consent** (if applicable).

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **Form 49467** — Background Check Authorization. Fingerprint-based FBI + state check (federal CCDBG requirement; renew every 5 years).
- **Indiana CPS check** (Child Protection Index).
- **National Sex Offender Registry (NSOR)** check.
- **Form 44129** — Caregiver Qualifications Verification.
- **Orientation** within 30 days of hire.
- **Pre-service health & safety training** (the 10 federal CCDBG topics) — completed **before** being counted in ratio.
- **First Aid + CPR** — current. Infant/toddler staff need infant/child CPR.
- **Annual training** — minimum **12 hours per year** (more at higher Paths to QUALITY levels).
- **TB screening** per local health authority.
- **Universal precautions / bloodborne pathogens** training.

## What must be on your walls
- Current FSSA license.
- License capacity + ages served.
- Emergency plan and evacuation route per room.
- Menus.
- Parent complaint contact info.
- Safe Sleep policy in infant rooms.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill — monthly**, logged.
- **Tornado drill — monthly during tornado season (March–June)**, logged.
- **Lockdown / shelter-in-place drill** per emergency plan.
- **Daily attendance** (sign-in/sign-out).
- **Medication log** — each dose, each day.
- **Incident / injury reports** (Form 55090).

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff on the floor. Fix any ratio gap **now**.
2. Pull 3 random staff files — Form 49467 background check, Form 44129 qualifications, 12-hour training log.
3. Pull 3 random child files — immunization record, Form 53466 health record.
4. Infant rooms — back sleep, no loose bedding, safe-sleep policy posted.
5. Fire drill log — current month initialed?
6. Tornado drill log — this month initialed (March–June)?
7. Medication cabinet — locked, in-date, matching Form 53455.
8. License posted and current.
9. Playground — fence integrity, gate, no hazards.
10. Review last inspection — any red Critical flag still open?

## What happens if you fail
FSSA issues findings per rule cite. You file a plan of correction. Noncompliance categorized as "critical" flips the **red Critical Health and Safety Violation flag** on your public provider page and stays until corrected. Serious or repeat noncompliance escalates to probation, suspension, or revocation under IC 12-17.2. Fraud-related referrals go to the FSSA OIG. Appeals go through the state administrative process.

## The weird Indiana gotcha nobody tells you about
**Your center's street address is public — but a licensed home's address is not.** Indiana Code 12-17.2-2-1(9) prohibits publishing the physical address of a licensed child care **home** in any public directory. So if you run a home, the FSSA directory shows only your city/county, not your street. This matters two ways: it limits how parents find you (you rely on word of mouth and CCR&R referrals), and it means FSSA's public inspection page generalizes your location. Centers get no such cover. The other gotcha: Indiana has a **third category** — Registered Child Care Ministries — which are faith-based, statutorily **exempt from licensing** but still required to register and meet minimum health and safety rules under IC 12-17.2-6. If you are a ministry that accepts CCDF, you now owe a bunch of additional standards beyond basic registration.

## For Magnus — how to sell to an Indiana owner
Biggest fear: a red **Critical Health and Safety Violation** flag on their public FSSA page — it's visible to every parent searching the Child Care Finder and doesn't come off until FSSA verifies the correction. Drop this in 30 seconds: "FSSA does ~28,000 monitoring inspections a year and any critical finding turns on the red flag on your Child Care Finder page until you correct it. Are you tracking the 5-year background-check refresh cycle and the 12-hour annual training per staff?" Today they are using paper files, the Paths to QUALITY tracker, and maybe a generic CCMS — none of which model the critical-violation category or the Form 44129 qualifications verification. The close: "We'll keep the red flag off your provider page."
