1. Licensing Authority

Rhode Island Department of Human Services (DHS), Office of Child Care — day care providers. Rhode Island Department of Children, Youth & Families (DCYF) — foster, child-placing, residential, and enforcement referrals for unlicensed day-care operators (post-2019 reorganization).

  • DHS Office of Child Care: (855) 697-4347
  • DHS Child Care regulations page: dhs.ri.gov/regulations
  • DCYF licensing: dcyf.ri.gov/services/licensing

6. Violations & Inspection Data

RI Auditor General (Aug 2025): Audited 50 licensed facilities. Found at least one health/safety violation at every facility. 60% in "significant noncompliance" (≥10% of H&S requirements unmet). 26 of 50 facilities had background-check errors. 232 facility findings + 920 documentation findings. 59 cases of Level 2/3 sex offenders living within 300 feet. DHS has pledged to visit every provider in the state. This is an active-catalyst market.

7. Outreach Signals

  • 2x unannounced monitoring visits per year — among the most aggressive inspection cadences in the country. Inspection-readiness tooling has obvious ROI.
  • "No exceptions" infant ratio — no combining, no averaging. Hard-coded workflow; easy to fail during lunch/transitions.
  • 45 sq ft per child for infants/toddlers — explicit physical-plant check; renovations have to pass re-inspection.
  • BrightStars ↔ CCAP — higher star rating = higher subsidy rate. Tight economic incentive to over-comply.
  • 2025 AG audit catalyst — DHS has committed to visiting every provider. Every facility's compliance exposure is about to surface publicly. Classic urgency play.
  • Dual-authority confusion (DHS vs. DCYF) — providers often misunderstand reporting channels. Educational content is a lead-magnet opportunity.
