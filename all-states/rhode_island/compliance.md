# Rhode Island — Child Care Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority

**Rhode Island Department of Human Services (DHS), Office of Child Care** (for child care centers, school-age programs, and family/group family child care homes).

**Rhode Island Department of Children, Youth & Families (DCYF)** licenses foster homes, child-placing agencies, and residential child care (not day care).

- DHS Office of Child Care: (855) 697-4347
- DHS Child Care regulations page: https://dhs.ri.gov/regulations
- DCYF licensing: https://dcyf.ri.gov/services/licensing

Note: Historical confusion arises because DCYF handled day care licensing prior to Rhode Island's 2019 reorganization; as of 2026 the **DHS Office of Child Care** is the primary day-care licensing authority, while **DCYF retains enforcement referral** for unlicensed operators and oversight of residential/placement facilities.

## Primary Regulatory Framework

- **218-RICR-70-00-1** — Child Care Center and School Age Program Regulations for Licensure (DHS)
- **218-RICR-70-00-2** — Family Child Care Home Regulations for Licensure
- **218-RICR-70-00-7** — Group Family Child Care Home Regulations for Licensure
- **218-RICR-20-00-4** — Child Care Assistance Program Rules and Regulations (subsidy program)
- **214-RICR-40-00-4** — Residential Child Care Regulations (DCYF)
- Enabling statutes: **R.I. Gen. Laws §42-72.1 et seq.** (DCYF child-care licensing) and **§40-13.2** (DHS child care licensing, post-2019).

## Facility Types Regulated (Day Care)

| Facility Type | RICR Part | Capacity |
|---|---|---|
| Child Care Center | 218-RICR-70-00-1 | 13+ children |
| School-Age Program | 218-RICR-70-00-1 | Ages 5–13, before/after school |
| Family Child Care Home | 218-RICR-70-00-2 | 1 provider, up to 6 children |
| Group Family Child Care Home | 218-RICR-70-00-7 | Provider + assistant, up to 12 children |

## Key Licensing Forms

- DHS **Center / Program License Application**
- DHS **Family Child Care Home / Group Family Application**
- **BrightStars QRIS participation agreement** (voluntary but linked to CCAP subsidy tiers)
- **Comprehensive Background Check** release forms — state BCI, FBI fingerprint, child abuse & neglect clearance, sex offender registry (every 5 years for providers, household members 18+, assistants)
- **RI Department of Health Immunization Record** for each child (compliant with R23-1-IMM)
- **Fire Marshal Certificate of Inspection** (state or local)
- **Department of Health Food Service Permit** (if meals prepared)

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes

**Child Care Centers (218-RICR-70-00-1):**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Younger Infants (6 weeks–12 months) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Older Infants (12–18 months) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Toddlers (18–36 months) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Preschool 3 (3 years) | 1:9 | 18 |
| Preschool 4 (4 years) | 1:10 | 20 |
| Preschool 5–6 (not in kindergarten) | 1:12 | 24 |
| School-Age (in K through grade 7, under 16) | 1:13 | 26 |

Group sizes by age of youngest child. **No exceptions to infant ratio** under any circumstance. At nap time for toddler/preschool groups, a single staff per group may supervise.

**Family Child Care Homes** (218-RICR-70-00-2): up to 6 children total; no more than 3 under age 2; no more than 2 under 18 months.

## Required Child Records

- Application form (child's name, DOB, parent/guardian contact, authorized pickup)
- Health exam evidence and current immunization record
- Emergency medical treatment authorization
- Documentation of injuries, accidents, illnesses
- Progress and development information
- Field trip authorizations
- Parental photo/video consent
- **For infants/toddlers (additionally):** developmental and health history, feeding habits, sleep patterns, communication methods, daily reports
- Daily attendance and sign-in/sign-out

**Retention:** minimum 3 years.

## Required Staff Records

- Personal data and job description
- **Comprehensive background checks** (state, federal, child abuse & neglect, sex offender, plus every 5-year refresh)
- Employment verification and references
- Health records per state immunization regulations
- Performance evaluations
- Professional development certification and training logs
- Orientation documentation
- Current CPR and pediatric First Aid
- Evidence of education qualifications per role

**Retention:** minimum 3 years after employee leaves.

## Inspection Cadence

Per 218-RICR-70-00-1: **"Child care centers will have unannounced monitoring visits at least two (2) times per year."** Complaint and follow-up inspections occur as warranted. Fire marshal inspection at initial licensure and periodically thereafter per local jurisdiction.

## Renewal Cadence

Licenses are issued for **two (2) years.** Renewal applications must be submitted **at least 30 days prior to the expiration** of the current license.

Initial licensure flow: programs receive a time-limited **Provisional License (6 months)**. Once a monitoring visit confirms compliance, the license converts to Regular status for the remaining 6 months of the first license year; subsequent cycles are 2 years.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements

- **Two unannounced monitoring visits per year** — stricter than the "one per license cycle" standard in most neighboring states (e.g., NH, MA).
- **No exceptions to infant ratios** — no combining, no averaging; rule is absolute.
- **45 sq ft of usable floor space per child** in infant/toddler classrooms is an explicit physical-plant requirement (218-RICR-70-00-1.8).
- **BrightStars QRIS** is tightly integrated with CCAP subsidy rates — higher BrightStars = higher reimbursement. Many providers are therefore strongly incentivized to comply beyond licensing minimums.
- **Enforcement**: Operating a Family Child Care Home without a license is referred by DCYF's Child Care Licensing Unit to the Attorney General's Office for prosecution.
- **RISES portal** (earlylearningprograms.dhs.ri.gov) publishes licensing status and BrightStars rating, so parents see any deficiency publicly.
- **Dual-authority quirk**: DHS regulates day care; DCYF handles residential and enforcement referrals. Keep the distinction clear in product copy.
