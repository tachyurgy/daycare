# Indiana Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
- **Agency:** Indiana Family and Social Services Administration (FSSA)
- **Division:** Office of Early Childhood and Out-of-School Learning (OECOSL) / Bureau of Child Care
- **URL:** https://www.in.gov/fssa/carefinder/

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **470 IAC 3-4.7** — Child Care Centers; Licensing
- **470 IAC 3-1.1** — Licensed Child Care Homes
- **470 IAC 3-4.5** — Unlicensed Registered Child Care Ministries
- **Enabling statute:** Indiana Code 12-17.2

## Facility Types Regulated
- **Licensed Child Care Center (LCC)** — non-residential facility caring for children not related to the caregiver
- **Licensed Child Care Home (LCCH)**
  - **Class I Home:** up to 12 children
  - **Class II Home:** 13–16 children
- **Registered Child Care Ministry (RCCM)** — faith-based, exempt from licensing but must register; separate minimum standards under IC 12-17.2-6
- **License-Exempt Providers** participating in CCDF subsidies

## Key Licensing Forms
- **Form 50076 / Initial Center Licensing Application** (through I-LEAD / ProviderConnect)
- **Form 49467 — Child Care Provider Background Check Authorization**
- **Form 44129 — Caregiver Qualifications Verification**
- **Form 55090 — Incident/Injury Report**
- **Form 53466 — Child's Health Record**
- **Form 53455 — Medication Administration Consent**
- **On My Way Pre-K** and **Paths to QUALITY™ (PTQ)** participation forms
- **Fire marshal inspection (IDHS)** and **local health department sanitation inspection**

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (470 IAC 3-4.7-47 — centers)
| Age band | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (under 16 mo) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Toddler (16–23 mo) | 1:5 | 10 |
| 2 years | 1:5 | 10 |
| 3 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 1:12 | 24 |
| 5 years (K) | 1:15 | 30 |
| School-age (6–14 yrs) | 1:15 | 30 |

Mixed-age: ratio defaults to youngest child's band; group size = lowest allowed group size for the youngest age represented.

**Class I Home:** max 12 children with 1 adult (additional adults needed for infants/toddlers).

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment form with emergency contacts & authorized pick-up list
- Immunization records per Indiana State Department of Health schedule
- Health/physical statement (within 1 year of enrollment)
- Individual care plan for children with special medical needs
- Medication authorization forms
- Allergy/dietary restriction documentation
- Signed parent handbook / policies acknowledgment
- Transportation consent (if applicable)

## Required Staff Records
- **National criminal history background check** (fingerprint-based FBI + state) — per federal CCDBG requirements; 5-year renewal
- **Indiana State Child Protection Index (CPS) check**
- **National Sex Offender Registry (NSOR) check**
- **Caregiver qualifications documentation** — HS diploma/GED minimum for adults counted in ratio; lead caregivers need ECE credential per 470 IAC 3-4.7-24
- **Orientation** (within 30 days of employment)
- **First Aid + CPR** current certification; infant/toddler staff require infant/child CPR
- **Annual training hours** — minimum 12 hours annually per CCDBG-aligned standard (more for higher PTQ levels)
- **Pre-service health & safety training** covering 10 federal CCDBG topics
- **TB screening** per local requirement
- **Universal precautions / bloodborne pathogens** training

## Inspection Cadence
- **At least one unannounced monitoring inspection annually** (federal CCDBG requirement)
- **Initial license inspection** on application
- **Complaint-triggered** investigations
- **Fire and sanitation** inspections coordinated with IDHS and local health

## Renewal Cadence
- **Licenses valid for 2 years** for centers and homes; renewal inspection required before expiration
- Provisional license for new programs typically 1 year

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Paths to QUALITY™ (PTQ)** — Indiana's 4-level QRIS; Level 1 = licensed/legally unlicensed registered, Level 4 = national accreditation. Required to participate in CCDF subsidy above base reimbursement.
- **On My Way Pre-K** — state-funded pre-K eligibility for providers at PTQ Level 3 or 4 (or accredited)
- **Registered Child Care Ministries** are church-operated and statutorily exempt from licensing but must register and comply with minimum health/safety rules (IC 12-17.2-6)
- **Indiana Code 12-17.2-2-1(9)** — prohibits publication of physical addresses of licensed child care **homes** in any public directory
- **Safe Sleep Campaign** — Indiana-specific training required for all infant room staff
- **Shaken Baby Syndrome** prevention training required
- **Child Care Development Fund (CCDF)** providers must meet additional health & safety standards beyond basic licensing
- **Emergency preparedness plan** including tornado drills (monthly during tornado season Mar–Jun)
