# Washington — Child Care Licensing Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority
- **Agency:** Washington State Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF), Licensing Division. (Prior to July 2018, licensing lived in DEL — Dept. of Early Learning. DCYF absorbed DEL functions.)
- **URL:** https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/early-learning-providers
- **Public "Child Care Check":** https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/services/earlylearning-childcare/child-care-check
- **Find Child Care:** https://www.findchildcarewa.org/

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Administrative rules:** Washington Administrative Code **Chapter 110-300** — "Foundational Quality Standards for Early Learning Programs" (applies to both licensed child care centers and licensed family child care homes). Chapter 110-301 covers school-age programs.
- **Governing statutes:** RCW 43.216 (DCYF), RCW 43.216.055 (Background checks), RCW 43.216.325 (Health and safety).

## Facility Types Regulated
- **Licensed Child Care Center** — non-residential, 13+ children.
- **Licensed Family Home Child Care** — provider's residence, up to 12 children depending on layout/capacity.
- **Licensed School-Age Program** (WAC 110-301) — before/after school, summer.
- **Outdoor Nature-Based Programs** — WAC 110-300 Subchapter F (WA was first state to license this model in 2019).
- **Overnight Care Programs** — special supplement (DCYF Form 15-895 planning form).
- **Exempt:** parent-participation cooperatives, seasonal camps <12 wks, Tribal providers (may opt into licensing), Head Start operating under federal performance standards alone.

## Key Licensing Forms (DCYF)
- **DCYF 15-955** — License/Certification Application (center or home).
- **DCYF 15-879** — Child Care Registration Form (enrollment).
- **DCYF 15-968** — Medication Authorization.
- **DCYF 15-970** — Individual Care Plan (children with special health needs).
- **DCYF 15-874** / **DCYF 15-875** — Center / School-Age Licensing Checklist (self-assessment).
- **DCYF 15-937** — Center/School-Age Background Check Checklist.
- **DCYF 15-949** — Family Home Background Check Checklist.
- **DCYF 15-963sp** — Facility Feasibility Checklist (site review).
- **DCYF 15-966** — Health Consultant Agreement.
- **DCYF 15-974** — Declaration of Compliance.
- **DCYF 15-895** — Overnight Care Planning Form.
- **Fire Inspection Checklist** (CTR or FH) — annual.
- **WAC 110-300 Policy Tip Sheet** — DCYF-published implementation guides.

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (WAC 110-300-0356, centers)
| Age band | Max group size | Ratio |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (birth – 11 months) | 8 *(or 9)* | 1:4 *(or 1:3 if group of 9)* |
| Toddlers (12 – 29 months) | 14 *(or 15)* | 1:7 *(or 1:5 if group of 15)* |
| Preschool (30 months – 6 years, not in K/elementary) | 20 | 1:10 |
| School-age (5 – 12 years, in K/elementary) | 30 | 1:15 |

- Staff-to-child ratio must be met at all times, including off-site trips and transportation.
- Mixed-age groups: ratio and group-size requirements of the **youngest** child apply.
- First/last 2 hours of day: mixed-age grouping permitted with youngest-child rule.
- **Family Home (WAC 110-300-0355):** capacity based on licensed square footage; max 12 children including any of provider's own children under age 12; no more than 6 non-ambulatory infants.

## Required Child Records
- DCYF 15-879 Child Care Registration form.
- Immunization records — **Certificate of Immunization Status (CIS)** meeting RCW 28A.210 child care/school schedule, or Certificate of Exemption (medical only for MMR/varicella from 2019 SB 5841; personal/philosophical exemption prohibited for MMR; religious still allowed for other immunizations).
- Written consent for emergency medical treatment, photo/media, field trips.
- Allergy & special-needs plan; DCYF 15-970 Individual Care Plan where applicable.
- Parent-handbook acknowledgment.
- Daily attendance.
- Medication authorization & administration log (DCYF 15-968).
- Incident/accident reports (provide copy to parent same day).
- Records retained per DCYF policy.

## Required Staff Records
- **Background checks (RCW 43.216.055):** MERIT (My Early achievers Registry of Information and Training) portal-based enrollment + fingerprint-based state/FBI history + state sex-offender registry + CPS/APS findings + out-of-state child abuse registry if resided elsewhere in prior 5 years. Must be re-initiated every **3 years**; conditional hiring requires completion of Portable Background Check before unsupervised contact.
- **MERIT Profile** — every licensed-program staff must have a MERIT account with credentials, training history, and STARS ID.
- **Education/credential:** Director/Program Supervisor must hold ECE State Certificate or higher (DCYF phased-in education requirements through 2030 per WAC 110-300-0100 series).
- **Orientation training** (DCYF-approved) within 90 days of hire — 20 hours covering topics required by CCDBG (health/safety, child development, regulations, abuse prevention).
- **Annual in-service training:** Minimum **10 clock hours per year** (WAC 110-300-0107) for providers with ≥12 months in the field; 30 hours in first year. Recorded in MERIT.
- **CPR/First Aid:** Pediatric CPR and first aid — all staff in ratio. At minimum 1 certified person present at all times.
- **Health consultant agreement** (DCYF 15-966) for programs serving infants, toddlers, or children with special care needs.
- **TB screening / health declaration** per local Public Health guidance.
- **Mandated reporter** training.

## Inspection Cadence
- **Licensing monitoring visits:** full monitoring inspections at least **once per year** (unannounced); DCYF uses a differential monitoring approach targeting higher-risk programs more frequently.
- **Renewal / re-licensing** inspection at end of license term.
- **Complaint investigations** any time.
- **Fire inspection** annually (State Fire Marshal, local fire authority, or — per FAQ — self-certification by provider if local authority cannot complete).
- **Health / environmental** inspection annually (local Public Health or Dept. of Health, depending on jurisdiction).

## Renewal Cadence
- **Child care centers:** initial license may be issued for 1 year; subsequent licenses are generally issued for **3 years** (standard term) for programs in continued compliance (per DCYF licensing policy).
- **Family home:** same 3-year standard term.
- **Probationary, provisional, conditional:** shorter-term instruments available when compliance history is intermittent.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Fair Start for Kids Act (2021):** expanded subsidy, capped family co-pays, added infant/toddler rate enhancements.
- **Early Achievers** — WA's QRIS, administered jointly by DCYF and the University of Washington Cultivate Learning. Participation required for providers receiving Working Connections Child Care subsidy above specified thresholds.
- **MERIT** (wa.gov) — mandatory training registry for all licensed-program staff.
- **STARS ID** — lifelong training identifier for ECE workforce.
- **Expulsion policy** — WAC 110-300-0186: suspension/expulsion of children is restricted; centers must document attempts to support before any dismissal.
- **Environmental health:** lead paint compliance for pre-1978 facilities; radon testing (below-grade classrooms); WA Dept. of Ecology vapor-intrusion review for sites on Hazardous Sites List.
- **Earthquake preparedness:** written plan with at least one drill per quarter (Pacific NW-specific).
- **Outdoor Nature-Based Program** rules (Subchapter F) are unique to WA — first-in-nation licensing model.
- **Overnight care** requires supplemental DCYF approval and written rest-period plan.
- **Safe-sleep:** WAC 110-300-0285 — AAP-aligned; specific rules on crib safety, no inclined sleepers.
- **Serious event reporting** — death, hospitalization, missing child, or critical injury: notify DCYF within 24 hours.
- **Food Program (USDA CACFP)** widely used; must meet DCYF nutrition rules even if not on CACFP.
