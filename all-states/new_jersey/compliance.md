# New Jersey — Child Care Licensing Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority
- **Agency:** New Jersey Department of Children and Families (DCF), Office of Licensing (OOL).
- **URL:** https://www.nj.gov/dcf/about/divisions/ol/licensing.html
- **Complaint/help line:** 1-877-667-9845

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Manual of Requirements for Child Care Centers:** N.J.A.C. 3A:52 (Title 3A, Chapter 52).
- **Background Check Manual of Requirements:** N.J.A.C. 3A:54.
- **Family Child Care Manual of Requirements:** N.J.A.C. 3A:51 (licensed family child care providers registered through county CCR&R agencies, pursuant to N.J.S.A. 30:5B-16 et seq. — the Family Day Care Provider Registration Act).
- **Governing statute:** Child Care Center Licensing Act, N.J.S.A. 30:5B-1 et seq.

## Facility Types Regulated
- **Child Care Center** (licensed directly by DCF OOL): serves 6+ children under 13 for <24 hrs/day.
- **Registered Family Child Care Home** (provider's home, 1–5 children): registered through county Child Care Resource & Referral (CCR&R) agency under N.J.A.C. 3A:51.
- **School-age child care programs** (N.J.A.C. 3A:52 Subchapter 8 / 8A): before/after school, summer programs.
- **Drop-in / short-term** centers — still require full licensure if serving 6+ children.
- License-exempt: parochial preschools ≥2.5 yrs operating ≤6 hrs/day (optional approval); day camps; facilities run by religious organizations meeting N.J.S.A. 30:5B-7 criteria.

## Key Licensing Forms
- **CCC-21** — Application for a License to Operate a Child Care Center.
- **CCC-2** — Application for Child Care Center License (paper fallback).
- **DCF Form CC-1** — Universal Child Health Record / Medical Examination Form.
- **DCF Form CC-2** — Immunization Record.
- **CCC-108** — Inspection/Violation Report (used by licensing inspector).
- **CCC-64** — Criminal History Record Information (CHRI) Request.
- **CP&P 1-3** (DCF child abuse) clearance form.
- **DCF-Form 701** — Statement of Intent (Fire Safety).
- **NJCCIS Registry** enrollment — separate professional development registration through New Jersey Child Care Information System.

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (N.J.A.C. 3A:52-4.3)
| Age band | Staff:child ratio | Max group size (primary caregiver) |
|---|---|---|
| Under 18 months (infants) | 1:4 | 4 per primary caregiver; 12 infants/room max |
| 18 months – <2½ years (toddlers) | 1:6 | 6; 18 toddlers/room max |
| 2½ – <4 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 1:12 | 20 |
| 5 years and older | 1:15 | 30 |

Reduced sleep/rest ratios (when conditions met): 1:10 infants, 1:12 older toddlers, 1:20 age 2½+.
Special-needs programs (≥50% children w/ special needs): tighter ratios (1:3 under 2½; 1:5 for ambulatory age 2½+).
Field trips: minimum 2 staff required.

**Family child care** (N.J.A.C. 3A:51): provider may care for up to 5 children including own under age 6, no more than 3 under age 2, no more than 2 under 12 months.

## Required Child Records
- Signed application for admission.
- **Universal Child Health Record** (DCF CC-1) signed by physician within 6 months of enrollment for children <2 yrs; annually thereafter.
- **Immunization record** (DCF CC-2) meeting N.J.A.C. 8:57-4 state schedule; medical or religious exemption documentation if claimed.
- Emergency contact & consent forms.
- Record of any medications, allergies, special diet, individual health care plan.
- Attendance record.
- Incident/injury reports.
- Records retained **minimum 1 year after discharge**.

## Required Staff Records
- **Background checks:** CARI (Child Abuse Record Information via DCF) + fingerprint-based state/FBI criminal history + NJCCIS sex-offender registry check. Refreshed every **3 years** for continuing staff (N.J.A.C. 3A:54). Prospective staff may not have unsupervised contact until CARI + fingerprint checks return clear.
- **Education/credential:** Group teachers must meet qualifications in N.J.A.C. 3A:52-4.6 (minimum 60 ECE semester credits or CDA or equivalent experience).
- **Training:** Minimum **20 hours per year** of professional development recorded in NJCCIS registry (DCF Professional Development Requirements, effective 2019). Orientation within 2 weeks covering topics required by CCDBG.
- **CPR/First Aid:** At least one staff on premises at all times with current pediatric CPR & first aid.
- **Physical / TB / health screening:** Pre-employment health appraisal per local health requirements.
- **Staff file retention:** 3 years after separation.

## Inspection Cadence
- **Annual unannounced monitoring inspections** during normal operating hours (N.J.A.C. 3A:52-2.3).
- **Renewal inspection** every 3 years prior to license expiration.
- **Complaint-based** inspections on receipt of credible allegation.
- **Fire inspection** annually by local or state fire marshal (Uniform Fire Code).
- Posted inspection reports are public at https://www.nj.gov/dcf/families/childcare/centers/ and via Child Care Explorer.

## Renewal Cadence
- Initial license: 3 years.
- **Renewal term: 3 years** for compliant centers. Provisional license (up to 6 months) issued when substantial compliance not met.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Grace's Law** — CCDBG/Background check requirements codified; all persons age 18+ in licensed family homes also subject to checks.
- **N.J.A.C. 3A:52-7.11 Environmental Health** — centers located in/on former industrial sites must demonstrate environmental compliance (lead, radon, vapor intrusion). This is monitored by NJDEP SRWMP alongside DCF — the NJDEP GIS dataset is used for contaminated-site proximity review.
- **Caring for Our Children** — incorporated by reference.
- **Required posted notices:** license, inspection reports (most recent), emergency exit plan, staff qualifications, USDA non-discrimination, Manual of Requirements summary for parents.
- **Lead & radon testing:** centers in multi-tenant buildings must document lead paint inspection; radon testing required for ground-floor/basement classrooms.
- **SIDS training:** mandatory for all infant-room staff; written safe-sleep policy.
- **Teacher/staff orientation** within 2 weeks must cover shaken baby, SIDS, food allergy, and mandated reporter content.
