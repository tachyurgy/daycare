# Ohio — Child Care Compliance Reference

## Licensing Authority
- **Agency:** Ohio Department of Children and Youth (DCY) — established 2023, assumed child-care licensing from the **Ohio Department of Job and Family Services (ODJFS)** in January 2025
- **Division:** Bureau of Child Care Licensing and Monitoring
- **URL:** https://childrenandyouth.ohio.gov/
- Public search portal: https://childcaresearch.ohio.gov/
- Provider portal: OCLQS (Ohio Child Licensing and Quality System) — https://oclqs.my.site.com/

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Ohio Revised Code (ORC) Chapter 5104** — Child Day-Care Licensing enabling statute
- **ORC § 5104.033** — Staff-to-child ratios (statutory)
- **Ohio Administrative Code (OAC) Chapter 5180:2-12** — Licensed Child Care Centers (formerly OAC 5101:2-12 under ODJFS; renumbered effective 01/02/2025)
- **OAC Chapter 5180:2-13** — Licensed Family Child Care Providers Type A and Type B
- **OAC 5180:2-12-18** — Group size and ratios for a licensed child care center
- **OAC 5180:2-12-03** — Compliance inspection and complaint investigation
- **OAC Chapter 5180:2-14** — Step Up To Quality (SUTQ) rating rules
- **OAC 3301-37** — Preschool programs operated by school districts (Ohio Department of Education — separate path)

## Facility Types Regulated
- **Licensed Child Care Center (CCC)** — 7+ children, non-residential
- **Licensed Type A Family Child Care Home** — 7–12 children in operator's residence
- **Licensed Type B Family Child Care Home** — up to 6 children in operator's residence
- **Registered Day Camp / Approved Day Camp**
- **Preschool operated by a school district** — regulated by ODE (not DCY)
- **Head Start agencies** — also subject to federal Head Start Performance Standards
- **Publicly Funded Child Care (PFCC)** providers — must accept Child Care Choice / subsidy vouchers

## Key Licensing Forms (filed through OCLQS)
- **JFS 01202** / **DCY CC-1202** — Application for a Child Care Center License
- **JFS 01258** — Staff Orientation Record
- **JFS 01257** — Director Qualifications Documentation
- **BCI & FBI criminal records check** (via Webcheck fingerprinting)
- **JFS 01335** — Child Enrollment and Health Information
- **JFS 01234** — Medical/Physical Care Plan
- **JFS 01217** — Communicable Disease Report
- **JFS 01297** — Child Medical Statement
- **JFS 01241** — Request for Child Care, subsidy program
- **JFS 01340** — Complaint Form
- **JFS 01305** — Incident/Injury Report
- **Fire Marshal Inspection Certificate**
- **Local Building Code Certificate of Occupancy**

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (ORC § 5104.033 / OAC 5180:2-12-18 App. A)
| Age band | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (< 12 months) | 1:5 (or 2:12 in same room) | 12 |
| 12–18 months | 1:6 | 12 |
| 18–30 months (young toddler) | 1:7 | 14 |
| 30–36 months (older toddler) | 1:8 | 16 |
| 3-year-olds | 1:12 | 24 |
| 4–5 years (preschool, not in K) | 1:14 | 28 |
| Kindergarten–10 yrs (school-age) | 1:18 | 36 |
| 11–14 years | 1:20 | 40 |

- Nap-time: ratios may be doubled for toddlers and preschoolers (max 2 hrs/day) with proper supervision; not applicable to infants
- At least 2 employees on premises any time 7+ children are in building
- Posted Appendix A required in the center

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment / admission form with parent info and authorized pick-ups
- Medical statement signed by physician (within 30 days of enrollment, updated per age)
- Immunization record compliant with Ohio Department of Health schedule
- Child's emergency transportation plan signed
- Allergy and special-needs plan
- Incident/accident log
- Medication administration records (written parent/physician authorization required)
- Daily attendance record
- Roster of assigned classroom/staff
- Written permission for water activities, field trips, and photography

## Required Staff Records (OAC 5180:2-12-09 et seq.)
- **BCI (Ohio Bureau of Criminal Investigation) fingerprint check** — at hire
- **FBI fingerprint check** — at hire; required for any applicant not an Ohio resident the past 5 years
- **Re-checks every 5 years**
- Sex offender registry check
- Central Registry / APS check
- **Child medical statement** / TB screening
- Staff application, references, and orientation documentation
- **Communicable disease training** (annual)
- **Child abuse recognition and reporting** training (annual)
- **Pediatric CPR & first aid** certification (at least one staff on-site)
- **Communicable disease training and management of spills of body fluids**
- **20 clock hours of approved training every 2 years** for staff and administrators (varies by role); first-year employees have additional first-year topics
- **Child Development Associate (CDA) / Associate or Bachelor's** per role requirements
- **Ohio Professional Registry (OPR)** enrollment required for all staff
- **Step Up To Quality (SUTQ)** staff credential records if participating in QRIS

## Inspection Cadence
- **Annual unannounced inspection** — at least once every 12 months (OAC 5180:2-12-03); all inspections must be unannounced per 2011 Ohio policy
- **Complaint inspection** — upon any reported complaint
- **Additional inspections** as needed for repeat/egregious violations
- **Fire-safety inspection** — annual by State Fire Marshal or local fire department
- **Food service license inspection** — annual (local health department)

## Renewal Cadence
- Child care center licenses **do not expire** as long as center remains in compliance (no fixed renewal term); however annual inspection and continuing compliance are required, and the license can be revoked or denied
- License amendments required for change in capacity, location, administrator, age range, or legal entity

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Step Up To Quality (SUTQ)** — mandatory QRIS for publicly funded (subsidy-receiving) programs; 1–5 star rating
- **Ohio Professional Registry (OPR)** — mandatory registration for all center staff
- **Publicly Funded Child Care (PFCC)** — providers accepting subsidies must meet higher SUTQ rating tier by year (1-star minimum, rising)
- **Child Care Choice Program** — state-funded voucher for families up to 200% FPL
- **Early Childhood Expansion** and **Early Head Start State Supplement** participants have layered requirements
- **Administrator's Credential** — Ohio-specific credential via OPR
- **OCLQS Portal** — all license transactions, training records, complaints, inspection reports flow through this system
- **ACA / COA / NAEYC / NAFCC / NECPA / NACCP / Cognia accreditation** — recognized in SUTQ points
- **Liability insurance requirement** — strongly recommended, required for PFCC
- Ohio requires **2 hours child abuse/recognition training annually**, in addition to 20-hour biennial
- **Safe sleep for infants** training required annually
- Sign-in/out systems required for all children
