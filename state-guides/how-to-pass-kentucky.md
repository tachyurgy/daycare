# How to Pass Your Kentucky Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Cabinet for Health and Family Services (CHFS), Office of Inspector General, Division of Regulated Child Care (DRCC)**. They are the only people who can issue, pause, or revoke your Kentucky license. Once you have a regular license, they come out **at least once a year** unannounced. Centers on probation (usually after a bad finding) get visited **every three months for two years**. Any time a parent files a complaint, a surprise visit can happen on top of that. Every inspection you receive shows up on your public record at **kynect**, the state's consumer search portal.

- Agency: https://www.chfs.ky.gov/agencies/os/oig/drcc/Pages/default.aspx
- Public inspection portal (kynect): https://kynect.ky.gov/benefits/s/child-care-provider

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

| Age | One staff for this many kids | Max children in the group |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (under 12 months) | 1:5 | 10 |
| Toddler 12–24 months | 1:6 | 12 |
| Toddler 24–36 months | 1:10 | 20 |
| 3–4 years | 1:12 | 24 |
| 4–5 years | 1:14 | 28 |
| School-age 5–7 | 1:15 | 30 |
| School-age 7+ (before/after school) | 1:25 | 30 |
| School-age 7+ (full day) | 1:20 | 30 |

Source: 922 KAR 2:120.

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Ratios apply **at all times — including naptime**. Do not round up. In a mixed-age room, you must meet the ratio for the youngest child in the room.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced and shows a state ID.
2. Checks your posted license and your posted ratios sheet.
3. Walks every classroom and counts kids vs. staff in each.
4. Pulls a sample of staff files: background check (CCBC), TB screening, CPR, pediatric first aid, orientation and mandated reporter training, and the annual performance review.
5. Pulls a sample of child files: enrollment agreement, Kentucky Certificate of Immunization, physical exam, emergency authorizations.
6. Looks at your fire drill log, transportation log, attendance log, and medication log.
7. Checks indoor square footage (35 sq ft per child) and outdoor space (60 sq ft per child).
8. Ends with an exit interview where they read out any citation ("non-compliance") and tell you the deadline to fix it.

The inspection report is the **Child Care Compliance Monitoring Report** filed into kynect within 2–4 weeks.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Kentucky
1. **Ratio not met during naptime or transitions** (922 KAR 2:120). This is the number one citation.
2. **Background check (CCBC) incomplete or expired** on a staff member (922 KAR 2:280). Every person must clear state check + FBI fingerprint + Child Abuse/Neglect registry + National Sex Offender Registry before unsupervised child contact.
3. **Immunization certificate missing** more than 30 days after enrollment (922 KAR 2:110).
4. **No TB screening** on file, or screening older than 2 years (922 KAR 2:110).
5. **Fire or emergency drill log missing dates**, or drills not being run monthly (922 KAR 2:120).

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Written enrollment agreement with parent, address, emergency contacts
- Kentucky Certificate of Immunization Status — within **30 days of enrollment**
- Physical examination record
- Signed emergency medical, transportation, field trip, and photo authorizations
- Allergy and special-needs plan
- Medication administration log (date, time, amount, staff name)
- Daily attendance record
- Incident / injury reports
- Weekly menus (keep 30 days)
- Transportation logs (keep **5 years** — unusual, KY-specific retention)

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Completed **Child-Care Background Check (CCBC)** — all 5 components cleared *before* first day
- TB screening — at hire and every 2 years after
- Pediatric CPR and First Aid (current)
- Orientation training (health & safety + mandated reporter)
- Safe Sleep / Shaken Baby Syndrome / AHT training (infant-room staff, before unsupervised contact)
- Medication administration training (DPH-approved)
- Annual written performance evaluation by director
- Annual in-service training log — **15 hours/year** for center staff (6 hours for certified family homes)

## What must be on your walls
- Current state license
- Staff-to-child ratio chart (one is provided free by Child Care Aware KY: https://www.childcareawareky.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Section-12-Forms-and-Postings-4-Licensed-Child-Care-Staff-to-Child-Ratio.pdf )
- Emergency evacuation routes (each room)
- Fire drill log (current)
- Menu (current week)
- Parent handbook and discipline policy
- Kentucky All STARS rating card

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly, documented
- **Severe weather / tornado drills** — periodically documented
- **Daily attendance** — sign-in / sign-out
- **Medication log** — per dose
- **Transportation log** — per trip, kept 5 years
- **Incident / injury log** — each event
- **Serious Incident Report** to OIG-DRCC — submit within 24 hours

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff in every room. Fix any ratio.
2. Confirm licenses, ratio chart, and evacuation routes are on the wall.
3. Look at the fire drill log — is this month's drill logged?
4. Open three random staff files. Check CCBC date, TB date, CPR date.
5. Open three random child files. Check immunization and emergency authorizations.
6. Walk the playground. Remove broken toys and standing water.
7. Lock up cleaning chemicals and medications.
8. Cribs: firm mattress, no blankets, no toys, one infant per crib.
9. Trash cans covered. Floors clean. Diaper area disinfected.
10. Take a deep breath. Be friendly. Inspectors remember attitude.

## What happens if you fail
Kentucky calls violations **"non-compliances."** You get a written plan of correction and a deadline. Serious or repeat findings can move you to **probationary license** (quarterly inspections for 2 years under 922 KAR 2:090), then **suspension** or **revocation**. You have the right to appeal using **Form OIG-DRCC-02**. Fees and serious-incident reports are also tracked. Every non-compliance shows up publicly on kynect for any parent to read.

## The weird Kentucky gotcha nobody tells you about
**Transportation logs must be kept for 5 years.** Most states say one year. Kentucky is the outlier. If you bus kids to/from school, to field trips, or anywhere, you must log every child on and off the bus, with times, and keep that paperwork for 5 full years. This is one of the first things a seasoned KY inspector pulls — especially if anyone filed a complaint about drop-off or pickup.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Kentucky owner
Their fear is a **kynect non-compliance showing up publicly** where every parent in Louisville/Lexington can see it before enrolling. Drop the 5-year transportation log retention rule in 30 seconds — nobody's compliance tool tracks that, and it's the fastest demo of "we actually know your state." Most KY owners today still use a paper binder + a Google Calendar reminder for CCBC 5-year renewals, which is exactly where they fail. Lead with "I'll show you every staff CCBC expiration and every child's immunization window right now" — they'll lean in. Probationary centers (quarterly inspections for 2 years) are the highest-pain, highest-willingness-to-pay segment; target anyone recently moved off probation on kynect.
