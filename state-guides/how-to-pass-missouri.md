# How to Pass Your Missouri Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **Missouri Department of Elementary and Secondary Education (DESE), Office of Childhood, Section for Child Care Regulation (SCCR)**. Yes — licensing moved from DHSS to DESE in **2021**. (Older web results still point you to DHSS. They're wrong.) Missouri runs **at least one unannounced annual inspection** per the federal CCDBG minimum. Separate annual fire safety and sanitation inspections happen in parallel. Complaints trigger unannounced visits anytime. Public sites:
- DESE Child Care: https://dese.mo.gov/childhood/child-care
- Show Me Child Care Provider Search (public inspection portal): https://healthapps.dhss.mo.gov/childcaresearch/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — RSMo Chapter 210, §§ 210.201–210.257.
- **The rulebook for centers + homes** — **5 CSR 25-500**, effective 2022 (migrated from 19 CSR 30-62 when licensing moved to DESE). Older PDFs in Google still cite the 19 CSR citations; current rule is 5 CSR 25-500.
- **Definitions + general provisions** — 5 CSR 25-400.
- **Subsidy standards** — 5 CSR 25-600.
- **Ratio rule** — 5 CSR 25-500.112. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/missouri/5-CSR-25-500-112
- **Inspection-process explainer (from DESE)** — https://dese.mo.gov/childhood/child-care/inspection-process

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
From 5 CSR 25-500.112 (centers). Mixed-age groups containing infants default to the infant 1:4 ratio. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

| Age band | One staff for every... | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Birth – 2 years (mixed infant/toddler) | 4 | 8 |
| 2 years (alone) | 8 | 16 |
| 3 years | 10 | — |
| 4 years | 10 | — |
| 3–4 years mixed | 10 | — |
| 5+ years (Kindergarten / school age) | 16 | — |

**Family Child Care Home:** up to 10 children with no more than 4 under age 2 (includes the provider's own kids under age 11).

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced.
2. Checks license is posted and current.
3. Walks every room, counts children, compares to staff present.
4. Pulls staff files — **Family Care Safety Registry (FCSR)**, fingerprint FBI, MO Child Abuse/Neglect Central Registry, Sex Offender Registry.
5. Pulls child files — immunization record (CCFS-201), health form (CCFS-200), medication log (CCFS-202).
6. Reviews emergency plan, drill logs (fire + tornado), incident reports.
7. Exit conference. Report posts to the Show Me Child Care Provider Search portal in near-real-time after the consultant finalizes it. Substantiated complaint summaries are inlined on your detail page.

**Heads up:** Missouri is rolling out the **Child Care Licensing Indicator System (CCLIS)** — a new, abbreviated monitoring model scheduled for statewide implementation **by June 2026**. Some of the report fields and visit cadence will change. Watch https://dese.mo.gov/childhood/child-care-licensing-indicator-system.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
Per 5 CSR 25-500 enforcement patterns and Missouri Child Care Aware's "Understanding Child Care Licensing Reports":
1. **Out-of-ratio** (5 CSR 25-500.112) — especially when an infant walks into a mixed room and bumps the whole group to 1:4.
2. **Background check gaps** — FCSR enrollment missed, FBI fingerprint not complete, or CA/N Central Registry not refreshed.
3. **Staff medical exam (CCFS-101) missing** or older than 12 months.
4. **Annual 12-hour continuing ed** not logged.
5. **Tornado drill** missing (Missouri-specific — rule requires drills during tornado season).

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Enrollment application** with emergency contacts and authorized pickups.
- **CCFS-200** — Child's Health & Emergency Form.
- **CCFS-201** — Immunization Record per DHSS schedule.
- **Physical exam** OR signed parent statement of health within 30 days of enrollment.
- **Medical / allergy action plans**.
- **CCFS-202** — Medication Authorization.
- **Signed authorizations** (transport, photo, emergency, field trips).
- **Daily attendance log**.
- **Safe Sleep policy signed** for infant rooms.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **Family Care Safety Registry (FCSR)** enrollment — initial and every 2 years.
- **Fingerprint-based FBI criminal background check** (federal CCDBG).
- **Missouri Child Abuse/Neglect Central Registry** clearance.
- **Sex Offender Registry** check.
- **CCFS-101** — Individual Medical Examination Report (within 12 months of employment).
- **TB screening** (current).
- **Pediatric CPR + First Aid** — current.
- **12 clock hours annual continuing education** (more for directors).
- **Pre-service orientation** before being counted in ratio.
- **CCDBG 10-topic pre-service training**.
- **Safe Sleep, SIDS, Shaken Baby Syndrome** training.
- **Director qualifications** per 5 CSR 25-500.182 (varies by capacity — 30 college credits with 6 child-related, OR CDA, OR experience).

## What must be on your walls
- Current DESE license.
- Most recent inspection report summary.
- Emergency plan and evacuation route per room.
- Menus.
- Safe Sleep policy in infant rooms.
- Discipline policy.
- Parent complaint hotline info.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drill** — regularly scheduled (quarterly is minimum practice; many centers monthly).
- **Tornado drill** — required periodically during tornado season (MO-specific).
- **Intruder / lockdown drill** — per your emergency plan.
- **Daily attendance** (sign-in/sign-out).
- **Medication log** (CCFS-202).
- **Incident reports** (CCFS-300).
- **Annual fire inspection** on file.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids in every room. Count staff on the floor. Watch for any infant mixed into a toddler/preschool room that trips the 1:4 rule.
2. Pull 3 random staff files — FCSR, FBI fingerprint, CA/N Registry, CCFS-101 medical, 12-hour training log, CPR card.
3. Pull 3 random child files — CCFS-200, CCFS-201, current allergy plan.
4. Infant rooms: back sleep, no loose bedding, safe-sleep policy on the wall.
5. Fire drill log — current month initialed?
6. Tornado drill log — initialed this season?
7. License posted and current (DESE-issued; make sure it's the new format, not the old DHSS one).
8. Medication cabinet — locked, in-date, matching CCFS-202.
9. Walk the playground — fence, gate, no hazards.
10. Check Show Me Provider Search — any open finding from last visit not marked corrected?

## What happens if you fail
DESE issues findings and a required Corrective Action Plan. Escalation ladder: probation, suspension, revocation (enforcement actions recorded on record and visible on the public provider search). Legacy records still cite 19 CSR 30-62 for pre-2022 violations; current cites are 5 CSR 25-500. Appeals go through the Missouri administrative process.

## The weird Missouri gotcha nobody tells you about
**Missouri rebuilt its whole licensing agency in 2021.** Authority moved from DHSS to DESE Office of Childhood; rules migrated from 19 CSR 30-62 to 5 CSR 25-500. Google still sends people to the old DHSS pages. The public "Show Me Child Care Provider Search" still runs on DHSS's old ASP.NET infrastructure even though DESE now owns the program. And there's a second wave coming: the **Child Care Licensing Indicator System (CCLIS)** is scheduled for statewide rollout by **June 2026** — which will change the inspection instrument and what shows up on your public record. Also unique to Missouri: the **Family Care Safety Registry (FCSR)** is a single clearinghouse that covers child care, elder care, and in-home workers — no other state packages background checks this way. If your FCSR enrollment lapses, every staff member tied to it is un-cleared in DESE's eyes.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Missouri owner
Biggest fear: a substantiated complaint summary showing up on the Show Me Provider Search page. It's inlined on their detail page for every parent to read. Drop this in 30 seconds: "Licensing moved from DHSS to DESE in 2021 — your rules are 5 CSR 25-500 now, not the old 19 CSR 30-62. CCLIS is rolling out statewide by June 2026. Are you tracking FCSR enrollment, the annual 12-hour CE, and making sure every infant-mixed room doesn't trip 1:4?" Today they are navigating a DESE portal for subsidy, a DHSS portal for the public search, and paper for CCFS forms — three systems, no single source of truth. The close: "We map every 5 CSR 25-500 cite to your staff and child files so you never have a CCFS form gap when a DESE consultant walks in."
