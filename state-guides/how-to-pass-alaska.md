# How to Pass Your Alaska Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
In Alaska, you face one of TWO licensors depending on where you operate:
- **Outside Anchorage:** a **Child Care Program Office (CCPO)** licensor from the **Alaska Department of Health, Division of Public Assistance**.
- **Inside Anchorage:** a **Municipality of Anchorage (MOA) Child Care Licensing** worker operating under parallel rules (Anchorage Municipal Code 16.55).

You'll get an **initial licensing inspection**, an **annual renewal inspection** (announced), and **random unannounced monitoring** plus complaint visits. Fire Marshal inspects; environmental health (state DEC or local) inspects. Serious incidents — deaths, hospitalizations, medication errors, abuse allegations — must be reported to CCPO **within 24 hours** per 7 AAC 57.545.

- CCPO landing: https://health.alaska.gov/en/division-of-public-assistance/child-care-program-office/
- AKCCIS provider portal / search: https://akccis.com/client/home
- MOA Child Care Licensing: https://www.muni.org/Departments/health/childcare/Pages/Regulations.aspx

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **Statute:** **AS 47.32** (Centralized Licensing of Entities That Provide Care); **AS 47.05.300 – 310** (Barrier Crimes Act); **AS 47.25.001 – .095** (Child Care Grant Program / subsidy).
- **Regulations:** **7 AAC 57** — Child Care Facilities Licensing. Key articles:
  - **7 AAC 57.010 – .050** — Application & licensing.
  - **7 AAC 57.200 – .299** — Administration (records, insurance, policies).
  - **7 AAC 57.300 – .399** — Facility & environment.
  - **7 AAC 57.500 – .599** — Care and Services (ratios, supervision, nutrition).
  - **7 AAC 57.505** — Ratios.
  - **7 AAC 57.545** — Serious incident reporting (24 hours).
- **Anchorage Municipal Code Chapter 16.55** — mirrors and sometimes exceeds 7 AAC 57.
- **Public records:** AS 40.25.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
**Child Care Centers — 7 AAC 57.505:**

| Age | Ratio | Max Group |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (birth – 18 mo) | 1:5 | 10 |
| Toddler (19 – 36 mo) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Preschool (3 – 4 yr) | 1:10 | 20 |
| Kindergarten / older | 1:14 | 28 |
| School-age (6+) | 1:18 | 36 |

**Home settings (7 AAC 57.405 / .900):** Child Care Home max 8 children under age 13 (no more than 3 under 30 months). **Group Child Care Home**: up to 12 with a second qualified caregiver.

**Warning:** Mixed-age groups default to the **youngest child's age band** for ratio and group size.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives (initial + annual by appointment; unannounced monitoring anytime; complaint visits as filed).
2. Confirms current license + **CC-61 Parent Guide** ("Teddy Bear Letter") and latest monitoring report are posted.
3. Walks rooms; counts kids and staff; checks 7 AAC 57.505 ratios and group sizes.
4. Pulls staff files — **Alaska Barrier Crimes** clearance (AS 47.05.300/310), State ABI fingerprint + FBI, sex offender registry, central child abuse registry, CPR/First Aid, TB, orientation (within 90 days), mandated-reporter training, **SEED Alaska Early Childhood Registry** training hours (12 – 24 hrs/year depending on role).
5. Pulls child files — enrollment, immunization per Alaska DOH schedule, health history + physical, daily attendance with sign-in/sign-out, incident logs.
6. Reviews serious-incident log and confirms 24-hour reporting compliance.
7. Writes deficiencies and a corrective action plan.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Barrier Crimes Act gaps** — AS 47.05.310 lists enumerated disqualifying crimes; a staff member who clears a generic background check can still be out of compliance under the Barrier Crimes list. Variance requests go through the DOH Background Check Unit.
2. **Late or missing 24-hour serious-incident reports** — 7 AAC 57.545 is strict; owners report to parents but forget CCPO.
3. **Ratio violations** — especially in mixed-age home settings where "no more than 3 under 30 months" is violated by an age transition.
4. **SEED registry gaps** — staff training hours not logged in SEED are effectively invisible to the licensor.
5. **Missing wall postings** — license, fire inspection, CC-61 Parent Guide, and most recent monitoring report must all be visibly posted.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment application with emergency contacts, authorized pickups.
- **Immunization record** per Alaska DOH schedule (or valid exemption).
- **Health history and physical exam**.
- Written permissions (transportation, outings, photos).
- Allergy / medical / individual care plan where needed.
- Daily attendance + sign-in/sign-out with caregiver verification.
- **Incident / injury logs** — serious incidents reported to CCPO within 24 hrs per 7 AAC 57.545.
- Medication administration record.

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application with education, training, experience.
- **Alaska Barrier Crimes check** per AS 47.05.300/310.
- **State ABI fingerprint + FBI**, sex offender registry, central child abuse registry.
- **Pediatric CPR / First Aid**.
- **TB screening** and health statement.
- Mandated reporter training acknowledgment.
- **Orientation within 90 days** of hire (per 7 AAC 57.535).
- **Annual training** — 12 – 24 hours depending on role, tracked in **SEED Alaska Early Childhood Registry** (seedalaska.org).
- Documented qualifications for director, lead teachers, aides.

## What must be on your walls
- Current CCPO (or MOA) license.
- **CC-61 Parent Guide / "Teddy Bear Letter"**.
- Most recent Fire Marshal inspection.
- Most recent environmental health inspection (DEC or local).
- **Most recent monitoring report** (required posting).
- Evacuation plan / emergency procedures.
- **Earthquake, tsunami (coastal), and wildfire** plans where applicable.
- Daily schedule.
- Menu.

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — documented.
- **Earthquake drills** — documented (Alaska is #1 for seismic activity).
- **Tsunami drills** — documented (coastal facilities).
- **Daily attendance** with sign-in/sign-out.
- **Medication administration record**.
- **Incident / injury logs** + 24-hr serious-incident reporting per 7 AAC 57.545.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff per room against 7 AAC 57.505; check home setting for "no more than 3 under 30 months".
2. Post current license, CC-61 Parent Guide, fire inspection, environmental inspection, and the most recent monitoring report — all 5 visible at entrance.
3. Pull a random staff file: Barrier Crimes clearance, ABI + FBI, TB, CPR/First Aid, SEED training log?
4. Pull a random child file: immunizations current? Physical on file? Sign-in/out today?
5. Medication locked; log current.
6. Earthquake drill log — last drill within required interval.
7. Diaper-change stations clean; gloves stocked.
8. Outdoor area safe; no bears drawn by food waste (document procedures for secured trash / food storage).
9. Fire extinguishers charged; exit paths clear of snow/ice.
10. Any incident in the last 30 days — was it reported to CCPO within 24 hours if it met the serious-incident threshold?

## What happens if you fail
CCPO (or MOA) can cite deficiencies, require a corrective action plan, issue a **provisional license**, or **suspend / revoke**. Findings attach to your public record; AKCCIS and — for Anchorage — the MOA Socrata `Childcare Inspections` dataset (`abmr-hyh6`) make them durably visible. You can appeal through DOH administrative hearings. For records, AS 40.25 presumes openness (child-abuse investigations exempt).

## The weird Alaska gotcha nobody tells you about
**Alaska is a DUAL-licensor state.** If you're in Anchorage, you operate under Alaska's 7 AAC 57 AND Anchorage Municipal Code 16.55 — and MOA sometimes imposes stricter rules. Owners who move from Fairbanks to Anchorage (or open a second site) assume CCPO rules are enough; they're not. Second gotcha: **Barrier Crimes** (AS 47.05.310) is a specific, enumerated list — a hire can pass state + FBI fingerprint and still hit a Barrier Crime and be disqualified without a variance. Third gotcha: the **24-hour serious-incident reporting rule** (7 AAC 57.545) is a top enforcement hook — a death, hospitalization, medication error, or abuse allegation must be reported to CCPO within 24 hours, not just to parents.

## For Magnus — how to sell to an Alaska owner
Their fear is the 24-hour serious-incident clock under 7 AAC 57.545 — miss a reporting deadline on a hospitalization and CCPO moves to provisional. The 30-second number: five wall postings required (license, CC-61, fire, environmental, latest monitoring report), plus 12 – 24 annual SEED training hours per staff. Alaska owners today track Barrier Crimes clearances, SEED hours, and 24-hour incident reports on whatever paper survives the winter. Lead with: "One phone screen that shows whether every staff has Barrier Crimes + SEED current, and a one-tap 24-hour incident report that auto-files with CCPO." Second angle: Anchorage owners run under both state AND MOA rules — your tool should cover both.
