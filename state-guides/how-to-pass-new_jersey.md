# How to Pass Your New Jersey Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **New Jersey Department of Children and Families (DCF)**, in the **Office of Licensing (OOL)**. They come out at least once a year for an **annual unannounced monitoring inspection**, plus a renewal inspection every 3 years, plus complaint visits any time. OOL posts every inspection report to the public Child Care Explorer portal within 1–3 weeks of the visit.

- Agency: https://www.nj.gov/dcf/about/divisions/ol/licensing.html
- Public inspection portal (Child Care Explorer): https://childcareexplorer.njccis.com/portal/

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The law** — Child Care Center Licensing Act, N.J.S.A. 30:5B-1 et seq.
- **The center rulebook** — Manual of Requirements for Child Care Centers, N.J.A.C. 3A:52. PDF: https://www.nj.gov/dcf/providers/licensing/laws/CCCmanual.pdf
- **The family child care rulebook** — N.J.A.C. 3A:51 (registered family providers, under N.J.S.A. 30:5B-16).
- **The background check rulebook** — N.J.A.C. 3A:54.
- **Ratio rule** — N.J.A.C. 3A:52-4.3. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-jersey/N-J-A-C-3A-52-4-3
- **Staffing rule** — N.J.A.C. 3A:52-4.2. https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-jersey/N-J-A-C-3A-52-4-2

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age band | Staff:child ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Under 18 months (infants) | 1:4 | 4 per primary caregiver; 12 infants/room max |
| 18 months – <2½ years (toddlers) | 1:6 | 6 per primary caregiver; 18 toddlers/room max |
| 2½ – <4 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 1:12 | 20 |
| 5 years and older | 1:15 | 30 |

Reduced ratios are allowed during sleep/rest periods if conditions are met (1:10 infants, 1:12 older toddlers, 1:20 age 2½+). **Field trips require a minimum of 2 staff**. Special-needs programs (≥50% children with special needs) run tighter ratios (1:3 under 2½; 1:5 ambulatory age 2½+). Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive unannounced and sign in.
2. They verify the license is posted with current expiration date.
3. They walk every classroom and count children against staff.
4. They pull child files looking for DCF Form CC-1 (health record) and DCF Form CC-2 (immunization).
5. They pull staff files looking for CARI + fingerprint + NJCCIS registry checks and the 20-hour annual PD log.
6. They review your written emergency plan and posted notices.
7. They check safe-sleep setup in infant rooms and allergy action plans.
8. They close out with **CCC-108 — the Inspection/Violation Report**. The report gets posted to the provider detail page on Child Care Explorer.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio violations** — cited as N.J.A.C. 3A:52-4.3.
2. **Incomplete staff background checks** — CARI, fingerprint, or NJCCIS sex offender check missing, cited under N.J.A.C. 3A:54.
3. **Missing 20 hours per year of professional development** recorded in NJCCIS — cited under the DCF PD rule, effective 2019.
4. **Missing DCF Form CC-1 health record or DCF Form CC-2 immunization record** in a child file — cited under N.J.A.C. 3A:52 subchapters 5 and 7.
5. **Posted-notice violations** — license, most recent inspection, Manual of Requirements parent summary, emergency exit plan, staff qualifications. Cited as posting rules in 3A:52.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **Signed application for admission.**
- **DCF Form CC-1** — Universal Child Health Record, signed by a physician within 6 months for kids under 2, annually after.
- **DCF Form CC-2** — Immunization Record, per N.J.A.C. 8:57-4 schedule. Religious or medical exemption must be documented.
- **Emergency contact and consent forms.**
- **Allergy, medication, and special diet notes** plus individual health care plan.
- **Daily attendance record.**
- **Incident and injury reports.**
- **Records retained minimum 1 year after discharge.**

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **CARI check** (Child Abuse Record Information through DCF).
- **Fingerprint-based state and FBI criminal history.**
- **NJCCIS sex offender registry check.**
- **All three refreshed every 3 years** per N.J.A.C. 3A:54.
- **Group teacher qualifications** per N.J.A.C. 3A:52-4.6 — minimum 60 ECE semester credits, CDA, or equivalent.
- **NJCCIS Registry enrollment** — required for every staff member.
- **20 hours per year of professional development** recorded in NJCCIS — DCF PD Requirements, effective 2019.
- **Orientation within 2 weeks of hire** covering shaken baby, SIDS, food allergy, and mandated reporter.
- **Pediatric CPR and First Aid** — at least one on premises at all times.
- **TB / health screening** per local health department.
- **Staff file retention: 3 years after separation.**

## What must be on your walls
- Your **current DCF license.**
- **Most recent inspection report.**
- **Emergency exit plan** in every room.
- **Staff qualifications.**
- **Information-to-Parents statement.** PDF: https://www.nj.gov/dcf/providers/licensing/CCL.Information.to.Parents.Statement.pdf
- **Manual of Requirements** parent summary.
- **USDA non-discrimination poster** (if CACFP).
- **Menu** (current week).

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly, per the written emergency plan required by 3A:52.
- **Lockdown and shelter-in-place drills** — per written plan.
- **Daily attendance.**
- **Medication log** — every dose, tied back to written authorization in the child's file.
- **Incident and injury reports.**
- **Safe-sleep policy acknowledgment** signed by parents of infants.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Stand in each classroom and count. Use the youngest child's ratio.
2. License on the wall? Current? Expiration visible?
3. Infant room: every baby on back, approved cribs, no blankets or inclined sleepers?
4. Pull 3 random child files. CC-1 health record current? CC-2 immunization record complete?
5. Pull 3 random staff files. CARI clear? Fingerprint clear? NJCCIS shows 20 PD hours this year?
6. Open the emergency folder. Fire drill logged for this month?
7. Walk the building for posted notices — license, last inspection, Information-to-Parents statement, emergency exit plan.
8. Check allergy action plans posted in classrooms where needed.
9. Confirm staff orientation was logged for anyone hired in the last 2 weeks.
10. If your center is on a former industrial site, confirm your lead/radon/vapor intrusion documentation is current (N.J.A.C. 3A:52-7.11).

## What happens if you fail
OOL can issue a **corrective action** with a due date, move you to a **provisional license** (up to 6 months) if you are not in substantial compliance, or pursue **denial, suspension, or revocation**. Each violation on CCC-108 is tied to a specific N.J.A.C. 3A:52 section and risk-classified as Critical, Significant, or Minor. Plans of correction are required. The full inspection report is posted publicly to Child Care Explorer. You have appeal rights through the NJ Office of Administrative Law. Parents can file complaints directly at 1-877-667-9845.

## The weird New Jersey gotcha nobody tells you about
NJ runs a **3-year license cycle** (initial and renewal) — stricter than most neighboring states that renew every 1 or 2 years. Layer on **20 hours of professional development every year** per staff in the NJCCIS registry — that's higher than most states' 10–16 hour floor. But the real gotcha is **N.J.A.C. 3A:52-7.11 Environmental Health**: if your center is in or on a former industrial site (which covers huge swaths of NJ), you have to document lead paint, radon, and vapor intrusion compliance — **NJDEP's environmental rules layer on top of DCF licensing**. Providers in multi-tenant buildings must document lead paint inspection; ground-floor and basement classrooms require radon testing. Owners moving from other states don't see this coming. NJ is the only state that essentially double-regulates your physical building with both the licensing agency and the environmental agency.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a NJ owner
NJ owners' biggest fear is the **3-year renewal** — it's a full re-licensure, not a rubber stamp, and any missing 20-hour PD log in NJCCIS for any staff member gets flagged. Drop this line: "Every one of your teachers needs 20 PD hours logged in NJCCIS every year. If one lapsed 12 months ago, the inspector sees it at renewal — and your license goes provisional." Also mention the **N.J.A.C. 3A:52-7.11 environmental stack** — NJDEP layers on top of DCF, and owners in urban/North Jersey especially get blindsided. Today they track PD on Excel spreadsheets and pray NJCCIS records are clean. A single missed CARI recheck (required every 3 years under 3A:54) can force a staff member out of ratio immediately. NJ is a compliance-heavy market and the owners feel it.
