# State Guides — How to Pass Your Daycare Inspection (All 50 States)

Plain-English inspection guides for every U.S. state. Written for real daycare owners, not lawyers. Every form number, statute cite, ratio, and URL is sourced from the state's own regulatory research files in `../all-states/{state}/`.

**Audience:**
1. Daycare owners (customers) — 7th-grade reading level, actionable.
2. Magnus (selling) — each guide ends with a private "For Magnus — how to sell to a {state} owner" section that's a 30-second pitch hook.

**Template:** every guide follows the same 12 sections (who's inspecting, the rulebook, ratios, what the inspector does, top 5 fails, child files, staff files, wall postings, drills, 10-min pre-inspection walk-through, what happens if you fail, state-specific gotcha, sources, sales hook).

## 2026 regulatory changes surfaced in the quirks sections

These are live or upcoming rule changes that could blindside an operator. Guides flag each one in their "weird gotcha" section.

- **Virginia — 8VAC20-821 effective 2026-02-01.** Retroactive 5-year background rechecks for all existing staff.
- **Kansas — HB 2045 transition 2026-07-01.** KDHE licensing moves to the new Office of Early Childhood.
- **Arizona — 9 A.A.C. 5 phase-in deadline August 2026.** 24-hr/yr CE becomes mandatory.
- **Missouri — CCLIS rollout June 2026.** Abbreviated-inspection model changes field/cadence structure.
- **New Mexico — Universal Child Care final regs (Oct 2025).** UCC-provider contract obligations phase in through 2026.
- **Wisconsin — infant/toddler rule update summer 2026.** Ratio bands revise on effective date.
- **Vermont — strictest-in-nation ratios.** 1:4 all the way to 24 months + 90% direct-care rule.
- **Idaho — 2025 HB 243 deregulation.** Point-based ratio (12 pts/staff max) replaces body counts.
- **Michigan — May 2025 rule rewrite.** R 400.8101 series renumbered; legacy citations no longer match.
- **Wyoming — CERTIFICATION not license.** Lapse = full re-application.
