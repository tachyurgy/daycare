# South Dakota Child Care Compliance

## Licensing Authority
**South Dakota Department of Social Services (DSS), Division of Child Care Services (CCS)**, with licensing functions administered through the **Office of Licensing and Accreditation (OLA)**. The provider search and inspection records are published on the OLA Constituent Portal (`olapublic.sd.gov`).

- Address: 700 Governors Drive, Pierre, SD 57501
- Phone: (605) 773-4766 | Toll-free: 800-227-3020
- Public Portal: https://olapublic.sd.gov/child-care-provider-search/

## Primary Regulatory Framework
- **Statute:** SDCL Title 26, Chapter 26-6 (Child Welfare; Foster Homes, Shelters, and Child Care).
- **Administrative Rules:** **ARSD Chapter 67:42** (Child Care Services). Key articles:
  - 67:42:01 — General provisions
  - 67:42:10 — Registered Family Day Care
  - 67:42:15 — Before and After School Programs
  - 67:42:17 — Licensed Child Care Centers and School-Age Programs

## Facility Types Regulated
1. **Licensed Child Care Program** (center) — 13+ children; full ARSD 67:42:17 applies.
2. **Before & After School Program** — school-age care operated outside school hours; ARSD 67:42:15.
3. **Registered Family Day Care** — in provider's home, up to 12 children; ARSD 67:42:10 / 67:42:17:21. Registration (not licensing) is required when caring for any children not related to the provider.
4. **Group Family Day Care** — small group home care.
5. **In-Home / Informal Providers** — exempt from registration/licensing, may participate in subsidy program as enrolled providers.

## Key Licensing Forms
- Child Care Licensing / Registration Application (available via OLA Constituent Portal login)
- Criminal background check request (DCI fingerprint card) — required for all applicants, employees, volunteers, adult household members
- Central Registry check (Child Abuse & Neglect) — mandatory for every adult in a child care setting
- Medical / Health Statement form for staff and enrolled children
- Immunization Record (SD DOH-approved form or physician equivalent)
- Fire Marshal inspection report (required for centers)
- Correction plans and variance requests via the portal

## Child-to-Staff Ratios & Group Sizes (Centers, ARSD 67:42:17:18 / 67:42:17:19)
| Age | Ratio (staff:children) | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infant (under 12 mo) | 1:5 | 10 |
| Toddler (12-23 mo) | 1:5 | 10 |
| 2 years | 1:5 | 10 |
| 3 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 4 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 5 years | 1:15 | 30 |
| School-age (6+) | 1:20 | 40 |

**Registered Family Day Care (ARSD 67:42:17:21):** maximum 12 children including provider's own, with sub-limits on infants (not more than 4 under 24 months, no more than 2 under 12 months).

## Required Child Records
- Enrollment application with emergency contacts and authorized pickups
- Current immunization record meeting SDCL 13-28-7.1 and SDDOH schedule (or religious/medical exemption affidavit)
- Medical / health statement
- Individual child care plan (for children with special needs / allergies)
- Written consent for transportation, field trips, photography
- Daily attendance records
- Incident/accident reports
- Medication administration logs (when applicable)

## Required Staff Records
- Application and resume documenting education / experience
- Central Registry check (SD Child Abuse & Neglect registry) — at hire and recurring
- State & FBI fingerprint-based criminal background check (SDCL 26-6-14.4)
- Sex offender registry check
- Reference checks (written)
- CPR & First Aid certification (valid, infant/child)
- Mandated reporter training acknowledgement
- Annual in-service training hours documentation (20 hours/year is the operating expectation for licensed center staff)
- TB screening / health statement

## Inspection Cadence
- **Initial licensing inspection** before issuance.
- **Annual monitoring inspection** for licensed and registered programs (one announced visit per year is the baseline).
- **Unannounced monitoring** visits may occur at any time.
- **Complaint-triggered inspections** as needed.
- All inspection results, corrective-action plans, and stipulation / consent agreements are published on the OLA public portal.

## Renewal Cadence
- **Licenses and registrations are issued annually** (one-year term). Providers must submit renewal application with updated background checks, fire and health inspections, and fees before expiration to avoid lapse.

## Notable State-Specific Requirements
- **Constituent Portal:** All application, renewal, incident reporting, and correction-plan activity runs through `olapublic.sd.gov`. This is the canonical interface.
- **Public posting of inspection results:** Every provider profile shows inspection history, compliance plans, corrective-action plans, and any stipulation/consent agreement for 3+ years.
- **Small state:** SD has roughly 700-750 licensed/registered programs statewide (centers + registered family day cares combined). Sioux Falls and Rapid City are the dominant markets.
- **Background check scope:** SDCL 26-6-14.4 mandates state & FBI fingerprint checks and Central Registry checks on all operators, employees, substitutes, volunteers, and adult household members before unsupervised access to children.
- **SDDOH immunization schedule** is the reference for child records; the state does not require HepA, but Hib, PCV13, DTaP, MMR, varicella, polio, and hep B are required.
- **CACFP / Head Start** is administered through DSS as well — many centers double-enroll, adding federal USDA record-keeping on top of state requirements.
