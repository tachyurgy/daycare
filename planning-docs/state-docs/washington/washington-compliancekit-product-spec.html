Section 01

Washington Regulatory Landscape

Washington's early learning system is governed by the Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF), a cabinet-level agency created in 2018 via HB 1661. The state licenses approximately 6,800 programs under a unified administrative code framework.

⚖

Governing Agency

Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF), created 2018 via HB 1661. Cabinet-level agency consolidating early learning, child welfare, and juvenile justice.

☰

Enabling Statute

RCW Chapter 43.216 provides the statutory foundation for all child care licensing in Washington State.

★

Licensing Reorganization

May 2025: Licensing Division reorganized so each licensor owns a provider for the entire life of the license. 6 DCYF regions statewide.

Administrative Code Structure

WAC Chapter Scope Facility Types Market Size
WAC 110-300 Family Home & Center Programs Family child care homes, child care centers ~6,500 programs
WAC 110-301 School-Age Programs Before/after school, summer programs ~300 programs
WAC 110-302 Outdoor Nature-Based Programs Forest schools, nature preschools Emerging (50+)
Market Breakdown: Of the ~6,800 licensed programs, 4,100+ are family child care homes, with the remainder split between centers and school-age programs. Family homes represent the largest segment and face the most complex ratio/capacity rules.

Public Data Systems & Portals

Child Care Check

findchildcarewa.org -- Public database showing licensing status, complaints, and Early Achievers rating. Drives reputation risk awareness and purchase motivation.

MERIT Workforce Registry

Formerly STARS. Tracks all staff training, credentials, and background checks. Every staff member gets a STARS ID. Training reimbursement up to $250/fiscal year.

WA Compass Provider Portal

Primary licensing portal for applications, inspection reports, and compliance communication between providers and DCYF.

data.wa.gov

Open data portal with machine-readable licensing datasets. Enables ComplianceKit data integration and market analysis.

Section 03

Violation & Enforcement System

Unlike Florida's formal Class 1-4 violation tiers, Washington does NOT use a tiered violation system. Violations are cited as WAC noncompliance, with severity determined contextually based on four factors.

Severity Determination Factors

1

Nature of Violation

What specific WAC provision was violated and how egregious the noncompliance is.

2

Threat to Children

Whether the violation poses an immediate, potential, or minimal threat to children's health and safety.

3

Prior History

Whether the provider has a pattern of similar violations or this is a first occurrence.

4

Corrective Action Taken

Whether the provider has already begun addressing the noncompliance before or during the inspection.

Enforcement Ladder

Washington uses a progressive enforcement model. Technical assistance must be offered before probation.

1

Technical Assistance Lowest

Must be offered before probation. Collaborative support to help providers understand and meet requirements.

2

Facility Licensing Compliance Agreement (FLCA)

Formal corrective action plan with specific timelines and milestones. Provider commits to documented improvements.

3

Probationary License Parent Notice Required

License placed on probation. Provider must notify all parents within 5 business days. Public record on Child Care Check.

4

Civil Monetary Penalties

Centers: $250 per violation per day. Family homes: $150 per violation per day. Each day of noncompliance counts as a separate violation. Fines due within 28 calendar days.

5

License Modification

Reduced capacity, restricted age groups, or other operational limitations imposed on the license.

6

License Suspension / Revocation Severe

Temporary suspension or permanent revocation of the child care license. Facility must cease operations.

7

Nonreferral Status Financial

Facility cannot accept children receiving child care subsidies. Significant revenue impact for providers serving low-income families.

Posting Requirement: Current and pending enforcement actions must be posted at the facility immediately and remain posted for a minimum of 2 weeks. Three years of inspection reports must be available for review upon request.

Section 05

Staff-Child Ratios

Washington's ratio system under WAC 110-300-0356 includes both standard and alternative configurations for centers. Family homes use an entirely different experience-based capacity system. Mixed-age rules add additional complexity.

Center Ratios WAC 110-300-0356

Age Group Ratio Max Group Size Configuration
Birth - 11 months 1:4 8 Standard
Birth - 11 months 1:3 9 Alternative
12 - 29 months 1:7 14 Standard
12 - 29 months 1:5 15 Alternative
30 months - 6 years 1:10 20 Standard
School-age (K+) 1:15 30 Standard

Mixed-Age Rules WAC 0357

Centers may combine ages during the first and last 2 hours of the operating day. When ages are mixed, the youngest child's ratio must be applied to the entire group.

Example: A group with one 8-month-old and nine 4-year-olds during morning drop-off requires 1:4 ratio for the entire group, not 1:10. This is one of the most common citation areas.

Family Home Capacity WAC 0355

Family homes use an experience-based capacity system that varies based on provider experience and staffing:

  • Alone, <1 year experience: Up to 6 children
  • Alone, 1-2 years experience: Graduated increase
  • With assistant, 2+ years: Up to 12 children
  • Infant-only programs: Up to 8 children at 1:4 ratio
ComplianceKit: The ratio calculator must account for experience level, assistant presence, and infant-only designations -- making this significantly more complex than center ratios.
Section 06

Training Requirements

Washington mandates a multi-layered training system: 30-hour pre-service, topic-specific training before unsupervised work, annual continuing education, and a 36-month EQEL cycle for leadership.

Pre-Service: Child Care Basics 30 Hours

Must be completed before license issuance OR before staff work unsupervised with children. Completed once -- not recurring. Must be finished within 90 days of enrollment.

Core Topics Covered

  • Abuse recognition & mandatory reporting
  • Emergency preparedness
  • Shaken baby syndrome prevention
  • Serving children experiencing homelessness
  • Medication management

Key Rules

  • One-time requirement (not annual)
  • Must complete within 90 days of enrollment
  • Tracked in MERIT system
  • Required before unsupervised care
  • Applies to all staff, not just leads

Required Before Unsupervised Work Within 3 Months of Hire

  • 1 Abuse & neglect recognition and mandatory reporting
  • 2 Emergency preparedness
  • 3 Shaken baby syndrome prevention (infant caregivers)
  • 4 Serving children experiencing homelessness
  • 5 Safe sleep training (annual renewal for infant caregivers)
  • 6 Medication management
  • 7 Blood & body fluids prevention (bloodborne pathogens)
  • 8 First Aid & CPR -- live hands-on required (ARC/AHA or equivalent)
  • 9 Food worker card -- local health jurisdiction, renewed before expiration

Annual Ongoing Training

Hours Required 10 hours/year
Clock Starts After 12 months of employment
Carryover Max 5 excess hours carry over
ComplianceKit Feature: Training hour tracker with automatic 5-hour carryover calculation and per-staff anniversary date tracking.

EQEL Training Cycle Leadership

Trigger Within 36 months of role assumption
Renewal Every 36 months
Content 10hrs child dev + 10hrs leadership
ECE Credential Deadline: August 1, 2026 deadline for ECE credential requirements under WAC 0100. Staff have 5 years to meet full educational qualifications.

Section 08

Documentation Requirements

WAC 110-300-0505 requires 14+ items to be posted or displayed at all times. Per-child, per-staff, and facility records must be maintained with specific retention periods ranging from 3 to 7 years.

Required Posted/Displayed Items WAC 110-300-0505

  • 1 Child care license
  • 2 Floor plan with emergency evacuation routes posted in each area
  • 3 Dietary restrictions and allergies (staff-accessible location)
  • 4 Handwashing practices posted at each sink
  • 5 Diaper changing procedure posted at each changing station
  • 6 Pesticide treatment notice
  • 7 Emergency numbers: 911, facility address, DCYF, Poison Center, CPS
  • 8 Notice of where emergency records are stored
  • 9 Current/pending enforcement actions (minimum 2 weeks posting)
  • 10 Safe sleep violation notices
  • 11 No Smoking / No Vaping signs
  • 12 Approved waivers and variances
  • 13 Insurance information or lapse notice
  • 14 Additional items per RCW 43.216.687

Per-Child Enrollment Records WAC 0460

  • Birth date, parent/guardian contacts
  • Emergency contacts
  • Authorized pickup persons
  • Special/individual needs plan with parent signature
  • Signed permissions:
    • Field trips
    • Transportation
    • Bathing
    • Water activities
    • Photo/video consent
  • Enrollment & withdrawal dates
  • Restraint documentation
  • Expulsion documentation
  • Developmental screening notifications

Per-Child Health Records

  • Immunization records (current)
  • Health history with individual care plans
  • Medication authorization and administration logs
  • Medical and dental provider information
  • Last physical and dental exam dates
  • Medical care consent form
  • Incident/injury reports

Per-Staff Records WAC 0115

Identity & Employment

  • Name, date of birth, job title
  • Employment start and end dates
  • Government-issued photo ID copy
  • SSN or EIN
  • Application and/or resume
  • Emergency contact information

Credentials & Training

  • Credentials documentation
  • Training proof (linked to MERIT)
  • Annual observation/evaluation records
  • Immunization records (centers)
  • Verified by licensee or director

Retention Periods

Record Type Retention Accessibility Notes
General records 3 Years Last 12 months on-site Older records within 2 weeks of written request
Attendance records 5 Years Last 12 months on-site Digital records explicitly accepted (WAC 0455)
Pesticide records 7 Years On-site Longest retention requirement in WAC 110-300
Digital Records: WAC explicitly accepts digital format for attendance (WAC 0455), staff records via MERIT (WAC 0115), and parent handbook distribution (WAC 0450). This provides clear regulatory backing for ComplianceKit's digital-first approach.
Section 09

Early Achievers Quality Rating System

Early Achievers is Washington's QRIS (Quality Rating and Improvement System) -- a 5-level rating framework that goes beyond basic licensing compliance to measure program quality. Participation is mandatory for subsidy-receiving sites.

Rating Levels

Level Description Requirements Visual
Level 1 Licensed Only Meets all WAC licensing requirements
Level 2 Enrolled & Progressing Enrolled in Early Achievers, working toward quality benchmarks
Level 3 Quality Standard Subsidy Target Subsidy sites must reach within 30 months of enrollment
Level 4 Quality Excellence ECEAP Target ECEAP sites must reach within 24 months. Video submission required.
Level 5 Highest Quality Exceeds all quality benchmarks. Recognized as exemplary program.
▶

Video Submission

Levels 3+ require video submission of staff interactions with children. Used to assess teaching quality and child engagement.

☆

Partners

Administered by DCYF in partnership with Child Care Aware of WA and University of Washington. Assessment trained by UW researchers.

✓

ComplianceKit Opportunity

Help providers prepare for Early Achievers assessment with readiness checklists, environment photo documentation, and self-assessment tools.
