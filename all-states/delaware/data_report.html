6. Violations & Inspection Data

Two complete, machine-readable datasets available via Delaware's Socrata Open Data Portal. No scraping needed. Delaware is the clearest bulk-compliance-data win in the entire research set.

Open-records backstop

7. Outreach Signals

  • Radical transparency — 5 years of non-compliance + complaints are public, including complaints that did not rise to regulation violations. Parents see everything. Every compliance slip is a permanent reputational fact. Core pitch.
  • Annual renewal + annual inspection — predictable yearly buying cycle.
  • DOE oversight (not HHS) — unusual nationally; compliance culture looks more like K-12 than like typical daycare licensing. Different buyer mental model; educators over-index on documentation.
  • Small state, concentrated geography — 60% New Castle, 20% Kent, 20% Sussex. Inspection logistics are simple; OCCL visits rapidly. A minor slip becomes a findings list fast.
  • STARS QRIS correlates with subsidy rate. Top-tier STARS operators are sophisticated buyers (close to ICP); lower-STARS operators are in active improvement mode (also high-intent).
  • Health & Safety Reviews mandatory for ALL staff (not just leads). Documentation burden scales with staff count.

8. Next-Step Data Acquisition

  1. Monthly re-pull of all four Socrata datasets; maintain join keys (license_number).
  2. Segmentation pipeline:
    • High-intent: any substantiated complaint in last 12 months OR any non-compliance finding not yet corrected.
    • Chronic: ≥3 findings in last 24 months.
    • Pristine exclude: zero findings in last 36 months.
  3. Exclude rows where provider roster shows enforcement_action != null or intent_to_revoke = true — active-crisis; not a clean sales fit.
  4. Enrichment for email via Hunter.io / Apollo using name + address.
  5. Segment by STARS level (ggpn-vz9t) — 3+ star operators are core ICP.
  6. DE FOIA backstop for inspection PDFs + historical enforcement actions.
