# How to Pass Your Washington Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector is a **licensor** at the **Washington State Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF)**, Licensing Division. Before July 2018, licensing lived at the Department of Early Learning (DEL); DCYF absorbed DEL. DCYF uses **differential monitoring** — lower-risk programs get a full inspection at least once a year (unannounced), higher-risk programs get visited more. Every valid complaint and adverse action is posted to the public Child Care Check portal (findchildcarewa.org).

- Agency: https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/early-learning-providers
- Public inspection portal (Child Care Check): https://www.findchildcarewa.org
- DCYF operator lookup line: 1-866-482-4325

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **The main law** — RCW 43.216 (DCYF authority), RCW 43.216.055 (background checks), RCW 43.216.325 (health & safety). https://app.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=43.216
- **The operating rulebook** — Washington Administrative Code Chapter 110-300 ("Foundational Quality Standards for Early Learning Programs"). https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=110-300
- **Ratio rule (centers)** — WAC 110-300-0356. https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=110-300-0356
- **Ratio rule (family home)** — WAC 110-300-0355. https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=110-300-0355
- **In-service training rule** — WAC 110-300-0107.
- **Safe sleep rule** — WAC 110-300-0285.
- **School-age program rules** — WAC 110-301.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)
| Age band | Ratio | Max group size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (birth – 11 months) | 1:4 (or 1:3 if group of 9) | 8 (or 9) |
| Toddlers (12 – 29 months) | 1:7 (or 1:5 if group of 15) | 14 (or 15) |
| Preschool (30 months – 6 years, not in K/elementary) | 1:10 | 20 |
| School-age (5 – 12 years, in K/elementary) | 1:15 | 30 |

Mixed-age rules: use the ratio and group size for the **youngest** child. Ratios apply **at all times**, including off-site trips and transportation. The first and last 2 hours of the day allow mixed-age grouping under the youngest-child rule. Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. They arrive unannounced and sign in.
2. They verify your license is posted and your Early Achievers rating is visible.
3. They walk every classroom and count children against staff.
4. They observe meals, transitions, and safe-sleep setup in infant rooms.
5. They pull child files looking for **Certificate of Immunization Status (CIS)** and enrollment forms (DCYF 15-879).
6. They pull staff files looking for MERIT profiles, background checks, and training hours.
7. They verify drill logs, especially the **quarterly earthquake drills** unique to WA.
8. They close out with a monitoring report citing specific WAC 110-300 sections, classified High / Medium / Low risk. The report is uploaded as a PDF to your Child Care Check page.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in this state
1. **Ratio and supervision violations** — cited as WAC 110-300-0356 (centers) or 110-300-0355 (family homes).
2. **Staff MERIT profile missing or incomplete background check** — cited under RCW 43.216.055; every staff member must have a MERIT account, a completed Portable Background Check, and STARS ID.
3. **Safe sleep violations** — cited as WAC 110-300-0285 (infant not on back, blankets in crib, inclined sleeper).
4. **Immunization not meeting RCW 28A.210 schedule** — especially missing MMR with no valid exemption (personal/philosophical exemption for MMR was banned by 2019 SB 5841).
5. **Missed 10 clock-hour annual in-service training** — cited as WAC 110-300-0107.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- **DCYF 15-879** Child Care Registration form.
- **Certificate of Immunization Status (CIS)** meeting RCW 28A.210. If exempt, a Certificate of Exemption — **no personal or philosophical exemption is allowed for MMR** (2019 SB 5841).
- **Emergency medical consent, field trip, photo/media permissions.**
- **DCYF 15-970 Individual Care Plan** if the child has special health needs.
- **Allergy and special-needs plan.**
- **Parent handbook acknowledgment.**
- **Daily attendance.**
- **DCYF 15-968 Medication Authorization** plus administration log.
- **Incident/accident reports** (copy to parent the same day).

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- **MERIT account** — mandatory. Includes STARS ID, training history, and credentials.
- **Portable Background Check** — fingerprint-based state and FBI, state sex offender registry, CPS/APS findings, out-of-state child abuse registry if lived elsewhere in last 5 years. Per RCW 43.216.055, refreshed every 3 years.
- **DCYF 15-937** (center) or **DCYF 15-949** (family home) Background Check Checklist.
- **Director / Program Supervisor** — ECE State Certificate or higher (phased-in through 2030 per WAC 110-300-0100 series).
- **Orientation training (DCYF-approved)** within 90 days of hire — 20 hours on CCDBG topics.
- **30 hours of in-service training in the first year**, then **minimum 10 clock hours per year** (WAC 110-300-0107). Recorded in MERIT.
- **Pediatric CPR and First Aid** — all staff in ratio; at least 1 certified present at all times.
- **DCYF 15-966 Health Consultant Agreement** if you serve infants/toddlers or children with special care needs.
- **TB screening / health declaration** per local Public Health.
- **Mandated reporter training.**

## What must be on your walls
- Your **current DCYF license.**
- **Your Early Achievers rating** (Washington's QRIS).
- **Most recent inspection summary.**
- **Emergency evacuation plan** in every room.
- **Safe sleep policy** in every infant room.
- **Daily schedule** by classroom.
- **Menu** (current week).
- **Expulsion policy** (WAC 110-300-0186 restricts suspension/expulsion; must document attempts to support first).
- **USDA non-discrimination poster** (if CACFP).

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — monthly.
- **Earthquake drills** — **at least one per quarter** (Pacific NW-specific, WAC 110-300 emergency preparedness).
- **Lockdown and shelter-in-place drills** — per written plan.
- **Daily attendance** maintained in real time.
- **Medication administration log** (DCYF 15-968).
- **Incident/accident reports** — parent gets a copy the same day.
- **Serious event reporting** (death, hospitalization, missing child, critical injury): notify DCYF **within 24 hours**.

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Stand in each classroom and count. Use the youngest child's ratio.
2. License on the wall? Early Achievers rating visible?
3. Infant room: every baby on back, crib-safe, no inclined sleepers (WAC 110-300-0285)?
4. Pull 3 random child files. CIS current? No MMR personal/philosophical exemption in file?
5. Pull 3 random staff files. MERIT profile up to date? Portable Background Check under 3 years old? 10 in-service hours logged for the year?
6. Open the emergency folder. Monthly fire drill logged? This quarter's earthquake drill logged?
7. Walk the playground. Safe for age group?
8. Check cleaning chemicals — locked, out of child reach.
9. For pre-1978 facilities, confirm lead paint documentation is current.
10. For below-grade classrooms, confirm radon testing is on file.

## What happens if you fail
DCYF uses a range of enforcement: **Technical Assistance**, a written **Facility Licensing Compliance Agreement (FLCA)**, moving to a **Probationary license**, **Summary Suspension**, **Revocation**, **Denial**, or **Non-Renewal**. Policy references: DCYF Policy 3.20.80 (Licensing Investigations) and 3.20.90 (Adverse Actions). Valid complaints and adverse actions appear publicly on Child Care Check within days of closing. You have appeal rights through the Office of Administrative Hearings. DCYF's 5-business-day first action and 45-day close target are set in the complaints workflow: https://dcyf.wa.gov/safety/child-care-complaints

## The weird Washington gotcha nobody tells you about
Washington has **two quirks no other state has**. First, **2019 SB 5841 banned personal and philosophical exemptions for the MMR vaccine** in child care and schools. You can still accept medical exemptions and limited religious exemptions (for other vaccines) — but a parent waving an "I don't want to vaccinate for MMR" form at you is not a valid exemption under WA law, and accepting it is an immediate cite against RCW 28A.210. Second, Washington is the **only state requiring quarterly earthquake drills** (at least one per quarter) on top of monthly fire drills. Pacific NW subduction zone reality. Owners from other states miss the earthquake drill log every time. Also worth knowing: WA is the **first and only state** to license **Outdoor Nature-Based Programs** under WAC 110-300 Subchapter F.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a Washington owner
WA owners' #1 operational pain is **MERIT** — they have to keep every staff member's MERIT profile current with training hours, STARS ID, credentials, and background check status, and MERIT is clunky. Drop this line: "A missed Portable Background Check renewal (3-year clock under RCW 43.216.055) kicks your teacher out of ratio immediately — and Child Care Check shows it publicly." Also lead with the **quarterly earthquake drill** — it's genuinely unique to WA, and most out-of-state franchise buyers don't know it exists. Remind them about the **2019 SB 5841 MMR exemption ban**: if even one child file still has a "personal exemption" signed off, that's a violation every visit. Today they manage all this in MERIT + spreadsheets + paper drill logs. WA providers want peace of mind before 7am Monday. That's what you're selling.
