Texas Regulatory Landscape

Texas child care operations are governed by the Health and Human Services Commission (HHSC) Child Care Regulation (CCR) Division. The CCR Division licenses, monitors, and inspects all child care operations in the state under Texas Human Resources Code, Chapter 42 and the Texas Administrative Code, Title 26, Chapters 744-749.

The primary standards governing licensed child care centers are found in Chapter 746 - Minimum Standards for Child-Care Centers. These standards cover every aspect of operation, from physical facility requirements to staff qualifications, child-to-staff ratios, health and safety protocols, and administrative record-keeping.

Market Opportunity: With over 14,000 licensed centers and a regulatory framework that demands meticulous documentation, the Texas market represents the single largest state opportunity for ComplianceKit. The majority of these centers still rely on paper-based compliance tracking.

What Happens During an Inspection

  1. Inspector arrives unannounced and presents HHSC identification to facility staff
  2. Walks through all areas accessible to children -- indoor classrooms, outdoor play areas, storage rooms, and sleeping/nap areas
  3. Reviews documentation in the office/admin area, cross-referencing staff and children's records against required forms
  4. Checks all posted items on walls -- license, emergency numbers, menus, activity plans, discipline policy, evacuation routes
  5. Observes staff-child interactions and ratios in real-time, counting heads in each classroom against age group requirements
  6. Documents any deficiencies found, noting the specific minimum standard violated and the severity weight
  7. Notifies the operation in writing of any violations before departing the facility
  8. Violations are posted to the public online record via the Search Texas Child Care website, visible to any parent

Inspector Tools

Inspectors use the CLASS system on a laptop or tablet during their visit, along with standardized inspection evaluation forms:

  • Form 7259 -- Personnel Records Evaluation
  • Form 7260 -- Children's Records Evaluation
  • Form 7261 -- Center Records Evaluation
Product Insight: ComplianceKit's inspection readiness dashboard mirrors exactly what inspectors check using Forms 7259, 7260, and 7261. By structuring our data model around these forms, we ensure that facilities can self-audit against the exact same criteria inspectors use.

Deficiency Weight System

Texas assigns each minimum standard a deficiency weight based on the level of risk the violation poses to children. This weight system drives the severity of enforcement actions and determines whether administrative penalties apply.

Medium-High
Medium
Medium-Low

Weight Risk Level Examples Consequences
High Greatest risk to children Background check violations, supervision failures, abuse/neglect incidents Administrative penalties up to $1,000 per violation; possible immediate adverse action
Med-High Significant risk Staff-to-child ratio violations, safety hazards (accessible pools, broken gates) Corrective action required; repeated violations escalate to probation
Medium Moderate risk Training requirement gaps, incomplete documentation, expired certifications Written plan of correction; follow-up inspection to verify
Med-Low Lower risk Minor facility maintenance issues, cosmetic repairs needed Documented deficiency; correction expected by next inspection

Product Insight: ComplianceKit color-codes every checklist item and alert using this exact five-tier weight system. High-weight items are surfaced first in the dashboard, ensuring operators focus on the violations that carry the most risk.

Documentation Requirements

Texas requires three categories of documentation to be physically present and inspection-ready at every licensed facility. This section maps every required document, what it contains, and how ComplianceKit digitizes it.

A. Inspector Evaluation Forms

These are the forms the CCR inspector uses to systematically evaluate the facility. Understanding what inspectors check item-by-item is the basis for ComplianceKit's self-audit feature.

Form 7259 -- Personnel Records Evaluation

For each staff member, the inspector verifies:

  • Background check clearances (DPS, FBI, DFPS)
  • Pre-employment affidavits
  • Training documentation and transcripts
  • Health statements
  • CPR and First Aid certifications (current and valid)
  • Professional development records and annual training hours
Form 7260 -- Children's Records Evaluation

For each enrolled child, the inspector verifies:

  • Admission information (Form 2935) with complete fields
  • Emergency contacts and authorized pickup persons
  • Immunization records (current per Texas DSHS schedule)
  • Allergy and medical condition documentation
  • Parent authorizations (field trips, photos, sunscreen, etc.)
  • Sign-in/sign-out logs (Form 2941) with parent signatures
Form 7261 -- Center Records Evaluation

Facility-wide records the inspector reviews:

  • Current license and permits
  • Plan of operation (Form 2948)
  • Liability insurance certificate (current)
  • Fire, health, and gas inspection records
  • Emergency preparedness and evacuation documentation
  • Posted menus and activity plans
  • Controlling persons documentation
  • Waivers and variances (if any)

B. Provider-Maintained Operational Forms

Form 1100 -- Daily Building & Grounds Checklist

24 items, completed Monday through Friday. This is the most operationally intensive recurring compliance task:

  • Environment free of choking objects (under-3 rooms)
  • Cloth toys washed weekly
  • Linens washed weekly
  • Sleeping equipment sanitized between children
  • Garbage cans inaccessible to children
  • Floors, ceilings, and walls in good repair
  • Areas well heated, lighted, and ventilated
  • Table tops sanitized when soiled
  • Hazardous materials stored properly
  • Electrical outlets childproofed (under-5 rooms)
  • Glass in sliding doors marked at eye level
  • Play materials safe and non-toxic
  • Bodies of water inaccessible
  • Active play space inspected before use
  • Broken equipment removed or repaired
  • Current menu posted
  • Current activity plans posted
  • Toxic/cleaning supplies out of reach
  • Toilet paper available in restrooms
  • Soap and disposable towels available
  • Naptime lighting appropriate for supervision
  • Adequate space between cribs/cots/mats
  • Nap mats arranged with walkway access
  • Televisions securely anchored
Form 7263 -- Emergency Practices Log

Monthly and quarterly drill tracking:

  • Fire drills -- Monthly, must evacuate in under 3 minutes
  • Shelter / severe weather drills -- 4 times per year
  • Lock-down drills -- 4 times per year
  • Carbon monoxide alarm tests -- Monthly
  • Smoke detector tests -- Monthly
  • Fire extinguisher checks -- Monthly

After receiving a penalty notice, the operation has a 30-day window to request a hearing to contest the finding. During probation, the facility remains open but is subject to increased monitoring visits.

Architecture

ComplianceKit is built as an offline-first Progressive Web App (PWA) to ensure reliability in field conditions where internet connectivity may be limited. The architecture prioritizes data integrity, real-time updates, and regulatory-grade audit trails.

FE
Next.js 15 PWA
Offline-first progressive web app with service worker caching for field use. App Shell architecture ensures instant load.
DB
Supabase
Postgres + Auth + Storage + Row-Level Security. Multi-tenant data isolation. HIPAA-eligible configuration.
RT
Realtime Ratios
Supabase Realtime subscriptions power live ratio monitoring. Changes propagate to all connected devices instantly.
PDF
Report Generation
React-PDF produces inspection-ready reports matching the format inspectors expect. Export to PDF with one tap.
PN
Push Notifications
Web Push API for expiration alerts, drill reminders, checklist nudges, and ratio warnings. Works on iOS and Android.
OCR
Document Scanning
Tesseract.js or Google Vision API for immunization card OCR. Extracts vaccine names, dates, and lot numbers automatically.

Key Architecture Decisions

  • Offline-first with sync: All checklists, attendance, and incident reports can be completed offline and sync when connectivity returns. Conflict resolution uses last-write-wins with server timestamps.
  • Row-Level Security: Supabase RLS policies ensure facilities can only access their own data. Staff see only their assigned classrooms. Parents see only their child's records.
  • Immutable audit trail: All compliance-critical records (checklist completions, drill logs, ratio snapshots) are append-only. Timestamps are server-generated and cannot be modified by clients.
  • Multi-state extensibility: Domain model supports state-specific configuration (ratio tables, form definitions, weight systems) to enable expansion beyond Texas without architectural changes.
Why PWA over native: Licensed child care centers range from large corporate chains to single-location family operations. A PWA eliminates app store friction, works on any device with a browser, and can be installed to the home screen. Offline support ensures the app works even in areas with poor connectivity -- a real concern for rural Texas facilities.
