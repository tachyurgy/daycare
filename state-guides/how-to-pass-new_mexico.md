# How to Pass Your New Mexico Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector works for the **New Mexico Early Childhood Education and Care Department (ECECD), Regulatory Oversight Unit, Child Care Services Bureau**. ECECD became a cabinet-level agency and took over child-care licensing from CYFD several years ago. New Mexico is now the **first state in the country to offer Universal Child Care**, with new rules finalized October 2025 — if you accept UCC funding, you have extra contractual obligations on top of the standard license. Every inspection survey is posted publicly on ECECD's Inspection Surveys page for **3 years**, and any parent can look up your history through **New Mexico Child Care Finder**.

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

**Centers where children are grouped by age** (8.16.2.23 NMAC):

| Age | One staff for this many kids | Max group |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (6 wks – 12 mo) | 1:6 | 12 |
| Toddlers (12 – 24 mo) | 1:6 | 12 |
| 2 years | 1:10 | 20 |
| 3 years | 1:12 | 24 |
| 4 years | 1:12 | 24 |
| 5 years | 1:15 | 30 |
| 6+ years | 1:15 | 30 |

**Centers where age groups are combined** (also 8.16.2.23):

| Age | Ratio | Max group |
|---|---|---|
| 6 wks – 24 mo | 1:6 | 12 |
| 2 – 4 yr | 1:12 | 24 |
| 3 – 5 yr | 1:14 | 28 |
| 6+ yr | 1:15 | 30 |
| 18–24 mo combined with 24–35 mo | 1:6 | 12 |

Ratios and group sizes go by the **youngest child** present. A **minimum of two staff** must be on-site at all times; one must be an "educator." If fewer than 7 children are present, the second staff may do support duties.

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up. Providers licensed before the current rule's effective date are grandfathered on group size but must always meet ratios.

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives (initial and renewal are scheduled; complaint and annual monitoring are unannounced).
2. Checks your posted license, posted ratios, group-size cards, and UCC participation status.
3. Walks each room, counts kids vs. staff by age.
4. Pulls staff files: background check (FBI NGI + state registry + NM child abuse/neglect + NM sex offender), director qualifications, **annual signed statement** certifying criminal history, 45-hour entry-level ECE course (within 6 months), **24 hours annual training**, CPR + First Aid with pediatric component, SIDS / shaken baby / safe sleep training.
5. Pulls child files: enrollment, NMDOH-compliant immunization record, health history, medication authorizations.
6. Reviews fire inspection report, sanitation inspection, food service inspection.
7. Checks **weekly playground equipment inspection log** (8.16.2.29 NMAC — unique NM rule).
8. Checks **director on-site ≥50% of core hours** — writes it as an observation.
9. Writes findings referencing the specific 8.16.2 NMAC subsection with corrective action deadline.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in New Mexico
1. **Ratio not met** (8.16.2.23 NMAC) — especially in mixed-age rooms.
2. **Annual signed staff statement missing** — every staff must sign annually certifying no disqualifying conviction; teen staff need parent/guardian signature.
3. **Weekly playground equipment inspections not documented** (8.16.2.29 NMAC). Most owners forget this weekly cadence.
4. **Annual training short** — 24 hours required, covering 7 competency areas over 2 years; 45-hour entry-level ECE course required within 6 months of hire.
5. **Substitute list** not maintained — program must have **at least two qualified substitutes** named with background check + orientation records.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment application (name, DOB, parent contacts, emergency contact)
- **Immunization record** per NMDOH schedule (or approved exemption)
- Health history and special-needs documentation
- Authorization for emergency medical treatment
- Medication authorization and administration log
- Allergy / special diet / special needs plan
- Signed acknowledgment of parent handbook
- Daily attendance record

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application, employment history, references
- Background check clearance (FBI NGI + state registry + NM child abuse/neglect + NM sex offender)
- Health certificate (TB screening, basic fitness-for-duty)
- Signed acknowledgment of orientation
- **Director qualifications** documentation
- **24 hours of annual training** (each staff >20 hrs/week, including the director) covering 7 competency areas
- **45-hour entry-level ECE course** or approved 3-credit course within **6 months of hire**
- CPR & First Aid with pediatric component
- SIDS / shaken baby / safe sleep training
- Infectious disease prevention & medication administration training
- **Annual signed statement** certifying criminal history and continued eligibility
- Training log with dates, clock hours, competency area, source

## What must be on your walls
- Current ECECD license
- Posted classroom capacities, ratios, and group sizes (required by rule)
- Fire inspection report
- Sanitation inspection report
- Evacuation routes each room
- Emergency operations plan summary
- Complaint hotline 1-888-351-0037

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — per emergency plan, documented
- **Weekly playground equipment inspection** (8.16.2.29 NMAC — New Mexico-specific)
- **Daily attendance**
- **Medication log**
- **Injury / incident log**
- **Emergency operations plan** — approved annually by licensing authority

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff in every room using the age-grouped or combined-age tables.
2. Confirm the posted ratio/group-size card is in each room.
3. Fire drill log — this month entered? This week's playground inspection logged?
4. Open three staff files: background check, annual signed statement, 24-hour training, 45-hour ECE (if within 6 months of hire), CPR/First Aid, SIDS training.
5. Open three child files: immunization, emergency medical authorization, allergy plan.
6. Substitute list: two qualified subs documented?
7. Director on-site clock: are you on-site 50% of core hours today?
8. Sleep areas: backs only, one infant per crib.
9. Playground: inspected this week?
10. Greet the inspector warmly. Offer a chair. Answer plainly.

## What happens if you fail
ECECD writes findings on the per-visit survey and posts a PDF for the public. Escalation goes **corrective action → sanctions (per 7.1.8 NMAC) → monetary penalty → conditions on license → suspension → revocation**. Late renewal (<30 days before expiration) incurs a **$25 late fee**. Automatic expiration occurs at midnight on the expiration date if not renewed. Appeals go through NMAC administrative hearings. Every survey from the last 3 years is on ECECD's public page; older data is available via an **IPRA** (Inspection of Public Records Act) request with a 15-calendar-day statutory response.

## The weird New Mexico gotcha nobody tells you about
New Mexico is the only state that requires a **weekly playground equipment inspection**, documented in writing, under 8.16.2.29 NMAC. Most states require monthly; New Mexico requires weekly. Plus, the **primary educator rule**: a child may have no more than **three primary, consecutive educators in any day**, and for children under 2, the same educator should supervise them **as much as possible**. This is a continuity-of-care rule that tripped up plenty of centers with rotating staff schedules. And the **director on-site ≥50% of daily core hours** is enforced — if your inspector shows up and the director is off-site for the day, you're cited. Build the weekly playground check into your Monday opening routine, lock the director schedule so one day a week has overlap, and keep the substitute list up to date.

## For Magnus — how to sell to a New Mexico owner
Their fear is **UCC contract non-compliance** cutting them off from Universal Child Care funding, and public survey PDFs on ECECD's page that any parent can download. Drop the **weekly playground inspection rule** in 30 seconds — almost nobody tracks this on a weekly cadence, and it's a real 8.16.2.29 NMAC citation. They're using paper files + the Nevada-registry-style training log + sticky notes for the 45-hour ECE six-month deadline. Say "I'll track every staff's 45-hour ECE clock, every annual signed statement, and your weekly playground inspections." Albuquerque, Las Cruces, Santa Fe, and Rio Rancho are the targets; UCC participation makes ECECD compliance financially existential — this isn't a nice-to-have.
