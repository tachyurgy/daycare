# How to Pass Your Nebraska Daycare Inspection
*Plain-English guide. Written for real owners, not lawyers. Last updated 2026.*

## Who's knocking on your door
Your inspector is a **surveyor** from the **Nebraska Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS), Division of Public Health, Licensure Unit, Office of Children's Services Licensing**. Newly licensed programs start on a **one-year Provisional License**. Inspections happen **at least once a year** for centers licensed for ≤29 children, and **at least twice a year** for centers licensed for ≥30 children. DHHS also does **monitoring inspections** unannounced at any time. Every disciplinary action is published in a **monthly "Negative Actions" PDF** and a **10-year cumulative PDF** on the DHHS Licensure page.

- Provider search: https://dhhs.ne.gov/Pages/Search-for-Child-Care-Providers.aspx

## The rulebook (this is what they measure you against)
- **This is the law:** Nebraska Child Care Licensing Act — Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-1908 et seq.
- **This is the grounds-for-discipline statute:** Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-1919. https://nebraskalegislature.gov/laws/statutes.php?statute=71-1919
- **This is the main rulebook:** Title 391 Nebraska Administrative Code — Children's Services Licensing. https://dhhs.ne.gov/licensure/Documents/CCC391-3.pdf
  - **Chapter 2:** Family Child Care Home I
  - **Chapter 3:** Child Care Centers + Family Child Care Home II
- **This is the ratio rule:** 391 NAC 3-006.15C
- **This is the immunization law:** Neb. Rev. Stat. §§ 71-1913.01 – 71-1913.03 and 173 NAC 4

## Your child-to-staff ratios (the #1 reason people fail)

**Child Care Center** — 391 NAC 3-006.15C:

| Age | Children per staff |
|---|---|
| 6 weeks – 18 months | 4:1 |
| 18 months – 3 years | 6:1 |
| 3 years | 10:1 |
| 4 and 5 years | 12:1 |
| School-age | 15:1 |

Compliance is determined **center-wide**. Only **direct-care staff** count in ratio. **At least two staff must be on premises at all times** except when ≤12 children are in care, or when all children are school-age and ≤15.

**Family Child Care Home I / II** use more restrictive ratios based on the home's license type and staffing.

**Warning:** Inspectors count heads on the spot. Do not round up. Staff must be **awake and alert at all times — including during naps** (391 NAC 3-006).

## What the inspector actually does when they walk in
1. Arrives unannounced (or for scheduled initial/provisional-to-operating visit). Shows DHHS ID.
2. Checks your posted license, roster, and Step Up to Quality rating card.
3. Walks every room and checks ratio center-wide.
4. Pulls staff files: **Nebraska State Patrol** criminal history + **FBI fingerprint** + **sex offender registry** + **child/adult central registry** checks, **Health Information Report**, director and teacher qualifications (3-006.04/05), orientation & training (3-006.08), report of any law enforcement contact.
5. Pulls child files: enrollment record, **immunization record** (within 30 days of enrollment per § 71-1913.01-03), health history, allergy plans, medication authorizations.
6. Reviews fire inspection, sanitation inspection, Emergency Action Plan, communicable disease notification policy.
7. Writes findings referencing specific 391 NAC sub-rules. Compliance report must be provided to the facility within **10 working days** of inspection.

## The 5 things that fail most inspections in Nebraska
1. **Ratio not met** (391 NAC 3-006.15C) — especially the 2-staff-on-premises rule.
2. **Immunization record not filed within 30 days** of enrollment (§ 71-1913.01-03).
3. **Health Information Report missing or outdated** on a staff member (3-006.03F).
4. **Background check gap** — State Patrol + FBI + sex offender + child/adult registry must all be current before unsupervised contact.
5. **Communicable disease notification failure** — parents must be notified **the same day** a reportable illness is observed or reported.

## Every child must have these papers in their file
- Enrollment record (name, DOB, parent contacts, emergency contacts)
- **Immunization record within 30 days of enrollment** (§ 71-1913.01-03; 173 NAC 4); updated at each new immunization
- Physician/APRN/PA certification of medical exemption OR parental non-immunization statement
- Health history & allergy / medical condition info
- Medication authorization and log
- Authorized pick-up list
- Attendance record
- Emergency Action Plan acknowledgment

## Every staff member must have these papers in their file
- Application & employment history
- **Criminal History Record Checks** — Nebraska State Patrol + FBI fingerprint + federal indices
- **Child/Adult Central Registry** + **Sex Offender Registry** checks (§§ 29-4001 – 29-4014)
- **Report of any law enforcement contact** (3-006.03C, 03D)
- **Repeat registry checks** (3-006.03E — periodic)
- **Health Information Report** (3-006.03F)
- Director qualifications documentation (3-006.04)
- Teacher qualifications — certificated or non-certificated (3-006.05)
- Substitute / volunteer / parent helper records (3-006.06)
- Orientation & training records (3-006.08)

## What must be on your walls
- Current license
- Step Up to Quality rating (if participating)
- Ratio chart
- Evacuation routes each room
- Emergency Action Plan summary
- Communicable-disease / exclusion policy
- Menu (current week)
- Complaint line: DHHS.LicensureUnit@nebraska.gov

## Drills and daily logs you can't skip
- **Fire drills** — per emergency plan, documented
- **Annual emergency drill** (at minimum)
- **Daily attendance**
- **Medication log**
- **Injury / incident log**
- **Communicable disease notifications** — same-day to families
- **Annual immunization report** filing (173 NAC 4)

## The 10-minute walk-through before the inspector arrives
1. Count kids and staff. Check 2-staff-on-premises unless at the <12 / school-age-<15 exemption.
2. Confirm license, ratio chart, Emergency Action Plan, exclusion policy posted.
3. Fire drill log — recent? Annual drill logged?
4. Open three staff files: State Patrol + FBI + sex-offender + child/adult registry dates, Health Information Report, training log.
5. Open three child files: immunization within 30 days, health history, allergy plan.
6. Cribs: backs only, firm mattress, one infant per crib, awake supervision at all times.
7. Lock chemicals and medications.
8. Menu posted; allergy plan visible.
9. Confirm communicable-disease notifications have gone out for any recent exposure.
10. Greet the inspector. Offer the file drawer key. Be calm and factual.

## What happens if you fail
DHHS may impose any of these on the monthly Negative Actions bulletin: **provisional license, suspension, emergency suspension, revocation, voluntary surrender, probation, denial, or a fine** (grounds enumerated in § 71-1919). Follow-up inspections within **60 days** verify correction (or sooner if the issue requires immediate correction — 391 NAC 3-005.04). Compliance reports are provided within 10 working days. Appeals go through a DHHS administrative hearing. Every action is posted in the **monthly ccnegactions PDF** (e.g., `03-26ccnegactions.pdf` for March 2026) and the **rolling 10-year archive PDF**. These are indexed and easy for any parent or reporter to find.

## The weird Nebraska gotcha nobody tells you about
Nebraska publishes a **monthly "Negative Actions Against Child Care Providers" PDF** on the DHHS Licensure site — posted around the 5th of each month, on a predictable URL pattern. That means any negative action you get shows up publicly as a **named PDF entry** within weeks. There's also a **10-year cumulative PDF** (2016–2026) that stays live. This is unusual transparency — most states hide disciplinary actions inside a per-licensee search; Nebraska publishes them in a bulletin. The other Nebraska-specific rule: **staff must be awake and alert at all times, including during naps** (no dozing with the kids). And **communicable disease notification to all enrolled families must go out the same day** any reportable illness is observed.
